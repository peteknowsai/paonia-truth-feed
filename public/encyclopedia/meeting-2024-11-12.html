Summary

Budget work session where the Verkada camera system was discussed. This is one of the "at least six public meetings" the board's response letter references. The meeting also included Pete McCarthy's public comments on the STR ordinance, submitted in writing and marked "PLEASE INCLUDE AS PUBLIC COMMENT FOR TONIGHT'S MEETING."

Key Moments

Verkada Camera Discussion (~15:17-17:30)

  • Wynn presents security camera plan covering: police department, back parking lot, hallway, town hall front, cashier room (overhead), parks (3 cameras: pavilion, facing state park, Smith Memorial center area), water plant, wastewater plant.
  • System identified as "Verkada" (transcribed as "verata"), cloud-based.
  • Data stored on Verkada's servers, minimum 30 days retention.
  • Budget: approximately $50,000 for all locations.
  • A trustee asks where the camera images go; Wynn explains cloud-based login system.

Budget (~later in session)

  • Cameras line item appears on page 72 of the budget document.
  • Discussion of $50,000 camera budget across multiple town facilities.

Pete's Written Comments

  • Pete submitted written public comments on the STR ordinance, marked for inclusion as public comment.

Significance

This meeting documents the Verkada discussion in a budget context. While cameras were "discussed," the transcript does not show a formal public hearing with citizen input on surveillance policy. The discussion is administrative (where cameras go, what they cost, how they store data), not a policy deliberation on whether the town should conduct surveillance.
