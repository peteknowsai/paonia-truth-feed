What Happens If Grant Funding Decreases?
Open question
Question69% of the town's budget comes from external grants and loans. What happens to town operations, staffing, and infrastructure projects if that funding decreases or becomes unavailable?

What the Record Shows

  • FY2026 budget: $16.16M total, only ~$4.6M own revenue. source
  • The mayor herself identified this vulnerability: public criticism "can place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies." source
  • The board characterizes the grants and loans as accomplishments to celebrate. source
  • Current federal policy (2025-2026) has included significant cuts to federal grant programs across multiple agencies.

What the Record Does Not Show

  • Whether the town has a contingency plan if grant funding decreases
  • What town services or positions depend on grant funding vs. own revenue
  • Which grants are one-time vs. recurring
  • Whether any current grants have conditions the town must maintain (e.g., staffing levels, reporting requirements)
  • What would happen to the 25.7 FTE positions if the budget contracted to own-revenue levels

Suggested CORA Requests

Request 1: Grant Inventory

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request a list of all active grants and loans held by the Town of Paonia as of the current date, including for each: the granting agency, amount, purpose, term/expiration, any conditions or covenants, and whether the funding is one-time or recurring.

Request 2: Contingency Planning

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request any documents, plans, or analyses prepared by or for the Town regarding fiscal contingency planning, revenue risk assessment, or operational continuity in the event of reduced grant or loan funding, for the period January 2023 to present.

Request 3: Grant-Funded Positions

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request a list of all town employee positions that are fully or partially funded by grants or external sources, including the funding source, percentage of salary covered, and the term of the funding.

Why This Matters

The mayor's own words acknowledge the dependency risk. If the town's financial health depends on maintaining the confidence of lenders, citizens should know what happens if that confidence wavers. A town that can only fund 28% of its budget from its own revenue is one grant cycle away from a fiscal crisis.
