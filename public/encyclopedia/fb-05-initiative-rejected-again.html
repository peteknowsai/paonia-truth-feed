Key Facts

  • Surveillance camera ban initiative submitted 2026-03-27, rejected 2026-04-03
  • This is the second citizen initiative rejected by Samira Vetter (Town Clerk). The first was a short-term rental initiative in May 2025.
  • Pattern: same clerk, same result, same "administrative" framework used to reject
  • Clerk's objections and McCarthy's responses:
    • Single-subject rule: Clerk says initiative covers surveillance cameras, contracts, body-worn cameras, and judicial remedies, violating single-subject. McCarthy argues these are standard components of a single ordinance about one subject (surveillance cameras on public property).
    • Body cam regulation / state law conflict: Clerk says initiative regulates body-worn cameras and conflicts with state law. Initiative Section 4(a) actually exempts body-worn cameras: "Body-worn cameras on law enforcement officers, governed by separate policy" are EXEMPT. McCarthy: "The Clerk is citing state preemption for a provision that says 'this doesn't apply to body cams.'"
    • STR initiative parallel: STR initiative said "no fees"; Clerk characterized it as "dictating fees." Camera initiative says "body cams exempt"; Clerk characterized it as "regulating body cams."
    • "Administrative function": Clerk says surveillance of public property is an administrative function citizens cannot legislate. McCarthy cites Colorado legislature passing laws restricting government surveillance (SB 22-113, HB 24-1130) and Article V, Section 1 of the Colorado Constitution guaranteeing citizen initiative power.
    • Voiding contracts: Clerk says voiding contracts that violate the ordinance is "administrative." McCarthy notes legislatures restrict government contracting routinely (procurement rules, prevailing wage, conflict-of-interest provisions).
    • Injunctive relief: Clerk says allowing citizens to seek injunctive relief is a "judicial function." McCarthy argues legislatures create causes of action; courts exercise judicial function by hearing them.
  • Two initiatives submitted, two rejected, zero allowed to reach voters
  • McCarthy filing formal response demanding acceptance within 5 business days
  • Will seek judicial review in Delta County District Court if refused
  • Resubmitting as separate single-subject petitions to remove the single-subject objection
  • Context: Town deployed $50,000 Verkada cameras with zero public comment, approved Daxbot sidewalk robots without public notice, called a special meeting within five days to respond to a flyer

Notable Quotes

"Same clerk. Same result. Same framework: call everything 'administrative' and refuse to let citizens vote."

"The pattern is: mischaracterize what the initiative says, then reject it based on the mischaracterization."

"Whether the government gets to point cameras at you in your town park is not an administrative detail. It's a fundamental policy question about the relationship between a government and its citizens."

"They can reject as many initiatives as they want. I'll keep submitting them."
