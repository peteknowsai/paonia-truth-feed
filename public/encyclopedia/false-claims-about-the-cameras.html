The Town Put False Claims About the Cameras in Writing

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
The Town Put False Claims About the Cameras in Writing
Analysis
Record
TypeAnalysis
First recorded2026-06-04
Topicssurveillance verkada cameras board-letter accountability

Most governing-body mistakes are spoken — a loose claim at a meeting, gone by the next agenda. This one is in writing, signed, and published on the Town's website. On March 30, 2026, all seven trustees signed an official "Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens" describing the Town's Verkada cameras. Two of its central claims are false, and the proof is in the Town's own records.

Claim one: "motion activated"

The letter called the cameras "motion activated." They are not. Verkada's own product documentation says the cameras record continuously, "unaffected by motion." The signed $50,288 contract specifies 30-day continuous retention. And the Town's audit log shows 21,245 live-stream views over six months — a usage pattern that only exists if the cameras are always on. Three independent sources, all the Town's own, contradict the single word the letter chose.

Claim two: "not face recognition"

The letter also said the system was "not face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." But Verkada lists facial recognition and photo-based person search as standard features of the platform the Town bought. The capability is documented by the vendor; the letter's denial is contradicted by the spec sheet of the product it describes.

The person who would have known

This was not a letter written in ignorance of the facts. The Administrator who coordinated it had signed the camera contract, held the highest permission level in the system, and queried the audit log four days before the letter published. The one person best positioned to know the cameras ran continuously was the person closest to the letter that called them motion-activated.

Cameras nobody voted for

The system itself arrived with no public mandate. In August 2025 the Board approved the 23-camera Verkada cloud-surveillance system unanimously, with zero public comment and no written policy governing who may watch, what is kept, or for how long. Then the logs showed the two-person clerk's office generated 53% of all camera activity. The Community Room camera — in the room where public meetings and public comment happen — was live-streamed 1,938 times in six months, 1,670 of them by a single Assistant Clerk, on a schedule that peaks on Sundays and after 5 p.m., when the Board does not meet and the office is closed.

The Board was told, in writing

These are not contradictions the Town has been left to discover on its own. On April 15, 2026, a resident filed written corrections with the Board, citing event codes and row counts straight from the Town's own audit log, and asked the trustees a direct question: was the March 30 letter accurate when it was published? The contradicting records are the Town's own — the contract it signed, the configurations it set, the audit log it produced. The letter has not been corrected.
