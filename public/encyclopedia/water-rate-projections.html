How Much Will Water Rates Increase?

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
How Much Will Water Rates Increase?
Open question
QuestionThe FY2026 budget states water rates "will need to continue to increase over time to support repayment of existing and future debt." How much, over what timeline, and what will residents actually pay?

What the Record Shows

  • $9.74M water infrastructure loan adds $263K/year in debt payments through 2045. source
  • The budget acknowledges rates must increase but does not quantify the increases.
  • The water tank rehab is currently ~$360K under the original $2.1M estimate, which is positive. source

What the Record Does Not Show

  • Projected water rate schedule for the next 5, 10, or 20 years
  • Current water rates vs. rates in comparable Colorado towns
  • What percentage of a typical household's bill goes to debt service vs. operations
  • Whether there is a rate study or consultant report guiding the increases
  • The interest rate on the $9.74M loan

Suggested CORA Requests

Request 1: Water Rate Projections

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request copies of any water rate studies, rate projection models, consultant reports, or internal analyses prepared by or for the Town of Paonia regarding projected water rate increases for the period 2024 through 2045, including any documents prepared in connection with the $9.74M water infrastructure loan.

Request 2: Loan Terms

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request a copy of the executed loan agreement(s) for the water infrastructure project(s) totaling approximately $9.74M, including all terms, interest rates, repayment schedules, covenants, and conditions.

Request 3: Current Rate Schedule

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request the current water rate schedule and any resolutions or ordinances establishing or modifying water rates since January 1, 2023.

Why This Matters

Every Paonia water customer will pay for this loan through 2045. Citizens are entitled to know the projected cost to their household and whether the town has a plan for managing the increases. This is basic fiscal transparency.
