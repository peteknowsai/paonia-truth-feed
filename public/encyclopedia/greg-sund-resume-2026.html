What the resume states

Credentials & education

  • ICMA Credentialed Manager since 2003 (ICMA member since 1998; affiliate from 1990).
  • Master of Public Administration, University of South Dakota (1989); B.S., Black Hills State University (1986).
  • Rotary District 5470 District Governor, 2023–2024.

Employment history (as listed)

Role Place Dates (per resume)
County Administrator Ellis County, Kansas 2010–2015
Public Works Director Hays, Kansas 2015–2017
City Manager Trinidad, Colorado 2017–2019
Interim City Administrator Walsenburg, Colorado 1/1/2020 – 11/30/2020
Interim Town Manager Telluride, Colorado 1/24/2022 – 4/15/2022
Interim Town Manager Mt. Crested Butte, Colorado 4/18/2022 – 12/31/2022
Interim Town Administrator Georgetown, Colorado 12/16/2024 – 4/15/2025

Signature accomplishment claimed

  • Early work on the purchase of the ~19,200-acre ranch south of Trinidad that became Fishers Peak State Park (Colorado's 42nd state park, announced September 12, 2019), working with the Trust for Public Land, The Nature Conservancy, Colorado Parks and Wildlife, and Great Outdoors Colorado. This is independently corroborated. See Greg R. Sund.

What the resume omits

The chronological list presents an unbroken management career, but it does not note the circumstances of two departures that are documented in the public record:

  • The Trinidad city manager role (2017–2019) ended with a resignation to avoid termination on September 5, 2019. Greg R. Sund
  • The Walsenburg interim role (2020) ended with an on-the-spot resignation during a December 2, 2020 council meeting after the council declined to extend his contract. Greg R. Sund

It also frames the post-2019 period as a series of senior appointments without noting that every position since 2019 has been interim/short-term, none converting to a permanent post (he was a finalist but not selected at Telluride in 2022 and Silt in 2024). Greg R. Sund
