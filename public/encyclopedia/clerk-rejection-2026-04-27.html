Full Text (transcribed from PDF, OCR-verified)

April 27, 2026

Peter McCarthy Melayna Rosa 119 ½ Dorris Ave Paonia, CO 81428

Re: Citizen Initiative Petition – Surveillance Policy Ordinance

Mr. McCarthy and Ms. Rosa,

I received your submission of a Citizen Initiative Petition for 'Paonia Short-Term Lodging Policy' on April 20, 2026.

The Ballot measure is hereby rejected. Please see the following explanation.

The subject of Surveillance on Public Property is a managerial and operational function of Town property, which is beyond the scope of citizen-initiated measures. Actions taken by a municipality to manage public spaces and rights-of-way, including monitoring and surveillance, are administrative in nature. The proposed ballot initiative seeks to control administrative actions by the Town, and the implementation of the proposal would require existing contractual obligations to be rescinded or modified. The formation, content, and amendment of contracts to which a Town is a party to further its policies are administrative matters, not suitable for an initiative.

Samira M Vetter, CMC Paonia Town Clerk

Defects on the Face of the Letter

1. Copy-paste error: rejects the wrong petition by name

The subject line says "Surveillance Policy Ordinance." The body opens by acknowledging receipt of "'Paonia Short-Term Lodging Policy'." These are entirely different petitions on different subjects. The body's opening sentence is a verbatim copy from a different rejection letter — almost certainly the April 13 STR rejection.

2. Statutory deadline missed

C.R.S. § 31-11-106 requires the Town Clerk to act within five business days of submission.

  • Submission: April 20, 2026 (Monday)
  • Five-business-day deadline: April 27, 2026 (Monday) at the latest
  • Rejection letter date: April 27, 2026 (last day, technically within the deadline)
  • Email transmission: April 28, 2026

The letter was dated the last possible day, but it was not transmitted on that date. Pete had to send a written demand on April 27 invoking the five-day deadline before the Clerk transmitted the rejection. The Clerk's apology email ("I mailed the letters as per statute and forgot to send the email") is itself an admission that electronic transmission lagged the statutory deadline.

3. Conclusory grounds, no caselaw

The entire substantive rejection is one paragraph that asserts:

  • Surveillance is "managerial and operational"
  • Surveillance is "administrative in nature"
  • Contract amendment is "administrative"

No Colorado caselaw is cited. No statutory authority is cited beyond the bare reference to the Colorado Constitution. The Clerk's theory now rests on a single repeated assertion — that the subject is "administrative" — without any analytical framework for distinguishing legislative from administrative action.

Theory Drift Across the Three Rejections

Rejection Date Primary theory used
#1 — original 2026-04-03 "Administrative function" (six grounds)
#2 — narrowed 2026-04-13 "Purely executive acts" (new label)
#3 — broadened policy 2026-04-27 "Managerial and operational function" (third label)

Each rejection uses a different doctrinal category to reach the same conclusion. The Clerk's theory of why the subject cannot be initiated has changed three times in 24 days while Pete's underlying subject (camera/surveillance policy) has not.
