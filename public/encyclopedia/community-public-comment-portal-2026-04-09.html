Key facts

  • The Town of Paonia operates an official online public comment portal at: https://townofpaonia.colorado.gov/board-meeting-agendas-and-minutes
  • The portal is the official channel for (1) requesting items be added to a BOT agenda, and (2) creating a documented public record tied to a specific meeting.
  • Comments submitted through the portal create a record the Town must reference and that can be cited in subsequent proceedings.
  • Ostrander recommends submitting through the portal and showing up to voice the same item during the public comment period — this pairs the written record with a verbal one.

Verbatim text

Hello Friends & Fellow Paonians 🌳,

I wanted to make sure you all aware, in case you are not already, that there is an official public comment portal for the Town of Paonia here https://townofpaonia.colorado.gov/board-meeting-agendas-and-minutes

It is very helpful for the purposes of adding items to BOT agenda meetings and documenting how, or if the Town is able to respond to public sentiment/ideas/concerns that comments are placed here and then also voiced at public hearings during public comment. This creates a public record that can be referenced and acted upon.

If anyone wishes to place a topic on the BOT agenda, please register it through this portal online and then please show up and ask for it in the public comment period during a meeting if you dont see it get placed on the agenda.

🗽 I will also bring forward the concerns you share with me. Utilizing this official public comment process at the same time will help us all move forward.

🎀 Please share this with your friends and neighbors and thank you for being mine!

👉 Also if you have not seen it already, Cedaredge which has a similar snowmelt fed water system has issued water restrictions as of last week.

Significance

The portal is a parallel track to the Rule 106 judicial review pathway. It puts the Trustees on notice in writing — they cannot later claim ignorance of the Clerk's pattern of rejecting citizen initiatives on shifting "administrative" theories. If the Trustees are formally informed via the portal and decline to instruct the Clerk to accept the surveillance initiative, that inaction becomes evidence of institutional bad faith, useful both politically and in the pending litigation.
