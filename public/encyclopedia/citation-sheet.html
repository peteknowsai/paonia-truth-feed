Summary

A one-page reference listing every factual claim from the "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator" flyer with its source. A separate 19-page sourced citation document with URLs and methodology exists.

Key Facts (with sources as cited)

  • Wynn fired unanimously from Neptune Beach Jan 2023 for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct." (News4Jax, WJCT, WOKV, First Coast News, Jacksonville.com)
  • Neptune Beach council accused Wynn of bullying staff, using profanity, storming out, turning off public Facebook comments. (Action News Jax, WOKV)
  • Wynn filed whistleblower lawsuit, Case No. 23-CA-8801, trial scheduled week of 2026-08-17. (Jacksonville.com, court records)
  • Wynn holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles. Prepares budget setting his own salary. $106K admin costs spread across four enterprise fund pages. (FY2026 Budget p.8)
  • Last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person, $480K was embezzled over five years (2007-2012). (9News, Denver Post, KVNF)
  • FY2026 fund balances projected to drop 23% ($5.4M to $4.1M). Bridge Reserve down 73.5%. (FY2026 Budget p.5)
  • FY2026 budget is $16.16M; only ~$4.6M is own revenue. $9.74M water loan adds $263K/year through 2045. (FY2026 Budget pp.4-5)
  • Water rates "will need to continue to increase." (FY2026 Budget p.5)
  • Staff: 19 to 25.7 FTE (+35%), admin up 57%, population up 1.1%, personnel costs up $530K (+28%). (FY2024/2025/2026 Budgets p.44, Census)
  • $50,288 on Verkada cameras. FTC fined Verkada $2.95M. 150K cameras breached in 2021. (FY2026 Budget p.7, FTC, High Country Spotlight)
  • Cameras approved unanimously with zero public comment, no surveillance policy. (Board minutes 2025-08-12)
  • Tree Board resigned citing frustration, liaison called "the town's spy." (Delta County Independent, 2025-10-09)
  • Comp Plan (2025-07-16) calls for urban forest investment; budget allocates $0. (Comp Plan p.42)
  • Capital: $1.02M generators, $500K meters, $250K squad cars, $196K vehicles, $85K sidewalk, $50K cameras, $35K repaving, $0 trees. (FY2026 Budget pp.68-76, 144)
  • Brunner elected April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7%), removed 4-1 after Wynn's conditional resignation. (KVNF)

Significance

This document is the factual backbone of the flyer and the subsequent board conflict. The board's response did not dispute any of the cited sources.
