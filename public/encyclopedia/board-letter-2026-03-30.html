Summary

A 27-citation formal complaint to the Board of Trustees documenting a pattern across four individuals who challenged or raised concerns about Town Administrator Stefen Wynn. The letter resurfaces an original complaint sent 2024-11-18 that received no response from any board member, and argues that the conduct standard applied to remove Trustee Brunner should be applied consistently to town staff.

Key Facts

  • Pete McCarthy sent a formal complaint to all board members on 2024-11-18 documenting statutory violations and retaliatory conduct by Wynn. Zero replies received.
  • Pete resigned from the Planning Commission on 2024-12-02, citing the board's silence. Zero replies received.
  • Four individuals identified as having challenged Wynn and faced consequences:
    1. Bill Brunner -- elected trustee, removed by 4-1 vote after Wynn submitted conditional resignation
    2. Pete McCarthy -- planning commissioner, hostile environment, complaint ignored, resigned
    3. Cory Heiniger -- Public Works Director, resigned with accusations against Wynn, not investigated
    4. Kaja Bowman -- Deputy Treasurer, approached mayor about complaint process, terminated 23 days later
  • The board defined "harassment" broadly enough to remove Brunner (asking questions, filing CORA requests, criticizing administration) but has not applied the same standard to Wynn or Vetter
  • Mayor Smith's agenda report for the 2026-03-30 special meeting called Pete's flyer "libelous"
  • The board proposed spending taxpayer money on display ads in three newspapers responding to Pete's flyer
  • Trustee Hunter's unsigned counter-flyer was coordinated with Wynn and Buchner per the mayor's report

Notable Quotes

  • Wynn called Pete's Planning Commission work "a waste of Town resources" (2024-11-14 email, Gmail ID: 1932c64a8b3ef824)
  • Wynn told Pete that Brunner had "nefarious ends" during the Brunner conflict period
  • Wynn: "I will not attend the December 2, 2024 meeting unless required to by the Board of Trustees"
  • Mayor's report: public criticism "can place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies"
  • Board's draft statement: "a few ill-willed players in Town harassing, bullying, and defaming Town Administrators"

Requests Made

  1. Written acknowledgment within 10 business days
  2. Written explanation of why the 2024-11-18 complaint received no response
  3. Formal investigation into the pattern of retaliation
  4. Consistent application of the Brunner conduct standard to Wynn and Vetter
  5. Confirmation of a functioning complaint process for the Town Administrator
  6. Review of Bowman's termination circumstances, including mayor's role

People Mentioned

  • Stefen Wynn -- Town Administrator, subject of the complaint
  • Samira Vetter -- Town Clerk, CORA obstruction and initiative rejection
  • Clayton Buchner -- Town Attorney, coordinated Hunter's counter-flyer, withheld legal analysis
  • Mayor Paige Smith -- disclosed complainant identity, called flyer "libelous"
  • Bill Brunner -- removed trustee, ongoing lawsuit
  • Kaja Bowman -- terminated Deputy Treasurer
  • Cory Heiniger -- resigned Public Works Director
  • Lucy Hunter -- unsigned counter-flyer, voted for Brunner removal

  • Board Oversight and Accountability -- failure to respond to complaints or investigate
  • Public Records Access (CORA) -- CORA obstruction, prior judicial finding of bad faith
  • Citizen Initiative Process -- rejection without adequate process
  • Pattern of Consequences for Raising Concerns -- four individuals, same outcome

Discrepancies Noted

  • The board characterized Pete's flyer claims as "misinformation and blatant falsehoods" but the board's response does not dispute the cited sources (adopted budgets, court records, news coverage). It reframes editorial conclusions as factual claims.
  • The mayor's report refers to the "anonymous author(s)" despite the board being aware Pete publicly claimed the flyer.
  • Hunter's rebuttal flyer was unsigned but treated as legitimate; Pete's signed, sourced flyer was called "libelous."
