Every dated event from the wiki, in chronological order. Sources linked for each entry.

2007-2012: The Embezzlement

  • A single Town of Paonia employee embezzled approximately $480,000 over five years. Financial authority had been concentrated in one person. (9News, Denver Post, KVNF) source

2020-01-06: Wynn Hired at Neptune Beach

  • Stefen Wynn begins employment as City Manager of Neptune Beach, Florida. source

2022: Neptune Beach Conflict

  • 2022-03-15: Wynn submits written complaint regarding suspected fraud in the building and permit department to City Auditors and City Council. source
  • 2022-08: Wynn reports police department budget overspending (over 100% of ad valorem budget). source
  • 2022-08-15: Wynn reports overspending and conflict of interest to Councilor Lauren Key. Key "harassed and verbally berated" Wynn at the City Council meeting that day. source
  • 2022-09-02: Wynn forced to apologize to Key. Told his apology was "halfhearted." Key stated she "welcomed his resignation." source
  • 2022-09-19: Wynn "lambasted" by Councilor Key at Council meeting, work performance "unjustly criticized." source
  • 2022-10-22: Wynn makes written complaint detailing harassment and claiming whistleblower status. source

2023: Firing, Lawsuit, and Paonia Hire

  • 2023-01-17: Neptune Beach City Council votes unanimously to fire Wynn. Stated grounds: "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city." Actual trigger cited: failure to ensure proper permitting for a water tower. (News4Jax, WJCT, WOKV, First Coast News, Jacksonville.com) source
  • 2023-05-16: Wynn files whistleblower retaliation lawsuit against Neptune Beach. Case No. 23-CA-8801, Duval County Circuit Court. source
  • 2023-07-11: Town of Paonia appoints Wynn as both Town Administrator and Town Treasurer. (Resolutions 2023-07 and 2023-08) source

2024: Brunner, McCarthy, and Oversight

  • 2024-04: Bill Brunner elected to Board of Trustees with 227 votes (67.7%). source
  • 2024-08-01: Wynn submits conditional resignation: he would stay only if Brunner was removed. source
  • 2024-08-13 to 2024-08-15: Brunner removal hearing. Mayor Smith serves as prosecutor. Charges: "harassment and abuse of position." Pete testifies that the harassment was reversed. event
  • 2024-08-15: Board votes 4-1 to remove Brunner. For: Stelter, Czech, Valentine, Hunter. Against: Swartz. event
  • 2024-08-30: Brunner announces plans to file lawsuit. (KVNF)
  • 2024-09-27: Pete files CORA request for M365 audit logs. source
  • 2024-09-30: Vetter denies the CORA request, claiming logs are "not in the Town's custody." source
  • 2024-09-30: Wynn forwards Pete's private policy critique to all trustees and commissioners with hostile editorial framing. source
  • 2024-10-31: Wynn forwards another of Pete's emails to everyone, publicly framing his comments as contradictory. source
  • 2024-11-04, 1:13 PM: Pete emails detailed questions to Wynn, requesting inclusion in that evening's meeting packet. Unable to attend due to flight delays. source
  • 2024-11-04, evening: At the Planning Commission meeting, Wynn says "oh we do have an email from Pete" as if discovering it for the first time. (YouTube video, timestamp ~13:41) source
  • 2024-11-05, 1:10 AM: Wynn forwards Pete's email to full Board and Planning Commission. Later claims he "spent a 14-hour day" preparing to address Pete's questions. source
  • 2024-11-12: Pete submits public comments on the STR ordinance, marked "PLEASE INCLUDE AS PUBLIC COMMENT FOR TONIGHT'S MEETING." Budget work session includes Verkada camera discussion. source source
  • 2024-11-14: Wynn emails Pete calling his work "a waste of Town resources," threatens to not attend meetings, uses his children to guilt Pete about a missed meeting. Forwarded to all trustees and commissioners. source
  • 2024-11-14: Wynn dismisses Pete: "Your role as a planning commissioner is advisory, not legislative." source
  • 2024-11-18, 7:49 AM: Pete files formal complaint to all 7 trustees. Subject: "Urgent: Statutory Violations and Retaliatory Conduct Requiring Immediate Board Action." Nine specific action items requested. Zero replies. source
  • 2024-11-20: Planning Commission work session. Wynn threatens to walk out when Pete asks process questions. Mayor and Wynn dismiss documentation requests in tandem. source
  • 2024-11-25: Pete contacts Jeff Roberts, Executive Director of CFOIC, about the CORA denial. Roberts responds same day: confirms Pete's legal position is correct and the CORA denial was improper. source
  • 2024-11-25: Pete appeals CORA denial to Wynn. Wynn: "I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to... even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform." source
  • 2024-12-02, 12:28 PM: Pete resigns from the Planning Commission. "Two weeks have passed since I submitted a detailed documentation of serious governance issues to this Board. Your complete silence speaks volumes." Zero replies. source
  • 2024-12-02: Commissioner Watson, at the meeting where the resignation is read, independently corroborates Pete's claims, reveals she was excluded from emails, accuses Wynn of slandering her to the Board. source

2025: Initiatives, Bowman, and Cameras

  • 2025-05-21: Pete submits citizen initiative petition on short-term rental regulations. event
  • 2025-05-28: Vetter rejects the initiative under C.R.S. 31-11-106(1). Rejection lacks specificity required by law. event
  • 2025-07-16: Town adopts Comprehensive Plan. INFRA GOAL 10 (p.42): "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees." source
  • 2025-08-12: Board approves Verkada cameras unanimously. Zero public comment. No surveillance policy adopted. source
  • 2025-09-16: Kaja Bowman approaches Mayor Smith at the request of several employees to ask about the complaint process involving the Town Administrator and HR. Mayor contacts Wynn, identifies Bowman by name, characterizes the inquiry as a complaint before Bowman says it is one. source
  • 2025-10-09: Bowman is terminated. 23 days after the inquiry. event
  • 2025-10-09: Delta County Independent reports Tree Board members resigned, felt "unheard for the past five years." Their liaison called himself "the town's spy." Public Works stopped meeting with them. source
  • 2025-10-22/23: Verkada cameras installed at Town Hall, Town Park, Public Works, water and wastewater plants. source
  • ~2025: Cory Heiniger resigns as Public Works Director with accusations against Wynn. Board does not investigate. source

2026 (March): The Flyer and Escalation

  • ~2026-03: Town Attorney Buchner's detailed legal analysis of the STR initiative, which existed at the time of rejection (May 2025), is released publicly. Nearly 10 months late. source
  • 2026-03-23: The "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator" flyer is created. The flyer PDF carries a CreationDate of March 23, 2026 in its metadata. The flyer carries McCarthy's name; every claim is cited to a public record. event
  • 2026-03-24, 11:07 AM MDT: The Town's own records internally name McCarthy as the flyer's author. An email originating in the Deputy Clerk's mailbox carries the subject "Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy"; the M365 audit log records the thread reaching leadership and trustee mailboxes within roughly the following hour. This is the earliest dated record in the set identifying the author, and it predates the Board's March 30 "anonymous author" characterization by six days. The finding rests on the audit-log subject line and timestamps; the message body is the subject of a pending records request. source
  • 2026-03-25: Pete distributes the "10 Things" flyer. event
  • 2026-03-26: Trustee Hunter posts unsigned counter-flyer throughout town. Coordinated with Wynn ("to ensure her flyer content was 100% accurate") and Buchner. source
  • 2026-03-27: Pete submits citizen initiative petition to ban surveillance cameras on public property.
  • 2026-03-30: Board holds special meeting to approve official response. Mayor's report calls flyer "libelous." Proposes taxpayer-funded display ads in three newspapers. Board votes 5-1 to publish. event
  • 2026-03-30: Pete sends 27-citation formal complaint letter to all trustees. source
  • 2026-03-30: Board publishes response without making the edits they voted to approve. "Erroneous and libelous" remains. source
  • 2026-03-31: Pete sends formal demand for retraction. 10 business day deadline. source
  • 2026-03-31: Trustee Hunter responds to the formal complaint in her individual capacity. Only board member to respond. Acknowledges complaint process gap. source
  • 2026-03-31: Board publishes edited version of the letter. "Erroneous and libelous," "smear campaign," "ill-willed players" removed. Substantive claims unchanged. source

2026 (April): Initiatives and Legal Action

  • 2026-04-03: Vetter rejects the surveillance camera initiative on six grounds. event
  • 2026-04-03: Pete submits formal response rebutting all six grounds, demands acceptance within 5 business days. source
  • 2026-04-06: Pete files CORA request for all communications about the "libelous" characterization, all drafts of the board's response, and all legal advice from Buchner on the use of "libelous." source
  • 2026-04-06: Pete resubmits revised, narrowed surveillance initiative with disputed provisions removed. Three split single-subject initiatives prepared. Rule 106 petition drafted. source source source
  • 2026-04-07: Election day.

2026 (May): CORA Productions and the Naming Record

  • 2026-05-08: The Town produces Wynn's November 4, 2024 outbound emails (CORA C 26-15) as a 165-page PDF. Several outbound messages are produced without their "Sent:" timestamp; PDF metadata shows the file was assembled in Microsoft Word. source
  • 2026-05-26: Deputy Clerk Santiago's response to the Finance Committee records request (CORA C 26-23) redirects to Board-packet payment-approval reports and produces no minutes, agendas, sign-in sheets, or logs, and no § 24-72-204(3) non-existence certification. On May 27 Vetter states on the record, "I don't believe it is written down anywhere." Closed without addressing five of six requested categories. source
  • 2026-05-27: The Town delivers the C 26-12 cure — the corrected M365 unified audit-log production. The cure cover letter admits four monthly exports had been missing from the April production, narrows the AuditData redactions from a 100% column-wide blanket to technical identifiers, and waives any additional charge. The cure data (retrieved and analyzed May 29) totals 10 uncapped CSVs, roughly 1.13 million rows, with AuditData largely un-redacted. The logged inbox-rule activity is benign: the bursts are ModifyMailboxRule resyncs for which Microsoft records no rule body, and every logged rule action is a move-to-folder with internal-only forwarding — no suppression mechanism appears in the data. The live question is now the scope and legality of the remaining redactions. source source source
  • 2026-05-27: The Town produces native .eml files for the Nov 4, 2024 Wynn emails (CORA C 26-21, follow-up to C 26-15). Timestamps are recovered for two of the four targeted emails; the other two productions are wrong-direction substitutions (inbound messages tagged MessageDirectionality: Incoming). source
  • 2026-05-28: Pete files a CORA request for communications regarding Town Administrator Wynn's non-renewal, communications with former Mayor Mary Bachran, and related executive-session records (period May 1–27, 2026). Acknowledged by Vetter May 29; stated deadline June 3. A parallel CCJRA request for police records concerning Wynn is filed the same day.
  • 2026-05-29 to 2026-05-30: A batch of records requests built from the C 26-12 audit-log subjects is sent: the flyer response-letter drafting records (the Mayor's "I need comments /no later than 2:00" thread, the draft Mayor's report, the "Revised Letter to Citizens" with retained drafts); the special-meeting / Planner-task / flyer-"safety" thread (including the internal Microsoft Planner task titled "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer"); a Verkada audit-log continuation; and Wynn-authored "threat/safety" communications. The "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer" task title is a second internal record naming McCarthy as the flyer's author. source source

What the Timeline Shows

The tempo tells the story without editorializing:

Response to formal complaints: 16 months, zero replies from any board member (except Hunter, 2026-03-31, after the flyer forced the issue).

Response to a citizen flyer: 5 days to special meeting, official statement, proposed taxpayer-funded newspaper ads.

Pattern across individuals:

  • Brunner: 3 months from election to removal (April-August 2024)
  • McCarthy: 2 weeks from complaint to silence; 2 more weeks to resignation (November-December 2024)
  • Bowman: 23 days from inquiry to termination (September-October 2025)
  • Heiniger: resigned with accusations, uninvestigated (2025)

Initiative process: Two submitted, two rejected, zero allowed to reach voters (May 2025, April 2026).
