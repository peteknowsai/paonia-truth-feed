Summary

Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

Date: Not explicitly stated (references a comment deadline of "this Friday the 22nd") Absent: Trustee John (surname unclear), Trustee Schwarz arrived late or remote

Key Decisions

  • BLM Oil & Gas Leasing Rule Comment Letter: Motion to approve the letter drafted by Citizens for a Healthy Community (CHC) with board-suggested amendments. Passed unanimously.
  • Audit RFP - Hinkle Company: Motion to accept the sole qualifying proposal from the Hinkle Company (Englewood, CO) for fiscal year 2022 audit at $25,000 flat fee (travel included), with option to continue for five years with small annual escalator. Passed unanimously.

Discussion Highlights

  • BLM Oil & Gas Leasing Rule: Natasha (executive director, Citizens for a Healthy Community) presented concerns about the Bureau of Land Management's proposed oil and gas leasing rule. The rule designates the upper North Fork as "high developmental potential" with no guardrails for lands that also have recreational, wildlife, cultural, or agricultural value. The board discussed amendments to the comment letter:
    • Add "agricultural" values alongside recreational throughout
    • Change "impacts" to "effects" (to align with NEPA terminology)
    • Change "and" to "or" in the geological hazard bullet (to broaden rather than narrow the protective language)
    • Add "production and pipelines" to the first bullet about oil and gas development
    • Add language about watershed and agricultural water concerns, including the river
    • Highlight that local agriculture serves a broader regional area, not just Paonia
  • Pipeline concerns: Board members noted the existing pipeline is at or near capacity, meaning new development would require additional pipeline infrastructure through landslide-prone terrain, with risks of methane spills and contamination of agricultural water.
  • Audit RFP: Town administrator reported significant difficulty finding an audit firm -- went out on bid net, directly solicited firms, and asked the state auditor for help. Received multiple declinations before the Hinkle Company responded. The audit is expedited to address fiscal year 2022 compliance by end of December. A "single audit" will likely be needed next year due to federal grant dollars for the Fifth and Grand project. Town lacks internal staff capacity to prepare audit materials and is relying on a third-party firm already engaged. The finance committee's role in the engagement conclusion was discussed.

Public Comment

  • No public comments were noted during either agenda item.

Notable Quotes

  • Natasha (CHC) on lease duration: "When a lease is issued, it's an option for the operator to show that they can produce within 10 years... however if they can show that they can produce within 10 years, that lease goes into perpetuity. So the life of a natural gas well is anywhere from 20 to 30 years."
  • Trustee (on agricultural impact): "It's not just 'oh we're a small town and we're worried about our food' -- it's no, we're a small town and worried about the food that goes to the surrounding area."
  • Town administrator on audit search: "I even pulled in favors with KPMG and their audit team actually said no."
