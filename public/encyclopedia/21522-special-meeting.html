Summary

Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

Date: Approximately February 8, 2023 (based on context -- discussion references Jan 30 protest period ending, Feb 22 deadline, and April 5 election) Format: Zoom

Key Decisions

  • Motion to amend agenda to add possible executive session for legal advice on the initiative -- passed (unanimous voice vote)
  • Motion to continue consideration of the initiated ordinance to the February 22nd meeting -- passed (unanimous voice vote)
  • Motion to enter executive session for conference with town attorney on legal questions related to the initiated ordinance (CRS 24-6-402(4)(b)) -- passed (voice vote, at least one "aye" in opposition heard but motion carried)

Discussion Highlights

  • Attorney Jeff Conklin presented his review of a citizen-initiated ordinance submitted by a petitioners' committee (Bill Bruner and Jeanette Bruner). The ordinance was filed with signatures on December 21; the protest period ended January 30. The board must act by February 22 or the ordinance goes to a special election (60-150 days out).

  • Conklin identified multiple provisions in conflict with state law, including:

    • Provisions attempting to create municipal court jurisdiction over ordinance disputes -- not permissible for a statutory town (jurisdiction set by state legislature in Title 13)
    • Provisions waiving governmental immunity -- conflicts with Colorado Governmental Immunity Act
    • Some provisions conflicting with the Colorado Open Records Act (CORA)

  • Provisions Conklin found potentially lawful: The ordinance's general concept of narrowing the town's discretion to withhold public records related to infrastructure and financial records could be accomplished through proper legislation.

  • The board cannot cherry-pick provisions -- they must adopt the ordinance as-is or refer it to voters in its entirety.

  • Trustee Knutson asked whether the petitioners' committee can amend the ordinance without going back to signers. Conklin said he's still researching that question.

  • Trustee Knutson asked whether other items could be added to a special election ballot if triggered. Conklin said yes, depending on the type of issue.

  • Trustee Bettinger asked about separating the CORA provisions from the municipal court/lawsuit provisions. Conklin suggested reserving procedural options for executive session.

  • Trustee Mack advocated for transparency measures beyond the ordinance: an ad hoc committee to review the town's records policy, posting violations on the website, and improving trust through proactive disclosure.

  • Trustee Smith noted the board is now between "a rock and a hard place" with only one week left before the deadline, and insufficient time to draft an alternative ordinance.

  • Bill Bruner (petitioner) explained the ordinance's intent: financial and infrastructure data (including engineering analysis) should not be shielded by work-product exemptions, citing the JDS Hydro drafts where "the whole report" was redacted. He expressed openness to compromise but said he won't withdraw "for a memo that says we'll try to do better." He suggested the board could adopt the ordinance as-is and then amend it after negotiation.

Public Comment

  • Mr. Marvel -- Supported the ombudsman concept, noting it has a long history in government. Said the board has had since August to address these issues and has delayed.
  • Stephen Keenan -- Urged more community involvement and audiovisual access (was redirected to stay on topic).
  • Ms. Watson -- Argued the town is not as transparent as claimed, citing improperly redacted liquor license applications and municipal candidate addresses. Supported adopting the ordinance as-is to force action: "if Bill just backs down you're going to be putting them off again and again."
  • Ms. Patterson -- Thanked Conklin for his public review. Noted the board discussed establishing clear CORA cost standards back in August but never followed through. Urged the board to create written guidelines regardless of the ordinance's fate.
  • Ms. Rosen -- Thanked Bruner and Watson, said her review of the town's water department records raised immediate concerns about transparency.

Notable Quotes

Jeff Conklin (town attorney): "To the extent that the proposed ordinance is in conflict with the open records act or state law or the Colorado Constitution, those are simply things that in my view, even if adopted, would be unlawful."

Bill Bruner (petitioner): "I've begged for communication and gotten none and we're kind of in a corner here. Compromise is certainly possible but I'm loath to just let go of everything for a song and a promise at this point."

Dave Knutson (trustee): "When a government says it'll do something, it will -- you don't have to remind it every six months."
