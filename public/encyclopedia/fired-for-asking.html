She Asked One Question. 23 Days Later, Fired.

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
She Asked One Question. 23 Days Later, Fired.

In September 2025, Deputy Treasurer Kaja Bowman went to Mayor Paige Smith on behalf of several coworkers and asked a single question: was there a process for raising a concern about the Town Administrator and HR? She had not filed a complaint. She was asking how.

The same day, her name went to the person she asked about

That same day, the Mayor took the question to Town Administrator Stefen Wynn — the very official the inquiry was about — and named Bowman. An employee who asked, in confidence, how to raise a concern found her name delivered to the subject of that concern within hours. The thing that protects people who report problems is the assurance that asking will not be turned against them. Here, asking was turned against her immediately.

Twenty-three days later

Twenty-three days after she asked, Bowman was terminated "without cause." The Town has never explained the basis for the firing. "Without cause" is a legal posture, not an account of what happened — and the timeline it sits inside is its own kind of explanation.

She was not the only one

Cory Heiniger, the Public Works Director, had already resigned quietly, with accusations against the administration that the Board did not investigate. Bowman's firing is one entry in a documented pattern: raise a concern about the administrator, and your time in town government ends. Each case has a separate official explanation. The pattern is what the explanations don't cover.

Why this matters

A government that punishes the act of asking how to raise a concern has closed the safest, quietest door an employee has. What is left after that door closes is silence — which is exactly what the record shows the Town received from everyone who watched what happened to her.
