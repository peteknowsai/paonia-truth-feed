The Case Against Mayor Smith

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
The Case Against Mayor Smith

A recall is the most direct tool residents have when the people in office stop answering to them. The recall now circulating is aimed at one official in particular — Mayor Paige Smith — and the reason is not a single decision anyone could chalk up to a bad day. It is a pattern, documented in the Town's own records, of using the power of the office against the residents it was meant to serve. Every fact below comes from public documents.

She handed an employee's name to the person she asked about

In September 2025, Deputy Treasurer Kaja Bowman went to Mayor Smith on behalf of several coworkers and asked a single question: was there a process for raising a concern about the Town Administrator and HR? She had not filed a complaint. She was asking how. The same day, the Mayor took that question to Town Administrator Stefen Wynn, named Bowman, and characterized her inquiry as a complaint before Bowman had ever called it one. Twenty-three days later, Bowman was terminated. The Town has never explained the basis for the firing.

She prosecuted the removal of the trustee voters had just elected

In April 2024, voters elected Bill Brunner to the Board with 67.7% of the vote. Months later, after Brunner began asking questions and filing records requests, Wynn submitted a resignation he would withdraw only if Brunner were removed. Mayor Smith brought the charges and served as the prosecutor at the removal hearing. On a 4–1 vote, the Board undid the choice the electorate had just made. The standard she prosecuted — that an official who targets another through misuse of office should be removed — is the same standard the record now raises against her.

She branded a resident's sourced facts "libelous"

In March 2026 a resident distributed a flyer citing the Town's own budgets, court records, and published news coverage. Mayor Smith's own agenda report to the Board called it "libelous." She wrote the Town's official response — the "Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens" — which branded the criticism "erroneous and libelous," brought it to the Board, and voted to publish it. A line-by-line review of that response found the Board disputed none of the flyer's underlying cited sources. She also proposed spending taxpayer money on paid display ads in three newspapers to answer it; the Board ultimately backed away from the ads.

The same letter misled residents about the cameras

That March 30 letter did something else. It told residents the Town's Verkada cameras were "motion activated" and "not face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." Both claims are false, and the proof sits inside the Town's own records: Verkada's product documentation, the signed $50,288 contract, and the Town's own audit logs all show the cameras record continuously. The evidence has been on the public record for weeks. The letter has never been corrected.

Why the recall, and why her

A mayor who exposes an employee's name for asking a question, prosecutes the removal of the colleague voters elected, and brands residents' documented criticism "libelous" has used the office against the public rather than for it. Mayor Smith has said her most important duty is to "protect the town staff." The records show what that has meant in practice. The Board has not corrected any of it, because the Board is part of it. The voters can. A recall puts the question exactly where it belongs — to the people of Paonia.
