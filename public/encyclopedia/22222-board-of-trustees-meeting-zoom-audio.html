Summary

Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

Date: February 22, 2022 (based on context clues: references to Feb 28 Monday meeting, March 10 next meeting, May 3 election)

Key Decisions

  • Consent agenda approved with corrections to prior meeting minutes (unanimous). Corrections included: misplaced C-DOT motion, clarification that "work session and special meeting" was one amended motion not two separate votes, request to reinstate roll-call vote tables for non-unanimous votes, spelling fix ("rescinds"), and addition of missing election motion.
  • Mayor directed to investigate AARP Community Challenge Grant options for Apple Valley Park improvements (passed, unanimous). Possible projects: walking path rehab, drainage fixes, skatepark area fencing removal, dark skies compliance.
  • Feb 28 work session: public questions to be submitted in writing to the mayor, who will ask them of the SGM expert presenter (passed). Intended to shield consultants from direct public cross-examination.
  • Archive Social subscription cancelled; town Facebook page retained (passed, unanimous). $3,000/year archiving service deemed not worth the cost. Tree Board Facebook page also approved for deletion.
  • Citizens' initiative referred to May 3 special election (passed). The initiative concerns transparency, CORA records access, and trustee access to facilities.
  • Attorney directed to draft a narrower ordinance addressing two issues -- disclosure of infrastructure/finance records and trustee access to records/facilities -- for consideration at the Feb 28 meeting (passed). If adopted, petitioner Bill Brunner indicated willingness to withdraw the broader initiative.
  • Ad hoc committee created to review town records policy for CORA compliance (passed). Volunteers: Trustee Mech, Trustee Smith.
  • Advisory Water Committee ordinance (01-2022) adopted with amendments: removal of second "whereas" clause referencing inapplicable statute (CRS 31-35-501), and striking of last two sentences in Section 2-10-40 regarding Robert's Rules restrictions on committee chair voting (both amendments passed).
  • SSG contract extension approved for month-to-month continuation through April (passed). Covers gap in water plant operations staffing.
  • JDS Hydro report discussion continued to March 10 meeting when JDS representative will be present (passed).
  • Filter Tech repair proposal approved for Lamborn Laser water treatment plant skid #2 repair, not to exceed 10% over quoted amount (~$8,000 + contingency) (passed).
  • Multi-jurisdictional hazard mitigation plan participation and grant support letter approved (passed).
  • IT/sound system audit continued to next meeting (passed).
  • Attorney directed to prepare liability waiver for three individuals who toured the water plant without authorization, indemnifying SSG, Josh Moore, and the Town (passed).
  • Disbursements approved: Accounts payable $61,656.77; loan payment $74,000; payroll $18,106.51; payroll taxes $7,718.90.

Discussion Highlights

  • Moment of silence held for "Dave," a community member who lived on Third Street.
  • New hybrid meeting system debuted with YouTube streaming and Zoom participation; technical issues noted (no video of podium on feed, no audio on YouTube initially).
  • School crossing safety: Tracy Moff reported that during her 5-week absence, no one covered the school crossing near the old Subway building. Children were crossing alone. She crosses at 7:30-8:00 AM and 3:25-4:00 PM. Volunteers needed.
  • Water tank estimates: Thomas Markle reported obtaining estimates for a new water tank, discussed at the Advisory Water Committee. He clarified he did not represent himself as associated with the town when gathering information.
  • ORC RFP published on the town website, through CML, state and national rural water associations. Submittal deadline: March 21, 2022.
  • New public works hire: Corey Heineker started on probationary basis as public works director. Has crew chief, project management, and SCADA experience. Dennis Rich (27-year town employee) gave positive review.
  • Police staffing: Officer Heineker (Corey's son Caden) leaving the town; his POST training costs to be reimbursed per contract. Two candidates interested but no applications received yet.
  • Tier 2 CDPHE notice sent to residents regarding missed chlorine and turbidity monitoring readings on January 1 and January 17. No public danger reported.
  • Water plant tour controversy dominated the meeting. Trustees Smith and Mech visited the water treatment plant without going through the town administrator. SSG (contractor Brianna Greer) sent a letter expressing serious concerns about liability, unauthorized access, and contractor employees being put in untenable positions. Trustee Paid resigned during the heated discussion. Extensive debate over proper protocols, trustee authority, contractor liability, and chain of command. Trustee Smith apologized for the process but stated the tour was invaluable and advocated for formalized tour procedures going forward.
  • SGM/JDS Hydro collaboration: The two firms are preparing a joint proposal for the board covering capital improvement design, construction standards, SOPs, and other support beyond staffing.
  • Treasurer position remains vacant (voluntary role). Discussion needed on whether to appoint someone or delegate duties to finance director.
  • Environmental stewardship concern: Trustee raised issue of blue snowmelt being dumped over storm drains and onto private plants, requesting better training for public works staff.
  • Audit deficiencies: Discussion about whether corrective actions from the 2021 audit have been completed and whether third-party verification is needed. Training recommended by auditor was a suggestion, not a requirement.

Public Comment

  • Thomas Markle (Third Street) -- Reported water tank estimate research; urged civility and kindness, comparing community treatment to water quality: "the words and energy that some people bring to these meetings is polluted."
  • Tracy Moff (Royal Road) -- Urgent plea for school crossing guard volunteers; children crossing unsupervised for five weeks.
  • Suzanne Watson -- Asked about the ORC RFP status; later expressed discomfort with email discussions happening outside public meetings; supported Apple Valley Park improvements.
  • Mr. Greene -- Presented tongue-in-cheek "medals of valor" to Trustees Mech and Paid for visiting the water treatment plant.
  • Mr. Keenan -- Called for improved civility; requested audio-visual equipment for public presentations; asked about alternative school building access with Delta County School District.
  • Mr. Patterson (Rio Grande) -- Spoke on behalf of the Red Feather Bowmen Association (established 1962), who are getting pushback from trail users at their longstanding outdoor shooting area at the river bottom, due to the town's trail routing decisions.
  • Bill Brunner -- Confirmed willingness to withdraw his citizens' initiative if the board adopts a narrower ordinance addressing records disclosure and trustee access; expressed concern about mayor potentially censoring written questions at the Feb 28 work session.
  • Mr. Brooke -- Urged mayor to faithfully present all public questions without censorship at the Feb 28 meeting.
  • Brianna Greer (SSG contractor) -- Delivered forceful statement about liability exposure from unauthorized plant tours; demanded the board clarify access protocols and requested liability waivers; stated Josh Moore was never authorized to give tours; noted her personal livelihood is at risk.
  • Chris Kendall (Minnesota Creek Road) -- Pointed out that SSG's contract already includes "Task 50: Board of Trustee Interaction" covering up to four hours of meetings per month; questioned roof repair invoice showing unusual hours.
  • Miss Patterson -- Asked whether the town could simply leave parks alone instead of constantly pursuing grants and improvements: "What is this chase after free money?"

Notable Quotes

Thomas Markle, on community discourse:

"What if the threat of losing a sense of community and kindness and civility felt as pressing as the thought of losing our water? To me the way that we treat each other is just as important to our happiness, to our long-term survival, as the water we drink."

Trustee Knutson, on consultant interactions with the public:

"One thing that's very embarrassing for an expert when they come to Paonia and they try to deliver expert opinions to the board -- they are grilled by our public. It is very off-putting because it tends to get the expert ground into the politics of where we are."

Bill Brunner, on the citizens' initiative and transparency:

"These citizens' petitions don't happen in a vacuum. They're happening for a reason. There's a lot of trust that has to be rekindled and there's a lot of transparency that has to happen. That's why these things are happening -- things are not working, so this is the recourse."
