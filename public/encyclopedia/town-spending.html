How the Town of Paonia spends public money, who controls the spending, and whether citizens have adequate visibility into the decisions.

Budget Overview (FY2026)

  • Total budget: $16.16M
  • Own revenue: ~$4.6M (28%)
  • Grants and loans: ~$11.5M (72%)
  • Fund balances: Projected to drop 23% in one year ($5.4M to $4.1M)
  • Source: FY2026 Adopted Budget, pp.4-5. source

Staffing

  • FY2024: ~22 budgeted FTE (some sources say 19, likely different baseline year)
  • FY2026: 25 regular full-time + 2 seasonal Parks + 1 part-time Municipal Judge (~25.7 FTE)
  • Admin positions grew 57%
  • Personnel costs rose $530K (+28%)
  • Population grew 1.1% in the same period
  • Sources: FY2024/2025/2026 Adopted Budgets p.44, U.S. Census. source

Board's position: Trustees approve staffing. Most hires filled existing vacancies. Infrastructure projects require adequate staff. source

Pete's position: Staff grew 6x faster than population. The question isn't whether the hires were approved, but whether the growth is proportionate. source

Financial Authority Structure

  • Wynn holds both Town Administrator and Town Treasurer roles (Resolutions 2023-07, 2023-08).
  • He prepares the budget that includes his own salary. $106K in admin costs allocated across four enterprise fund pages. source
  • The board says the Administrator "does NOT determine his own salary" and that trustees conduct performance reviews. source
  • Internal controls: Caselle government accounting software, Professional Management Solutions (third-party), Finance Committee (Mayor + one Trustee review invoices). source

Historical context

  • 2007-2012: A single employee embezzled approximately $480,000 over five years when financial authority was concentrated in one person. (9News, Denver Post, KVNF) source
  • The board says current controls make a repeat impossible. Pete says the structural risk (one person at the center) is the same; controls are processes that can be overridden by the person who implemented them.

What the Record Does Not Show

  • Line-by-line comparison of actual spending vs. budgeted amounts for prior fiscal years
  • How the $106K in admin costs breaks down across the four enterprise fund pages
  • Whether the Finance Committee has ever flagged or rejected a disbursement
  • How Wynn's salary compares to administrators in comparable Colorado towns
  • Whether the embezzlement-era controls were considered adequate at the time
