Is There a Functioning Complaint Process?

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
Is There a Functioning Complaint Process?
Open question
QuestionDoes the Town of Paonia Board of Trustees have a functioning process for receiving and acting on complaints about the Town Administrator?

What the Record Shows

  • Pete McCarthy's formal complaint (2024-11-18) documenting specific statutory violations received no response from any board member for 16+ months.
  • Pete's resignation letter (2024-12-02) citing harassment and retaliation received no response.
  • Cory Heiniger's resignation with accusations against Wynn was not investigated.
  • Kaja Bowman asked Mayor Smith whether there was a process for making a complaint on 2025-09-16. Instead of answering the process question, the mayor disclosed Bowman's identity to Wynn. Bowman was terminated 23 days later.

What Could Answer This

  • Town code or board policies regarding complaint procedures
  • Board meeting minutes discussing any of the above complaints
  • Any written response to any of the complaints documented above

Partial Response

On 2026-03-31, Trustee Hunter responded to Pete's formal complaint in her individual capacity. She is the only board member to respond. Key points from her response:

  • Acknowledged receipt and stated she takes the contents seriously
  • Agreed a complaint process should exist: "A municipality should have a clear, documented, and accessible process that ensures complaints of this nature are received by the Board directly, not routed through the subject of the complaint."
  • Stated she intends to raise this as a matter requiring the Board's attention
  • Could not commit to a specific outcome or timeline on behalf of the Board

source

Status

Partially addressed. One board member (Hunter) has acknowledged the gap and committed to raising it. No Board-level action documented as of 2026-04-06. The formal complaint letter's request for "confirmation that the Board has a functioning process" remains unanswered by the Board as a body.
