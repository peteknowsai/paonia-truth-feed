What was said

McCarthy read a prepared statement and then announced a recall. The statement, facts-first:

  • Greg Sund's last permanent job was Trinidad city manager; in September 2019 he resigned to avoid being fired. Every position since has been a short-term interim (Walsenburg, Telluride, Mt. Crested Butte, Georgetown). In Walsenburg he resigned mid-meeting the night the council voted against keeping him.
  • A Trinidad department head's written complaint states Sund told the town's finance director, "this is not a democracy."
  • The churn is the Colorado norm: Ouray's council hoped to end a "merry-go-round of chief executive officers"; the Delta County Independent called Paonia "the fifth administrator in six years." See The Revolving Door: A Canvass of Manager Turnover in 20 Colorado Towns.
  • On process: the Board has known since May 8 that Wynn was leaving, yet produced a single-candidate special meeting with the packet posted the day before. "Why the rush? Why is there only one name?"

The recall announcement

McCarthy announced he is filing a petition to recall Mayor Paige Smith, and — because a Colorado municipal recall ballot names a successor — put his own name forward as the replacement candidate, framing it as a willingness to be accountable rather than only to criticize.

Significance

The statement put Sund's record and the single-candidate process on the meeting record before the Board's discussion. The concerns it raised (no slate, no reference checks, 24-hour notice) were then echoed by Trustee Foster, and the motion to hire died for lack of a second. source
