Summary

Meeting focused on the Comprehensive Plan update, specifically new elements (health/environment, zoning) and the Housing Element. Pete McCarthy submitted written comments via email but was unable to attend due to flight delays. His email and its handling became a recurring theme throughout the meeting.

Key Moments

~13:02 - The Email Discovery (VIDEO EVIDENCE)

This is the critical moment referenced in the Reporter Package. Stefen Wynn appears to discover Pete's email for the first time during the meeting, despite it being sent at 1:13 PM that day.

Before the meeting is called to order, a commissioner mentions receiving a text about Pete's emails. The exchange reveals confusion and possible non-distribution of Pete's written comments:

"So I got some kind of a text today about emails that Pete sent some emails and I don't have anything from him"

"Did people get emails from Pete?"

Then at approximately 13:41, Wynn's reaction:

"Oh we do have an email from Pete. So it says, due to work travel and weather related flight delays I'll be unable to attend tonight's meeting. I respectfully request the town staff print and include these comments in tonight's packet for discussion."

The "oh we do have an email" phrasing suggests the email was not distributed to commissioners in advance, despite Pete sending it hours earlier with explicit instructions to include it in the packet. Staff then scrambles to print it.

~16:01 - Waiting for Pete's Comments

While Wynn prints Pete's comments, the commission waits:

"So I'm kind of waiting for Pete, that's you know his comments are him and he obviously guess he's not on... it didn't sound like he could join electronically either."

~17:01 - Roll Call

Roll call confirms: Watson (present), Howe (present), Smith (present), McCarthy (absent, noted via email), Hunter (present). Quorum established.

~18:07 - Chair Sets Tone, References Civility

Watson addresses the packed agenda and references Pete's concerns:

"Some of that was put there, what I thought, to address some of Pete's concerns. Indeed I think we all probably have them, but it would be nice to be here inclusive to talk about some of this."

Watson then pivots to a civility reminder:

"Civility. I just want to remind people that especially the Board of Trustees, you have a code of conduct and personal attacks are not permitted."

~19:42 - Housing Element Deferred for Pete

The commission acknowledges they should wait for Pete to discuss the housing element:

"The housing element we have Pete's comments on but with him not being here he had identified... yeah I would really like to see him here for that too."

Watson makes a motion to defer agenda item one "until the next meeting when Pete can be here." Motion passes.

~20:32 - Multiple Commissioners Want Pete Present

"It would be nice if Pete were here."

~24:49 - Pete's Housing Concerns Acknowledged

During discussion of the comprehensive plan process:

"Pete's things there, I just don't know if the public is going to look at these things in pieces."

~1:52:45 - Land Use Code Review

During later discussion of the housing element contents:

"That's actually one of the ones that Pete did like."

~2:52:23 - Scheduling Next Meeting Around Pete

Discussion of scheduling the next work session considers Pete's availability:

"Pete, I don't think he was totally satisfied with the Mondays, so on this you want used to do Wednesday?"

~3:06:54 - Closing, Interest in Pete's Input

"Interested to what Pete has to say."

Relevance to Wiki Issues

  • Email Transparency: The handling of Pete's email is central evidence. An email sent hours before the meeting was apparently not distributed to commissioners. The "oh we do have an email" moment suggests either negligence or deliberate suppression of written public comments.
  • Housing Element: Pete's written comments on the housing element were a significant agenda item, deferred to the next meeting (November 20) so he could be present.
  • Comprehensive Plan: Broader discussion about community outreach failures, underrepresented community members not being reached.
  • Planning Commission Authority: The commission is doing substantive review work but appears dependent on staff (Wynn) to distribute materials and manage communications.

Procedural Notes

  • Pete followed proper procedure: sent email to staff with explicit instructions to distribute and print for the packet.
  • Staff did not distribute the email to commissioners before the meeting.
  • The commission deferred housing element discussion specifically to include Pete.
  • Multiple commissioners expressed wanting Pete present for substantive discussion.
