The PD's written certifications

The production email states, in response to the request's specific items:

  • Records of the PD being "directed, requested, or asked to investigate, evaluate, monitor, or take any action concerning Pete McCarthy, or concerning any other private citizen who was publicly criticized by the town Administrator" — "No such record exists."
  • Records of "referral or coordination between the Paonia Police Department and an outside law enforcement agency" — "No such record exists."
  • Written communications (emails, letters, memos) — referred to the Town Clerk as custodian of email accounts (see C 26-28).

P26-0323 in detail (the "86 WYNN" case)

From Officer K. Heiniger's narrative (May 8, 2026):

"Stefen … requested to make a formal criminal complaint. … Stefen was irate and displayed his phone screen to me. On Stefen's phone screen, I observed a Facebook post with tan and dark blue seashells formed in a manner to be legible '86 WYNN'. … Stefen claimed this term of '86' is a threat to his life."

"In Stefen's statement, he identified another involved party as Melayna Rose. … Melayna was found to be a commentator to the '86 WYNN' post by Pete."

"However, I know the term '86' to be used as a slang term for trespassing parties from a bar setting or the removal of an item from a menu at a restaurant. Through my training, knowledge, and experience, I have never heard of '86' being a term to harm someone resulting in death."

"In the CRS 18-9-111: (8) This section is not intended to infringe upon any right guaranteed to any person by the first amendment … These posts would be Pete's first amendment, and at this time, I don't find any of the information provided by Stefen as an imminent threat to himself or family, with a result of death or bodily harm."

"And in 18-8-704 and 18-8-706: To my knowledge and findings within this incident, Stefen is not a victim or a witness to a crime at this time. This case will be changed from a harassment report to an information report and closed with an unfounded status."

Wynn's handwritten statement (same day) attributes "threatening messaging" to "Pete McCarthy & his partner Melayna Rose," calls the post "a direct reference to terminating my life," and describes the No Kings rally as "a 'no kings' rally that was intended to make me fear for my life through the in-flux of people into our community for a peaceful protest."

The public post had roughly ten distinct commenters (jokes, water-system complaints, "86 has never been a reference to murder," and former trustee Rick Stelter's insults directed at McCarthy). Wynn's complaint named exactly one commenter: McCarthy's partner. Her full comment ends, "We have a lovely town, so let's have lovely people working to protect it."

Within the same hour as the PD visit (2:02 PM complaint; 2:50 PM email), Wynn emailed Mayor Smith and the Trustees — CC Chief Laiminger and Officer Heiniger — his "Notice of Intent Not to Renew Employment Agreement and Safety Concerns," naming McCarthy and citing the same three criminal statutes. The PD closed the complaint as unfounded the next day. (The email is in the case file: P26-0323 EMAIL.pdf.)

Significance

  • Every report of "threats" arising from political speech was closed without investigation; two were expressly closed unfounded, and two officer narratives invoke the First Amendment by name.
  • The PD certified no record exists of any request to investigate McCarthy or any other citizen critic — the law-enforcement framing existed in the Administrator's reports, not in any police action.
  • P25-0765 (the campaign-sign report naming Bowman) falls 11 days after Bowman's HR-process inquiry to the Mayor and 12 days before her termination (see pattern-of-retaliation).
  • P26-0081 documents the Administrator using a town police officer to serve employment papers on a terminated employee at home.
