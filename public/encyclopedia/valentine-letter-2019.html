Critical context for weighing this source

Michael Valentine was appointed interim Trinidad city manager when Sund resigned, and was later made the permanent city manager (April 2020). The most detailed "why Sund should go" document in the record was therefore written by the person who succeeded him in the job. That does not make its specifics false — but it is an interested party's advocacy delivered to council on the eve of the decision, not a neutral or adjudicated finding. Read it as documented allegation, weighed accordingly. None of the allegations below resulted in a filed lawsuit on the record. See Greg R. Sund.

What the letter alleges (paraphrased; short quotes marked)

Management style and morale

  • A sustained collapse in staff morale and a lack of "leadership and direction"; long-tenured employees said to be considering leaving.
  • Sund accused of pitting departments and individuals against one another.
  • When the (female) finance director presented data contradicting his position at a department-head meeting, Sund is said to have become combative, told her "this is not a democracy," and left the room.
  • Department-head meetings described as a dreaded waste of time; staff said to "fear retaliation, retribution, and shame."

Legal-risk personnel handling

  • Personnel matters allegedly mishandled in ways that "may open the City up to lawsuits up to and including discrimination."
  • Sund allegedly told a supervisor that only police and fire personnel are important, then denied saying it at a later meeting.

Keeping council and staff in the dark

  • Items allegedly placed on council agendas without council being fully informed.
  • The La Puerta development agreement (involving utilities, city infrastructure, and DOLA grant funds) allegedly held by the developer for months, with staff and the finance director given the document only hours before a meeting; Sund allegedly remarked that city staff couldn't get projects done anyway.
  • A zoning rewrite allegedly pushed over the objections of staff, the planning consultant, and the consultant's attorney.
  • A landfill enterprise fund + one-cent sales-tax ballot package allegedly advanced against staff advice.

Capital-project missteps

  • Sister Blandina Park design and a developer's request to run a road through the park surfaced in public meetings with staff blindsided.
  • A "Noah's Ark" building allegedly built as a shell with no utility stub-outs or poured floor, raising later costs.

Hiring / org-chart churn

  • Key positions left open for months; the City Clerk who had been acting Assistant City Manager stepped down; a pattern of hiring new people rather than holding existing staff accountable.

Significance

The letter is the only detailed, on-the-record account of why Trinidad's council moved against Sund. Council itself never stated a public reason beyond the mayor's expressed disappointment about pre-decision rumors. Its central charge — a manager who steamrolls staff and withholds information from the elected board — is the specific governance failure mode this project tracks. Its evidentiary weight is limited by the author's direct interest. See Interim TM Candidate Greg Sund — What the Record Shows.
