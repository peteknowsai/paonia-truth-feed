Access to public records through the Colorado Open Records Act (CORA), including the town's history of compliance and obstruction.

Timeline

Brunner CORA case

  • Bill Brunner filed CORA requests as part of his oversight work.
  • The town filed a "reverse" CORA lawsuit.
  • Judge Steven Schultz found the town "failed to exercise reasonable diligence or reasonable inquiry" and ordered the town to pay Brunner's legal costs and attorney fees. (CFOIC reporting) source

McCarthy M365 audit log request (2024)

  • 2024-09-27: Pete files CORA request for M365 audit logs (admin activity, May-Jul 2024). Town email forwarding to his personal address had mysteriously stopped working. source
  • 2024-09-30: Vetter denies the request, citing C.R.S. 24-72-203(2)(a), claiming logs are "not in the Town's custody." Provides no explanation of who has them or where they are.
  • 2024-09-30: Pete pushes back: the Town is the M365 licensee and data owner. Cites Leonard v. Interquest. Vetter re-quotes the same statute.
  • 2024-11-18: Pete's formal complaint specifically requests the Board obtain MailItemsAccessed audit logs to verify Wynn's claim of not seeing the Nov 4 email. Board takes no action.
  • 2024-11-25: Pete contacts Jeff Roberts, Executive Director of the Colorado Freedom of Information Coalition (CFOIC).
  • 2024-11-25: Roberts responds same day confirming Pete's legal position is correct. Notes C.R.S. 24-72-203(2)(a) is a notification requirement, not a basis for denial. The records custodian can only say "not in our custody" and must direct the requester to whoever does have them.
  • 2024-11-25: Pete appeals directly to Wynn. Wynn responds: "I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to... even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform and I'm doing this to accommodate you."
  • 2024-12-02: Roberts follows up: CORA violations are enforced in district court; 14 days' written notice required before filing suit.

CORA request for "libelous" communications (2026)

  • 2026-04-06: Pete files a CORA request seeking three categories of records: (1) all communications between Smith, Wynn, Buchner, and trustees regarding Pete's flyer (March 25-30, 2026); (2) all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes of the board's response letter; (3) all legal opinions or advice from Buchner regarding use of "libelous," "libel," or "defamation." source

Pattern

The town has:

  1. One judicial finding of CORA bad faith (Brunner case)
  2. One CORA denial confirmed improper by the CFOIC Executive Director (McCarthy audit logs)
  3. Multiple pending CORA requests as of April 2026, with extensions taken on simple records requests
  4. One fulfilled but heavily redacted CORA response (C 26-09 Verkada) with $167.90 in fees for what was largely automated exports

For a running record of every CORA request, deadlines, fees, extensions, and outcomes, see CORA Request Tracking Log.

Key People

  • Samira Vetter -- Town Clerk, processes CORA requests, denied the audit log request
  • Bill Brunner -- prevailed in CORA case
  • Jeff Roberts -- CFOIC Executive Director, confirmed McCarthy's CORA denial was improper
