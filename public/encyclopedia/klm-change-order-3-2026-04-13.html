Recommended motion (in packet)

"I move to approve Change Order #3 for KLM Engineering in the amount of $26,700.00, increasing the total not to exceed contract amount to $190,423.00, and authorize the Town Administrator to execute the agreement."

Contract trajectory

Item Amount Notes
Original Agreement $97,860.00 Hourly, not-to-exceed
Change Order No. 1 $32,935.00 Additional 3 weeks AMPP coating inspection T&M incl. mobilization @ $1,568/day
Change Order No. 2 $32,928.00 Additional 1 week AMPP inspection T&M incl. mobilization @ $1,568/day
Change Order No. 3 $26,700.00 Additional 1 week through 4/13/2026 @ $1,568/day
New project total $190,423.00 Hourly, not-to-exceed

Change Order #3 tacks on roughly 17 inspector-days at $1,568/day from a single one-week period through April 13, 2026.

Background (staff report)

"The Town of Paonia is currently undertaking the 2MG finished water storage tank relining project, which includes coating inspection services performed by KLM Engineering, Inc. … Due to project delays and extended construction duration associated with the contractor's performance, additional inspection time has been required to maintain appropriate oversight and ensure compliance with coating specifications and industry standards. Change Order #1 and Change Order #2 were previously approved to account for these extended inspection periods. Change Order #3 reflects an additional one week of AMPP coating inspection services billed on a time and materials basis, including mobilization, through April 13, 2026, at a daily rate of $1,568.00, for a total of $26,700.00."

Note from staff: "It should be noted that a final change order from KLM Engineering is anticipated and will be presented to the Board at the May 12, 2026 meeting. This final change order will reflect total inspection costs through substantial completion of the project."

So this is not the last change order. A fourth is already expected on May 12.

Budget snapshot in packet (project-wide)

Engineer's POC: $2,181,300 Total Costs Incurred to Date: $1,622,959 (incl. MPC Disbursement 4.28.26) Remaining Obligated Under Existing Contracts: $279,832 Projected total Cost (current commitments): $1,902,791 Remaining Margin (after CO #3): approx. $278,300 (previously approx. $305,000)

So the project is still tracking under the engineer's $2.18M cost estimate by ~$278K — and CO #3 narrows that margin by about $27K. Per the staff report, the overruns are blamed on "the contractor's performance" — i.e. NPC, not KLM.

Notable

  • KLM's role is inspection of the coating, not the relining itself. Each change order so far has been a "we needed another inspector-week because the contractor took longer." Original assumption was ~62 inspector-days; the contract will land at ~121 inspector-days if a final CO #4 is similar in size.
  • The "approx. $278,300" remaining margin is before CO #4 hits next month. If CO #4 is comparable to CO #1–#3, expect a chunk of that to be eaten on the May 12 vote.
  • See Water Infrastructure and Debt for the broader $9.74M loan picture and the companion tank decision also on the April 28 agenda.
  • Daily rate of $1,568 = ~$196/hr at an 8-hr day, consistent with AMPP-credentialed coating inspectors traveling from Minnesota.
