What the Board Did Not Dispute

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
What the Board Did Not Dispute

A systematic comparison of every factual claim in the 10 Things flyer against the board's official response and Hunter's rebuttal flyer. For each claim: was the underlying fact disputed, or only the interpretation?

1. Fired unanimously from Neptune Beach

Pete's claim: Neptune Beach fired Wynn unanimously in January 2023 for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct."

Board's response: Confirms Wynn "disclosed that he had filed a Whistle Blower Discrimination Lawsuit against his previous employer." Does not dispute the firing, the unanimity, or the stated grounds.

Hunter's response: Acknowledges the firing. States the behavioral accusations are "subjective, unsubstantiated, and unverified by independent investigation." Does not dispute the firing itself.

Fact disputed? No. Both responses confirm the termination while framing it differently.

2. Accused of bullying and toxic workplace

Pete's claim: Neptune Beach council members accused Wynn of bullying staff, using profanity, storming out, turning off public Facebook comments.

Board's response: Does not address this claim.

Hunter's response: States claims are "subjective, unsubstantiated, and unverified."

Fact disputed? No. Hunter characterizes the claims as unverified but does not dispute that they were made.

3. Controls all the money, alone

Pete's claim: Wynn holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles, prepares the budget that determines his own salary, $106K in admin costs spread across four enterprise fund pages. Last concentrated authority led to $480K embezzlement.

Board's response: Describes internal controls (Caselle software, Professional Management Solutions, Finance Committee). States "The Administrator does NOT determine his own salary."

Hunter's response: Same framing. Describes controls.

Facts disputed? Partially. Both dispute "controls all the money alone" by describing oversight mechanisms. Neither disputes that Wynn holds both roles, that admin costs are spread across four fund pages, or the $106K figure. The embezzlement history is not disputed. The salary claim is disputed: the board says trustees determine raises via performance reviews, while Pete's claim is that Wynn "prepares the budget that determines his own salary" (i.e., he drafts the budget document that includes his compensation).

4. Savings are disappearing

Pete's claim: FY2026 fund balances projected to drop 23% ($5.4M to $4.1M). Bridge Reserve down 73.5%.

Board's response: "Saving up reserves and then drawing them down for infrastructure projects is RESPONSIBLE PLANNING, NOT FISCAL DISTRESS."

Hunter's response: Same framing. Characterizes drawdowns as intentional.

Facts disputed? No. The 23% decline and 73.5% Bridge Reserve decline are not disputed. The disagreement is about what the numbers mean: responsible planning vs. fiscal concern.

5. 69% of budget is grants and loans

Pete's claim: FY2026 budget is $16.16M; only ~$4.6M is own revenue. $9.74M water loan adds $263K/year through 2045.

Board's response: States Pete believes "grants and loans are not the correct path forward." Describes the $10.8M in water infrastructure projects.

Hunter's response: "Grants and loans are typically how municipalities pay for large/overdue infrastructure projects."

Facts disputed? No. The 69% figure, the $16.16M total, the $4.6M own revenue, and the $9.74M loan are not disputed. The board built a straw man: Pete never said grants are wrong; he said 69% dependency creates vulnerability. The board's response addresses the straw man, not the actual claim.

6. Grew the bureaucracy 6x faster than population

Pete's claim: Staff went from 19 to 25.7 FTE (+35%), 57% increase in admin positions. Population grew 1.1%. Personnel costs up $530K (+28%).

Board's response: "This is patently false." Explains trustees approve staffing. States most new hires filled existing vacancies.

Hunter's response: "Though a few new positions were created, the majority of new employees were hired to fill already existing, open positions."

Facts disputed? The board says "patently false" but does not provide alternative staffing numbers. Neither response disputes the 19-to-25.7 FTE figure, the 57% admin increase, the 1.1% population growth, or the $530K personnel cost increase. The dispute is about characterization: the board says existing vacancies were filled; Pete says the bureaucracy grew.

Discrepancy noted: The Facebook posts reference both 19 and 22 as baseline FTE numbers, suggesting different baseline years. The specific numbers need verification against the actual budget documents.

7. Surveillance cameras with zero public comment

Pete's claim: $50K+ on Verkada cameras, FTC fined $2.95M, 150K cameras hacked in 2021, approved unanimously with zero public comment, no surveillance policy.

Board's response: Cameras discussed at "at least six public meetings." "These are not nefarious face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." Cameras "reduced vandalism."

Hunter's response: Acknowledges "No, there was no public comment, because no one from the public cared to speak on the matter." Confirms 8/12/2025 approval date.

Facts disputed? The $50,288 cost, the FTC fine, the 2021 hack, the zero public comment, and the lack of surveillance policy are all confirmed or not disputed. Hunter explicitly confirms zero public comment. The board claims the cameras are "not face recognition," but Verkada's own product documentation shows the cameras include facial recognition, People Analytics, and photo-based person search as standard features. source

8. Trees: $0 in the budget, told to "pound sand"

Pete's claim: Budget allocates $0 to dedicated tree care. Tree Board resigned. Liaison called himself "the town's spy." Volunteers told to "pound sand."

Board's response: Claims $40K in two budget line items (Streets 10-45-71 Right-of-Way Maintenance $25K, Parks 10-46-71 $15K). States "Volunteers were never told to 'pound sand.'" Tree Board suspended via Resolution 03-26 due to expired terms.

Hunter's response: Same $40K claim. States town is hiring arborist consultant.

Facts disputed? Partially. The board cites $40K, but these are Right-of-Way Maintenance line items (covering sidewalks, curbs, and general maintenance), not dedicated tree care, planting, or urban forestry. The Comprehensive Plan (July 2025, p.42) explicitly calls for urban forest investment; the budget allocates $0 to that specific goal. The Delta County Independent reported Tree Board members felt "unheard for the past five years" and the liaison called himself "the town's spy." The "pound sand" phrasing is disputed; the underlying frustration is documented.

9. Shopping, not governing

Pete's claim: Lists specific capital expenditures ($1M generators, $500K meters, $250K squad cars, etc.) and $0 for trees.

Board's response: "This is blatantly false." Describes each expenditure as necessary.

Hunter's response: Same framing.

Facts disputed? No. The dollar amounts are not disputed; they come from the adopted budget. The disagreement is whether the priorities constitute "shopping" (Pete's characterization) or "responsible planning" (the board's characterization). This is a policy disagreement, not a factual one.

10. Same pattern from Florida

Pete's claim: Anyone who asks questions gets pushed out. Brunner removed after Wynn threatened to resign.

Board's response: "This is blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation."

Hunter's response: Same language.

Facts disputed? The board simultaneously denies the pattern and acknowledges the litigation about it. Brunner's election, Wynn's conditional resignation, and the 4-1 removal vote are documented facts from KVNF and other sources. The characterization of whether this constitutes a "pattern" is the dispute. The board's own language ("blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation") is internally contradictory.

Of the 10 claims, zero underlying factual sources were disputed. The board's response characterizes Pete's editorial opinions as "misinformation" and "blatant falsehoods" without identifying a single specific factual error. Every disagreement is about interpretation, framing, or characterization, not about what the cited sources actually say.
