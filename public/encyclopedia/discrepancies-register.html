1. "Not face recognition" vs. Verkada product documentation

Board's claim: "These are not nefarious face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." source

Record: Verkada cameras include facial recognition, "People Analytics" (filter by clothing color, apparent sex, backpack), photo-based person search across all camera history, and license plate recognition. These are standard product features listed on Verkada's own website. source

Status: Unresolved. It is unclear whether the board was aware of these capabilities when it made this claim.

2. Voted to edit, published unedited

Board's action: Voted at the March 30 meeting to edit the statement to remove "assertions and characterizations that aren't necessary" before publication. source

Record: The version initially published was identical to the unedited draft. "Erroneous and libelous," "smear campaign," "ill-willed players," and "deliberately damaging" all remained. The edited version was published later. source source source

Status: Resolved. Both versions exist in the record. The board voted one thing, did another, then corrected it afterward.

3. "Anonymous author(s)" vs. the Town's own records naming McCarthy

Mayor's report (March 30, 2026): Refers to "the attached anonymous flyer" and the "anonymous author(s)." source

Record: The Town's own Microsoft 365 records show it identified the author days earlier. By March 24 — while the flyer was being handed out in town — an internal email titled "Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy" had reached Town leadership by 11:07 AM, and the Town opened a Microsoft Planner task titled "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer." Six days later, the Mayor's official report still called the author "anonymous" while the Board weighed taxpayer-funded newspaper ads in response. full analysis source

Status: Contradicted by the Town's own records. The "anonymous" framing in an official document is irreconcilable with internal records that named McCarthy as the flyer circulated.

4. Hunter's flyer "on her own accord" vs. coordinated with Wynn and Buchner

Mayor's report: "This was done on her own accord, after consultation with the Administrator (to ensure her flyer content was 100% accurate) and the Town Attorney. This did not involve me or any other Trustee." source

Record: The mayor's own language creates a contradiction. A flyer vetted by the subject of the criticism (Wynn) and the Town Attorney (Buchner) is not purely "on her own accord." The mayor's characterization of independence and the facts about coordination don't align.

Status: Noted. The mayor appears to distinguish "involvement" (policy direction) from "consultation" (content review). Whether a trustee's flyer vetted by the administrator and attorney is truly independent is a question of interpretation.

5. Wynn "discovering" Pete's email at the meeting

On camera (Nov 4, 2024): Wynn says "oh we do have an email from Pete" as if seeing it for the first time. (YouTube, Planning Commission Meeting, ~13:41) source

Record: Pete sent the email at 1:13 PM that day. The M365 MailItemsAccessed audit logs would show exactly when Wynn opened it. Pete's formal complaint requested the Board obtain these logs. The Board never did. The CORA request for those logs was denied by Vetter on grounds confirmed improper by the CFOIC. source

Status: Unresolved. The audit logs that would resolve this question exist but have not been obtained.

6. "Blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation"

Board's statement: The claim that "anyone who asks questions gets pushed out" is "blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation." source

Internal contradiction: The statement simultaneously denies the pattern and acknowledges that the town is being sued over it. If the claim is "blatantly false," the litigation should be frivolous. If the litigation is serious enough to mention, the claim isn't blatantly false. source

Status: Noted. This is a logical contradiction in the board's own published statement.

7. $40K "for trees" vs. Right-of-Way Maintenance

Board's claim: "Two new budget line items for trees totaling $40,000: Streets 10-45-71 Right-of-Way Maintenance $25,000 and Parks 10-46-71 Right-of-Way Maintenance $15,000." source

Record: Right-of-Way Maintenance is a Public Works category covering sidewalks, curbs, and general maintenance. The Comprehensive Plan adopted July 16, 2025 (INFRA GOAL 10, p.42) explicitly calls for urban forest investment: "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees." The budget allocates $0 to that specific goal. Tree Board members resigned, citing frustration and feeling "unheard for the past five years." source

Status: Unresolved. The board claims the $40K is "for trees." The budget line items are general Right-of-Way Maintenance. Whether this qualifies as dedicated tree funding is a factual question that could be resolved by examining what the line items have historically been spent on.

8. Board's response "cites nothing" vs. "Statement of Fact"

Board's framing: The response is titled "Statement of Fact" and the mayor's report describes it as "a statement of fact clarifying approved budgets, purchases and purchasing policy." source

Record: The board's response does not cite a single page number, budget document, news article, or public record. It uses characterizations ("patently false," "blatantly false," "responsible planning") without providing sources. Pete's flyer cites adopted budgets with page numbers, six news outlets, court records, Census data, FTC filings, and Delta County Independent reporting. source

Hunter's flyer: Does cite some sources (budget references, meeting minutes dates), making it more substantive than the board's official response.

Status: Noted. The board's "Statement of Fact" contains no citations. The document most accurately described as sourced fact-checking is actually Hunter's rebuttal, not the board's.

9. Staffing baseline: 19 FTE vs. 22 FTE

Pete's flyer: "Staff went from 19 to 25.7 FTE." source

Facebook post #4: References "FY2024 Adopted Budget: 22 budgeted FTE." source

Possible explanation: The 19 figure may be from an earlier budget year (FY2023) and the 22 from FY2024, showing the growth started before FY2024. Or different counting methodologies. The +35% figure Pete cites appears to use 19 as the baseline.

Status: Minor discrepancy in Pete's own documents. The direction (significant staffing growth outpacing population) is not disputed by the board.

10. "Charges of financial malfeasance" vs. what Pete actually wrote

HCS article (Marty Durlin, April 2026): "the charges of financial malfeasance made by McCarthy against Wynn"

Record: Pete's flyer states: "The town's savings are disappearing. FY2026 fund balances projected to drop 23% ($5.4M to $4.1M). Bridge Reserve down 73.5%." It cites FY2026 Budget p.5. The word "malfeasance" does not appear in the flyer, the citation sheet, or any of Pete's four Facebook posts. source source

Pete's flyer also states Wynn "controls all the money, alone" and notes the historical embezzlement (2007-2012), but frames this as a structural risk ("last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person"), not an accusation that Wynn committed fraud.

Significance: "Malfeasance" is a legal term meaning corrupt official conduct. Reporting that fund balances are declining per the adopted budget is a factual observation, not a criminal accusation. The reframing escalates a budget critique into an allegation of corruption that Pete never made.

Status: Unresolved. HCS has not cited a source for the word "malfeasance."

11. "Controls all the money, alone" vs. "blatantly false"

Flyer claim (item #3): Wynn "controls all the money, alone." Consolidated Administrator and Treasurer into one role. Prepares the budget. Historical context: the 2007-2012 embezzlement of $480K under a similar concentration of authority. source

Town's response: "This is blatantly false." Cites four controls: Finance Committee (Mayor + 1 Trustee) reviews every invoice, Caselle accounting software, Professional Management Solutions (third-party), and Board-set salary. source source

Record: Three of the four flyer facts are undisputed — Wynn holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles (Resolutions 2023-07, 2023-08); he prepares the budget; the 2007-2012 embezzlement happened under controls. Of the four controls the Town cites, three lack public evidence of actual function: no Finance Committee invoice-review log is published; Caselle user permissions and audit trails are the subject of three pending CORA requests (filed April 1, April 12, and April 19, 2026); PMS's actual scope of work is undocumented on the record. Board approval of salary is documented. See full analysis.

Status: Unresolved. "Blatantly false" is an assertion about controls whose effectiveness has not been independently verified. The pending Caselle CORA requests are the mechanism that will show which characterization the record supports.

12. Finance Committee records "accessible" vs. "not written down"

Town Clerk (C 26-23, May 2026): Declined to certify that no responsive records exist, saying she "provided you with a way to access the records that were responsive" — pointing to the Board-packet payment-approval reports. source

Same response: The Clerk also said she does "not believe it is written down anywhere," and the Town produced none of the requested minutes, agendas, sign-in sheets, logs, or charter. source

Status: Unresolved. The response treats the records as both accessible (so non-existence cannot be certified) and as not existing in writing — and a payment-approval report is not the Committee's minutes, agendas, or logs.

13. C 26-11 drafts "already publicly available" vs. the Town's own audit log naming non-published drafts

Town Clerk (C 26-11 response, 2026-04-21): In response to the request for "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" of the March 30 Board letter, Vetter wrote that "The two drafts of the letter requested are already publicly available here:" and gave two URLs — the CivicClerk agenda-packet version and the townofpaonia.colorado.gov "Letter to Paonia Citizens - 03312026.pdf." Any communication involving the Town Attorney was withheld as privileged, with no privilege log. source

Record: The Town's own Microsoft 365 audit log — produced under C 26-12 — names additional, non-published draft threads sitting in officials' mailboxes in the same window: Re: draft "letter from Trustee" (3/27–3/28; paiges/samirav/stefenw), Response to the Citizen's 10 things flyer . I Need comments /no later than 2:00 tomorrow (3/28–3/31; paiges/ptownlegal), and RE: PLease see the attached drafft Mayor's report for the Special Meeting (3/27–3/29; lucyh/paiges/samirav). None of these is the CivicClerk or townofpaonia.colorado.gov published version, and none was produced or logged as withheld. source

Caveat: The audit log records only subject lines, timestamps, and which mailbox holds each item — not message bodies, authorship, or recipient lists. It cannot prove what these drafts said, only that they existed in those mailboxes at those times.

Status: Contradicted by the Town's own records. The "already publicly available" framing equated "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" with the two finished published letters, while the Town's audit log shows non-published draft threads — some sitting only in non-attorney mailboxes — that the production neither disclosed nor logged. Separately, the C 26-12 audit-log production itself first omitted true March and post-April-9 (the windows holding these drafts and the April 21 post-closure deletions), completeness arriving only after objection.
