How Was the Verkada Surveillance System Approved?

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
How Was the Verkada Surveillance System Approved?
Open question
QuestionWhat was the procurement and approval process for the approximately $50,000 Verkada surveillance camera deployment on public property in Paonia?
Record
TypeOpen question
First recorded2026-04-05
Last updated2026-04-08
Topicssurveillance verkada cameras spending process

What the Record Shows

  • The town deployed a Verkada surveillance camera system for $50,288 total ($41,593 materials + $8,695 labor), purchased through Mitchell and Company as vendor-of-record. Quote dated 2025-07-08; signed by Stefen Wynn as Town Administrator & Treasurer on 2025-08-18. source
  • The system includes 23 cameras across 5 sites: Town Hall (13 cameras), Town Park (6), Wastewater Treatment Plant (2), Water Treatment Plant (2). source
  • All cameras are configured with a maximum retention of 30 days. source
  • Cameras were discussed at budget meetings between October 2024 and December 2025. source
  • The Nov 12, 2024 budget work session included a camera presentation, but discussion was about locations and cost, not surveillance policy or vendor capabilities. source
  • Board approved unanimously on 2025-08-12 with zero public comment. source
  • Hunter's rebuttal acknowledges no public comment occurred.
  • The Town has no written policy, procedure, or guideline governing use, access, or retention of Verkada footage. CONFIRMED by the Town's response to CORA C 26-09 item 4, which produced manufacturer device configuration screenshots in place of a policy.
  • The Town has 11 users with access to the system, including three Organization Admins: Wynn, Police Chief Matt Laiminger, and Mitchell and Company contractor Kirk Hinderberger. source
  • Verkada cameras include facial recognition, AI-powered "People Analytics," photo-based person search, and Axon Evidence integration. It is unclear whether these capabilities were evaluated or disclosed during the approval process. source

What the Record Still Does Not Show

  • Whether alternative vendors were considered or whether Mitchell and Company was sole-sourced
  • Whether the AI surveillance capabilities (facial recognition, People Analytics) were presented to the Board
  • Whether Verkada's history (employee harassment, 150K camera breach, FTC fine) was disclosed to the Board
  • Whether a surveillance policy was considered before or alongside the purchase
  • The internal correspondence among Wynn, Laiminger, and Poulos about selection, procurement, and deployment (withdrawn from CORA C 26-09 scope after initial fee estimate)

Suggested CORA Requests

Request 1: Verkada Contract and Procurement

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request a copy of the contract, purchase order, or agreement between the Town of Paonia and Verkada, Inc. for the surveillance camera system, including all terms, pricing, service level agreements, data retention policies, and any renewal or licensing terms. Also request any competitive bids, sole-source justifications, or vendor selection documents.

Request 2: Verkada Capabilities Disclosure

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request all sales proposals, product specifications, demonstrations, or marketing materials provided by Verkada, Inc. to the Town of Paonia, including any materials describing facial recognition, People Analytics, AI video analytics, or person-tracking capabilities.

Request 3: Camera Policy and Access

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request any policy, procedure, or guideline governing the use of the Verkada camera system, including: who has access to the footage, retention periods, circumstances under which footage may be shared with outside agencies, and any integration with Axon Evidence.com or other law enforcement platforms. If no such policy exists, I request confirmation that no policy has been adopted.

Request 4: Board Discussion of Surveillance

Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request minutes, agendas, and any staff reports or presentations from the "at least six public meetings" between October 2024 and December 2025 where the camera proposal was discussed.

Other Ways to Get Answers

  • Ask at a board meeting during public comment: "Did the board know the Verkada cameras include facial recognition and AI person-tracking when it approved the purchase?"
  • Request the topic be placed on a future board meeting agenda for discussion

Status

Partially answered by CORA C 26-09 (2026-04-07). Confirmed: total cost ($50,288), signed contract date (2025-08-18), vendor (Mitchell and Company), camera count (23), retention policy (30 days), and critically, no written policy exists. Vendor selection process, capability disclosure to the Board, and internal correspondence about procurement remain open.
