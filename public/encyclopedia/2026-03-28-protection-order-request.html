Administrator Asks Police Chief How to Seek a Protection Order Against a Critic

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia

What Happened

On Saturday 2026-03-28 at 9:25 AM, Town Administrator Stefen Wynn emailed Police Chief Matt Laiminger (CC Officer G. Henderson) under the subject "Bullying and Harassment on Social Media in Advance of 500-600 Political Activists in Town for a Rally." The email concerns the image of Wynn superimposed on King Leopold II that circulated ahead of the "No Kings" rally and the "10 reasons the town administrator … should be fired" flyer.

Wynn wrote:

"Who does this need to be forwarded to at the county level to begin seeking a protection order from Mr. McCarthy and his accomplices, namely Kaja Bowman, who was seen handing this flyer out…"

He stated that he had "terminated her employment with the Town in October 2025" and that "she is also my lot line sharing neighbor." (source)

The Forwarded Attorney Inquiry

Minutes earlier, at 9:18 AM, Wynn had emailed his private attorney under the same subject line, and he forwarded that message to the Chief in the same email. In it he asked:

"I've already had two elected officials reach out to me about what is being posted… Has this reached a level that has met the statute level for public officials?"

The Town later withheld this forwarded message from CORA production as attorney-client privileged, though the client had already shared it with the Police Department. See Paonia PD 'No Kings Rally' Email Production (CCJRA, June 18, 2026) for the withholding and the waiver question.

Context

  • This is the morning of the rally. No police action followed; the PD's June 4 production certified "no record exists" of being asked to investigate or act concerning McCarthy (Paonia PD Records Production (CCJRA, June 4, 2026)).
  • Six weeks later, on 2026-05-08, Wynn made a formal criminal complaint against McCarthy over a Facebook post; it was closed unfounded on First Amendment grounds the next day (What the Police Found: Unfounded).
  • The protection order Wynn asked about on March 28 reappears in his 2026-05-08 letter to the Board, which announced he was "seeking a civil protection order."
