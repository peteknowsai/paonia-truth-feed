Why this meeting was called

To interview a single interim town-administrator candidate, Greg Sund, and consider a consultant contract bridging the departure of Town Administrator Stefen Wynn (leaving mid-July 2026). This followed the May 22 special meeting on the nonrenewal and search process.

What happened

  • One candidate. Sund was the only name before the Board, sourced through the CCMA senior advisor, who reported "no other interim candidates." See The CCMA Pipeline: How the Same Managers Circulate Through Colorado Towns.
  • The interview. Sund fielded questions on continuity, grant-funded water projects, public works, citizen committees, and HR. He confirmed making the "this is not a democracy" remark to Trinidad's finance director (attributing it to board direction) and described how towns frequently part ways with managers amid board turnover. Full quotes: Special Meeting — Interim Administrator Interview (2026-05-29).
  • Public comment. Pete McCarthy read a statement laying out Sund's record and the rushed, single-candidate process, then announced a petition to recall Mayor Paige Smith, naming himself as the successor candidate.
  • The hire stalled. A motion to enter the consultant agreement died for lack of a second. Trustee Foster objected to hiring within a 24-hour window without reference checks: "cutting corners only sets us up for criticism in the future."

Outcome

The Board set a follow-up special meeting for Wednesday, June 3, 2026 at 5:30 p.m. to complete reference checks, allow contract revisions, and decide. No hire was made on May 29.
