Item H.8 — "Whether to Turn Some Cameras Off"

"Consideration of Whether to Turn Some Cameras Off while an Adequate Framework of Governance is Put Into Place" — Town Staff & Board of Trustees

Staff report: Wynn Memo and Attachments — Verkada Review (April 28, 2026 Packet)

Despite the agenda title implying a turn-off decision, staff recommendation is "provide policy direction" — no motion to disable any cameras is offered. The packet bundles a Wynn executive memo, a "Camera Discussion" Q&A document, notes from an April 22 meeting with Verkada, the PD's existing surveillance program report, individual department use statements (PW, Clerk), an attorney response on Colorado/federal law, and Verkada vendor resources.

Key concessions in this packet vs. the April 14 Wynn memo:

  • Two cameras inside the building have active audio and have been used "to see how staff interacted with difficult situations."
  • Models confirmed not to have audio: D50, D80, CD22, CH52-E, CM22, CM41-S, Bullet series. All other models can have audio toggled.
  • Public-input claim downgraded from "discussed at six separate public meetings" to "(verify) I believe…"
  • Funded from "general fund, water enterprise, sewer enterprise, trash enterprise — of which sales tax capital improvement, surplus disposal of equipment, and back the badge were all utilized." No grants were specifically for cameras.
  • Excluded recreational areas: Apple Valley Park, Tennis/Pickleball Courts, former football fields.

ICE-warrant scenario as recorded by staff in the Camera Discussion: "If ICE were to come to Verkada with a federal warrant, their answer would be we don't own that data, the user owns that data and federal agencies would need to go to the user with that warrant."

Item H.9 — Draft Surveillance Camera Ordinance (tabled from April 14)

Source: Draft Surveillance Camera Ordinance (Hunter / Mejorado, tabled from April 14)

Submitted by Trustees Hunter and Mejorado. Packet states "The Draft Ordinance is NOT up for adoption at this time. Possible motions include edits to the Draft and directing Staff for further edits / review."

Notable structural provisions:

  • §4 restricts authorized purposes to a closed list (facility/infrastructure protection, parking/parks/recreation, criminal-activity deterrence, employee/public safety, and resolution-authorized traffic monitoring).
  • §5 requires a Board-adopted written Use Policy with a 90-day max retention and a complete inventory of all cameras (location, field of view, purpose).
  • §6 requires a Privacy Impact Assessment before any new install. Existing cameras get a PIA within 90 days of adoption.
  • §8 requires a written/electronic public access log for every footage view, treated as a CORA-able public record. 72-hour breach-notification window.
  • §10 flatly prohibits facial recognition, license plate aggregation, protected-class targeting, sharing with federal immigration enforcement absent a court order, and any commercial use.
  • §11 sets a 180-day Compliance Deadline for existing systems; non-compliant cameras suspend, then decommission after another 30 days.
  • §14 effective date is 30 days after final passage and publication.

Item H.10 — SGM Task Order: Repurpose Temporary Tank

Source: SGM Task Order: Repurpose Temporary Potable Tank to Permanent Raw Water Storage Tank

Decision tonight: $10,000 to SGM for design + permitting work to convert the temporary potable storage tank (installed for the 2MG tank relining project) into a permanent raw-water storage tank.

Decision later: $212,300 to actually purchase the tank (currently held back from this vote).

Staff report and SGM letter both state CDPHE has "no specific design criteria" for raw water tanks; SGM frames this as the project being able to "proceed relatively unchecked" via a "courtesy" CDPHE submittal.

Source: KLM Engineering Change Order #3 — 2MG Tank Coating Inspection
