Key Facts

  • Pete McCarthy distributed a flyer titled "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator," citing adopted budgets, news articles, court records, and meeting minutes
  • The Board of Trustees called a Special Meeting - Board Response to 10 Things Flyer to approve a taxpayer-funded "Statement of Fact" rebutting the flyer
  • The Mayor's agenda packet called the flyer "libelous," "misinformation," and a "smear campaign"
  • McCarthy filed a formal complaint to every board member in November 2024 documenting misconduct by the Town Administrator. No board member replied. He resigned as Planning Commissioner two weeks later; no board member replied to that either.
  • Contrast: 16 months of silence on a formal complaint vs. 5 days to respond to a flyer
  • Mayor's report expressed concern the flyer could "place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies"
  • Four individuals pushed out under a common pattern:
    • Bill Brunner: elected trustee (67.7% of vote), removed Brunner Removal Hearing and Vote after Stefen Wynn threatened to resign unless Brunner was removed. Brunner is now suing for reinstatement.
    • Pete McCarthy: Planning Commissioner, filed formal complaint, received no response, resigned
    • Cory Heiniger: former Public Works Director, resigned with accusations against Wynn in his resignation letter. Board did not investigate.
    • Kaja Bowman: former Deputy Treasurer, approached Mayor Paige Smith on 2025-09-16 to ask about the complaint process on behalf of multiple employees. Mayor contacted Wynn and identified Bowman by name before Bowman stated she was making a complaint. Bowman was fired Kaja Bowman Termination, 23 days later.
  • Legal analysis of "libelous" claim:
    • Wynn is a public official; Sullivan standard requires proof of "actual malice"
    • Colorado has anti-SLAPP protections; Town could end up paying McCarthy's legal fees
    • Truth is an absolute defense to defamation
    • Board's rebuttal does not dispute a single source, only disputes opinions about what the facts mean
  • Trees: Board cited $40K in "Right-of-Way Maintenance," but that is a Public Works category covering sidewalks, curbs, and general maintenance, not dedicated tree care. The Comprehensive Plan (adopted July 2025) calls for urban forest investment. Budget allocates $0. Tree Board was suspended; members resigned.
  • Cameras: $50K Verkada system, FTC fine against Verkada, zero public comment on approval
  • Lucy Hunter and McCarthy discussed the flyer publicly online; Mayor's packet still called McCarthy "anonymous"

Notable Quotes

"Sixteen months of silence on a signed, formal complaint. Five days to respond to a flyer."

"She's not worried that what I said is wrong. She's worried that the people who loan the town money might start asking the same questions I've been asking."

"You cannot libel someone with their own budget. You cannot defame a public official by reading his own words back to him."

"That is four people: an elected trustee removed, a planning commissioner silenced, a public works director who resigned with complaints, and a deputy treasurer fired for asking on behalf of her colleagues how to file a complaint. Four different roles, four different time periods. One common factor."
