Original Request

The request asked for all records of Town of Paonia Finance Committee meetings from January 1, 2024 through May 9, 2026, across six categories:

  1. Meeting minutes
  2. Agendas
  3. Sign-in sheets or attendance records
  4. Logs, journals, or tracking spreadsheets the Committee produces or maintains
  5. Written communications between Committee members regarding Committee business
  6. Any resolution, ordinance, policy, or written charter establishing the Committee, its membership, authority, schedule, or procedures

The request expressly invoked C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3) and asked for written confirmation of non-existence if any responsive records did not exist.

What Was Produced

The May 26 response, signed by Assistant Clerk Santiago, directed the requester to the Board meeting packets posted on the Town website — specifically the "Disbursements" entry in each meeting's consent agenda — and offered an in-person appointment to review physical invoices. The Disbursements entry contains a Payment Approval Report generated by the Town's accounting system, bearing handwritten initials in the upper corner and small marks alongside line items.

No minutes, agendas, sign-in sheets, or logs were produced. No § 24-72-204(3) non-existence statement was issued. The letter declared the request closed.

Disputed Defects

  1. Redirect to an adjacent record. A Payment Approval Report is an accounting-system output with initials. It is not a meeting minute, agenda, sign-in sheet, or deliberative log. The response treated production of one record type as responsive to a request for a different record type.

  2. No § 204(3) certification. Neither the requested records nor the requested written confirmation of non-existence was provided. The request had named § 24-72-204(3) in advance.

  3. Closed without addressing five of six categories. The letter declared the request closed while leaving categories 1 through 5 unaddressed; only the disbursement-report material spoke to category 6 in any form.

The Custodian's Position (On the Record, May 27)

After a six-category itemized follow-up was sent to Vetter and the full Board on May 27, Vetter replied the same day. Her reply stated she did not believe written Finance Committee procedures exist anywhere, and that she located no resolution or document specific to committee roles, offering Resolution 2024-09 as the "closest" record rather than as responsive.

Vetter also declined to certify non-existence. Her stated reason was that she could not say no responsive records exist because she "provided you with a way to access the records that were responsive" — treating the existence of one record type (the disbursement reports) as discharging a request for different record types. The existence of one category does not establish the existence or non-existence of another.

These statements are paraphrased from the May 27 Gmail exchange; the short quoted phrase above is the custodian's own wording. The follow-up correspondence is at raw/documents/cora-requests/cora-c26-23-follow-up.md.

Net Result

Five of six requested categories remain unaddressed: the Town has neither produced minutes, agendas, sign-in sheets, logs, or member communications, nor certified in writing that they do not exist. A § 24-72-204(4) follow-up — a grounds-for-denial demand that boxes each category into produce / cite-an-exemption / concede-non-existence — is the next step on record. As of this writing that follow-up is a drafted Gmail message awaiting the requester's review; see the CORA tracking log for its status.

What the Records Do and Do Not Show

What the response shows is narrow and concrete: the Town points to initialed disbursement reports as its Finance Committee record and has not, to date, identified any other written Finance Committee record from the 28-month window requested.

What the records do not show is a deliberative paper trail. Across the produced material and the custodian's statements, there appears to be no written record that the Finance Committee ever questioned, flagged, delayed, or rejected an expenditure. The Board has described the Committee in prior correspondence as an internal control. The records produced under C 26-23 do not document any instance of that control operating to push back on a payment. This is the absence the request was designed to test; it is stated as an absence in the produced record, not as proof that no such pushback ever occurred. See Has the Finance Committee Ever Flagged Anything? and "Controls all the money, alone": what the evidence actually shows.
