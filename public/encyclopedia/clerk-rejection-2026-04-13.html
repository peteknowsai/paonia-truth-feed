Full Text

April 13, 2026

Peter McCarthy Melayna Rosa 119 ½ Dorris Ave Paonia, CO 81428

Re: Paonia Surveillance Camera Prohibition Ordinance

The Ballot measure is hereby rejected. Please see the following explanation.

The proposed initiatives seek to amend or supplant administrative decisions and actions. Decisions that require careful study and specialized expertise, as well as discretionary judgment, generally are administrative in nature, and thus are outside the scope of the initiative power reserved to the people under state constitution; additionally, the government decision to place security cameras for the security of Town property and Town assets is not the implementation of public policy, but instead, such decisions are executive acts.

The attached petition is administrative in nature in that it seeks to control the Town's executive function in the management, operation, and security of Town property and assets.

The proposed ordinance encroaches on the administrative processes reserved to the administrative staff of the Town as authorized by the BOT, and allowing the initiative process to either dictate or negate executive actions normally undertaken by Town staff has been determined to be a misuse of the initiative process and contrary to the initiative rights established by the Colorado Constitution, Article V, Section 1, ¶ 5.5.

Therefore:

  1. Prohibiting the installation, operation, or maintenance of surveillance cameras on public property are purely executive acts, and thus, the proposed measure cannot be placed on the ballot.

  2. Requiring the removal of surveillance cameras on public property are purely executive acts, and thus, the proposed measure cannot be placed on the ballot.

  3. The body-worn camera exemption is only applicable to the prohibition delineated in the proposed measure and does not stand alone as proposed measure and therefore cannot be individually severed from the ordinance intended purpose.

Samira M Vetter, CMC Paonia Town Clerk

Grounds Cited

# Ground Theory
1 Prohibition is a "purely executive act" Camera installation/operation
2 Removal requirement is a "purely executive act" Mandatory decommissioning
3 Body-worn camera exemption is not severable (Pete had narrowed to camera ban only)

Notable Theory Shift

Compared with the April 3 rejection:

  • The Clerk dropped the "single-subject" argument, the "state-law conflict" argument, the "judicial function" argument, and the "voter-approval invalid" argument.
  • The Clerk shifted from labeling the measure "administrative" to labeling it "executive acts" — a different doctrinal category. The terms are not interchangeable in Colorado initiative law.
  • The Clerk simultaneously asserts both labels in the same letter ("administrative in nature" and "purely executive acts").
  • The letter cites no Colorado caselaw distinguishing legislative, administrative, and executive functions.
  • The "discretionary judgment" theory cited has no statutory or caselaw anchor in the letter.

Procedural

  • Pete had narrowed the original petition specifically to address the April 3 grounds. The Clerk's response was to invent a new theory rather than evaluate the narrowed text.
  • Buchner (Town Attorney) presumably advised on this rejection but is not signatory.
