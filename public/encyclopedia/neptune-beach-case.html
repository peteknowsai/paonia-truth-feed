Summary

Stefen Wynn sued Neptune Beach under Florida's Public Whistleblower Act (Section 112.3187, Florida Statutes) after his termination on 2023-01-17. He claims he was fired in retaliation for reporting financial misconduct, conflicts of interest, and budget violations involving city officials. Neptune Beach claims the firing was for cause related to a water tower permitting failure.

Wynn's Claims (from the Complaint)

  • Wynn began employment as City Manager on or about 2020-01-06.
  • On 2022-03-15, he submitted a written complaint regarding suspected fraud in the building and permit department to the City Auditors and City Council.
  • In August 2022, he reported police department budget overspending (over 100% of the ad valorem budget) both verbally and in writing.
  • He reported a potential conflict of interest: City Councilor Lauren Key's husband, Commander Michael Key, was second in charge of the police department and its fiscal functions. Wynn reported overspending and procurement violations by Commander Key.
  • On 2022-08-15, Wynn reported the overspending and conflict to Councilor Key directly, telling her the police budget was "seriously overspent" and "violated the Department's procurement policy."
  • On 2022-08-15, at a City Council meeting, Councilor Key "harassed and verbally berated" Wynn, wanting to discuss his reporting to the Police Chief.
  • Wynn was summoned to meet with Councilor Key, yelled at, told to never make allegations again. When he said she was "towing a thin ethical line," she shouted at him to "go ahead and file an ethics complaint against her."
  • On 2022-09-02, Wynn was forced to apologize to Key; told his apology was "halfhearted." Key stated she "welcomed his resignation."
  • On 2022-09-19, Wynn was "lambasted" by Councilor Key at a Council meeting, his work performance "unjustly criticized."
  • On 2022-10-22, Wynn made another written complaint detailing the harassment and claiming whistleblower status.
  • On 2023-01-17, Wynn was terminated. Stated grounds: failure to ensure proper permitting for a water tower ("Green Monster") built as part of a Publix Supermarket.
  • Wynn claims the water tower permitting was the Council's responsibility to know about, not his, and that the Building Official (Eric Pardee) was the responsible party but was not fired.
  • Post-termination, Neptune Beach allegedly gave bad employment references.

Key Details

  • Count I (only count): Public Whistleblower Retaliation under Section 112.3187, Florida Statutes
  • Claims exceed: $50,000 exclusive of costs and interest
  • Relief sought: Damages (general, compensatory, emotional), injunctive relief including reinstatement, attorney's fees
  • Trial: 4-day jury trial, week of 2026-08-17, 9:30 AM, Duval County

Parallels to Paonia

The complaint describes a pattern strikingly similar to the Paonia situation:

  • City manager reports financial misconduct to governing body
  • Governing body retaliates rather than investigating
  • Manager is terminated on pretextual grounds
  • The person who reported wrongdoing faces consequences; the wrongdoing itself is not addressed

The board complaint letter notes: "Two lawsuits, two jurisdictions, the same pattern."

However, it is important to note that these are Wynn's allegations in his own lawsuit. Neptune Beach's side of the story is presented in their motions to dismiss and answer, which raise affirmative defenses. The case has not yet been tried.

The City's Side

The City's fullest account is its Amended Motion for Summary Judgment (2026-05-25). In brief: Wynn was the City's chief executive officer and therefore could not "blow the whistle" to himself about departments he supervised; his claimed disclosures were an auditor questionnaire on which he affirmatively denied awareness of fraud, and letters describing internal-controls work that was his own job; and the firing was for documented cause — the City's evidence shows he received the water tank site plan and dimensions in June 2022 and personally picked the tank's color, then told the Council in December 2022 he was seeing the site plan for the first time. See the source page for the full breakdown, including the Whitmore declaration (Wynn allegedly asking to pre-screen records gathered for a public records request) and the felony accusation against Councilor Key that the City Attorney concluded was wrongful.
