What Was Requested

Three categories of records, covering March 25 through March 30, 2026 — the six-day window in which Lucy Hunter distributed her flyer, the Board held a special meeting about Pete, and the Board published its March 30 letter calling Pete's flyer "libelous":

  1. All communications (email, text, Microsoft Teams, written correspondence) between any combination of Mayor Paige Smith, Wynn, Buchner, and any Trustee, regarding Pete McCarthy, Pete's flyer, or the Board's written response.
  2. All drafts, revisions, and tracked changes of the March 30 response letter.
  3. All legal opinions, memoranda, or written advice from Buchner regarding use of "libelous," "libel," or "defamation" in connection with Pete McCarthy, his flyer, or any citizen communication.

See CORA Request - Libelous Communications for the original request.

What Was Produced

Four documents total. All four are emails. No text messages, no Teams messages, no other written correspondence.

1. Pete's own 3/30 complaint email

  • From: Pete McCarthy → all seven board members, cc'd Samira Vetter
  • Time: 3/30/2026 3:29 PM
  • Content: Pete's own formal complaint transmitting board-letter-2026-03-30.pdf.

This is a record Pete wrote and sent himself.

2. Paige's internal forward to Wynn

  • From: Paige Smith → Stefen Wynn
  • Time: 3/30/2026 3:37 PM (8 minutes after Pete's email)
  • Content: Forward of Pete's complaint. No added commentary, just the Outlook boilerplate signature and CORA disclaimer.

The Mayor forwarded a complaint about the Town Administrator directly to the Town Administrator within 8 minutes. The complaint is about the pattern of routing complaints about Wynn through Wynn.

3. Lucy Hunter's 3/26 email to the Mayor about her flyer

  • From: Lucy Hunter → Paige Smith
  • Time: 3/26/2026 10:03 AM
  • Subject: "My Flyer"
  • Content: Lucy acknowledges she created and distributed the "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator" flyer of her own accord, "didn't tell you or any other Trustee what I was doing," and "did just ask Stefen to review for accuracy as he was the subject of much of it." Apologizes for difficulties this may cause the Board.

This email was sent four days before the special meeting and five days before the Board's letter calling the flyer's subject-matter "libelous." Lucy wrote the flyer. Wynn reviewed it. Then the Board called the same subject-matter libelous.

4. Lucy Hunter's 3/30 reply to Pete

  • From: Lucy Hunter → Pete McCarthy
  • Time: 3/30/2026 6:04 PM
  • Content: Individual-capacity acknowledgment of Pete's complaint. Declines to address substance citing "pending litigation" and "personnel matters." States: "A municipality should have a clear, documented, and accessible process that ensures complaints of this nature are received by the Board directly — not routed through the subject of the complaint."

Lucy is the only Trustee who responded to Pete's complaint. The same Trustee who authored the flyer the Board had called libelous 2.5 hours earlier.

What Was NOT Produced

No emails calling or scheduling the March 30 special meeting

Under Colorado's open-meetings law (C.R.S. 24-6-402), a municipality must post notice of a special meeting at least 24 hours in advance. The March 30 special meeting therefore had to be scheduled by at least March 29. Someone on the board coordinated that scheduling. Someone drafted the notice. Someone posted it to the Town website.

Not a single email about that process was produced. No Trustee-to-Trustee scheduling email. No email from or to Vetter about posting the notice. No email about the agenda. No email from the Mayor to any other Trustee about calling the meeting.

If those communications happened by email, they were withheld or not searched. If they happened any other way (phone, in person, text, Teams), the request explicitly asked for text and Teams messages. None were produced.

No draft or tracked-changes version of the letter

The request asked for "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" of the March 30 letter. The response points to two already-publicly-posted versions:

  • The final agenda packet version: https://paoniaco.portal.civicclerk.com/event/562/files/agenda/1320
  • The revised public version dated March 31: https://townofpaonia.colorado.gov/sites/townofpaonia/files/documents/Letter%20to%20Paonia%20Citizens%20-%2003312026.pdf

No draft with tracked changes. No Word version with revision history. No email circulating a draft for comment. A seven-Trustee board does not produce a joint written statement without somebody drafting it, circulating it, and collecting edits. None of that internal product was produced.

No responses from any other Trustee

Pete's 3/29–3/30 correspondence was addressed to all seven board members. The response produces one reply (Lucy Hunter, individual capacity). The other six trustees and the Mayor are absent from the production. Either no one else responded in any form during those six days (implausible given they convened a special meeting about him), or those responses were withheld.

All attorney communications withheld as privileged

Item 3 — Buchner's legal advice about use of "libelous" — was withheld entirely. The clerk cites:

  • C.R.S. § 24-72-204(1)(a) — CORA exemption for records required to be kept confidential by state law
  • C.R.S. § 13-90-107(1)(b) — attorney-client privilege evidentiary rule
  • C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)(a)(IV) — specific CORA exemption for records of attorney-client communications

No privilege log was provided. There is no way for the requester or a reviewing court to assess the scope of what was withheld. Colorado courts have held that blanket assertions of privilege without a privilege log are disfavored; the custodian must identify each withheld record by date, author, recipient, and general subject-matter.

Redaction Rationale

The response letter identifies no itemized redactions in the four produced emails beyond a black box over a name in the cc line of Lucy's 3/30 reply ("Paonia Legal" — presumably the Buchner email address).

Timing Analysis

  • Request filed: 2026-04-06 at 11:22 AM MDT
  • Acknowledged: 2026-04-07 at 9:03 AM MDT, deadline set to April 10 (Friday)
  • Extension taken: before April 10, pushing deadline to April 22
  • Response issued: 2026-04-21

The clerk's office used 14 of the 15 available working days for a request whose responsive universe is six calendar days of email between seven named officials and the Town Attorney. The production consists of four emails, two of which (Lucy Hunter wrote) are surface-level, plus Pete's own email and a one-click forward.

Significance

This response is the central record showing the Town is either withholding or not preserving internal deliberations around the decision to convene a special meeting about a citizen and publish a letter accusing him of libel.

Specifically, either:

  1. Material exists and was withheld in violation of CORA. The attorney-client privilege claim covers the Buchner advice. It does not cover trustee-to-trustee communications. It does not cover emails from the Mayor to Trustees about scheduling a special meeting. If those records exist and were not produced, that is an unlawful denial.

  2. Material exists on non-email channels that were not searched. The request included text messages, Microsoft Teams messages, and written correspondence. If the Board coordinated the special meeting and the public letter through Teams, personal email, phone, or in-person meetings, CORA still covers those records (or documents them as not existing in searchable form).

  3. Material does not exist because coordination happened off the record. If seven elected officials coordinated a special meeting about a citizen without any written communication about scheduling, agenda, or response drafting, that is itself a process problem — a board that operates outside of records law is a board that cannot be held accountable through records law.

Each of the three possibilities supports the same conclusion: the Board's March 30 action against Pete was developed through channels that are invisible to the public.

The audit log now NAMES the withheld drafts

The Town's own Microsoft 365 unified audit log — produced under C 26-12 (cure dated 2026-05-27) — records, by subject and timestamp, draft and comment-collection threads from the same window that fit "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" but were not among the two already-published letters Vetter pointed to, and were not logged as withheld:

  • Re: draft "letter from Trustee" — 3/27 15:48 → 3/28 16:44; in the paiges, samirav, and stefenw mailboxes — a draft trustee letter circulating among the Mayor, the Clerk, and the Town Administrator, with no attorney on it.
  • Response to the Citizen's 10 things flyer — 3/28 20:47; paiges.
  • Response to the Citizen's 10 things flyer . I Need comments /no later than 2:00 tomorrow — 3/28 21:08 → 3/31 01:33; paiges and the ptownlegal Town legal mailbox — the comment-collection thread on the response letter itself.
  • RE: PLease see the attached drafft Mayor's report for the Special Meeting — 3/27 20:16 → 3/29 00:13; in the lucyh, paiges, and samirav mailboxes — a same-window special-meeting drafting item with no attorney on it. The subject describes a Mayor's report; with no body in the log, whether it overlaps the published letter cannot be confirmed from the subject alone, so it is named as an undisclosed same-window drafting item rather than asserted as a letter draft.
  • Revised Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens including the agreed upon changes approved by the BoT at t... — 3/31 15:57 → 3/31 18:25; paiges, ptownlegal, rubens. (Dated 3/31, one day after the request's "created prior to or on March 30" cutoff — noted for completeness, not claimed as squarely in-scope.)

Caveats. The audit log contains only subject lines, timestamps, operations, and the mailbox each item sits in — no message bodies and no To/Cc recipient lists. It proves these subjects existed in those mailboxes at those times; it does not establish their contents, authors, or direction. The drafts in the lucyh/paiges/samirav/stefenw mailboxes carry no attorney, so the work-product and attorney-client rationales do not reach them; the items touching ptownlegal may be defensible as privileged, but the Town provided no privilege log identifying any withheld record by date, author, recipient, and general subject (see Clayton Buchner). The net finding: the Town equated "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" with the two finished, published versions, while its own records name draft and comment threads it neither produced nor logged. This is the basis for the follow-up request built on the C 26-12 audit-log subjects (see CORA Request Tracking Log).

Deletions on the day of closure

The C 26-12 audit log also shows several of these responsive items moved to Deleted Items after C 26-11 was filed (April 6) — three of them in the Clerk's own (samirav) mailbox within about 25 minutes on April 21, the day the production was certified complete and the request closed: Re: draft "letter from Trustee" (3:28 PM), Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy (3:50 PM), and the draft Mayor's report (3:53 PM). The draft Mayor's report was also moved to Deleted Items in the paiges mailbox on April 23. MoveToDeletedItems moves an item to the Deleted Items folder — ordinarily recoverable, not permanent destruction — so this is a timing fact requiring explanation, not proven spoliation. It is the basis for the preservation/litigation-hold demand in the 2026-05-31 supplemental request, and for raising abuse of records (C.R.S. § 18-8-114) for independent review. See Pattern of CORA Obstruction and Discrepancies Register.
