They Removed the Trustee You Elected

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
They Removed the Trustee You Elected

In April 2024, the voters of Paonia elected Bill Brunner to the Board of Trustees with 67.7% of the vote — one of the clearest mandates a candidate can get in a town this size. Four months later, the same Board removed him on a 4–1 vote. The people who undid the election were not the voters. They were the officials the voters had elected him to oversee.

What he actually did

Brunner began doing what oversight requires: asking questions, filing records requests, and pushing for answers about how the Town was being run and how its money was being managed. None of that is misconduct. It is the job. A trustee who never asks an uncomfortable question is not doing oversight at all.

The resignation that was really an ultimatum

In August 2024, Town Administrator Stefen Wynn submitted a resignation — but made it conditional. He would withdraw it only if Brunner were removed from the Board. In other words, the Town's most powerful unelected official told the elected Board to choose between keeping him and keeping the trustee voters had just chosen. The Board chose him.

Mayor Paige Smith brought the charges and personally served as the prosecutor at the removal hearing. On a 4–1 vote, the Board overturned the result of an election held four months earlier.

The standard they set — and who it now points to

The charges against Brunner were "harassment and abuse of position" — resting in part on his asking pointed questions and filing records requests. That became the Brunner standard: the bar the Board itself set for stripping an official of office. The same record shows the bar has never been applied to the unelected officials whose documented conduct meets or exceeds it on every measure — beginning with the Administrator whose conditional resignation supplied the leverage to remove Brunner in the first place. Brunner is suing over his removal.

Why this is part of the pattern

Brunner is not an isolated case. He is the first of several people who, in different roles, raised concerns about the administration and did not last long afterward. Read alone, the Town has an explanation for each. Read together, as the record lays them side by side, they describe one consistent outcome.
