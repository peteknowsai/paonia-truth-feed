Summary

A detailed timeline documenting a single thread: Stefen Wynn received an email from a Planning Commissioner hours before a public meeting, then acted on camera as if he had never seen it. When Pete asked the Board to pull the audit logs that would prove it, the Town Clerk blocked the records request. When the Colorado Freedom of Information Coalition confirmed the denial was improper, nobody acted.

Detailed Timeline

Date Event
2024-09-27 Pete files CORA request for M365 audit logs (admin activity, May-Jul 2024). Town email forwarding to his personal address had mysteriously stopped working.
2024-09-30 Vetter denies the request, citing C.R.S. 24-72-203(2)(a), claiming logs are "not in the Town's custody." Provides no explanation of who has them or where they are.
2024-09-30 Pete pushes back: the Town is the M365 licensee and data owner. Cites Leonard v. Interquest. Vetter re-quotes the same statute.
2024-11-04, 1:13 PM Pete emails detailed questions to Wynn via his official Planning Commission address, requesting they be included in the meeting packet for that evening's meeting. Unable to attend due to flight delays.
2024-11-04, evening At the Planning Commission meeting, Wynn says: "Oh, we do have an email from Pete" as if discovering it for the first time. (Video: YouTube, Town of Paonia Planning Commission Meeting, Nov 4 2024, timestamp ~13:41)
2024-11-05, 1:10 AM Wynn forwards Pete's email to the full Board and Planning Commission. Later claims he "spent a 14-hour day" preparing to address Pete's questions.
2024-11-12 Pete submits public comments on the STR ordinance to Wynn, marked "PLEASE INCLUDE AS PUBLIC COMMENT FOR TONIGHT'S MEETING."
2024-11-14 Wynn dismisses Pete: "Your role as a planning commissioner is advisory, not legislative."
2024-11-18 Pete files formal complaint to all 7 trustees. Subject: "Urgent: Statutory Violations and Retaliatory Conduct Requiring Immediate Board Action." Specifically requests Board obtain MailItemsAccessed audit logs for the Nov 4/12 emails to prove or disprove Wynn's claim. Nine specific action items requested. Zero trustees responded.
2024-11-25 Pete contacts Jeff Roberts, Executive Director of the Colorado Freedom of Information Coalition (CFOIC), regarding Vetter's September CORA denial.
2024-11-25 Roberts responds same day: confirms Pete's legal position is correct. Notes C.R.S. 24-72-203(2)(a) is a notification requirement, not a basis for denial. The records custodian can only say "not in our custody" and must direct the requester to whoever does have them. Suggests also requesting the Town's M365 contract with Phonz+ (IT vendor).
2024-11-25 Pete files second CORA request for M365 and IT services documentation.
2024-11-25 Pete appeals the original CORA denial directly to Wynn. Wynn responds: "I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to... even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform and I'm doing this to accommodate you."
2024-12-02 Roberts follows up: CORA violations are enforced in district court; Pete must give 14 days' written notice before filing suit.
2024-12-02 Vetter responds to second CORA request with an attachment.
2024-12-02, 12:28 PM Pete resigns from the Planning Commission. "Two weeks have passed since I submitted a detailed documentation of serious governance issues to this Board. Your complete silence speaks volumes."

Key Emails Referenced

Email #1: Pete's Questions to Wynn (Nov 4, 2024, 1:13 PM)

Three areas: (1) Procedural authority, citing C.R.S. 31-23-204 on Planning Commission autonomy; (2) Meeting conduct complaints (staff calling Points of Order, Hunter's hostile conduct, staff member Ruben muting Pete mid-sentence); (3) Housing Element objection (28 new undocumented policies, omission of STR regulation which had community's highest-voted priority with 21 votes).

Email #2: Formal Complaint to Board (Nov 18, 2024)

Requested M365 MailItemsAccessed audit logs that would show: exact time email was sent, when/if it was accessed, through which client/device, and all subsequent access patterns. Also documented: Wynn threatening to skip Dec 2 meeting, bypassing Planning Commission on STR ordinance (violating C.R.S. 31-23-306), commissioners being muted, 28 new housing policies without documentation.

Email #3: CFOIC Confirms Denial Improper (Nov 25, 2024)

Jeff Roberts confirmed Pete's position was correct and the CORA denial was improper. Suggested requesting the M365 contract with Phonz+ and invoking C.R.S. 24-72-204(4) to demand written grounds for denial.

Email #4: Wynn Deflects CORA Appeal (Nov 25, 2024)

Wynn: "I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to... even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform and I'm doing this to accommodate you."

Email #5: Resignation (Dec 2, 2024, 12:28 PM)

References "the systematic dismantling of Planning Commission authority and experiencing persistent hostility." Cites the Nov 20 work session transcript documenting: Wynn threatening to abandon duties when asked basic process questions, Mayor and Administrator working in tandem to dismiss requests for documentation, repeated requests for written policy explanations being stonewalled.

Video Evidence

YouTube: Town of Paonia Planning Commission Meeting, November 4, 2024 Timestamp: ~13:41 What happens: Wynn says "oh we do have an email from Pete," acting as though seeing it for the first time. Pete had sent the email hours earlier at 1:13 PM.

The M365 MailItemsAccessed audit logs would show exactly when Wynn opened the email, on which device, and how many times he accessed it before the meeting. Pete asked the Board to obtain these logs. They never did.
