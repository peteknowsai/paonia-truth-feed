The Camera Ordinance Item (G.1)

Proponents: Trustees Lucy Hunter and Hector Mejorado. Status: Presented for discussion and edits, not for adoption. Staff Report explicitly states: "The Draft Ordinance is NOT up for adoption at this time. Possible motions include edits to the Draft and directing Staff for further edits / review."

Draft ordinance key provisions:

  • §5 — Mandatory Use Policy adopted by Board resolution before any camera operates, with inventory, access rules, retention cap, complaint process, training requirements.
  • §6 — Privacy Impact Assessment required before new installations; existing cameras must submit a PIA within 90 days of adoption.
  • §10 — Prohibited uses including facial recognition or biometric identification (unless a separate ordinance is adopted after public hearing), LPR aggregation, tracking on protected characteristics, federal immigration sharing without court order, and commercial use.
  • §11 — 180-day compliance deadline for existing cameras; non-compliant systems suspended, then decommissioned.
  • §9 — Annual public report on cameras, access log summary, complaints, incidents.
  • §8(b) — Access log is a public record subject to CORA.

Cited recital: "WHEREAS, the Federal Trade Commission has taken enforcement action against Verkada, Inc. relating to data security and access control failures, underscoring the importance of robust governance requirements for cloud-connected surveillance systems."

Administrator's Rebuttal Memorandum

At the April 14 meeting Town Administrator Stefen Wynn submitted a seven-page written memorandum titled "Verification and Clarification of Claims Regarding the Verkada Security Camera Systems." See Wynn Memorandum: Verification and Clarification of Claims Regarding the Verkada Security Camera Systems for the full text and The Administrator's April 14 Memorandum Compared to the Audit Log for the analysis comparing the memo's claims to the audit log.

Key positions in the memo:

  • States that People Analytics and advanced analytics "were disabled" as of Wynn's review on 2026-04-14.
  • Characterizes the POI-related audit log activity as "two isolated audit log entries" coinciding with "system installation and testing in October 2025."
  • Defends the log volume as routine and explains Community Room camera access as operationally justified.
  • Endorses adoption of the Hunter/Mejorado ordinance as "appropriate, prudent, and I highly encourage adopting."

Public Comment

Pete McCarthy addressed the Board during public comment on agenda item G.1. Prepared remarks asked the Board to:

  1. Correct the March 30 letter statement that the cameras "are not face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus," which is contradicted by the Town's own audit log.
  2. Turn off and physically cover every public Town camera until policy and compliance questions are resolved.
  3. Support Pete's citizen initiative to place the camera question on the ballot.

On 2026-04-15 Pete filed a written follow-up titled Factual Corrections to the Administrator's April 14, 2026 Memorandum citing event codes and row counts from the audit log that contradict the memorandum's characterization of the data.

Mayor's Comment on Verkada (Direct Quote from Meeting)

Near the end of the G.1 discussion, after public comment, Mayor Smith addressed the draft ordinance's recital regarding Verkada's FTC enforcement action:

"We also need to answer some of these questions about Verkada. I read the whereas. I have no idea what they did wrong. And if it meets what people think it does... I think that's important to get straight, because we may be knocking a piece of software that maybe doesn't deserve it."

Source: meeting audio transcription, raw/transcripts/2026-04-14-regular-board-meeting.txt.

This is the same Mayor who signed the March 30, 2026 Letter to Paonia Citizens describing the 10 Things flyer as "misinformation and blatant falsehoods" and called the Mayor Report agenda item in which she recommended taxpayer-funded display ads across three newspapers to rebut it. The 10 Things flyer stated: "$50K+ on Verkada cloud cameras — a company fined $2.95M by the FTC," with "FTC.gov" cited as source. This is a verified public federal consent order.

The March 30 letter characterized this claim as misinformation. The April 14 comment acknowledges the Mayor was unfamiliar with what Verkada "did wrong" when she made that characterization.

Outcome

Draft ordinance was not adopted. Board discussion and potential directions to staff for further edits are the subject of the meeting audio, which is being transcribed.
