Summary

Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting -- April 28, 2020 (Virtual/Zoom)

Key Decisions

  • Consent agenda approved (March 10 minutes + Rio Bravo liquor license renewal) -- passed unanimously
  • March 24 minutes approved with additions to Finance/Personnel and Tree Board committee reports -- passed unanimously
  • April 14 minutes approved with corrections to police report and Government Affairs/Public Safety committee report -- passed unanimously
  • Disbursements approved -- accounts payable $18,745.76, Summit transfer $50,000, payroll $19,480.93, payroll taxes $17,444.26, payroll direct deposit $19,480.93 -- passed unanimously
  • Mary Bachran sworn in as Mayor after resigning her trustee seat; outgoing Mayor Charles Stewart presided over transition
  • New trustees sworn in -- Tammy Mach (oath), Dave Knutson (affirmation), others
  • Karen Budinger appointed to fill vacancy from Bachran's resigned trustee seat, as next-highest vote-getter in recent election -- passed unanimously
  • Bill Bear appointed Mayor Pro-Tem -- passed unanimously
  • Committee appointments made (mayoral): Public Works (Bear, Mach), Government Affairs/Public Safety (Michelle, Nick), Finance/Personnel (Knutson, Budinger); Tammy Mach to Tree Board; Knutson as local government designee to COGCC; Budinger as Chamber of Commerce liaison
  • Work session scheduled for May 12 at 5:00 PM to review budget adjustments -- passed unanimously
  • Local disaster declaration extended through June 9 related to COVID-19 pandemic -- passed unanimously
  • Letters of interest requested for Planning Commission and Personnel Liaison Group, due May 29

Discussion Highlights

  • COVID-19 response: Administrator Ferguson reported 34 positive cases in Delta County (18 confirmed tests, 16 symptomatic close contacts), 267 hospital tests, 24 health department tests. Testing parameters expanding to include asymptomatic people. Town Hall reopening delayed past May 4; clear shields, one-way traffic flow, distancing measures being implemented.
  • Open Media livestreaming: Town pursuing Open Media for Zoom and livestreaming of meetings at $6,000/year, fully covered by a grant for communities under 5,000 people.
  • Bond sale completed: Bond closed that day, money in account, USDA payoff scheduled for Friday. Average interest rate of 2.9%. Treasurer Jones and staff praised for the work.
  • Budget density concerns: Trustee Bachran requested simpler budget summaries, charts, and quarterly comparisons. Administrator Ferguson cautioned that the pendulum has swung from "not enough information" to "too much" and urged any format changes be done publicly. Finance Committee and auditor already exploring simplified templates.
  • Budget reduction planning: Staff anticipating 10% revenue decrease ($56,000-$60,000 in sales tax). Discussion covered potential cuts: building inspector contract labor, hiring freeze, board members forgoing pay, cutting CML convention funding, reducing building inspector trips (possibly using Zoom inspections), evaluating police staffing given school closures and cancelled festivals. Ferguson pushed back that workload has actually increased with COVID protocols.
  • State/federal COVID relief: Ferguson stated reimbursement is "speculative" at this point; the local disaster declaration is maintained to qualify if programs materialize.
  • Abandoned trailer at old sewer plant: Police chief reported an abandoned, unregistered trailer with no VIN; tow companies reluctant; town will likely absorb removal cost. Suggestion made to post "no parking" signage.
  • Street sweeping: Ongoing; Public Works developing a notification program for residents to move vehicles.
  • Rupe Excavating invoice: Board requested clearer invoice descriptions to distinguish Clark Avenue sewer project charges from other Clark Avenue work.
  • Billboard sign concern: Public commenter Suzanne raised question about a billboard at Grand Avenue and 1st Street advertising an out-of-town business, asked how it squares with the town's sign code. Chief Ferguson said he'd look into it.

Public Comment

  • Deborah Spiegel asked about street sweeping completion on her street; told sweeping would continue with a planned notification system
  • Suzanne (caller) raised concerns about phone numbers being read aloud on Zoom, asked about alternative options for the abandoned trailer (free dump day, "free" sign), questioned legality of a billboard sign on Grand/1st, and asked about Zoning Board of Adjustment and Planning Commission vacancies

Notable Quotes

  • Outgoing Mayor Charles Stewart: "It has been a tremendous learning experience. At times it's been a challenge but it has been extremely rewarding. This board has always been civil. I think they've always been motivated by what you think is in the best interest of the town, although you may disagree on some issues there's been a lot of unanimous votes and I think sometimes people forget that."

  • Trustee Bear to outgoing Mayor Stewart: "I would personally like to thank you, Charles, for your work as mayor. I have used you as a mentor and have taken copious notes on how you run meetings and your civility and how you treat the public. Hopefully I will be as good a mayor as you have been."

  • Administrator Ferguson on budget transparency: "That pendulum tends to swing wide both directions. Previously there used to be less information provided and then the tone from some members of the board and the public was that we were suppressing information and not giving enough. So now we provide an extensive amount of information and we do hear that it can be a little confusing and too much."
