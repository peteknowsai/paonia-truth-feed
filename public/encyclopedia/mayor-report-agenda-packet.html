Summary

The mayor's report for the March 30 special meeting, setting the agenda for the Board's response to Pete's 10 Things flyer.

Key Contents

Agenda Item: "Trustee approval of a statement of fact clarifying approved budgets, purchases and purchasing policy to be distributed publicly."

Background:

  • "The Town became aware of the attached anonymous flyer (Att. 1.) on March 25th and subsequently learned that this flyer was being distributed throughout Town."
  • Refers to "the anonymous flyer" despite Pete having publicly claimed it.

Mayor's stated concern about lenders:

  • "One of the most dangerous consequences of these kind of untrue accusations being made against our Town is that it can place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies."
  • "If this unfounded negativity is left unanswered, then perception becomes reality and a lasting impression may be erroneously created that we are operating outside of the public interest, making us a lending risk in the present and into the future."

Proposed action:

  • Approve the public statement with changes and publish through display ads
  • Submit to Merchant Herald, High Country Shopper, and Delta County Independent as half-page or full-page display ads
  • "By doing so, we can directly control when and where it's published."
  • Budget: "Cost of display ads"

On Hunter's counter-flyer:

  • "Trustee Hunter recognized the need for an immediate response to set the record straight and created the attached flyer (Att. 6.) on March 26th and posted it throughout Town."
  • "This was done on her own accord, after consultation with the Administrator (to ensure her flyer content was 100% accurate) and the Town Attorney."
  • "This did not involve me or any other Trustee."

Attachments listed:

  1. Anonymous "10 Things Paonia should know about its Town Administrator" flyer
  2. Draft Statement of Fact (letter) to Paonia citizens
  3. Merchant Herald display ad price list
  4. High Country Shopper display ad price list
  5. Delta County Independent display ad price list
  6. Flyer refuting the statements made in the original anonymous flyer

Notable Quotes

  • Calls the flyer "the libelous flyer" in the section about Hunter's response
  • "misinformation and blatant falsehoods"
  • "untrue accusations"

Significance

This document reveals:

  1. The mayor's primary concern was not that the flyer was factually wrong, but that it could affect lending relationships
  2. The Board proposed spending taxpayer money on newspaper display ads to respond to a citizen's criticism
  3. Hunter's counter-flyer was coordinated with both Wynn and Buchner, confirmed in the mayor's own report
  4. The mayor continued to call the flyer "anonymous" in an official document despite knowing Pete had publicly claimed it

Discrepancies

  • The mayor states Hunter's flyer "did not involve me or any other Trustee," but the flyer was vetted by the Town Administrator and Town Attorney. The degree to which a trustee's flyer coordinated with the administrator and attorney but no other trustees is itself noteworthy.
  • Refers to "anonymous author(s)" when Pete had publicly identified himself.
