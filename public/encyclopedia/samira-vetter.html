Key Actions

CORA obstruction

  • Denied Pete McCarthy's CORA request for M365 MailItemsAccessed audit logs on the pretext that records were "held by a third party." Those logs would have verified whether Wynn accessed a specific email on 2024-11-12. source
  • The Town of Paonia has a prior judicial finding of CORA bad faith: Judge Steven Schultz found the town "failed to exercise reasonable diligence or reasonable inquiry" in the Brunner CORA case and ordered the town to pay legal costs. source

Initiative rejections

  • Rejected Pete McCarthy's STR citizen initiative on 2025-05-28 under C.R.S. 31-11-106(1). Rejection lacked specificity required by law. source
  • The Town Attorney's detailed legal analysis existed at the time of rejection but was withheld from the petitioner for nearly ten months. source
  • Rejected the surveillance camera initiative on 2026-04-03 on six grounds. source event
  • All six grounds were rebutted in a formal written response. source
  • Pete prepared split single-subject initiatives, a revised narrowed petition, and a Rule 106 petition draft for judicial review. source source
  • Pattern across both rejections: characterize provisions as "administrative" to place them beyond citizen initiative authority. In both cases, the clerk's stated basis mischaracterizes the initiative text.

Workplace conduct

  • Kaja Bowman observed Vetter openly expressing negative, inappropriate, and disparaging views about Pete McCarthy in the town offices on multiple occasions. source

Verkada camera usage (Oct 2025 – Mar 2026)

The CORA C 26-09 audit log exports document the following activity by Vetter in the Town's Verkada surveillance system:

  • 394 total events across the logged period. source
  • 360 live stream events across multiple cameras.
  • Verkada access group: "Town Hall Only", but logged activity shows views of Public Works yard cameras including Shop Door, West Side Shop, Shop Parking Lot, and Bulk Fill Station.

Specific dated sessions:

  • 2026-03-06 (Friday, 6:53 PM – 9:00 PM MDT): 44 events after regular office hours including 8 "Camera Audio Changed" events on the Community Room camera. source
  • 2026-03-11 (Wednesday, 1:21 PM MDT): 71 events in a single session including 7 "Video History Streamed" events on the Front Desk camera — scrubbing recorded past footage of the Town Hall counter where citizens file records requests and petitions. source
  • 2026-03-13 (Friday, 11:27 AM MDT): 23 events viewing multiple cameras including Front Desk, in immediate sequence with Wynn's session on the same cameras starting at 11:29 AM. source
  • 2026-03-25 (Wednesday, 8:42 – 8:56 AM MDT): 78 events in 14 minutes on the morning the 10 Things flyer was distributed. Viewed every camera the Town owns, with 18 views of the Grand Ave. exterior camera (facing Town Hall's front). Also performed User Settings Modified during the same session. source

CORA C 26-09 administration

  • Fulfilled CORA C 26-09 on 2026-04-07 after charging $167.90 in fees for 5 hours of staff time.
  • In response to item 4 (written policies, procedures, or guidelines governing Verkada use), produced no written policy and instead referred the requester back to Verkada device configuration screenshots: "See screenshots referenced above under 'Data retention policy…'"
  • Did not produce the "purpose" metadata (archive labels, descriptions, case numbers, notes) explicitly requested in item 3.
  • Redacted internal user IDs while leaving full user names and email addresses visible, citing phishing and social engineering risk as the rationale.
