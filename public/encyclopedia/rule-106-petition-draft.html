Summary

Draft petition for judicial review under C.R.C.P. 106(a)(4) (abuse of discretion) and mandamus under C.R.C.P. 106(a)(2) to compel Town Clerk Vetter to accept the surveillance camera initiative petition for circulation.

Claims

First Claim: Abuse of Discretion

Argues the Clerk exceeded her authority under C.R.S. 31-11-106(1) by adjudicating the constitutionality and enforceability of the initiative rather than simply determining whether it proposes legislation. Addresses each of Vetter's six rejection grounds:

  1. Single-subject: One subject (prohibiting cameras), structural provisions aren't independent subjects. Cites In re 2007-2008 #62.
  2. Body-worn cameras: Initiative exempts them; exemption is opposite of regulation.
  3. "Administrative function": Surveillance is legislative policy. Cites City of Montrose v. PUC three-part test. Cites Colorado SB 22-113, HB 24-1130.
  4. Contracts: Voiding violating contracts is standard enforcement.
  5. Injunctive relief: Creating a cause of action is legislative, not judicial.
  6. Voter approval: Even if invalid, severability clause saves the rest.

Second Claim: Mandamus

Argues accepting petitions that propose legislation is a ministerial duty. The initiative proposes legislation on its face under the Montrose test and Vagneur liberal construction mandate.

Relief Requested

  • Rule to show cause
  • Declaration of abuse of discretion
  • Declaration that initiative proposes municipal legislation
  • Order to accept petition for circulation
  • Costs

Key Legal Citations

  • Vagneur v. City of Aspen, 295 P.3d 493 (Colo. 2013) -- initiative power "liberally construed"
  • City of Montrose v. PUC, 629 P.2d 619 (Colo. 1981) -- legislative act test
  • In re Title, Ballot Title & Submission Clause for 2007-2008 #62, 370 P.3d 628 (Colo. 2016) -- single-subject
  • C.R.S. 31-11-106(1) -- clerk's narrow authority
