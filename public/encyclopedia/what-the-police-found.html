What the Police Found: Unfounded

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia

The Case Against the Critic traced, through audit-log subject lines, how the administration relabeled a sourced political flyer and a rally as a "threat / safety / litigation" problem — and noted that the message bodies, and any police outcome, were unproduced. They are now produced: the Paonia PD's CCJRA production of June 4, 2026 (Paonia PD Records Production (CCJRA, June 4, 2026)), the Town Clerk's C 26-28 CORA production of June 11, 2026 (CORA C 26-28: The Bodies Behind the Threat/Safety Subjects), and the PD's second CCJRA production of June 18, 2026 — the "No Kings Rally" emails (Paonia PD 'No Kings Rally' Email Production (CCJRA, June 18, 2026)). This page states what they show.

Three reports of "threats" — zero investigations

In 2026, Town Administrator Stefen Wynn took residents' political speech to the Paonia Police Department three times:

  1. March 12 (P26-0191) — a resident "making statements towards him on Facebook." Information only; no investigation.
  2. April 21 (P26-0289) — two citizen voicemails left at the Town front desk (one complaining about camera spending — "maybe you should fix the potholes on Third Street first. Have a great day." — one criticizing the Clerk), forwarded under the subject "Angry emails, maybe a threat?" Officer Henderson: "I did not find any indication of a direct and/or immediate threat. The situation was found to involve free speech as governed by the 1st Amendment of the US Constitution… This case is unfounded."
  3. May 8 (P26-0323) — a "formal criminal complaint" over a Facebook post of seashells spelling "86 WYNN," with requested charges under C.R.S. 18-9-111 (harassment), 18-8-704 (intimidating a witness), and 18-8-706 (retaliation against a witness). Officer Heiniger: "86" is bar/restaurant slang; "These posts would be Pete's first amendment"; Wynn is "not a victim or a witness to a crime at this time." Closed UNFOUNDED.

The PD also certified in writing that no record exists of the Department being asked to investigate or monitor McCarthy or any other citizen critic, and no record of referral to any outside agency.

The selectivity

The "86 WYNN" post had roughly ten distinct commenters — jokes, water complaints, "86 has never been a reference to murder," and former trustee Rick Stelter's insults aimed at McCarthy ("garage dwelling drone"). Wynn's complaint named exactly one commenter as an involved party: McCarthy's partner, whose comment reads in relevant part, "We have a lovely town, so let's have lovely people working to protect it."

A complainant genuinely reporting a threat reports the threat. Selecting, from a crowded thread, only the critic's partner is consistent with using criminal process to reach a critic's family — the same move as the false neighbor claim below.

The false statement to the Board and the Police Chief

On March 26, 2026 at 3:13 PM, Wynn wrote to the Mayor, all Trustees, and Chief Laiminger — in the email about the posters of his image as King Leopold II that appeared ahead of the No Kings rally:

"…our neighbor is one of the people behind posting and distributing flyers across town."

The neighbor was not involved. The posters were McCarthy's — made, posted, and publicly acknowledged by him — and Wynn's own May 8 police statement attributes exactly that imagery to McCarthy ("a pattern created by Mr. McCarthy through multiple medias, including … intentionally posting images of my face superimposed on King Leopold II with a flyer/poster during a 'no kings' rally"). His own complaint contradicts what he told the Board about his neighbor, and no correction to the Board appears in the record. (Note: these No Kings posters are distinct from the "10 Things" flyer, whose distribution the Town separately attributed to McCarthy in its internal March 24 email — see They Knew It Was McCarthy — Then Called Him “Anonymous”.) Wynn's May 8 letter to the Board repeats the association ("the overlap between these individuals and the escalating hostility directed at my family has become increasingly apparent").

The March 28 protection-order request

On Saturday March 28, 2026, the morning of the rally, Wynn emailed Chief Laiminger (CC Officer Henderson) and asked: "Who does this need to be forwarded to at the county level to begin seeking a protection order from Mr. McCarthy and his accomplices, namely Kaja Bowman, who was seen handing this flyer out." The "this" is the King Leopold II image and the "10 reasons … should be fired" flyer (Paonia PD 'No Kings Rally' Email Production (CCJRA, June 18, 2026)). He noted he had terminated Bowman in October 2025 and that she is his "lot line sharing neighbor."

In the same email Wynn forwarded a message he had sent his private attorney minutes earlier, asking whether the speech "has reached a level that has met the statute level for public officials" and stating "I've already had two elected officials reach out to me about what is being posted." The Town withheld that forwarded message from CORA production as attorney-client privileged, although Wynn had already shared it with the Police Department; see Paonia PD 'No Kings Rally' Email Production (CCJRA, June 18, 2026) on the waiver.

This March 28 request to the Chief sits in tension with the June 4 certification that "no record exists" of the Department being "directed, requested, or asked to … take any action concerning Pete McCarthy."

The May 8 sequence

  • 2:02 PM — Wynn at the PD, "irate," demanding the criminal complaint (P26-0323).
  • 2:50 PM — Wynn emails the Board "Notice of Intent Not to Renew Employment Agreement and Safety Concerns," CC Chief Laiminger and Officer Heiniger, citing the same three criminal statutes, describing the rally — "a peaceful protest" in his own police statement — as "intended to make me fear for my life," and announcing he is "seeking a civil protection order."
  • May 9 — the written report closes the complaint: unfounded; First Amendment.

One fact in this sequence rests on a firsthand account rather than a produced record, and is flagged as such: McCarthy reports that the police told him directly that Wynn was informed his complaint was unfounded First Amendment speech at the time it was made — i.e., before the 2:50 PM letter went to the Board. The written report memorializing that finding is dated May 9. If the verbal-notice account is accurate, the letter was sent over, not in ignorance of, the police's answer.

The Board received the 2:50 PM narrative. Nothing in the record shows the Board has seen the next-day police finding.

The standard the Board itself set

In August 2024 the Board removed an elected trustee for "harassment and abuse of position" — conduct that consisted of questions, records requests, and criticism (The Brunner Standard). The same Board has yet to agendize: an Administrator who repeatedly invoked criminal process against residents' protected speech, was rebuffed by its own police department in First Amendment terms, and made a demonstrably false written accusation against an uninvolved family to the Board and the Chief of Police.
