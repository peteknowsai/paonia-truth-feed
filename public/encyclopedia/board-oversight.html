Board Oversight and Accountability

From Paoniapedia, the free encyclopedia of the Town of Paonia
Board Oversight and Accountability
Issue
Record
TypeIssue
First recorded2026-04-05
Topicsboard oversight complaint accountability

Whether the Board of Trustees has a functioning process for receiving, investigating, and acting on complaints about town staff, and whether it exercises oversight of the Town Administrator.

Timeline

  • 2024-08-15: Board removed elected Trustee Brunner by 4-1 vote after Town Administrator Wynn submitted conditional resignation. event
  • 2024-11-18: Pete McCarthy sent formal complaint to all board members documenting statutory violations and retaliatory conduct by Wynn. Zero replies. source
  • 2024-12-02: Pete resigned from Planning Commission citing board's silence. Zero replies. source
  • ~2025: Cory Heiniger resigned as Public Works Director with accusations against Wynn. Board did not investigate. source
  • 2025-10-09: Kaja Bowman terminated 23 days after inquiring about the complaint process. Board's role unclear. event
  • 2026-03-30: Board called special meeting within 5 days to respond to a citizen's flyer; the same board took no action on a formal complaint for 16 months. event

Current Status

As of 2026-04-05, there is no public evidence that the board has a functioning process for receiving and acting on complaints about the Town Administrator. Three formal complaints/resignations with accusations against Wynn are documented; none received a response or investigation.

Key People

  • Mayor Paige Smith -- brought Brunner charges, disclosed Bowman's identity
  • Stefen Wynn -- subject of multiple complaints
  • Bill Brunner -- removed after exercising oversight
  • Kaja Bowman -- terminated after inquiry about complaint process
