Summary

Special Town Board Meeting — Emergency Sewer Line Repair

Key Decisions

  • Motion to approve up to $350,000 for emergency sewer line repair — Passed unanimously (voice vote, all ayes)

Discussion Highlights

  • The town's sewer main in the alley of the 100 block has catastrophic failures: collapsed pipe under First Street, missing pipe sections, breaks, and cracks throughout nearly the entire 533-foot run. Very few 40-foot sections are intact.
  • The bank had Southwest Systems jet and camera their service line; the contractor (Kevin) discovered the problem was in the town's main, not the bank's line.
  • The manhole on the south side of First Street is so deteriorated it can be broken apart by hand.
  • Town Administrator Leslie noted the irony: they had just submitted a grant for the Doris line, but it was this different line that failed — and this will be more expensive.
  • Leslie contacted DOLA (Department of Local Affairs) about loan funds to cover the cost, with the possibility of refinancing through CDPHE later. She emphasized not wanting to drain the town's reserves.
  • Corey (Public Works) explained the repair would effectively give the town new line from this section all the way to behind Town Hall. The only remaining old section would be between there and Third Street.
  • Corey had reached out to Tribble & Sons (unavailable) and R's (the only contractor currently working on a bid). Trustee Thomas requested due diligence calls to other contractors.
  • Leslie stressed this is a public health emergency — sewer lines can't wait for a prolonged bidding process. Construction was hoped to begin by the next regular meeting (Tuesday).
  • A planning commission update was deferred to the regular Tuesday meeting.

Public Comment

  • No public comment period was held (emergency single-item agenda).

Notable Quotes

  • Leslie (Town Administrator): "These are one of — this is a time when a town has to do what it has to do for public health reasons."
  • Leslie: "This is a sewer line, you can't just sort of delay for costs. You got to fix it."
  • Trustee Thomas: "I would like at least a call to any other capable contractors that we've used in the past... just so that we can get at least two or three bids."
