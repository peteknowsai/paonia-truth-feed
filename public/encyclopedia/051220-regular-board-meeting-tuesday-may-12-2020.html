Summary

Town of Paonia Board of Trustees -- Work Session & Regular Meeting

Date: Approximately late May 2020 (based on context: COVID references, Mother's Day water break, April budget actuals)

Key Decisions

  • Consent agenda approved (as amended): Liquor licenses and minutes approved, with contingency that Paradise Theater's special event permit for Picking in the Park (August) requires proof of insurance before license is issued
  • Planning Commission appointment: Mayor appointed Karen Budinger to replace Bill Baer on the Planning Commission
  • Tree Board ordinance (first read): Motion passed to adopt Ordinance 2020-XX modifying Article 7 (tree board) -- removed Section 7-590 (dead/diseased tree removal on private property), changed board composition to minimum 2 / maximum 6 members, changed role to strictly advisory on public trees. Second read at next meeting.
  • Municipal Judge interview committee appointed: Committee will consist of Town Attorney Bo, Chief Ferguson, Trustee Michelle Meck, and Trustee Dave Johnson
  • Town Administrator discussion -- no formal action: Board directed the mayor to begin discussions with interim administrator Corinne about extending her contract. Dave Johnson and Karen Budinger presented a decision document with five options. No permanent hire decision made; to be revisited at next meeting.
  • Fence ordinance removed from agenda by voice vote, referred back to Governmental Affairs committee

Discussion Highlights

  • Budget review (work session): Finance Officer Cindy walked through the 2020 budget-to-actual reports department by department. Key takeaways:

    • Sales tax revenue trending positively despite COVID, partly due to online sales (Amazon charging town sales tax for all 81428 zip codes, including county residents outside town limits). Potential future clawback acknowledged but considered years away.
    • Building permit fees not recovering costs of building inspector service. Governmental Affairs committee working on solutions including stacking inspections to one day/week and reviewing permit requirements.
    • COVID budget revision prepared with department heads -- line items categorized as fixed, caution, or reallocatable. 10% sales tax reduction assumed as precaution ($60K hit), though staff doesn't expect actual losses.
    • Legal expenses flagged as a concern -- already at $67K with only $63-64K budgeted. Board urged to consider ways to reduce legal costs.
    • Water fund: moratorium on water taps but some pre-moratorium revenue came in as a bonus. Working capital reserves adequate but not robust; policy target is 25-50% of expenditures.

  • SCADA system for 1-million-gallon water plant: Staff presented Brown's Hill Engineering proposal -- two options: purchase package ($62K) or 5-year lease ($102K total, $1,710/month). Staff prefers lease for 24-hour response guarantee, ongoing support, and equipment refresh at end of term. Current Paul system poorly supported (vendor based in New York). Board gave direction to proceed with due diligence -- references, contract review by legal, and coordination with ongoing asset inventory by SGM. No formal vote.

  • GIS mapping: SGM arriving within two weeks for 4-6 week GIS mapping project. Public Works marking waterlines, meters, sewer manholes in advance. System will track infrastructure, trees, sidewalks, and more.

  • Coordinated election deadlines: July 24 deadline to notify intent to participate in November ballot (marijuana question mentioned); September 4 for finalized ballot language.

  • COVID update: 55 cases in Delta County. County submitted variance request to CDPHE to create its own reopening plan.

  • Water line break on Mother's Day: Break near Dry Gulch, likely caused by water hammer. Repaired quickly but secondary break 200 feet away at a previous repair site. Minor water interruption.

  • Town Administrator position: Extended discussion with community input. Options presented: (1) negotiate contract with Corinne, (2) recruit externally, (3) run without administrator, (4) share administrator with Hotchkiss, (5) open. Johnson and Budinger recommended 3-year contract with Corinne. Community members split -- some praised Corinne's competence, others advocated for an outside search. Concerns raised about nepotism (husband is police chief), budget constraints for hiring both an administrator and clerk, and Paonia's track record of difficult administrator hires.

  • Whistleblower/retaliation policy: Finance & Personnel committee presented draft language for personnel handbook. Board asked to review before next meeting.

  • Spring cleanup: Staff proposed postponing from June to fall for budget caution. No decision recorded.

Public Comment

  • Dan Watson: Asked for page number references during budget discussion; questioned whether all 81428 zip code purchases are actually paying town sales tax, noting his own out-of-town orders show lower rates
  • Suzanne Watson: Requested Governmental Affairs committee reconvene on fence ordinance with new committee members; asked about tree board and municipal court meeting schedules being publicly posted
  • Deborah: Noted fence ordinance wasn't properly noticed for discussion; advocated for a formal discussion process
  • Thomas Marco (302 2nd Street): Argued the town has no authority over trees on private property per CRS 31-15-702; later argued for an outside administrator search, saying current staff salaries are among the highest in town and the community deserves the best candidate possible
  • Steve (remote): Recommended contacting Air Diffusion (airdiffusion.com), a family-owned water treatment company based in Chicago that has done work in Colorado; strongly endorsed retaining Corinne
  • Jamie: Pointed out the budget cannot support both a new administrator and a clerk simultaneously
