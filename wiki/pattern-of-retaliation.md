# Pattern of Retaliation

The public record documents a sustained pattern in which individuals who challenged [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Stefen Wynn]], raised concerns about his conduct, or inquired about the Town's complaint process faced consequences. Seven people are documented in the record across a 19-month window. The Board of Trustees has not opened a formal investigation of the pattern.

This page synthesizes across primary sources. Each claim is cited. The list is chronological.

## The Documented Cases

### 1. Bill Brunner, Elected Trustee (April 2024 – August 2024)

- Elected April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7 percent). [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|event]]
- Began exercising oversight of Town operations shortly after taking office.
- On 2024-08-01, [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]] submitted a conditional resignation to the Board stating he would withdraw it if Brunner was removed. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Removed by 4-1 vote on 2024-08-15. The charges were "harassment and abuse of position," specifically "harassing and bullying town staff to include statements of incompetence and assertions of criminal behavior." No written code of conduct was cited. The actual conduct at issue was asking questions, filing CORA requests, and criticizing administration. [[the-brunner-standard|analysis]]
- Filed a lawsuit challenging the removal. Active as of 2026-04-08.

### 2. Pete McCarthy, Planning Commissioner (Appointed August 2024 – Resigned December 2024)

- Appointed to Planning Commission in August 2024.
- Testified at the Brunner removal hearing that the harassment dynamic had been reversed in the Board's presentation. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Between 2024-09-30 and 2024-11-14, Wynn forwarded McCarthy's private emails to all trustees and commissioners with hostile editorial framing on at least three occasions. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- On 2024-11-04, submitted detailed questions about a project via his official Planning Commission email at 1:13 PM for inclusion in that evening's meeting packet. At the meeting around 1:37 PM, Wynn said "Oh, we do have an email from Pete" as if discovering it for the first time. Video is preserved on YouTube. [[meeting-2024-11-04|source]]
- On 2024-11-14, Wynn called McCarthy's Planning Commission work "a waste of Town resources" and dismissed his role with "Your role as a planning commissioner is advisory, not legislative." [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- On 2024-11-18, filed a formal written complaint with the Board documenting statutory violations, hostile conduct, and retaliatory behavior. Requested investigation. Zero trustees responded. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- On 2024-12-02, resigned from the Planning Commission, citing the Board's silence. The resignation was read into the record at the December 2 meeting. Zero trustees acknowledged. [[meeting-2024-12-02|source]]
- [[commissioner-watson|Commissioner Watson]] independently corroborated the hostile environment at the same meeting and stated she had been excluded from internal communications and that Wynn had slandered her to the Board. [[meeting-2024-12-02|source]]

### 3. Cory Heiniger, Public Works Director (Resigned 2025, exact date in record)

- Resigned with accusations against [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]] in his written resignation letter. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- The Board did not open an investigation into the accusations in the resignation letter. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

### 4. Kaja Bowman, Deputy Treasurer (Hired May 2024 – Terminated October 9, 2025)

- At the request of several town employees, approached [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]] on 2025-09-16 to ask whether there was a process for making a complaint to the Board involving the Town Administrator and HR. Bowman had not stated she was making a complaint. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]] [[kaja-bowman|person page]]
- Later on 2025-09-16, the Mayor contacted [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]], identified Bowman by name, and characterized her inquiry as a complaint before Bowman ever said it was one. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Between 2025-09-16 and 2025-09-18, Wynn made backdated additions to Bowman's personnel file, entering notes about incidents dated as far back as 2024-12-07. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- On 2025-09-18, Wynn pressured Bowman to issue a write-up against her direct report without permitting investigation of the underlying incident. Wynn told Bowman: "we might have an issue with your job" if she refused. Bowman refused. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- On 2025-10-07, Bowman issued a verbal warning to her direct report under duress. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- On 2025-10-09, Bowman was terminated "without cause" as an at-will employee. 23 days after her initial inquiry to the Mayor. [[2025-10-09-bowman-termination|event]]
- On 2025-10-10, the Town posted a replacement position publicly. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Bowman filed a complaint with the Colorado Department of Labor. The Department declined to allocate investigative resources.

### 5. Clint Rose, Town Employee (Terminated November 20, 2025)

- Terminated from employment with the Town of Paonia on or about 2025-11-20. The specific role and stated basis for termination are not yet documented in the public record.
- At 4:06:40 PM MST on 2025-11-20, [[ruben-santiago|Assistant Town Clerk Ruben Santiago]] executed three Verkada system actions in the same second: turned audio recording on TOP-HALL-CAM02 to 100 percent, turned audio recording on TOP-HALL-CAM03 to 100 percent, and created a permanent archive labeled **"Clint Rose Exits post-termination."** on TOP-HALL-CAM02. [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|source]]
- The audio activation occurred in the same log second as the archive creation, indicating that the audio capture was specifically enabled for the archive. [[verkada-usage-patterns|source]]
- The archive preserves footage of an employee being walked out of Town Hall after termination, with deliberately enabled audio. It still exists in the Town's Verkada account as of 2026-04-08.

### 6. "Morgan" (Former Town Employee, Terminated Using Archived Footage)

- A former Town employee. Identity is known but not disclosed here pending her consent to public identification.
- **Earlier history with the administration**: On September 18, 2025, [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Wynn]] pressured [[kaja-bowman|Deputy Treasurer Kaja Bowman]] to write Morgan up without permitting investigation of the underlying incident. Bowman refused. Bowman was terminated October 9, 2025 (case 4 above).
- At 11:33:20 AM MST on 2025-11-21, [[ruben-santiago|Ruben Santiago]] scrubbed Video History on TOP-HALL-CAM02 to footage from approximately 1:58 PM MST on 2025-11-20 (the previous day and the same day as the Clint Rose termination), turned audio to 100 percent, and created a permanent archive labeled **"Morgan Reading Email in Ruben's Office."** [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|source]]
- On 2025-11-21 at 2:20 PM MST, Santiago downloaded the archive. On 2025-11-21 at 5:06:40 PM MST, Santiago viewed the archive at the exact second [[samira-vetter|Town Clerk Samira Vetter]] logged into the Verkada system, and toggled the camera audio twice. [[verkada-usage-patterns|source]]
- **The Town subsequently used this archived footage as part of the basis for terminating Morgan.** Morgan was informed of the recording's existence at the time she was fired, when the Town disclosed it as part of the justification for the termination. She is no longer employed by the Town.
- The full sequence — Wynn pressuring Bowman to write Morgan up in September → Bowman refusing → Bowman fired in October → Verkada installed October 22 → Morgan archived November 21 → Morgan terminated using that archive — is documented across the audit log produced in [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|CORA C 26-09]] and the firsthand account from Bowman.

### 7. "Suzie Kaldis" (December 19, 2025 Incident Archive)

- On 2025-12-19 at 3:33 PM MST, [[ruben-santiago|Ruben Santiago]] created a permanent archive on TOP-HALL-CAM02 labeled **"Suzie Kaldis incident - 12/19/2025."** [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|source]]
- The nature of the "incident" and the relationship of Suzie Kaldis to the Town are not yet documented in the public record.

## The Verkada Infrastructure as Part of the Pattern

The Town's Verkada surveillance system was installed on 2025-10-22, thirteen days after [[kaja-bowman|Kaja Bowman]]'s termination. On the first day of installation, [[ruben-santiago|Ruben Santiago]] configured the facial recognition infrastructure in the following specific actions, recorded in the audit log [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|source]]:

- Created a "Persons of Interest" (LPOI) list
- Built an Analytics Dashboard subsequently named "Ruben's Dashboard"
- Configured automated Alert Rules with filter IDs
- Queried the LPOI list and the identity database (IDENTITY_PEOPLE_LIST_BULK_GET events)

Verkada's People Analytics product is marketed and documented as a facial recognition platform. The Persons of Interest feature enrolls specific individuals whose faces the system will automatically recognize on any camera.

Within the first 30 days of installation, the Assistant Clerk had created permanent video archives labeled with the names or identities of three specific Town employees (Clint Rose, "Morgan," Suzie Kaldis). Subsequent months added additional archives. Total archives of interior Town Hall cameras identifying specific persons by label: at least 4 through March 2026.

The [[board-letter-2026-03-30|board's March 30, 2026 statement]] to citizens asserted that the Verkada system was "motion activated" and "not face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." The Town's own CORA response to request C 26-09 produced records that directly contradict both assertions. See [[verkada-usage-patterns|verkada-usage-patterns]] for the full analysis.

## Common Actors Across Cases

The same officials appear in multiple cases.

**[[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]], Town Administrator**
- Initiated the conditional resignation that led to Brunner's removal (case 1)
- Forwarded McCarthy's private emails with hostile framing, called his work "a waste of Town resources" (case 2)
- Received accusations in Heiniger's resignation letter; no investigation (case 3)
- Directly pressured Bowman, backdated her personnel file, and terminated her (case 4)
- Holds Organization Admin access to the Verkada system under which cases 5, 6, and 7 occurred
- Accessed the Verkada audit log on 2026-03-26, one day after Pete's 10 Things flyer was distributed

**[[mayor-smith|Paige Smith]], Mayor**
- Presided over the Brunner removal (case 1)
- Received and did not respond to McCarthy's formal complaint (case 2)
- Disclosed [[kaja-bowman|Bowman]]'s identity and framed her inquiry as a complaint to [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]], triggering the sequence that led to her termination 23 days later (case 4)
- Signed the March 30, 2026 statement that misrepresented the Verkada system capabilities

**[[samira-vetter|Samira Vetter]], Town Clerk**
- Observed by Bowman on multiple occasions making disparaging statements about Pete McCarthy in the Town offices (case 2, contemporaneous with case 4) [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Dismissed employee complaints made to her in her HR role as "he is the boss, so he can act how he wants" (corroborated in Bowman's documented account)
- Logged into the Verkada system on 2025-11-21 at the moment Santiago viewed the "Morgan Reading Email" archive (case 6)
- Swept every Verkada camera in the system in 14 minutes on 2026-03-25, the morning Pete's 10 Things flyer was distributed
- Rejected Pete's citizen initiatives on 2025-05-28 and 2026-04-03 [[initiative-process|source]]
- Fulfilled CORA request C 26-09 with column-wide find-and-replace redactions and a $243.45 fee for less than an hour of actual work [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|source]]

**[[ruben-santiago|Ruben Santiago]], Assistant Town Clerk**
- Built the facial recognition infrastructure on Verkada installation day (2025-10-22)
- Created the labeled employee archives in cases 5, 6, and 7
- Generated 16,653 events in the Verkada system, or 51.5 percent of all activity, including 1,670 live views of the Community Room public meeting space [[verkada-usage-patterns|source]]
- Reports to [[samira-vetter|Samira Vetter]]

## What the Record Shows

Seven documented cases across 19 months. The same senior officials appear in multiple cases. The pattern includes:

- **Removal of an elected official** by a 4-1 vote after the Administrator threatened to quit if he remained seated
- **Formal written complaints** submitted to the Board and met with no response
- **Resignations of senior staff** citing conduct concerns, no investigation
- **Termination of a rank-and-file employee** 23 days after an inquiry about a complaint process, with contemporaneous backdating of her personnel file
- **Video archive preservation** of a terminated employee being walked out, with audio capture deliberately enabled at the moment of creation
- **Video archive preservation** of a currently-employed colleague in a private interior Town Hall space, without the subject's knowledge
- **Facial recognition surveillance infrastructure** built on the first day the Town's Verkada system was online, 13 days after the most recent documented termination
