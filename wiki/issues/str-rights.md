# Short-Term Rental Rights Initiative

A citizen initiative petition ("Paonia Short-Term Lodging Policy") establishing land use policy for short-term lodging in Paonia. Approved for circulation by [[samira-vetter|Town Clerk Samira Vetter]] on 2026-04-13. Proponents: Pete McCarthy and Melayna Rosa (119 ½ Dorris Ave). Requires 57 signatures of active in-town registered voters (5% of the 1,143 active voters as of 2026-04-07).

## What It Does

The ordinance has two core land-use rules:

1. **Principal residence hosting is a permitted use in every zoning district.** No special-use permit, conditional-use permit, or other discretionary land-use approval is required. If a Paonia resident lives in their home as their principal residence, they may host short-term guests (fewer than 30 days) without discretionary approval.

2. **Non-resident short-term rentals are permitted only in non-residential zoning districts.** Prohibited in any zoning district where single-family or multi-family residential use is a permitted use. Permitted in any non-residential zoning district.

### Key Definitions

- **Principal residence** -- established by two or more of: Colorado driver's license or state ID at the address, Colorado voter registration at the address, motor vehicle registration at the address, or federal/state income tax return designating the address as primary. Occupancy of 183+ days per year creates a rebuttable presumption of principal residence. A person may have only one principal residence.
- **Short-term lodging** -- rental or hosting of guests for compensation for fewer than 30 consecutive days.
- **Non-resident short-term rental** -- any short-term lodging in a dwelling unit that is not the host's principal residence.
- **Residential zoning district** -- any zoning district where the Town's code designates single-family or multi-family residential use as a permitted use.

### Structural Provisions

- **General laws preserved.** Building, fire, noise, nuisance, and health regulations of general applicability continue to apply, provided they do not single out or discriminate against short-term lodging.
- **Severability clause** preserves remaining provisions if any section is held invalid.
- **Effective 30 days after certification of election results.**

## Why This Initiative

On April 1, 2025, 70.4% of Paonia voters rejected restrictive STR regulations. This initiative honors that mandate by:

- Expanding resident hosting rights beyond current policy (no discretionary approval in any zone)
- Drawing one clear line: absentee commercial lodging operations belong in commercial zones, not in residential neighborhoods where they convert scarce single-family housing stock into full-time hotels during a housing crisis

The land-use framing matters. Classification of permitted and prohibited land uses within zoning districts is a legislative act of general applicability under Colorado law -- squarely within the citizen initiative power. This framing, and the pared-down structural provisions in v5, are what distinguished the approved version from the May 2025 submission the Clerk had previously rejected. See [[initiative-process]] and [[2025-05-28-str-initiative-rejection]].

## Evolution of the Draft

- **v4 Final Draft** (2026-03-30) contained broader structural provisions including an explicit "no license or fee" subsection, an "unlawful to operate, advertise, or offer" clause, a conflicts-with-existing-law section, a citizen-enforcement section allowing any registered elector to seek injunctive relief with attorney fees, and an amendment-and-repeal clause requiring voter approval.
- **v5 PRINT** (2026-04-06, the version approved) strips the petition to its land-use core: the two permitted/prohibited rules, definitions, preservation of general regulations, severability, and effective date. The removed provisions are precisely the categories the Town Clerk had used to reject the earlier STR petition and the surveillance initiatives ("administrative" contract voiding, citizen-enforcement as "judicial function," voter-lock as exceeding scope). v5 was engineered to clear those objections.

## Legal Basis

Paonia is a statutory municipality organized under C.R.S. § 31-1-203. Per *City of Idaho Springs v. Blackwell*, 731 P.2d 1250 (Colo. 1987), creating regulatory categories is a legislative act. Per *Margolis v. District Court*, 638 P.2d 297 (Colo. 1981), prospective regulations are legislative. Classification of permitted and prohibited land uses within zoning districts is a core legislative function under *Vagneur v. City of Aspen*.

As a statutory town, Paonia must follow C.R.S. 31-11-104, which grants broad initiative powers. Circulation and collection are governed by Title 31, Article 11.
