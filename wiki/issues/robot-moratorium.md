# Robot Moratorium Initiative

A citizen initiative petition to establish a temporary moratorium on autonomous robots operating in public spaces within the Town of Paonia.

## What It Does

- Prohibits operation of self-propelled devices operating without continuous human control in public spaces (streets, sidewalks, parks)
- Establishes no fees, licenses, or permits
- Classifies violations as civil infractions reportable by any person
- Automatically expires December 31, 2027

## Exceptions

- Emergency response robots operated by public safety agencies

## Why This Initiative

In June 2025, autonomous robots appeared on Paonia streets without public notice, explanation, or opportunity for citizen input. No one explained who owned them, what they were doing, or what data they were collecting.

This unilateral deployment of surveillance-capable technology, combined with the town's existing $52,000 Verkada camera system, demonstrates a pattern of expanding surveillance infrastructure without democratic participation. [[surveillance|See the surveillance issue]]

The moratorium is intentionally temporary. It pauses autonomous robot operations to allow time for proper public discussion and regulatory framework development.

## Status

- Petition prepared for submission
- No fees, licenses, or permits required, making the "administrative" rejection argument inapplicable

## Legal Basis

Per C.R.S. 31-15-101 and 31-15-401, statutory municipalities have police power authority to protect public health, safety, and welfare. Establishing a moratorium on potentially hazardous or intrusive technology is a classic exercise of legislative power. Per *Vagneur v. City of Aspen*, creating temporary moratoria while policy is developed is quintessentially legislative.
