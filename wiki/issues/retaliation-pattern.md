# Pattern of Consequences for Raising Concerns

The public record documents four individuals in different roles and time periods who challenged or raised concerns about [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Stefen Wynn]]. Each faced consequences. The board has not investigated the pattern.

## The Record

### 1. [[bill-brunner|Bill Brunner]], Elected Trustee
- Elected April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7%)
- Began exercising oversight
- Wynn submitted conditional resignation: he would stay if Brunner was removed
- Removed by 4-1 vote on 2024-08-15
- Filed lawsuit (active)
- [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|Full event page]]

### 2. Pete McCarthy, Planning Commissioner
- Testified at Brunner hearing that harassment dynamic was reversed
- Subjected to hostile environment from town staff
- Filed formal complaint 2024-11-18 -- zero response
- Resigned 2024-12-02 -- zero response

### 3. [[cory-heiniger|Cory Heiniger]], Public Works Director
- Resigned with accusations against Wynn in his resignation letter
- Board did not investigate

### 4. [[kaja-bowman|Kaja Bowman]], Deputy Treasurer
- Inquired about complaint process on 2025-09-16
- Mayor disclosed her identity to Wynn
- Terminated 2025-10-09, 23 days later
- [[2025-10-09-bowman-termination|Full event page]]

## What the Board Has Said

The board's draft "Statement of Fact" (2026-03-30) described the claim that "anyone who asks questions gets pushed out" as "blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation." The statement simultaneously denied the pattern and acknowledged that the town is being sued over it.

## Ongoing Litigation

- **Brunner v. Town of Paonia** -- Delta County District Court. Challenges removal.
- **Wynn v. City of Neptune Beach** -- Case No. 23-CA-8801, Duval County Circuit Court, FL. Whistleblower retaliation. Jury trial scheduled week of 2026-08-17. (This case is from Wynn's prior employment, showing a similar pattern alleged in a different jurisdiction.)
