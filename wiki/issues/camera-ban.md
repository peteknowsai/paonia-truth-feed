# Camera Ban Initiative

A citizen initiative petition to prohibit government surveillance cameras on public property in the Town of Paonia.

## What It Does

- Prohibits the Town from installing, operating, or maintaining surveillance cameras on any public property
- Requires removal of existing cameras within 90 days
- Prohibits third-party contracts that circumvent the prohibition
- Voids any contracts that violate the ordinance
- Allows citizens to seek injunctive relief for enforcement
- Requires voter approval for any future amendments

## Exceptions

- Temporary cameras for live-streaming public meetings (recordings deleted after 30 days)
- Body-worn cameras on law enforcement officers (governed by separate policy)
- Cameras used temporarily for specific criminal investigations pursuant to a warrant

## Why This Initiative

The Town approved a $52,000 Verkada cloud camera system with zero public comment. [[surveillance|Full background on the surveillance issue]]

The system includes capabilities the board's response did not mention: facial recognition, "People Analytics," photo-based person search, license plate recognition, and integration with Axon's law enforcement platform. [[verkada-10-things|source]]

No surveillance policy exists governing access, retention, sharing, or oversight of the footage.

## Legal Basis

The initiative establishes legislative policy prohibiting government surveillance cameras, a fundamental policy choice about the balance between security and privacy. Per *Vagneur v. City of Aspen*, 295 P.3d 493 (Colo. 2013), courts must "liberally construe" the citizen initiative power.
