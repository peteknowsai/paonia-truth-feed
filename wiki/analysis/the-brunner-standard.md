# The Brunner Standard

On August 15, 2024, the Board of Trustees removed elected [[bill-brunner|Trustee Brunner]] by a 4-1 vote. The charges were "harassment and abuse of position," defined as "harassing and bullying town staff to include statements of incompetence and assertions of criminal behavior." No written definition of harassment was cited. No code of conduct was referenced.

This analysis applies the standard the board established in the Brunner case to the documented conduct of [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Wynn]] and [[samira-vetter|Town Clerk Vetter]], using only facts already in the wiki with source citations.

---

## What Got Brunner Removed

The board's charges against Brunner, per KVNF and the board complaint letter:

1. "Statements of incompetence" about town staff
2. "Assertions of criminal behavior"
3. "Harassing and bullying town staff"
4. Filing CORA requests
5. Asking questions about town administration

No specific incidents were documented publicly. The standard was subjective and broad. [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|source]]

---

## Wynn's Documented Conduct, Measured Against That Standard

### "Statements of incompetence" about a public official
- Called Pete McCarthy's Planning Commission work "a waste of Town resources" (2024-11-14 email). [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Told Pete that Brunner had "nefarious ends." [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Dismissed Pete's role: "Your role as a planning commissioner is advisory, not legislative." [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]

Brunner was removed for "statements of incompetence" about staff. Wynn made statements disparaging a commissioner's work and characterizing an elected official's motives. By the Brunner standard, these are equivalent or worse.

### Public humiliation pattern
- Forwarded Pete's private emails to all trustees and commissioners with hostile editorial framing on at least three occasions (2024-09-30, 2024-10-31, 2024-11-14). [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

Brunner was not documented as publicly forwarding anyone's private correspondence. Wynn's conduct goes beyond what Brunner was accused of.

### Threatening to abandon duties
- "I will not attend the December 2, 2024 meeting unless required to by the Board of Trustees." [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- Submitted a conditional resignation on 2024-08-01, using his job as leverage to remove an elected official. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- At the November 20, 2024 work session, threatened to walk out when asked process questions. [[meeting-2024-11-20|source]]

Brunner was not accused of threatening to abandon his duties. This is a category of conduct the board did not address at all.

### Creating a hostile workplace
- [[kaja-bowman|Kaja Bowman]] observed Wynn and staff (including Vetter) openly expressing negative, inappropriate, and disparaging views about Pete in the town offices on numerous occasions. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- [[commissioner-watson|Commissioner Watson]] accused Wynn of slandering her to the Board of Trustees. [[meeting-2024-12-02|source]]

Brunner was accused of creating a hostile environment for staff. Multiple witnesses (Bowman, Watson) describe Wynn creating a hostile environment for commissioners.

### Acting deceptively on the record
- Received Pete's email at 1:13 PM on November 4, 2024. At the meeting that evening, said "oh we do have an email from Pete" as if discovering it for the first time. Video evidence exists. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]

Brunner was not accused of deception. Wynn's conduct on camera raises questions about candor on the public record.

## Vetter's Documented Conduct, Measured Against the Standard

### CORA obstruction
- Denied Pete's CORA request for M365 audit logs on grounds confirmed improper by the CFOIC Executive Director. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- The Town has a prior judicial finding of CORA bad faith (Brunner case, Judge Schultz). [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

Brunner was in part removed for filing CORA requests. Vetter improperly denied them.

### Initiative obstruction
- Rejected two citizen initiative petitions. In both cases, the stated basis for rejection has been disputed as mischaracterizing the initiative text. [[initiative-process|issue]]
- Withheld the Town Attorney's legal analysis of the first initiative for nearly ten months. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

### Workplace conduct
- Bowman observed Vetter openly expressing negative, inappropriate, and disparaging views about Pete in the town offices. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

By the standard applied to Brunner ("harassing and bullying"), Vetter's documented workplace conduct toward a commissioner meets the same threshold.

## The Asymmetry

| Conduct | Brunner (removed) | Wynn (no action) | Vetter (no action) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Statements disparaging officials/staff | Charged | Documented (multiple instances) | Documented |
| Filing/blocking CORA requests | Filed requests (charged) | Deflected CORA appeal | Improperly denied requests |
| Creating hostile environment | Charged | Witnessed by Bowman, Watson | Witnessed by Bowman |
| Threatening to abandon duties | Not charged | Documented (3 instances) | N/A |
| Public humiliation | Not documented | Forwarded private emails 3x | N/A |
| Deception on the record | Not charged | Video evidence (Nov 4 email) | N/A |
| Coercion/leverage | Not charged | Conditional resignation | N/A |

The standard set by removing Brunner for "asking questions" and "statements of incompetence" has not been applied to documented conduct by Wynn and Vetter that meets or exceeds the same threshold on every measure.

---

## Note

This analysis applies the board's own standard to the documented record. It does not argue what the standard should be. The question is consistency: if the bar is where the board set it in August 2024, the record shows it has not been applied equally.
