# Five Questions This Board Must Answer to Restore Its Integrity

**Read at the April 28, 2026 Regular Meeting of the Paonia Board of Trustees by Pete McCarthy.**

This Board has a pattern: it picks which citizen questions to answer, and deflects the rest. Until each of the five below is answered clearly, with documents, on the record, this Board's integrity is not restored.

## The Five Questions

**One. Mayor Smith.** On September 16, 2025, Kaja Bowman, our neighbor, a working mother, asked you one question: is there a process for reporting concerns about the Town Administrator. Same day, you gave him her name. Twenty-three days later, she was fired "without cause." Two years ago you got Trustee Brunner removed for "harassment and abuse of position." By your own standard, Mayor. What is this.

**Two. This Board went silent.** November 18, 2024, I sent every trustee a written complaint. Statutory violations. Retaliation. Nine action items. Zero replies. Two weeks later I resigned. Zero replies. Sixteen months. Then Wynn told you he was hurt by a flyer. Special meeting within days. Whose harm matters, and who decides.

**Three. Mayor Smith. Again.** Your published letter says, in your words: *"Erroneous and libelous statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position."* Wynn was fired by Neptune Beach, unanimously, for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct." Six newspapers reported it. Your own letter does not dispute it. Name the lie. Or retract "libelous" and apologize.

**Four.** On camera, November 4, 2024, the Town Administrator said, *"oh, we do have an email from Pete,"* as if seeing it for the first time. He had received that email hours earlier. The Town's email logs would have shown exactly when he opened it. I filed a CORA for those logs. Samira Vetter denied it. The denial was improper. **The records have since aged out.** The Town ran out the clock on evidence the Town Administrator lied to us on camera. When does this Board fix that.

**Five.** The Town has said facial recognition was never on. The Verkada audit log says otherwise: facial recognition was activated, and the Police used it. The cameras have been turned on Town employees. On November 20, 2025, the Assistant Town Clerk used a camera in this building to permanently record an employee being walked out after being fired. He labeled the archive *"Clint Rose Exits post-termination,"* and turned audio on two cameras to 100 percent in the same log second. Another archive that week was used to fire the employee it captured. When was facial recognition on. Who was on the People of Interest list. By the records. Not by belief.

Until each of these is answered clearly, on the record, with documents, this Board's integrity is not restored. To the trustees taking office tonight: the pattern is now yours to extend, or to end.

Pete McCarthy

## Detail and Documentation

The questions above are not rhetorical. Each rests on a specific public record. The Board does not need to take anyone's word for any of it. The receipts are below.

### 1. The firing of Kaja Bowman, Mayor Smith's role, in dates

This is not a personnel mystery. The chain of events that ended in [[kaja-bowman|Kaja Bowman]]'s termination begins in [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]]'s office, with conduct by the Mayor that the Mayor herself, in 2024, prosecuted as removable when committed by another elected official.

| Date | Event | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 2025-09-16 | Bowman approaches Mayor Paige Smith at the request of several Town employees and asks whether there is a process for making a complaint to the Board involving the Town Administrator and HR. Bowman does not state she is making a complaint. | [[board-letter-2026-03-30]] |
| 2025-09-16 (same day) | Mayor Smith contacts Town Administrator Wynn, identifies Bowman by name, and characterizes the inquiry as a complaint before Bowman has done so. | [[board-letter-2026-03-30]] |
| 2025-09-18 | Wynn pressures Bowman to write up another employee (Morgan) without permitting investigation of the underlying incident. Bowman refuses. | [[pattern-of-retaliation]] |
| (between Sept and Oct) | Wynn makes backdated entries in Bowman's personnel file. | [[board-letter-2026-03-30]] |
| 2025-10-09 | Bowman terminated. Stated reason: "without cause." | [[board-letter-2026-03-30]] |
| 2025-10-22 | Verkada surveillance system installed at Town Hall, 13 days after Bowman's termination. | [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs]] |

Twenty-three days separated Bowman asking the Mayor a procedural question and her termination. Colorado's at-will doctrine does not authorize retaliation against an employee for raising, or being perceived to raise, a workplace concern. The proximity, the **same-day name disclosure by the Mayor**, the **mischaracterization of a procedural inquiry as a complaint** before the inquirer had said it was one, and the backdated personnel file together establish, at minimum, the inference of retaliation.

**The standard Mayor Smith herself applied to another elected official.** In August 2024, Mayor Smith served as the prosecutor in the removal hearing of Trustee [[bill-brunner|Bill Brunner]]. The charges she prosecuted: **"harassment and abuse of position."** A 4–1 Board vote removed Brunner. Mayor Smith argued that conduct by an elected official that targets another individual through misuse of office is removable conduct.

By that standard, Mayor Smith's own articulated standard, what is the conduct of disclosing an employee's identity to the subject of her inquiry on the same day the inquiry was made, and characterizing the inquiry as a complaint before the employee had done so, in a chain of events that ended 23 days later in the employee's termination "without cause"?

The Board may not be able to discuss personnel specifics in open session. The Board **can** state, with documented evidence, that retaliation was specifically considered and ruled out by counsel, or it cannot. The Mayor **can** explain why her conduct here does not meet the standard she prosecuted in 2024, or she cannot. If she cannot, the public is reading the record correctly, and this Board has a question of its own to answer about whether the standard the Mayor articulated in the Brunner matter applies equally to the Mayor.

### 3. The Mayor's "libelous" charge, the exact words

A statement is libelous, in Colorado, only if it is (a) a statement of fact, (b) false, (c) published, (d) causing reputational harm, (e) made with the requisite degree of fault.

The sentence at issue, from the Board's official "Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens" published after the [[2026-03-30-special-meeting|2026-03-30 special meeting]], is:

> **"Erroneous and libelous statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position."**

The flyer's statement that the published Board letter is responding to: that **[[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] was fired unanimously by Neptune Beach in January 2023 for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct."**

Six independent news sources document that firing and those grounds. The Board's own [[final-published-letter|published response]] does **not** dispute that Wynn was fired by Neptune Beach. [[hunter-rebuttal-flyer|Hunter's separate campaign flyer]] also does not dispute the firing. The Board discussed editing the statement before publication and chose to publish it with the "libelous" framing intact (the [[original-draft-with-libelous|original draft]] also included "smear campaign," "ill-willed players," and "deliberately damaging misinformation," which were edited out). Reporter Tarnow recommended removing the libel-related assertions; the Board did not.

The Mayor owes the public, on the record:

- Identification of which specific factual statement in the flyer she contends is false (the firing, the unanimous vote, the cited grounds, or some other).
- The factual basis for that contention.
- A written public retraction of the word "libelous" and a public apology if she cannot produce the above.

A public official labeling documented political speech "libelous" without substantiation is itself a form of intimidation of citizen participation. It is not a matter this Board can defer.

### 4. The CORA denial that ran out the clock on a deception on camera

The substance of what the Town's CORA obstruction has hidden is not paperwork. It is the evidence the Town Administrator lied to the public on camera.

**The November 4, 2024 statement.** At a Planning Commission meeting that evening, [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]] said *"oh, we do have an email from Pete,"* as if discovering it for the first time. (YouTube, Town of Paonia Planning Commission Meeting, November 4, 2024, ~13:41.) Pete had emailed Wynn the questions hours earlier, at 1:13 PM that same day, on Wynn's official Town address.

**The records that would have proved it.** Microsoft 365 unified audit logs include `MailItemsAccessed` events that record exactly when an email is opened, on what client (desktop Outlook, OWA, iPhone Mail, etc.), from what IP, and how many times. For the November 4 email, those logs would have shown, to the second, when Wynn read it, on what device, and how many times before he stood at the meeting and acted as if he hadn't.

**The CORA denial that ran out the clock.**

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| 2024-09-27 | Pete files CORA for M365 audit logs (admin activity, May–July 2024). |
| 2024-09-30 | Town Clerk [[samira-vetter|Samira Vetter]] denies the request, citing C.R.S. 24-72-203(2)(a) and claiming the logs are "not in the Town's custody." Provides no further explanation. |
| 2024-09-30 | Pete pushes back: the Town is the M365 licensee and data owner, citing *Leonard v. Interquest*. Vetter re-quotes the same statute. |
| 2024-11-04, 1:13 PM | Pete emails questions to Wynn for that evening's Planning Commission meeting. |
| 2024-11-04, evening | Wynn says on camera "oh, we do have an email from Pete" as if seeing it for the first time. |
| 2024-11-18 | Pete's formal complaint to the Board specifically requests the Board obtain the `MailItemsAccessed` logs that would prove or disprove the on-camera statement. **No trustee responds.** |
| 2024-11-25 | Jeff Roberts, Executive Director of the Colorado Freedom of Information Coalition (CFOIC), confirms in writing that Vetter's denial under § 24-72-203(2)(a) was improper, that statute is a notification requirement, not a basis for denial. |
| 2024-11-25 | Pete appeals to Wynn directly. Wynn responds: *"I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to … even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform and I'm doing this to accommodate you."* |
| (subsequent) | The 2024 M365 audit logs age out of M365 retention. |

The records that would have settled the question of whether the Town Administrator lied on camera no longer exist.

**The CORA pattern continues.** [CRS § 24-72-204](https://leg.colorado.gov/sites/default/files/images/olls/crs2018-title-24.pdf) governs grounds for denial of inspection of public records. The statute requires:

- That the custodian state, in writing, the **specific** statutory exemption that justifies any withholding (§ 24-72-204(3)(b));
- That a denial cite the specific authority on which it rests, not a generalized claim of privilege, breadth, or burden;
- That fees be limited to actual research-and-retrieval costs, with the first hour of research free in many circumstances (§ 24-72-205(6));
- That a privilege log be produced when attorney-client or work-product privilege is asserted, sufficient for the requestor and a court to evaluate the claim, typically date, sender, recipient, and subject, with the body redactable but the metadata not.

Recent CORA fulfillments by this Town have done all of the following:

- Redacted entire subject lines across email productions without an itemized exemption per redaction.
- Withheld attorney-board correspondence without producing a privilege log meeting the standards above.
- Reversed an earlier denial by the Town Clerk on the M365 audit-log records (the request that produced [[cora-c26-12-m365-audit-logs-response|C 26-12]]) without published legal explanation for the reversal.
- Charged the requestor for the labor of redaction in instances where the redactions themselves lack documented statutory basis.

Each, in isolation, is a CORA defect. Together, they form a pattern of obstruction with standing to be enforced in district court.

### 5. The facial-recognition contradiction and the named-employee archives

Public records produced under [[cora-c26-09-verkada-audit-logs|CORA C 26-09]] (the Town's complete Verkada audit log, October 3, 2025 through March 26, 2026) document two distinct categories of facts the Town has not addressed:

**Category A, Facial recognition was activated and used.** The Town's prior position has been that facial recognition was never in use. The audit log shows the feature was activated and accessed by Police Department personnel. The April 24 staff memorandum ([[wynn-verkada-memo-2026-04-24|Item H.8]]) addresses the question with hedging language, "I believe," "in my professional opinion," "it is my understanding" or "I understand" appear nineteen times in that memo, but does not produce the precise records.

**Category B, Permanent video archives have been created on interior Town Hall cameras, labeled with employees' names.** The audit log records 19 `ARCHIVE_START` events. Five archives are labeled with the names of specific individuals, including four named Town employees. Specifically (full detail in [[employee-surveillance-archives]]):

- **2025-11-20, 16:06:40 MST**, User: [[ruben-santiago|Ruben Santiago]]. Camera: TOP-HALL-CAM02. Label: **"Clint Rose Exits post-termination."** In the **same log second** as the archive creation, Santiago set the audio recording on TOP-HALL-CAM02 and TOP-HALL-CAM03 to 100 percent, explicitly enabling audio capture for the archive.
- **2025-11-21, 11:33:20 MST**, User: Ruben Santiago. Camera: TOP-HALL-CAM02. Label: **"Morgan Reading Email in Ruben's Office."** This archive was subsequently used by the Town as part of the basis for terminating the employee it captures.
- **2025-11-21, 17:06:40 MST**, Town Clerk Samira Vetter logs into the Verkada system at the exact second Santiago is viewing the Clint Rose archive, indicating coordinated review.
- 2025-12-19, 2026-02-26, 2026-02-27, 2026-03-11, and others, additional Santiago archives by name or descriptive label.

Before any further vote on the Town's surveillance system, and before any vote or amendment of the [[draft-surveillance-ordinance-2026-04-28|draft ordinance under Item H.9]], this Board is owed and the public is owed:

- The exact dates facial recognition was first activated and last deactivated, with the user identity attached to each event.
- The complete contents of the People of Interest list, every individual added, the staff member who added them, the date added, the date removed.
- The complete list of permanent video archives, the user who created each, the audio configuration at time of creation, and any employment action that followed.
- Every administrator-level audit log entry for the activation and deactivation of any AI feature on the Verkada platform.

These records exist in the platform by design. The question is whether this Board will demand them, before voting, or vote without them.
