# Paonia Is Hiring a Town Manager: Here's What Happened May 29

*Published by paoniatruth.site. A plain-language recap for everyone who couldn't be in the room.*

Most people in town don't know this is happening, so here it is in plain terms: **Paonia is in the middle of hiring the most powerful job in town government**, the Town Administrator, and a special meeting on **Friday, May 29** nearly locked in a candidate before most residents knew there was a meeting at all.

Here's what you missed, and what comes next.

## Why we're hiring

Town Administrator [[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]]'s contract is not being renewed; he leaves in **about six weeks** (mid-July). The Board needs someone to fill the seat in the meantime, an "interim" administrator, and then run a search for a permanent one. The May 29 meeting was about the interim.

## The candidate: one name

The Board interviewed a single candidate: **Greg Sund**, of Trinidad. To be fair to him, he is an experienced, credentialed manager, and he helped bring Fisher's Peak State Park into being. But his record has a clear shape worth knowing:

- His last **permanent** job, Trinidad city manager, ended when he **resigned to avoid being fired** in 2019.
- **Every job since has been a short-term interim** (Walsenburg, Telluride, Mt. Crested Butte, Georgetown). In Walsenburg he resigned in the middle of a council meeting the night that board declined to keep him.

His full record, with sources, is here: [[greg-sund|the candidate's file]].

## It wasn't really a "search." It was a phone call.

Asked why only one name was on the table, staff explained: they called a senior advisor at the **Colorado City/County Management Association** (the managers' own professional association), who gave them Sund's number and told them there were **"no other interim candidates."** The Town found its *previous* interim the same way. No local candidate was sought. The contract itself was **posted only the day before** the meeting. How this pipeline works: [[the-ccma-pipeline|the full breakdown]].

## What it would cost

The contract pays about **$72.50 an hour**, on the order of **$140,000 to $150,000 a year** at full time, and the **Town would pay half of the manager's housing.** In a town with a serious housing shortage, that means relocating and housing an out-of-towner for a few months, rather than training someone who already lives here. The citizen alternative, grow our own, is laid out in [[admin-search|How Paonia Should Hire Its Next Administrator]].

## The "democracy" moment

When his record came up, Sund confirmed he once told a city finance director, "this is not a democracy," explaining it as direction from that board about who makes decisions. Minutes later, a former Paonia mayor told the Board from the floor, "we are a republic… so we are not a democracy." A town government is, in fact, a democratic institution, and how the people who run it talk about that is worth noticing. More: [[interim-tm-candidate-greg-sund|the analysis]].

## What actually happened: no hire (yet)

Here's the part that matters most: **the Board did not hire him.** A motion to approve his contract **died for lack of a second** after a trustee said she was not comfortable hiring anyone within 24 hours and without checking references: "cutting corners only sets us up for criticism in the future."

Instead, the Board scheduled a **follow-up special meeting for Wednesday, June 3, at 5:30 p.m.** to check references and decide. **You can be there.** Full quotes and the meeting record: [[2026-05-29-meeting-transcript]].

## The bigger picture

At the same meeting, a petition to **recall Mayor Paige Smith** was announced from the public-comment podium. See [[pete-mccarthy-statement-2026-05-29|the statement]].

## "But we have to hire someone." True. So let's do it right.

The board's honest reply is that the seat still needs filling. Fair enough. But the choice is not "this one candidate today, or no administrator at all." That is a false choice, and it's the one being used to rush the decision. There are constructive, doable paths here that don't require pretending we can conjure a finished local manager overnight:

- **Make the interim a real bridge, not a back door.** A short interim with a firm, written end date is reasonable. Cap it so it can't quietly become the permanent answer by default.
- **Even a fast hire can be an honest one.** Ask the same network for more than one name. Take a week. Check references *before* the vote, not after, which is exactly what one trustee insisted on, and why nothing was decided May 29.
- **Use the bridge time to design the permanent search right.** Define what Paonia actually needs, consider splitting the overloaded Administrator/Treasurer role, and seat residents on the search. See [[admin-search|the citizen proposal]].
- **Don't accept "we can't afford to do it right."** Running a genuine, open search is more doable today than it has ever been. The tools and the know-how exist to do this in the open, without simply outsourcing the choice to a single phone call.

The point isn't "just hire a local." It's: fill the seat honestly, keep the interim small and time-boxed, and don't let a rushed 24-hour decision quietly set the permanent direction of the whole town.

## The bottom line

This is not really about whether Greg Sund is a good manager. It's about how a small town fills its most important job: quietly, on short notice, with one name handed over by an outside network, instead of in the open, with real choices, and a look at our own neighbors first. The next chapter is **June 3 at 5:30 p.m.** If you care who runs this town, that's the room to be in.
