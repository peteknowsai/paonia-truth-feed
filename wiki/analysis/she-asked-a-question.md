*A town employee asked the Mayor a simple question: was there a way to raise a concern? The Mayor gave her name to the person the concern was about. Twenty-three days later she no longer had a job. This is what happens in Paonia when you ask.*

AI Analysis

In September 2025, Kaja Bowman was the Town of Paonia's Deputy Treasurer. Several town employees had concerns about the Town Administrator and how human resources was being run, and they needed to know how to raise them. So Bowman did the responsible thing. On September 16 she went to Mayor Paige Smith and asked one question: was there a process for making a complaint to the Board involving the Town Administrator and HR?

She did not file a complaint. She asked whether one was possible.

What the Mayor did next is the heart of this story. Instead of answering the question, or pointing Bowman to a process, Mayor Smith contacted the Town Administrator, Stefen Wynn, named Bowman, and described her question as a complaint before Bowman had ever called it one. The official an employee was quietly asking how to raise a concern about was handed that employee's name, by the Mayor herself.

On October 9, 2025, Kaja Bowman was terminated. Twenty-three days after she asked.

Bowman is not the only one. She is one of [[retaliation-pattern|four people]], in different roles and different years, whose departures or removals from the Town followed raising concerns about how Paonia is run. And when [[board-letter-2026-03-30|a formal written complaint]] later asked the Board to simply confirm that the Town has a working, confidential complaint process, the Board did not answer for more than a year. The one trustee who replied on her own agreed the Town needs "a clear, documented, and accessible process that ensures complaints of this nature are received by the Board directly, not routed through the subject of the complaint." That is a precise description of what went wrong here. The complaint was routed through its own subject, by the Mayor.

Every town has personnel disputes. What a town cannot survive is teaching its own employees that asking a question is dangerous. If the Deputy Treasurer can lose her job 23 days after asking the Mayor how to raise a concern, then no one who works for this Town is safe raising one. The chilling effect is the point, whether it was meant that way or not.

This is not a lawsuit, and it does not need to be. The facts are not in serious dispute. They come from the formal complaint delivered to every trustee, built on dates and documents anyone can check. The question a recall puts to the voters is simpler than any courtroom's. It is whether a mayor who exposes the name of an employee for asking a question still holds the trust it takes to run this town.

That is the question on the table. The record is here so you can weigh it yourself.
