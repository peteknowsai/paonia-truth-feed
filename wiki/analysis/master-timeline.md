# Master Timeline

Every dated event from the wiki, in chronological order. Sources linked for each entry.

## 2007-2012: The Embezzlement

- A single Town of Paonia employee embezzled approximately $480,000 over five years. Financial authority had been concentrated in one person. (9News, Denver Post, KVNF) [[citation-sheet|source]]

## 2020-01-06: Wynn Hired at Neptune Beach

- [[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] begins employment as City Manager of Neptune Beach, Florida. [[neptune-beach-case|source]]

## 2022: Neptune Beach Conflict

- **2022-03-15**: Wynn submits written complaint regarding suspected fraud in the building and permit department to City Auditors and City Council. [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2022-08**: Wynn reports police department budget overspending (over 100% of ad valorem budget). [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2022-08-15**: Wynn reports overspending and conflict of interest to Councilor Lauren Key. Key "harassed and verbally berated" Wynn at the City Council meeting that day. [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2022-09-02**: Wynn forced to apologize to Key. Told his apology was "halfhearted." Key stated she "welcomed his resignation." [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2022-09-19**: Wynn "lambasted" by Councilor Key at Council meeting, work performance "unjustly criticized." [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2022-10-22**: Wynn makes written complaint detailing harassment and claiming whistleblower status. [[neptune-beach-case|source]]

## 2023: Firing, Lawsuit, and Paonia Hire

- **2023-01-17**: Neptune Beach City Council votes unanimously to fire Wynn. Stated grounds: "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city." Actual trigger cited: failure to ensure proper permitting for a water tower. (News4Jax, WJCT, WOKV, First Coast News, Jacksonville.com) [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2023-05-16**: Wynn files whistleblower retaliation lawsuit against Neptune Beach. Case No. 23-CA-8801, Duval County Circuit Court. [[neptune-beach-case|source]]
- **2023-07-11**: Town of Paonia appoints Wynn as both Town Administrator and Town Treasurer. (Resolutions 2023-07 and 2023-08) [[citation-sheet|source]]

## 2024: Brunner, McCarthy, and Oversight

- **2024-04**: [[bill-brunner|Bill Brunner]] elected to Board of Trustees with 227 votes (67.7%). [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|source]]
- **2024-08-01**: Wynn submits conditional resignation: he would stay only if Brunner was removed. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-08-13 to 2024-08-15**: Brunner removal hearing. [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]] serves as prosecutor. Charges: "harassment and abuse of position." Pete testifies that the harassment was reversed. [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|event]]
- **2024-08-15**: Board votes 4-1 to remove Brunner. For: Stelter, Czech, Valentine, [[lucy-hunter|Hunter]]. Against: Swartz. [[2024-08-15-brunner-removal|event]]
- **2024-08-30**: Brunner announces plans to file lawsuit. (KVNF)
- **2024-09-27**: Pete files CORA request for M365 audit logs. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-09-30**: [[samira-vetter|Vetter]] denies the CORA request, claiming logs are "not in the Town's custody." [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-09-30**: Wynn forwards Pete's private policy critique to all trustees and commissioners with hostile editorial framing. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-10-31**: Wynn forwards another of Pete's emails to everyone, publicly framing his comments as contradictory. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-11-04, 1:13 PM**: Pete emails detailed questions to Wynn, requesting inclusion in that evening's meeting packet. Unable to attend due to flight delays. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-11-04, evening**: At the Planning Commission meeting, Wynn says "oh we do have an email from Pete" as if discovering it for the first time. (YouTube video, timestamp ~13:41) [[meeting-2024-11-04|source]]
- **2024-11-05, 1:10 AM**: Wynn forwards Pete's email to full Board and Planning Commission. Later claims he "spent a 14-hour day" preparing to address Pete's questions. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-11-12**: Pete submits public comments on the STR ordinance, marked "PLEASE INCLUDE AS PUBLIC COMMENT FOR TONIGHT'S MEETING." Budget work session includes Verkada camera discussion. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]] [[meeting-2024-11-12|source]]
- **2024-11-14**: Wynn emails Pete calling his work "a waste of Town resources," threatens to not attend meetings, uses his children to guilt Pete about a missed meeting. Forwarded to all trustees and commissioners. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-11-14**: Wynn dismisses Pete: "Your role as a planning commissioner is advisory, not legislative." [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-11-18, 7:49 AM**: Pete files formal complaint to all 7 trustees. Subject: "Urgent: Statutory Violations and Retaliatory Conduct Requiring Immediate Board Action." Nine specific action items requested. **Zero replies.** [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-11-20**: Planning Commission work session. Wynn threatens to walk out when Pete asks process questions. Mayor and Wynn dismiss documentation requests in tandem. [[meeting-2024-11-20|source]]
- **2024-11-25**: Pete contacts Jeff Roberts, Executive Director of CFOIC, about the CORA denial. Roberts responds same day: confirms Pete's legal position is correct and the CORA denial was improper. [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-11-25**: Pete appeals CORA denial to Wynn. Wynn: "I'm not the correct person to send a CORA request to... even forwarding this request is not a duty I am obligated to perform." [[reporter-package-email-coverup|source]]
- **2024-12-02, 12:28 PM**: Pete resigns from the Planning Commission. "Two weeks have passed since I submitted a detailed documentation of serious governance issues to this Board. Your complete silence speaks volumes." **Zero replies.** [[meeting-2024-12-02|source]]

## 2025: Initiatives, Bowman, and Cameras

- **2025-05-21**: Pete submits citizen initiative petition on short-term rental regulations. [[2025-05-28-str-initiative-rejection|event]]
- **2025-05-28**: Vetter rejects the initiative under C.R.S. 31-11-106(1). Rejection lacks specificity required by law. [[2025-05-28-str-initiative-rejection|event]]
- **2025-07-16**: Town adopts Comprehensive Plan. INFRA GOAL 10 (p.42): "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees." [[citation-sheet|source]]
- **2025-08-12**: Board approves Verkada cameras unanimously. Zero public comment. No surveillance policy adopted. [[hunter-rebuttal-flyer|source]]
- **2025-09-16**: [[kaja-bowman|Kaja Bowman]] approaches Mayor Smith at the request of several employees to ask about the complaint process involving the Town Administrator and HR. Mayor contacts Wynn, identifies Bowman by name, characterizes the inquiry as a complaint before Bowman says it is one. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2025-10-09**: Bowman is terminated. 23 days after the inquiry. [[2025-10-09-bowman-termination|event]]
- **2025-10-09**: Delta County Independent reports Tree Board members resigned, felt "unheard for the past five years." Their liaison called himself "the town's spy." Public Works stopped meeting with them. [[citation-sheet|source]]
- **2025-10-22/23**: Verkada cameras installed at Town Hall, Town Park, Public Works, water and wastewater plants. [[verkada-10-things|source]]
- **~2025**: [[cory-heiniger|Cory Heiniger]] resigns as Public Works Director with accusations against Wynn. Board does not investigate. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]

## What the Timeline Shows

The tempo tells the story without editorializing:

**Response to formal complaints:** 16 months, zero replies from any board member (except Hunter, 2026-03-31, after the flyer forced the issue).

**Response to a citizen flyer:** 5 days to special meeting, official statement, proposed taxpayer-funded newspaper ads.

**Pattern across individuals:**
- Brunner: 3 months from election to removal (April-August 2024)
- McCarthy: 2 weeks from complaint to silence; 2 more weeks to resignation (November-December 2024)
- Bowman: 23 days from inquiry to termination (September-October 2025)
- Heiniger: resigned with accusations, uninvestigated (2025)

**Initiative process:** Two submitted, two rejected, zero allowed to reach voters (May 2025, April 2026).
