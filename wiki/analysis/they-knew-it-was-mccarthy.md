# They Knew It Was McCarthy — Then Called Him "Anonymous"

The Mayor's report for the March 30 special meeting characterized the author of the [[ten-things-flyer|"10 Things" flyer]] as anonymous; the Board's published [[board-letter-2026-03-30|March 30 response letter]] then adopted that framing. The Town's own Microsoft 365 unified audit log — which the Town produced under CORA matter C-26-12 — records internal communications that named McCarthy as the author days earlier. This page sets the records side by side, in order.

A note on sourcing: the timeline below rests on the email, calendar, and Microsoft Planner **subjects and timestamps** recorded in the audit log. The audit log captures mailbox, operation, timestamp, and subject; it does not record message recipients, and it does not contain the message bodies. The bodies, attachments, and full headers have not been produced. A pending CORA request for the response-letter drafting records ([[cora-tracking-log|C 26-25]]) seeks part of this material; the bodies behind the naming subjects below remain unproduced. Where a finding rests on a subject line rather than a produced body, that is noted.

---

## The timeline

### By Tuesday, March 24, 2026 — the flyer is circulating

The [[ten-things-flyer|"10 Things" flyer]] was being handed out in town by March 24; the Town's internal reaction to it (below) is dated that morning. The flyer carried McCarthy's name; the Town's later "anonymous" framing is the subject of this page.

### Tuesday, March 24, 2026, 11:07 MDT — the Town names the author

An internal email originates in the Assistant Clerk's mailbox (Ruben Santiago) with a subject line that attributes the flyer to McCarthy by name: "Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy." The audit log records the thread fanning across Town leadership mailboxes — Administrator, Mayor, Clerk, town legal, the Police Chief, shared boxes, and trustees — within roughly the following hour, the Administrator's mailbox being the heaviest toucher.

The subject line names McCarthy. This is the earliest dated record in the set that identifies the author, and it predates the public letter by six days. (Source: audit-log subject and timestamps; the body is part of the pending request.)

### The internal task: "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer"

The Clerk's office opened an internal Microsoft Planner task whose title names McCarthy and the flyer together: "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer." Comment activity on that task appears in the log later in April. The Town's own workflow system catalogued the flyer as a McCarthy matter under a CORA tracking number. (Source: audit-log Planner task title and comment-notification metadata.)

### March 31, 2026 — McCarthy's name on a privileged forward to the Police Chief

A forwarded thread carries the subject "Attorney Client privilege: FW: Formal Demand for Retraction and Notice of Personal Legal Liability ... from Pete McCarthy." The forward, recorded across the Mayor, Administrator, Clerk, town legal, and Police Chief mailboxes, names McCarthy in its subject. The underlying document was McCarthy's own inbound retraction demand. (Source: audit-log subject and routing; body and privilege basis are part of the pending request.)

### Monday, March 30, 2026 — the public letter calls the author "anonymous"

Six days after the March 24 "by McCarthy" email, the Mayor's report for the [[2026-03-30-special-meeting|March 30 special meeting]] referred to the flyer's author as anonymous. The same response described the flyer as "libelous," and the Board's discussion that period included pricing out paid newspaper display ads to rebut it. The published [[board-letter-2026-03-30|response letter]] to citizens carried the anonymous-author framing.

---

## The contradiction, stated plainly

The records in the Town's own audit log show that, by March 24, internal Town communications named McCarthy as the flyer's author. The Town then opened an internal task naming him and forwarded a document naming him. On March 30, the Town's public-facing report described the author as anonymous.

The "anonymous author" characterization is contradicted by the Town's own records. The records establish that the Town possessed the author's name before it publicly described the author as unknown.

## What the records show — and what they do not

What the records show:
- The flyer was being handed out in town by March 24.
- A March 24 internal email subject named McCarthy as the author and reached leadership mailboxes.
- An internal Planner task was titled "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer."
- A March 31 forward to leadership and the Police Chief named McCarthy in its subject.
- The March 30 public report called the author anonymous.

What the records do not yet show:
- The message bodies behind those subject lines. The audit log records subjects, timestamps, mailboxes, and operations, not the text of the emails. The bodies have not been produced; a pending request ([[cora-tracking-log|C 26-25]]) seeks the related response-letter drafting records.
- The recipient lists. The audit log does not log recipients; routing above is inferred from the mailboxes in which each subject appears.

The naming point does not depend on the bodies. The subject "Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy," the task title "C 26-11 McCarthy -Flyer," and the March 31 forward subject each name McCarthy on their face. The March 24 email predates the March 30 "anonymous" framing by six days; the March 31 forward falls within the same response episode and shows the naming continued after the public letter.
