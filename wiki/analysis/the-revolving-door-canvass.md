# The Revolving Door: A Canvass of Manager Turnover in 20 Colorado Towns

*An analysis published by paoniatruth.site. It is not a Town document.*

The claim that small Colorado towns churn through professional managers is testable. There is no clean published statistic, so we built one: a canvass of 20 small Colorado towns, reconstructing the sequence of town managers/administrators over roughly the past 10–15 years from local press, town websites, and council minutes.

## Headline numbers

Across the 20-town sample (≈2012–2025 window):

- **~2.3 managers per town, per decade** (≈3 distinct managers per town over the window).
- **~3-year median tenure** — the pooled average (~4.5 years) is dragged up by a few long-serving outliers; the typical town turns its manager over about every three years.
- **~1 in 4** managers (15 of ~61) carried an **interim** title.
- **A manager was fired, placed on leave under investigation, or forced out in at least 8 of the 20 towns.**

By this measure **Paonia is worse than the sample**, not better: its own newspaper called the pre-Wynn era "the fifth administrator in six years."

## A smaller pattern inside the big one

The same individuals recur across towns — a small, mobile pool, not 20 independent labor markets. Examples surfaced in the canvass: Andrea Phillips (Mancos → Pagosa Springs), David Torgler (Hayden → Delta → Cedaredge), Russ Forrest (Snowmass → Gunnison → Vail), and [[greg-sund|Greg Sund]] (Trinidad → Walsenburg → Telluride → Mt. Crested Butte → Georgetown → now Paonia). This is what the [[the-ccma-pipeline|CCMA pipeline]] looks like from the towns' side.

## Method and caveats

- This is a **paoniatruth.site canvass**, not a peer-reviewed study. Town sequences were reconstructed from local newspapers (Crested Butte News, The World Journal, Telluride Daily Planet, Ouray County Plaindealer, Post Independent, Aspen Times, Delta County Independent, Gunnison Country Times, and others), town websites, and council minutes.
- **Confidence is marked per town.** "Solid" = named, corroborated sequence with dates; "Medium" = endpoints and counts confirmed but one transition date or mid-window interim is approximate.
- Tenure figures are **approximate**, and the average is sensitive to a few long-serving outliers (Mt. Crested Butte ~16 yr; Carbondale ~11 yr; Buena Vista ~10 yr) — which is why the **median (~3 years)** is the more honest summary.
- Two very small towns with promoted-from-within clerks (Crawford, Olathe) were left out as count-only; including them would *lower* the churn average, so the figures here are conservative for the smallest towns.
