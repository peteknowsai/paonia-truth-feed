# The Case Against the Critic

In late March 2026 a sourced political flyer circulated in Paonia naming specific concerns about Town governance. The Town's own Microsoft 365 unified audit log, produced under CORA, records what followed in the administration's mailboxes and calendars: a sequence of email and calendar items whose subject lines reframe the flyer's author, and an associated rally, as a question of threat, safety, and litigation.

This page lays out that sequence chronologically. A hard limit governs everything below.

## What this evidence is — and is not

Every item described here is a **subject line, timestamp, mailbox, and operation** drawn from the audit log. The log proves a message or calendar item existed in a given mailbox at a given time. It does **not** log recipients, and — critically — it does **not** contain message bodies. None of the email or calendar bodies below have been produced or read.

So this page states what the metadata shows. It does not assert the contents of any unread email. The bodies are the subject of pending requests: CORA C 26-28 to the Town Clerk and a separate Colorado Criminal Justice Records Act (CCJRA) request to the Police Department for the PD threads. Until those bodies are produced, the subject lines are evidence of how the matter was *labeled* internally, not proof of what was said inside.

That distinction is the whole point. A subject line that reads as alarm is not, by itself, proof of a genuine threat — nor proof of a manufactured one. It is proof of a chosen frame.

## The "threat / safety / litigation" subjects

These items sit in the mailbox of Town Administrator and Treasurer [[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] and, in the audit log, are attributed to him as the mailbox owner. Local times are Colorado (the audit log records UTC).

- **Mar 12, 2026** — a Wynn-authored item whose subject ties a "Meeker Resident" to "Safety."
- **Apr 2, 2026** — "CRCP 106 - Threatened Litigation." (CRCP 106 is the Colorado Rule of Civil Procedure governing review of governmental action.)
- **Apr 7, 2026** — two separate items both titled around "Threat of Litigation."
- **Apr 20, 2026** — an item whose subject reads, in part, "Angry emails, maybe a threat?"

Across roughly five weeks the recurring label moves from "safety" to "threatened litigation" to a self-questioning "maybe a threat?" The bodies that would establish what each message actually described are unread and pending.

## The Police Department angle

Two threads sit in Police Department mailboxes — not in the administrative or legal mailboxes — which places a political flyer and a political rally inside a law-enforcement frame.

- A thread between Chief Laiminger and Officer Henderson, dated in the Mar 28 – Apr 27 window, whose subject characterizes social-media activity as "bullying and harassment" ahead of an expected 500–600 political activists for the rally.
- A thread, in the same March window, framed around a "No Kings Rally," "Fear for Safety," and flyers posted "with my Image," ahead of the Mar 28 rally.

These are PD-mailbox subjects. No attorney appears on the "bullying and harassment" chain in the audit log. What the officers wrote, and whether any law-enforcement action followed, is not in the metadata; the PD bodies are the subject of the pending CCJRA request.

## The coordination angle: two invites, minutes apart

On the evening of Apr 8, 2026, two calendar invites were created back-to-back in the same sitting:

- **22:33 UTC** — "Attorney Performance Appraisal review," distributed to Wynn, the shared Town legal mailbox, and clerk/mayor/staff. This invite was seeded by a Mayor Smith email on Apr 1 ("Attorney Performance Appraisal and can we meet tomorrow") and a same-day reply from the legal mailbox.
- **22:38 UTC** — "Discuss Citizen Complaints re: Administrator."

The second item is notable for one reason the forensics make explicit: it is the **only** subject that appears jointly in Trustee [[lucy-hunter|Lucy Hunter]]'s, Wynn's, and the Town attorney's mailboxes. Hunter sent an "Accepted" at 22:47 UTC on Apr 8; Wynn accepted on Apr 9. The two meetings — an attorney/personnel review and a meeting about complaints regarding the Administrator — were calendared minutes apart.

The audit log does not preserve attendee lists, locations, start times, or agendas for these invites — only the subject and the mailbox actions. So what was discussed is not in the record. What the record shows is that the two items were set up in the same sitting and that the citizen-complaints meeting is the single subject tying all three principals together.

## The throughline

Read across the items, the metadata documents an effort to relabel a fact-based flyer and a political rally as a safety, bullying, and litigation problem — the predicate that pulls in the Police Department and the Town attorney. This fits the pattern catalogued in [[retaliation-pattern]] and [[pattern-of-retaliation]]: the same author who was already a named, tracked CORA requester (see [[they-knew-it-was-mccarthy]]) becomes the subject of "threat" and "safety" framing within weeks of the flyer.

Two cautions keep this honest:

1. The framing is established by **subjects**, not bodies. Whether any message described conduct that a reasonable person would call a threat is exactly the question the pending bodies would answer. This page does not pre-judge it.
2. The throughline is a pattern of *labeling*, traced across mailboxes. It is not a finding that any single official acted unlawfully. The audit log shows what was filed under what subject and when; it does not show intent.

## What would resolve it

The bodies. CORA C 26-28 seeks the email and calendar bodies behind the threat/safety/litigation subjects and the Apr 8 invites; the CCJRA request seeks the PD threads. Each is tracked in the [[cora-tracking-log|CORA tracking log]]. Production of the bodies would convert these labels into either substantiated concerns or an evidenced reframing. Withholding them leaves the subject lines as the only record of how the Town characterized its critic.
