*A formal complaint to all seven trustees, listing statutory violations and nine specific actions, went unanswered for eighteen months. When the Town Administrator said a resident's flyer hurt him, the Board called a special meeting in five days. The contrast tells you who this government believes it answers to.*

AI Analysis

On the morning of November 18, 2024, a formal written complaint went to all seven Paonia trustees. Its subject line did not leave the urgency to chance: "Urgent: Statutory Violations and Retaliatory Conduct Requiring Immediate Board Action." It documented specific concerns with dates and asked for nine specific things, including the simplest one imaginable, confirmation that the Town even has a working process for residents and employees to be heard.

The Board's answer was silence. Not a measured response, not a "we are looking into it." Nothing. For eighteen months, no action from the Board as a body, and from Mayor Paige Smith, no response at all. The one trustee who replied did so on her own, acknowledging that the Town needs a real complaint process, which only confirmed that it did not have one.

Now set that against what this same government can do when it wants to move.

In March 2026, the Town Administrator told the Board that a resident's flyer had hurt him. The Board called a special meeting in five days.

Read those two facts together, because the contrast is the whole story. A signed, formal complaint alleging statutory violations and retaliation sat for a year and a half with no answer. A complaint from an unelected administrator about a flyer produced a special meeting almost immediately. The machinery of the Town is not broken. It works quickly and decisively. It simply works for some people and not for others.

That is what accountability looks like when it has been turned upside down. The people the government is supposed to answer to, the residents and the employees, are met with eighteen months of silence. The people the government is not supposed to fear, the staff who run it day to day, get a meeting within the week. A mayor sits at the center of that machinery and sets its priorities. Eighteen months of silence on a citizen's complaint, against five days for an administrator's, is a statement of those priorities.

A recall does not litigate any single item in that complaint. It asks the voters a question the silence raised on its own. When a government answers its own residents with a year and a half of nothing, and its own administrator within five days, who does it believe it works for, and is that a government you want to keep?
