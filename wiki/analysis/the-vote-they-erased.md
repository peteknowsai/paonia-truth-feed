*In 2024, Bill Brunner won a seat on the Paonia Board of Trustees with more than two-thirds of the vote. Months later, four people removed him from it. This is how an election in Paonia was undone, and who undid it.*

AI Analysis

In the spring of 2024, the voters of Paonia elected Bill Brunner to the Board of Trustees. He did not squeak in. He won with more than two-thirds of the vote, one of the strongest mandates any trustee on the current Board can claim. The people had spoken, clearly, about who they wanted representing them.

By that August, Brunner was gone. Not recalled by the voters who chose him. Removed by four of his colleagues in a single meeting.

Here is how it happened. The Town Administrator, Stefen Wynn, put his own resignation on the table and made clear he would withdraw it only if Brunner were removed from the Board. That set up a plain choice: the administrator the Town hired, or the trustee the voters elected. Mayor Paige Smith chose the administrator. She did not stay out of it, and she did not leave it to the voters. She brought the charges against Brunner herself, and she personally prosecuted his removal.

On August 15, 2024, the Board voted four to one to remove him. The decision of more than two-thirds of Paonia's voters was canceled by four people, at the urging of an unelected employee, in one night.

Consider what that means for your own vote. An election is a promise that the person you choose will hold the seat. If a mayor and a handful of trustees can erase that choice whenever an administrator demands it, then the promise is empty, and your ballot only counts until it becomes inconvenient. What happened to Brunner is not really about Brunner. It is about whether elections in Paonia mean anything once the votes are counted.

A mayor is supposed to defend that promise, not lead the effort to break it. In this case the Mayor was not a bystander. She authored the charges and argued for removal, spending the authority of her office to undo what the voters had just decided.

A recall does not ask a judge to rule on any of this. It asks the voters to. It puts the question back where the Brunner removal took it from: in the hands of the people of Paonia. The question is whether a mayor who helped erase one election can be trusted to respect the next one.
