# CORA C 26-28: The Bodies Behind the Threat/Safety Subjects

**Raw files:** `raw/documents/cora-responses/c26-28/`
**Produced by:** Town Clerk Samira Vetter, 2026-06-11 (22 attachments: redacted PDFs, native `.eml` files, one screenshot)
**What it is:** The email bodies behind the "threat / safety / litigation" subject lines that [[the-case-against-the-critic]] could previously cite only as audit-log metadata. The bodies are now produced. The metadata-only caveats in that page are resolved for these items.

## What was produced

- **"No Kings Rally Saturday Fear for Safety with Flyers Posted Around Town with my Image"** — Wynn to Mayor & Trustees, CC Chief Laiminger and all staff, 2026-03-26 3:13 PM, Importance: High. Announces leaving town for the rally weekend. Contains the assertion: **"our neighbor is one of the people behind posting and distributing flyers across town."** The flyers at issue are the King Leopold II posters that appeared ahead of the rally — McCarthy's, publicly acknowledged, and attributed to McCarthy by Wynn's own subsequent May 8 police statement ([[ccjra-pd-records-2026-06-04]]). The neighbor had no involvement. (Distinct from the [[ten-things-flyer|"10 Things" flyer]], which the Town's internal 2026-03-24 email separately attributed to McCarthy — [[they-knew-it-was-mccarthy]].)
- **"Angry emails, maybe a threat?"** — the 2026-04-20/21 forward of two citizen voicemails (camera-spending complaint; criticism of the Clerk) that became PD case P26-0289, closed **unfounded** as "free speech as governed by the 1st Amendment" ([[ccjra-pd-records-2026-06-04]]). Three near-duplicate forward variants were produced.
- **"Potential Issue for My and Family's Safety from Meeker Resident Visiting Paonia"** (2026-03-12) and **"FB Message Board Threat 3.12.2026.jpg"** — the Facebook-statements matter that became PD case P26-0191 (information only).
- **"Potential Issue for Safety"** (redacted).
- **"Informational Update: Community Center Closure and Threat of Litigation"** — the "threatened litigation" framing in an operational update.
- Native `.eml` files and the Clerk's response letter.

## Significance

1. **The frame was the Administrator's, not law enforcement's.** Read with the PD production, each "threat/safety" subject traces to a Wynn-authored report or forward; police action followed none of them.
2. **The false neighbor statement is now in hand, verbatim, with its distribution list** — the Board and the Police Chief received, in writing, an accusation against an uninvolved family; the Administrator's own later police statement attributes the posters to McCarthy, and no correction to the Board appears in the record.
3. **The March 12 "safety" framing predates the [[ten-things-flyer|10 Things flyer]] (March 23–25)** — the pattern of converting criticism into a safety narrative was already in motion before the flyer existed.
