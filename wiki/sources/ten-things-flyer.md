# "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator" - Original Flyer

**Tagline:** "All facts from public records"

## The 10 Things

1. **Fired unanimously from Neptune Beach** in Jan 2023 for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city." Paonia hired him six months later. (News4Jax, WJCT, WOKV, First Coast News, Jacksonville.com)

2. **Accused of bullying employees and creating a toxic workplace.** Neptune Beach council members accused Wynn of bullying staff, using profanity, storming out of offices, turning off public comments on the city's Facebook page. (Action News Jax, WOKV)

3. **Controls all the money, alone.** Consolidated Administrator and Treasurer into one role. Prepares the budget that determines his own salary. $106K in admin costs spread across four enterprise fund pages. Last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person, $480K was embezzled over five years (2007-2012). (Town budgets, 9News, Denver Post, KVNF)

4. **The town's savings are disappearing.** FY2026 fund balances projected to drop 23% ($5.4M to $4.1M). Bridge Reserve down 73.5%. Meanwhile new patrol cars, street sweeper, trucks keep coming. (FY2026 Budget p.5)

5. **69% of the budget is grants and loans. If they stop, Paonia collapses.** FY2026 adopted budget is $16.16M; only ~$4.6M is own revenue. $9.74M water loan adds $263K/year through 2045. Water rates "will need to continue to increase." (FY2026 Budget pp.4-5, High Country Spotlight)

6. **Grew the bureaucracy 6x faster than the population.** 19 to 25.7 FTE (+35%), 57% increase in admin positions. Population grew 1.1%. Personnel costs up $530K (+28%). (FY2024/2025/2026 Budgets p.44, Census)

7. **Surveillance cameras in Town Park, pointed at where your family gathers.** $50K+ on Verkada cloud cameras. Company fined $2.95M by FTC. 150K cameras breached in 2021. Board approved unanimously, zero public comment. No surveillance policy exists. (FY2026 Budget p.7, FTC, High Country Spotlight, Board minutes 8/12/2025)

8. **Century-old trees dying. $0 in the budget.** Tree Board volunteers resigned, ignored, surveilled. Their own liaison called himself "the town's spy." Public Works won't even meet with them. But there's $50K for cameras and new trucks for the fleet. (Delta County Independent, Oct 2025)

9. **This isn't governing, it's shopping.** $1M backup generators, $500K cellular meters, $250K squad cars, $50K surveillance cameras, $43K trash cans, $33K sidewalk robots. And $0 for trees. (FY2026 Budget pp.68-76, 144)

10. **Same pattern from Florida: anyone who asks questions gets pushed out.** Trustee Brunner filed CORA requests and questioned a water tap sale. Three months into his elected term, Wynn strong-armed the Board: remove him or I resign. They expelled Brunner. A hired administrator used his job as leverage to remove an elected official. (KVNF, High Country Spotlight, Delta County Independent)
