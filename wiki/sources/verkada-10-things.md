# "10 Things You Should Know About Paonia's New Town Cameras"

**Raw file:** `raw/documents/paonia-verkada-10-things.pdf`
**Type:** Public interest summary / citizen flyer
**Date:** ~2025 (references install date of Oct 22/23, 2025)
**Author:** Pete McCarthy
**Subtitle:** "A public interest summary based on town records, news reports, and federal filings"

## The 10 Things

### 1. They're Verkada cameras.
Board approved a $52,000 Verkada security camera system. Locations: Town Hall, Town Park, Public Works Shop, Lamborn Water Treatment Plant, Wastewater Treatment Plant. Install scheduled for October 22/23, 2025.

### 2. Verkada employees used their own cameras to sexually harass female coworkers.
In 2020, male employees used Verkada's facial recognition to photograph women around the office and shared images with sexual captions in a private Slack channel called "RawVerkadaDawgs." Three employees were fired. (The Verge, Oct 26, 2020)

### 3. Verkada was hacked. Badly.
In March 2021, hackers breached 150,000 Verkada cameras, accessing live feeds inside hospitals, jails, schools, police stations, Tesla factories, and Cloudflare offices. Over 100 Verkada employees had "super admin" access to every customer's cameras. (Bloomberg / The Verge, March 2021)

### 4. The FTC fined Verkada $2.95 million.
In 2024, the FTC found Verkada failed to secure customer video data, made false claims about HIPAA compliance and security practices, and spammed potential customers. (FTC Press Release, August 2024)

### 5. These aren't just cameras. They have built-in AI surveillance.
Verkada cameras include facial recognition, "People Analytics" that can filter by clothing color, apparent sex, and whether someone is carrying a backpack, plus license plate recognition and heat mapping. All processed on the camera itself.

### 6. You can upload a photo to find someone.
Verkada's People Analytics lets users upload a photo or use a phone camera to search for a "person of interest" across all camera history. "That's not a security camera. That's a tracking system."

### 7. All footage is stored in Verkada's cloud, not locally.
Unlike traditional security cameras with local storage, Verkada footage goes to their cloud servers. The town does not have exclusive physical control of the recordings.

### 8. Verkada is a major federal government contractor.
Sells to federal agencies through GSA Schedule, NASA SEWP V, and DLA contracts via Carahsoft partnership (as of January 2026). Serves defense, energy, and administrative federal customers.

### 9. There is no policy governing who can access footage or share it.
Verkada integrates directly with Axon's Evidence.com, the digital evidence platform used by law enforcement nationwide. Without a surveillance policy, there are no clear rules about when footage gets pulled, who authorizes it, how long it's retained, or whether it can be shared with outside agencies.

### 10. $52,000 of public money, zero public debate.
Approved as a budget line item alongside patrol car lettering and garbage rates. No public discussion of surveillance policy, acceptable use guidelines, or community input on where and how these cameras operate.

## Significance

This document provides substantially more detail on the Verkada system than the original 10 Things flyer. The AI capabilities (facial recognition, People Analytics, photo-based person search) and the Axon Evidence integration are not mentioned in the board's response or Hunter's rebuttal. The board's response states only that "These are not nefarious face recognition, or private property surveillance apparatus." The Verkada product documentation shows they do include facial recognition and AI-powered person tracking.
