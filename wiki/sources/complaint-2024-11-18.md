# Pete McCarthy's Formal Complaint to the Board of Trustees (November 18, 2024)

**Raw file:** `raw/documents/board-complaint/complaint-2024-11-18.md`
**Type:** Formal written complaint to all Board of Trustees members
**Date:** Monday, November 18, 2024, 7:49:51 AM MST
**From:** Pete McCarthy, Planning Commissioner
**To:** Mayor Paige Smith (PaigeS@townofpaonia.com), Trustee John V. (JohnV@), Trustee Rick Stelter (RickS@), Trustee Kathy Swartz (kswartz@), Trustee Walter Czech (WalterC@), Trustee Lucy Hunter (LucyH@), Trustee Mike Heck (MikeH@)
**CC:** Clay Buchner, Town Attorney
**Subject:** "Urgent: Statutory Violations and Retaliatory Conduct Requiring Immediate Board Action"
**Gmail ID:** 1933fc05a5611370

## Summary

On November 18, 2024, Planning Commissioner Pete McCarthy sent a formal written complaint to all seven members of the Board of Trustees. The complaint documented a pattern of statutory violations, professional misconduct, meeting management violations, and administrative irregularities by Town Administrator [[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] and the Town's administrative leadership.

The complaint was triggered by a November 12, 2024 email from Wynn that McCarthy characterized as exemplifying a hostile and retaliatory environment. The complaint requested nine specific actions from the Board, including a formal investigation, review of Microsoft 365 audit logs, and the establishment of accountability measures.

**Zero trustees responded.** Not one. No acknowledgment, no question, no form response. Gmail thread records confirm no replies were received from any board member. Two weeks later, on December 2, 2024, McCarthy resigned from the Planning Commission ([[resignation-2024-12-02|resignation letter]]).

## Full Text

> Dear Board of Trustees,
>
> I am writing to formally document a pattern of conduct that fundamentally undermines the integrity of our town governance and poses a direct threat to the proper functioning of the Planning Commission. While the culminating incident involves a particularly troubling communication from Town Administrator Stefen Wynn dated Nov 12, 2024, these issues represent systemic failures that demand immediate Board intervention.
>
> The gravity of this situation is best demonstrated by Mr. Wynn's own words in his recent email, which exemplifies the hostile and retaliatory environment that has become normalized in our town operations. Mr. Wynn wrote:
>
> > "Quite frankly, I will not attend the December 2, 2024, meeting unless required to by the Board of Trustees. I have done what has been asked of me from a majority vote of the Board of Trustees and gone above and beyond to accommodate you... it's becoming a waste of Town resources."
>
> This statement alone demonstrates multiple serious issues:
>
> - A senior administrator threatening to abandon his duties
> - Characterization of basic job requirements as "accommodation"
> - Dismissal of statutory obligations as "waste of Town resources"
> - Clear retaliatory intent against legitimate oversight
>
> The documented issues fall into several concerning categories:
>
> **1. Statutory Violations:**
>
> - Systematic displacement of Planning Commission authority in violation of C.R.S. § 31-23-306
> - Bypassing required Planning Commission review of the STR ordinance
> - Exclusion of legally required Housing Element from the Comprehensive Plan
> - Deviation from Board directive regarding Housing Needs Assessment incorporation
>
> **2. Professional Misconduct:**
>
> - Demonstrably false statements, including Mr. Wynn's claim that "I spent a 14-hour day ready to go over what you asked, and you couldn't give me the courtesy of letting me know that you wouldn't attend" — despite documented email records showing 5-hour advance notification
> - Use of personal circumstances for professional intimidation: "I too have children, three of them in fact"
> - Direct threats to withhold performance of duties unless "required by the Board of Trustees"
> - Pattern of hostile and retaliatory communication
> - Deliberate misrepresentation of facts regarding meeting notification
>
> **3. Meeting Management Violations:**
>
> - Commissioners being muted during official proceedings
> - Improper staff intervention in parliamentary procedure
> - Insufficient notice periods for meetings
> - Deliberate exclusion of commissioner input
>
> **4. Administrative Irregularities:**
>
> - Introduction of 28 new housing policies without documentation or explanation
> - Dismissal of highest-voted community priority (STR regulation)
> - Characterization of statutory compliance as "above and beyond accommodation"
> - Refusal to provide written explanations for significant policy decisions
>
> The parallels between these documented behaviors and the recent removal of a trustee for alleged procedural violations are particularly troubling. They suggest an inconsistent application of professional standards that appears to protect administrative overreach while punishing proper oversight attempts.
>
> This pattern creates an untenable situation where commissioners are actively prevented from fulfilling their statutory duties through a combination of intimidation, procedural obstruction, and outright hostility. Mr. Wynn's email perfectly encapsulates this dynamic — attempting to shame a commissioner for family obligations while simultaneously using his own family circumstances as justification for professional discourtesy.
>
> I maintain comprehensive documentation of the incidents mentioned above, including:
>
> - Email exchanges with complete headers
> - Meeting recordings and minutes
> - Public engagement voting records
> - Timeline of irregular meeting schedules and notifications
>
> Additionally, I formally request that the Board obtain and review:
>
> - Microsoft 365 audit logs (MailItemsAccessed) for the Nov 12, 2024 email exchange, which would verify:
>   - Exact time my email was sent
>   - When/if it was accessed
>   - Through which client/device it was accessed
>   - All subsequent access patterns
>
> These logs would definitively prove or disprove Mr. Wynn's claim about lack of notification and his multiple statements that he was unaware of my email. As these are Town records on Town-owned systems, they should be readily available to the Board.
>
> Given the severity of these issues, I formally request that the Board:
>
> 1. Initiate an immediate formal investigation into these patterns of conduct
> 2. Review all documentation of statutory violations
> 3. Establish clear protocols for staff-commissioner interactions
> 4. Address the retaliatory behavior and intimidating environment
> 5. Ensure compliance with state law regarding Planning Commission authority
> 6. Create accountability measures for professional conduct standards
> 7. Review all email communications between staff and commissioners
> 8. Establish clear consequences for professional misconduct
> 9. Obtain and review Microsoft 365 audit logs to verify the accuracy of Mr. Wynn's statements regarding email notifications
>
> The integrity of our planning process and the trust of our community depend on addressing these issues promptly and thoroughly. Mr. Wynn's email represents not just a single incident of unprofessional conduct, but rather the culmination of a pattern that threatens the fundamental operation of our town government.
>
> I remain available to provide any additional documentation needed to address these serious concerns.
>
> Respectfully,
> Pete McCarthy
> Planning Commissioner
>
> cc: Clay Buchner, Town Attorney

## Key Facts

- **Nine specific requested actions** — not vague or ambiguous. Each was a concrete, reviewable step the Board could have taken.
- **Named statutory citation** — C.R.S. § 31-23-306, the Colorado statute governing Planning Commission authority.
- **Specific evidence offered** — email exchanges with headers, meeting recordings, voting records, notification timelines.
- **A specific M365 audit log request** — MailItemsAccessed records for the November 12, 2024 email exchange, which would have verified whether Wynn had actually read a Planning Commission commissioner's email before denying it at a public meeting.
- **Delivered to all seven trustees** simultaneously with the Town Attorney CC'd, removing any plausible deniability about who received it.

## The Board's Response

**Zero replies.** Not one trustee responded. The [[board-letter-2026-03-30|board-letter-2026-03-30]] resubmission references this silence directly: "Gmail thread records confirm no responses were received from any board member to either the complaint or the resignation."

Two weeks later, on December 2, 2024, McCarthy resigned from the Planning Commission, citing the Board's silence as one of the stated reasons. See [[resignation-2024-12-02]].

The complaint was never investigated. None of the nine requested actions were taken. The M365 audit log request was never fulfilled. The Board did not acknowledge receipt.

## Significance

This is the foundational document in what has since become a documented pattern of non-response to complaints about [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Stefen Wynn]]. At the time it was sent, the complaint was pre-retaliation-pattern: it described Wynn's conduct toward one commissioner, with specific statutory citations, over a specific time window.

Subsequent events (the resignation of [[cory-heiniger|Public Works Director Cory Heiniger]], the termination of [[kaja-bowman|Deputy Treasurer Kaja Bowman]], the installation of the Verkada surveillance system, and the creation of employee-named camera archives) have all occurred after this complaint was sent and ignored. The pattern the complaint warned about has continued and expanded.

The complaint was resubmitted on March 30, 2026 as part of the [[board-letter-2026-03-30|updated 27-citation formal complaint]], with the original letter included as an attachment and its non-response cited as the first documented failure of the Board's complaint process.
