## Key Facts

- **Flock Safety** operates 80,000+ automated license plate reader (ALPR) cameras nationwide with cross-agency data sharing
- Cities canceling or pausing Flock contracts: **Mountain View, Santa Cruz, South Pasadena, Flagstaff, Denver, Richmond** (CA temporarily disabled then re-signed 4-3)
- **Dunwoody, GA** spent $360K building a real-time crime center around Flock, now deferring renewal over privacy concerns
- **404 Media** revealed (May 2025) local police repeatedly searched Flock databases to assist **ICE immigration enforcement**, even in jurisdictions with no-cooperation policies
- School police departments made dozens of national searches for immigration enforcement (reported by The 74)
- Security researchers (Benn Jordan, Joshua Michael, Jon Gaines) demonstrated: cameras could be penetrated by pushing a button 3 times with an Android diagnostic tool; passwords hardcoded in firmware; live video feeds accessible on open internet
- Flock changed terms of service removing assurance it "does not own and will not sell customer data" -- company says redundancy cleanup, critics say it expands AI training rights
- **Ring/Amazon** ended partnership with Flock after Super Bowl ad backlash showed how cameras could track people
- DHS has $1B Palantir contract for deportation targeting, Clearview AI for facial recognition -- Flock data adds to this ecosystem
- California law (2015) bars sharing ALPR data with federal/out-of-state agencies; Mountain View still found unauthorized federal access in 2024 audit
- EFF calls it a systemic problem: "This is not just a Flock problem... There's more to it than stolen cars and missing persons"

## Relevance to Paonia

Paonia installed a **$50,288 Verkada camera system** with zero public comment and no surveillance policy ([[surveillance|full issue]]). While Verkada is a different vendor than Flock, the parallels are direct:

- Both are networked camera systems purchased by small-town governments without public input
- Verkada was fined $2.95M by the FTC (2024) for failing to secure customer data -- Flock faces similar security vulnerability disclosures
- The ICE data-sharing risk applies to any networked camera system accessible to law enforcement
- Multiple cities are now requiring independent security audits and surveillance policies before renewing contracts -- Paonia has neither
- The Guardian quotes a resident: "I can't open up a barbershop or a Chick-fil-A without a safety inspection" -- Paonia's cameras had less public scrutiny than a restaurant permit
