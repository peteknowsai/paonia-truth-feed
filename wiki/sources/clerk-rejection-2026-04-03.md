# Clerk Rejection: Surveillance Camera Initiative (April 3, 2026)

**Recipients:** Peter McCarthy, Melayna Rosa
**Submission rejected:** "An Initiated Ordinance of the People of the Town of Paonia, Colorado, Prohibiting Government Surveillance Cameras on Public Property and Providing for Enforcement" (received March 27, 2026)

## Full Text

> April 3, 2026
>
> Peter McCarthy
> Melayna Rosa
> 119 ½ Dorris Avenue
> Paonia, CO 81428
>
> Mr. McCarthy & Ms. Rosa,
>
> The submitted citizen initiative and proposed ballot measure, that were received by the Clerk's Office on March 27, 2026, are hereby rejected for the reasons outlined below.
>
> The measure does not comply with the single-subject requirement set forth in Article V, Section 1(5.5) of the Colorado Constitution. If a proposed measure is too broad to be assigned a title addressing only one subject, it is not eligible for placement on the ballot. The proposed measure includes multiple distinct subjects, including but not limited to surveillance of public property, contracting with third parties, body-worn cameras for law enforcement, and judicial remedies for citizens.
>
> In addition to the single-subject deficiency, the following subject matter concerns are noted:
>
> - **Body-Worn Cameras:** This area is governed by the Enhance Law Enforcement Integrity Act. Key aspects are controlled by state statute, and local measures may not conflict with or override state law.
>
> - **Surveillance of Public Property:** The management and operation of Town property are administrative functions and are not within the scope of citizen-initiated legislation.
>
> - **Sections 2 and 5 (Contracts):** The approval, modification, or voiding of contracts are administrative matters and are not legislative in nature; therefore, they are not subject to citizen initiative.
>
> - **Section 6 (Injunctive Relief):** The provision of injunctive relief is a judicial function and cannot be established through local legislation.
>
> - **Section 7 (Voter Approval for Amendment/Repeal):** Requiring voter approval to amend or repeal an ordinance has been determined to be invalid under Colorado law.
>
> Based on the foregoing, the proposed measure does not meet the legal requirements for placement on the ballot or the collection of signatures.
>
> Samira M. Vetter, CMC
> Paonia Town Clerk

## Grounds Cited

| 4 | Contracts are "administrative" | Sections 2 and 5 |

| 6 | Voter-approval clause invalid | Section 7 (no authority cited) |

## Notable

- No Colorado caselaw cited for any ground.
- The single-subject argument under Article V, § 1(5.5) addresses statewide ballot measures, not municipal initiatives under C.R.S. § 31-11-106.
- Section 4(a) of the rejected initiative *exempted* body-worn cameras; the Clerk treated the exemption as regulation.
- Pete McCarthy filed a [[rejection-response-to-vetter|written rebuttal]] the same day demanding acceptance within 5 business days.
