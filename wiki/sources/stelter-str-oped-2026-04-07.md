# OpEd: A Responsible Path Forward on Short Term Rentals in Paonia

**Raw file:** `raw/articles/stelter-str-oped-responsible-path-2026-04-07.md`
**Type:** Op-ed / public statement
**Date:** 2026-04-07
**Author:** Rick Stelter, Mayor Pro Tempore and Chair, Paonia STR Ad Hoc Committee
**Publication:** Delta County Independent

## Summary

Rick Stelter outlines the STR Ad Hoc Committee's approach to short-term rental regulation. The op-ed frames STR regulation as mandatory ("Regulation is not optional") and describes the committee process, board-established licensing requirements, and outstanding policy questions.

## Key Claims

- STR regulation is a non-negotiable government responsibility related to life, health, and safety
- The Board has already established licensing, annual renewal, fees, building code compliance, inspection authority, zoning adherence, proof of ownership, and non-transferability as administrative elements
- The committee includes elected officials, Town administration, residents, and an STR host
- Committee recommends extending the enforcement moratorium through end of 2026
- A professional facilitator will be brought in for public meetings

## Notable Omissions

- No mention of the April 1, 2025 voter rejection of more restrictive STR regulations
- No mention of pending citizen initiatives on STR policy
- No mention of the Planning Commission's exclusion from STR ordinance review (a statutory concern raised by [[pete-mccarthy]])
- No mention of the community's highest-voted priority (STR regulation with 21 votes) being omitted from the Housing Element draft

## Context

This op-ed was published the same week Pete McCarthy resubmitted a revised STR citizen initiative petition. The framing of regulation as mandatory and licensing as board prerogative directly conflicts with the initiative's approach of permitting principal residence hosting by right with no license or fee. The inclusion of "Town administration" (Stefen Wynn) on the committee is notable given the documented [[pattern-of-retaliation]] against those who challenge Wynn's authority.
