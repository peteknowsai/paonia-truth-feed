# Lucy Hunter's Rebuttal Flyer - "10 Things" Response

**Raw file:** `raw/documents/their-responses/lucy-hunter-rebuttal-flyer.pdf`
**Type:** Unsigned counter-flyer
**Date:** 2026-03-26 (one day after Pete's flyer)
**Author:** [[lucy-hunter|Trustee Lucy Hunter]] (unsigned; attribution from [[mayor-report-agenda-packet|mayor's report]])
**Coordination:** Vetted by [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Wynn]] ("to ensure her flyer content was 100% accurate") and [[clayton-buchner|Town Attorney Buchner]], per the mayor's report.

## Summary

A point-by-point response to Pete's "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator" flyer. Unlike the board's official letter, this flyer includes some source citations (budget references, meeting minutes dates). It responds to each of Pete's 10 points with bold counterarguments.

## Key Claims

1. **Neptune Beach**: Acknowledges Wynn was fired. Notes he "filed a lawsuit claiming he was actually fired in retaliation for being a whistleblower." States the behavioral accusations are "subjective, unsubstantiated, and unverified by independent investigation" and that "Wynn's position is that the behavioral accusations were a pretext assembled to justify a politically motivated termination."

2. **Bullying accusations**: Says claims are "subjective, unsubstantiated, and unverified by independent investigation." Cites News4Jax Jan 18, 2023 and WOKV.

3. **Controls the money**: "This is blatantly false." Describes Finance Committee (Mayor and a Trustee review every invoice), Caselle software, Professional Management Solutions (third-party firm). States "The Administrator does NOT determine his own salary!" Notes the Board conducts performance reviews and determines raises.

4. **Savings disappearing**: "Saving up reserves and then drawing them down for infrastructure projects is RESPONSIBLE PLANNING, NOT FISCAL DISTRESS." States fund balances are "well within appropriate levels" and "well above its minimum policy threshold."

5. **69% grants/loans**: "Grants and loans are typically how municipalities pay for large/overdue infrastructure projects, this is nothing new, and this is not limited to Paonia."

6. **Grew bureaucracy**: "Though a few new positions were created, the majority of new employees were hired to fill already existing, open positions."

7. **Cameras**: "Yes, because Town-owned facilities have been vandalized and damaged, requiring tax-payer funded repairs." Acknowledges "No, there was no public comment, because no one from the public cared to speak on the matter." States "There is no expectation of privacy in a public space." Cites 8/12/2025 Regular Board Meeting Minutes.

8. **Trees**: Claims $40,000 in two budget line items (Streets 10-45-71 Right-of-Way Maintenance $25,000 and Parks 10-46-71 $15,000). States "Volunteers were never told to 'pound sand'." Cites 2/10/2026 Regular Board Meeting Minutes.

9. **Shopping**: "This is blatantly false." Defends each expenditure as necessary.

10. **Same pattern from Florida**: "This is blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation."

## Notable Admissions

- Acknowledges Wynn was fired from Neptune Beach (does not dispute the facts, frames them differently)
- Acknowledges cameras were approved with "no public comment" but frames this as nobody caring rather than lack of process
- Does not dispute the specific dollar amounts or budget page numbers Pete cited
- Cites the 8/12/2025 board meeting as the camera approval date, confirming Pete's citation

## Discrepancies with Other Sources

- Flyer was unsigned but the mayor's report treats it as a legitimate response. Pete's signed, sourced flyer was called "libelous."
- The flyer was coordinated with Wynn (the subject of the criticism) and Buchner, per the mayor's own report. A trustee's response vetted by the person being criticized raises questions about independence.
- On trees: the $40K cited is in Right-of-Way Maintenance, which is a general Public Works category covering sidewalks, curbs, and maintenance, not dedicated tree care. The Comprehensive Plan (July 2025) explicitly calls for urban forest investment; the budget allocates $0 to that goal.
- States "Volunteers were never told to 'pound sand'" but the Delta County Independent (Oct 9, 2025) reported Tree Board members felt "unheard for the past five years" and their liaison called himself "the town's spy."
