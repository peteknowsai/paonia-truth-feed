# Split Surveillance Initiative Petitions (A, B, C)

**Raw file:** `raw/documents/initiative-fight/split-initiatives-ready-to-file.md`
**Type:** Initiative petition package with cover letter
**Date:** ~2026-04-04
**Author:** Pete McCarthy
**Recipient:** [[samira-vetter|Town Clerk Samira Vetter]]

## Summary

Three single-subject initiative petitions prepared for simultaneous submission, splitting the original surveillance camera initiative into separate narrowly scoped petitions. Designed to eliminate the single-subject and "administrative" objections raised in the two prior rejections.

## Key Facts

### Initiative A: Surveillance Camera Prohibition
- Prohibits installation and operation of surveillance cameras on public property
- Requires removal of existing cameras within 90 days of enactment

### Initiative B: Citizen Oversight of Surveillance Spending
- Requires voter approval before the Town may expend funds on surveillance technology
- Establishes democratic control over surveillance-related budget decisions

### Initiative C: Citizen Standing to Enforce Surveillance Ordinances
- Grants registered electors of Paonia standing to bring suit enforcing surveillance-related ordinances
- Creates a citizen enforcement mechanism independent of Town administration

### Cover Letter
- Notes two consecutive rejections, both on "administrative" grounds
- CC'd to [[clayton-buchner|Town Attorney Clayton Buchner]], [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]], the Colorado Attorney General, and the Colorado Freedom of Information Coalition (CFOIC)
