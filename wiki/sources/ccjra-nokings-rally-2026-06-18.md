# Paonia PD "No Kings Rally" Email Production (CCJRA, June 18, 2026)

**Raw files:** `raw/documents/police-records/nokings-rally-ccjra2-2026-06-18/` (one PDF, five native `.eml` files)
**Produced by:** JoAnn Katzer, Public Service Tech, Paonia Police Department
**Date produced:** 2026-06-18
**Cover note:** "attached are the emails that the Town Clerk regarding the 'No Kings Rally'. The Town Clerk stated that she could not find any emails related to the 'Ten Things' flyer."

This is the second Paonia PD production under the Colorado Criminal Justice Records Act — the "bullying and harassment" / rally thread that [[what-the-police-found]] and [[ccjra-pd-records-2026-06-04]] flagged as still pending. It contains two things the wiki did not previously have in hand: the March 28 email in which the Administrator asks the Police Chief how to obtain a protection order against McCarthy and a named neighbor, and the message the Administrator had forwarded from his private attorney minutes earlier.

## What was produced

**1. The March 28 email to the Police Chief (PDF).**

[[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] to Chief Matt Laiminger, CC Officer G. Henderson, Saturday 2026-03-28 9:25 AM. Subject: "Bullying and Harassment on Social Media in Advance of 500-600 Political Activists in Town for a Rally." The body concerns the image of Wynn superimposed on King Leopold II that circulated ahead of the "No Kings" rally and the "10 reasons … should be fired" flyer. It asks:

> "Who does this need to be forwarded to at the county level to begin seeking a protection order from Mr. McCarthy and his accomplices, namely Kaja Bowman, who was seen handing this flyer out…"

It adds that he "terminated her employment with the Town in October 2025" and that "she is also my lot line sharing neighbor."

**2. The forwarded attorney message (same PDF).**

Below the message to the Chief, Wynn forwarded an email he had sent at 9:18 AM that same morning to his private attorney (richard@wlelegal.com), under the same subject. The PDF marks this portion as withheld:

> "Attorney-client privileged communication withheld pursuant to C.R.S. §§ 24-72-204(1)(a), 13-90-107(1)(b), and 24-72-204(3)(a)(iv)…"

The text of that forwarded message is present in the produced document. In it, Wynn describes the King Leopold image and the rally and asks his attorney:

> "I've already had two elected officials reach out to me about what is being posted and circulated on social media and town again. Has this reached a level that has met the statute level for public officials?"

(One sentence describing a family member is omitted here as not material to the public record.)

**3. The March 26 "Fear for Safety" email and four read receipts (`.eml`).**

The original 2026-03-26 3:13 PM email to the Mayor and Trustees (CC Chief Laiminger and the Town account), the body of which was separately produced as [[cora-c26-28-threat-safety-bodies|C 26-28]], together with four Outlook "Read:" receipts confirming who opened it:

## The attorney-client withholding

The Town withheld the forwarded message as attorney-client privileged. The same message had already been forwarded by Wynn, the client, to Chief Laiminger and Officer Henderson, who are third parties outside the attorney-client relationship. Colorado law treats the attorney-client privilege (C.R.S. 13-90-107(1)(b)) as waived when the client voluntarily discloses the communication to a third party. The communication the Town later withheld as confidential was distributed by the client to the Police Department on the same morning he sent it to his lawyer.

## Discrepancy with the June 4 certification

The June 4 PD production ([[ccjra-pd-records-2026-06-04]]) certified in writing that "no such record exists" of the Department being "directed, requested, or asked to investigate, evaluate, monitor, or take any action concerning Pete McCarthy, or concerning any other private citizen who was publicly criticized by the town Administrator." This production contains a March 28 email in which the Administrator asks the Chief of Police where to go "to begin seeking a protection order from Mr. McCarthy and his accomplices, namely Kaja Bowman." Both documents are reproduced as produced; readers can weigh whether the March 28 request falls within the category the June 4 letter certified as empty.

## Significance

- The "protection order" and "statute level for public officials" language shows the Administrator seeking a legal mechanism to reach a critic's political speech — the King Leopold image and the "10 reasons … should be fired" flyer — and a named neighbor he had fired five months earlier.
- "I've already had two elected officials reach out to me" places members of the elected body in contact with the Administrator about the posts by March 28.
- The read receipts establish that the Mayor and at least two Trustees opened the March 26 "Fear for Safety" email, which named the neighbor as "one of the people behind posting and distributing flyers" — the accusation [[what-the-police-found|Wynn's own later police statement attributes instead to McCarthy]].
