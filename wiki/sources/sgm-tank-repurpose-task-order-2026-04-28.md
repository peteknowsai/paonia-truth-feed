# SGM Task Order — Convert Temporary Potable Tank to Permanent Raw Water Storage

**Raw file:** `raw/meeting-agendas/2026-04-28/regular-packet.pdf` (pp. 604–605)
**Type:** Staff report + SGM letter proposal
**Date:** April 24, 2026
**Author of staff report:** Stefen Wynn, Town Administrator & Treasurer
**SGM author:** Brandyn Bair, PE — Wastewater Team Leader, SGM (Glenwood Springs)
**Agenda item:** H.10 — Consideration of Task Order from SGM to Repurpose Temporary Tank to Raw Water Storage Tank

## Recommended motion (in packet)

> "I move to approve the task order with SGM in an amount not to exceed $10,000 for design and permitting services to convert the temporary potable water tank into a permanent raw water storage tank, and to authorize the Town Administrator and Treasurer to execute all necessary documents."

## Background (staff report)

> "The Town of Paonia previously installed a temporary potable water storage tank to support operations during the 2MG tank relining project. With that project nearing completion, staff has evaluated opportunities to repurpose existing infrastructure in a cost effective and operationally beneficial manner. SGM has provided a proposal to convert the temporary potable water tank into a permanent raw water storage tank."

Key claim: "Following coordination with CDPHE, SGM has indicated that **there are no specific design criteria governing raw water storage tanks.** As a result, the conversion can proceed with a streamlined process. SGM will prepare a courtesy submittal to CDPHE to formally document the change in use, ensuring the tank is incorporated into the Town's water system inventory and avoiding potential concerns during future sanitary surveys."

Scope of work in the conversion:
- Revised site plan reflecting new inlet and outlet connections to the raw water system;
- Abandonment/disconnection of existing potable connections;
- Photographic documentation of modifications;
- Memorandum describing the completed work.

## Budget

- **60-50-20: $10,000 for SGM Design work (Decision Tonight)**
- **60-50-70: $212,300 to purchase temporary tank (Decision Later)**

So the April 28 vote is only on the $10,000 SGM design fee. The separate ~$212K to actually purchase the tank (rather than return it) is held back for a later decision.

## SGM letter (verbatim, p. 605)

April 24, 2026 letter from Brandyn Bair, PE (SGM, Glenwood Springs office):

> "After multiple discussions with CDPHE, we present the following. CDPHE doesn't have any design criteria for raw water storage tanks allowing the Town to proceed relatively unchecked. Given that the temporary potable water storage tank was already approved through CDPHE with proper overflow and vent calculations, SGM will provide CDPHE a 'courtesy' submittal for switching the tank from potable use to raw water storage. This ensures the tank becomes part of the inventory and doesn't create any red flags during sanitary surveys performed by CDPHE. This submittal will contain new site plan with new inlet and outlet connections to the raw water line, and abandonment/disconnection of current inlet and outlet piping from the potable line. We'll also provide pictures of the new connections and abandonment of the potable water connections along with a memo describing the whole project."

> "We estimate fees at $10,000 to complete this work. Changes in scope requested by the Town or due to circumstances beyond SGM's control may require a contract Change Order."

Contact: Brandyn Bair, 970-384-9024.

## Notable

- The "no specific design criteria" framing appears twice — both Wynn's staff report and the SGM letter. SGM's letter calls this a "relatively unchecked" path. That language is unusual for a regulatory rationale.
- This is a **second-life** decision: the temporary potable tank was rented/installed solely to bridge the [[klm-change-order-3-2026-04-13|2MG tank relining project]]. Rather than returning it, staff is proposing to convert it into a permanent raw-water tank — meaning the Town would also have to follow through on the $212,300 purchase later (held back from this vote).
- "Repurposing" reframes the spend: the $212,300 wasn't a budgeted permanent capital purchase; it would convert a temporary rental into a kept asset. The vote on April 28 only commits $10K, but it sets the trajectory.
- A "courtesy" CDPHE submittal carries no formal review weight — it's a notification to keep the tank from being flagged in future sanitary surveys, not an approval of design.
