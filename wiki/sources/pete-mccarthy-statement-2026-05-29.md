# McCarthy Public Comment & Recall Announcement (2026-05-29)

**Raw file:** `raw/research/greg-sund/podium-statement.html` (prepared reading copy)
**Delivered:** Public comment, 2026-05-29 special meeting (video `NFQKdNRzjtw`, ~[44:00–47:30])
**Speaker:** Pete McCarthy (paoniatruth.site)

## What was said

McCarthy read a prepared statement and then announced a recall. The statement, facts-first:

- [[greg-sund|Greg Sund]]'s last permanent job was Trinidad city manager; in September 2019 he **resigned to avoid being fired**. Every position since has been a short-term interim (Walsenburg, Telluride, Mt. Crested Butte, Georgetown). In Walsenburg he resigned mid-meeting the night the council voted against keeping him.
- A Trinidad department head's [[valentine-letter-2019|written complaint]] states Sund told the town's finance director, "this is not a democracy."
- The churn is the Colorado norm: Ouray's council hoped to end a "merry-go-round of chief executive officers"; the Delta County Independent called Paonia "the fifth administrator in six years." See [[the-revolving-door-canvass]].
- On process: the Board has known since May 8 that Wynn was leaving, yet produced a single-candidate special meeting with the packet posted the day before. "Why the rush? Why is there only one name?"

## The recall announcement

McCarthy announced he is **filing a petition to recall Mayor [[mayor-smith|Paige Smith]]**, and — because a Colorado municipal recall ballot names a successor — **put his own name forward** as the replacement candidate, framing it as a willingness to be accountable rather than only to criticize.

## Significance

The statement put Sund's record and the [[the-ccma-pipeline|single-candidate process]] on the meeting record before the Board's discussion. The concerns it raised (no slate, no reference checks, 24-hour notice) were then echoed by Trustee Foster, and the motion to hire **died for lack of a second**.
