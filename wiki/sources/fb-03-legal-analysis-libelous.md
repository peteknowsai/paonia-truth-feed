## Key Facts

- Board's response cites zero sources, zero documents, zero page numbers. McCarthy's flyer has a 19-page citation document.
- Board called McCarthy's work "misinformation," "blatant falsehoods," "deliberately damaging," a "smear campaign," and "libelous" without identifying a single false statement of fact
- **Neptune Beach:** Board's response mentions Wynn filed a whistleblower lawsuit, which confirms the firing. "You can't file a wrongful termination lawsuit if you weren't terminated." Six published news sources confirm unanimous firing for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct." Wynn's lawsuit goes to trial August 2026, Duval County Circuit Court Case No. 23-CA-8801.
- **Budget authority:** Administrator holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles, prepares the budget that sets his own salary, allocates $106K in admin costs across four enterprise fund pages (Page 8, FY2026 Adopted Budget)
- **Fund balances:** 23% projected decline in a single year, from Page 5 of the FY2026 budget. Board calls this "responsible planning."
- **Grants:** Board built a straw man, claiming McCarthy said "grants and loans are not the correct path forward." McCarthy said 69% of budget is external funding, creating fiscal vulnerability. Town took on $9.74M water infrastructure loan adding $263K/year in debt payments through 2045. Budget states water rates "will need to continue to increase over time."
- **Staffing:** 19 to 25.7 FTE (+35%), admin positions up 57%, population up 1.1% (U.S. Census). Personnel costs rose $530K (+28%).
- **Embezzlement parallel:** Last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person, $480K was stolen over six years (2007-2012). Sources: 9News Denver, Denver Post, KVNF.
- **Trees:** Right-of-Way Maintenance is not dedicated tree care. Comprehensive Plan (adopted July 2025, INFRA GOAL 10, Page 42) calls for urban forest investment. Budget allocates $0. Tree Board suspended via Resolution 03-26. Tree Board members resigned, saying they felt "unheard for five years"; town liaison called himself "the town's spy" (Delta County Independent, October 2025).
- **Cameras:** $50,288 Verkada system. FTC fined Verkada $2.95M (August 2024). Hackers accessed 150,000 Verkada cameras in 2021. No surveillance policy. Approval came with zero public comment (Board meeting minutes, August 12, 2025).
- Board's statement approved 5-1, posted on Town website, states: "Erroneous and libelous statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position."
- McCarthy calls for resignation of [[mayor-smith]]
- McCarthy sent formal demand for retraction to each board member who voted yes
- Threatens legal action against every board member who voted to publish, individually
- Has consulted with attorneys
