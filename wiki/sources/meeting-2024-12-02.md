# Planning Commission Meeting - December 2, 2024

**Body:** [[Planning Commission]]
**Type:** Regular Meeting
**Date:** December 2, 2024
**Chair:** [[Suzanne Watson]]
**Present:** Watson (Chair), Commissioner Smith, Commissioner Hunter, and others
**Absent:** [[Pete McCarthy]] (resigned)
**Staff:** [[Stefen Wynn]] (Town Administrator)

## Summary

The meeting where [[Pete McCarthy]]'s [[Resignation Letter]] is read into the public record. Chair [[Suzanne Watson]] reads the letter, then launches into a forceful statement about the pattern of administrative overreach that caused the resignation. The meeting becomes contentious when Watson raises the [[STR Ordinance]] bypass of the Planning Commission, and is shut down by the mayor and administrator. This meeting is the clearest on-the-record evidence that the problems Pete identified were shared by other commissioners.

## Key Moments

### ~11:16 - Resignation Letter Read Into Record

Chair Watson introduces the letter before the agenda is even approved:

> "So I have a letter of resignation here. I don't know, have you guys heard anything about this? I did hear about it, haven't read it. So our commissioner McCarthy is resigning from the Board of Trustees... I'm going to read out his letter of resignation for the record."

### ~11:57 - The Resignation Letter (Full Text Read Aloud)

Watson reads Pete's letter in full:

> "I hereby resign my position as Planning Commissioner effective immediately. Two weeks have passed since I submitted a detailed documentation of serious governance issues to this board. Your complete silence speaks volumes about the state of oversight in our town."

> "I take this step only after witnessing the systematic dismantling of the Planning Commission authority and experiencing persistent hostility while attempting to fulfill my duties."

> "The November 20th work session transcript clearly documents: the Town Administrator threatening to abandon his duties when I asked basic process questions; the mayor and administrator working in tandem to dismiss legitimate requests for documentation; my repeated requests over two months for written policy explanations being stonewalled; a pattern of administrative retaliation when commissioners raise concerns."

> "The Planning Commission has been reduced to a ceremonial role, expected to rubber stamp decisions without prior review or documentation. When commissioners attempt to exercise meaningful oversight they face hostility and dismissal from town administration."

> "Your continued silence regarding these serious issues makes clear that proper oversight is not a priority for this board. I cannot in good conscience continue serving on the commission where asking questions and requesting documentation is met with threats and retaliation."

> "The community deserves better than performative governance. My duty is to the citizens of the town. Since I can no longer effectively fulfill that duty in this compromised environment, I must step aside. For the record, Pete McCarthy, former Planning Commissioner."

### ~14:31 - Watson's Response: "Very Disappointed"

Watson reacts with a statement that validates Pete's concerns:

> "So I'm obviously very disappointed in this. The Planning Commission is an opportunity for citizens to start becoming involved in their local government... and should be an opportunity for us to start learning our code, learning how it relates to the laws, the processes."

> "The whole fact that citizens are, we are the 'we the people,' we are the governance of this town. And I'm incredibly disappointed that this commissioner... we didn't... there's this environment that has allowed this to happen. And instead of bringing people up and letting the new people catch up and understand the processes and how things work, it's just like there's this steamroller going on."

### ~15:47 - Watson Raises STR Ordinance Bypass

Watson connects Pete's resignation to the broader pattern by raising the [[STR Ordinance]]:

> "It looks like there's some correspondence that we got in the form of an email that Stephan Wynn passed to us, the Town Administrator, with a correspondence between Commissioner McCarthy and the town attorney. And it was in response to the adoption of an ordinance dealing with short-term rentals."

> "He wants to understand the rationale for bypassing the normal process, which in our code chapter 16, the zoning code, confers some involvement to the Planning Commission as a... giving a recommendation to the Board of Trustees before an ordinance is adopted."

### ~16:39 - Shut Down: "Not the Forum"

The administrator (or mayor) immediately tries to stop Watson:

> "Can I ask how this is relevant to the agenda?"

Watson pushes back:

> "This is relevant because someone has just... it's not on the agenda. We're not... this is not an action item and I'm making a statement here as the head of the Planning Commission."

> "I'm not sure it's... it is. Okay, call the police and arrest me."

### ~17:01 - "You Are Not a Lawyer"

The exchange escalates as Watson cites specific code sections:

> "The legislative... the attorney, we got an email and I'm sharing this with... I understand reading the letter of resignation to explain why Commissioner McCarthy is not here. You're inappropri... okay, take it to the Board of Trustees please."

Watson cites code section 16-4-30 on public hearing requirements for zoning amendments. She is told:

> "Our attorney made a statement. You are not... he did not reference this, Suzanne."

> "I am. My concerns and this is exactly what's happening. There's no communication with the Planning Commission. There's no honest discussion. It's you and Stephen Wynn taking the authority away from these... this is absolutely not true."

### ~19:11 - "We Just Had a Planning Commissioner Resign"

Watson connects the dots explicitly:

> "We just had a planning commissioner resign because of this issue that's going on. The way you and Stephan and this thing that you do together."

> "We don't do anything together. It's all in public."

### ~20:39 - Watson's "Steamroller" Statement

> "How incredibly hard it is to participate in this body when we have had this administrator who has undermined me privately to the Board of Trustees. Yes, he slandered me."

Watson describes being shut out of communications:

> "Stephan's been emailing Planning Commission members and I haven't been receiving the correspondences because I didn't have an email address. And then when I asked for the emails from Samira the clerk, I'm given the date of the email but there's no text and there's no body."

### ~21:57 - "Total Outsiders"

> "I'm trying to explain to you, we feel like total outsiders."

> "Who's we?"

> "Well I don't know, probably... I'm not speaking for... I mean McCarthy, but I'm sure he has the same feelings as me. He's obviously just resigned as an outsider."

### ~22:15 - Emails Going Back and Forth

> "Everything that is done with the Planning Commission is done in public, and that's why I'm speaking about this now. Because these emails that keep going back and forth, they're disturbing to me."

### ~25:41 - Complaints Referenced

Watson references the formal complaint process:

> "Complaints... so do we have a... do you want to approve the agenda as is written?"

The meeting moves on, the complaints topic dropped without resolution.

## Relevance to Wiki Issues

- **[[Resignation Letter]]:** Read in full into the public record. The text is preserved in the video recording and this transcript.
- **[[STR Ordinance]]:** Watson identifies the same issue Pete raised: the Board of Trustees adopted a zoning ordinance (STR regulations) without the Planning Commission recommendation required by Chapter 16 of the town code. The attorney's response cited a different code section and did not address 16-4-30.
- **[[Email Transparency]]:** Watson reveals she was excluded from email communications between the administrator and other commissioners. When she requested the emails through the town clerk, she received dates but no content. This is the same pattern Pete experienced.
- **[[Formal Complaint]]:** Watson references complaints, indicating Pete's formal complaint documentation had been submitted but received no response (the "complete silence" referenced in the resignation letter).
- **[[Planning Commission Authority]]:** Watson, the commission chair, independently corroborates Pete's claims about being treated as an outsider, undermined, and denied information. This is not one disgruntled commissioner; the chair herself identifies the same pattern.
- **[[Stefen Wynn]]:** Accused by Watson of slandering her privately to the Board of Trustees, selectively emailing commissioners, and working in tandem with the mayor to suppress oversight.

## Procedural Notes

- Watson read the resignation letter before the agenda was formally approved, drawing an objection.
- Watson was told she was "not a lawyer" when citing code sections.
- Watson was told to "take it to the Board of Trustees" rather than discuss it at the Planning Commission.
- The mayor's response was to characterize Watson's concerns as procedurally improper rather than address the substance.
- Watson's statement "call the police and arrest me" reflects her awareness that she was being shut down and her refusal to comply.
- Commissioner Smith confirmed she "hadn't read" the resignation letter before the meeting, suggesting it was not distributed by staff to commissioners.
