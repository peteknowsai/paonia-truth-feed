## Key Facts

- **Neptune Beach:** Board's response confirms the firing by mentioning Wynn's whistleblower lawsuit. "You can't file a whistleblower lawsuit against a former employer unless that employer fired you." Lawsuit: Duval County Circuit Court, Case No. 23-CA-8801, trial August 2026.
- **Budget authority:** Administrator holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles, prepares the budget that determines his own salary, allocates admin costs across four separate enterprise fund pages (Page 8, FY2026 Adopted Budget)
- **Embezzlement parallel:** $480,000 stolen 2007-2012 when financial authority was concentrated in one person (9News Denver, Denver Post, Oct 31, 2012; KVNF, Oct 28, 2015). Same structural risk exists now with consolidated Administrator/Treasurer role.
- **Fund balances:** 23% projected decline in a single year (Page 5, FY2026 budget)
- **Grants and loans:** 69% external funding ($16.16M budget, ~$4.6M own revenue, Pages 4-5). $9.74M water infrastructure loan, $263K/year in debt payments through 2045. Budget states water rates "will need to continue to increase over time to support repayment of existing and future debt" (Page 5).
- **Staffing:** FY2024: 22 budgeted FTE. FY2026 (Page 44): 25 regular full-time + 2 seasonal Parks + 1 part-time Municipal Judge, approximately 25.7 FTE. 35% increase. Admin positions grew 57%. Personnel costs rose $530K (+28%).
- **Trees:** Streets 10-45-71 and Parks 10-46-71 "Right-of-Way Maintenance" are Public Works line items covering sidewalks, curbs, and general maintenance, not dedicated tree care. Comprehensive Plan (adopted July 16, 2025), INFRA GOAL 10 (Page 42): "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees." Budget allocates $0. Tree Board suspended via Resolution 03-26. Tree Board members resigned (Delta County Independent, October 9, 2025), said they felt "unheard for the past five years," town liaison called himself "the town's spy."
- **Cameras:** $50,288 Verkada system. FTC $2.95M fine (August 2024). 150,000 Verkada cameras breached by hackers in 2021. No surveillance policy. Zero public comment at approval (Board meeting minutes, August 12, 2025). Board says "they're not face recognition cameras," but no one claimed they were.
- **Capital improvements list from FY2026 budget (Pages 68-76 and 144):** $1,024,542 backup generators; $500,000 cellular meters; $250,000 squad cars; $196,000 vehicle replacement; $85,000 sidewalk/ADA; $50,000+ security cameras; $35,018 street repaving; $0 trees
- **Board's "enviable position" claim** contrasted with: 23% fund balance decline, 69% external funding, $263K/year debt through 2045, Tree Board resigned, Public Works Director resigned, Deputy Treasurer fired, elected trustee removed, Planning Commissioner resigned
- Board's first published version contained "erroneous and libelous," "smear campaign," and "ill-willed players." These were scrubbed after the meeting but before the initial internet publication (per McCarthy's claim in this post).

## Notable Quotes

> "You're 'addressing' my claim by... confirming it. You can't file a whistleblower lawsuit against a former employer unless that employer fired you."

> "You built a straw man, called it my argument, and knocked it down. That's not a rebuttal. That's a magic trick for people who don't read budgets."

> "Processes can be overridden by the person who implemented them. That was the lesson from 2007-2012. You apparently didn't learn it."

> "It answers nothing. It addresses nothing. It cites nothing. It's three pages of 'trust us' from a board that ignored a formal complaint for sixteen months."

## Discrepancies

- This post states the Board "scrubbed" the language ("erroneous and libelous," "smear campaign," "ill-willed players") "after the meeting" but before internet publication. Post fb-02 states they "published it without changing a word." These two accounts appear to conflict on the timeline of when (or whether) edits occurred. The meeting video and cached versions of the published statement could clarify.
- Staffing figures differ slightly between posts: fb-01 and fb-03 say "19 to 25.7 FTE," while this post says "22 budgeted FTE" for FY2024 and 25.7 for FY2026. The 22 figure comes with a specific budget citation (FY2024 Adopted Budget); the 19 figure may refer to an earlier year. Both could be accurate if they reference different baseline years.
