# Original Draft "Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens" (Unedited)

**Raw file:** `raw/documents/their-responses/original-draft-with-libelous.pdf`
**Type:** Draft official letter from Board of Trustees
**Date:** 2026-03-30 (pre-meeting draft)
**Author:** Offices of the Mayor & Board of Trustees

## Summary

The original, unedited draft of the Board's response to Pete's 10 Things flyer. This version was voted on at the [[2026-03-30-special-meeting|March 30 special meeting]]. The Board voted to edit out personal attacks before publication, but this version was initially published without changes. It was later edited.

## Key Language (Original Version)

Opening paragraph:
- "the anonymous author(s) penned a list of misleading and false accusations"
- "deliberately damaging and inaccurate attempted 'gotchas,' meant to denigrate the Town Administrator"

On Neptune Beach:
- **"Erroneous and libelous statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position."**

On Pete:
- "The author(s) have attempted to offer a spin on factual information in order to paint the Town Administrator in a negative light, when he has in fact served our community with distinction, integrity, and professionalism."

On critics:
- "There has been a pattern of a few ill-willed players in Town harassing, bullying, and defaming Town Administrators."
- "this smear campaign"
- "deliberately damaging misinformation and deliberately damaging statements being passed as fact"

## Substantive Claims by the Board

The board's specific rebuttals (unchanged between draft and final):

- **Neptune Beach**: Confirms Wynn was interviewed publicly and disclosed his whistleblower lawsuit. Does not dispute the firing.
- **Governance**: Describes Administrator/Trustee form of government. Does not dispute that Wynn holds both Administrator and Treasurer roles.
- **Grants/Loans**: $10M total ($10,803,000 for three water infrastructure projects). Claims Pete believes "grants and loans are not the correct path forward." Pete's actual claim was about dependency on 69% external funding.
- **Staffing**: "This is patently false." Explains Trustees approve staffing levels. Does not dispute the 19-to-25.7 FTE numbers.
- **Finances**: Describes internal controls (Caselle software, Professional Management Solutions, Finance Committee). Does not dispute dual Administrator/Treasurer role.
- **CIP**: Describes five-year capital improvement plan. Does not dispute the specific dollar amounts Pete cited.
- **Trees**: Claims $40K in "Right-of-Way Maintenance" line items (Streets 10-45-71: $25K, Parks 10-46-71: $15K). States Tree Board suspended via Resolution 03-26 due to expired terms and a resignation. States town hiring certified arborist consultant.
- **Cameras**: States cameras discussed at "at least six public meetings" between October 2024 and December 2025. Claims cameras "reduced vandalism considerably." Does not dispute $50,288 cost, FTC fine, or lack of surveillance policy.

## What the Board Does Not Dispute

The board's response does not dispute any of the following facts Pete cited:
- Wynn was fired unanimously from Neptune Beach
- The grounds were "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct"
- Staff grew from 19 to 25.7 FTE
- Fund balances projected to drop 23%
- 69% of budget is external funding
- $50,288 spent on Verkada cameras
- Verkada was fined $2.95M by FTC
- Cameras approved with zero public comment
- $0 dedicated to tree care in budget
- Tree Board members resigned citing frustration

## Discrepancies

- The Board voted to edit this version to remove personal attacks before publication. The unedited version was published first. It was later quietly edited to produce the [[final-published-letter|final version]]. [[fb-04-point-by-point-rebuttal|source]]
- The board calls the flyer claims "misinformation" and "blatant falsehoods" but disputes none of the cited sources. Their disagreements are with Pete's editorial characterizations of what the facts mean, not with the facts themselves.
