## What Was Requested

Three categories of records, covering **March 25 through March 30, 2026** — the six-day window in which [[lucy-hunter|Lucy Hunter]] distributed her flyer, the Board held a [[2026-03-30-special-meeting|special meeting]] about Pete, and the Board published its March 30 letter calling Pete's flyer "libelous":

1. **All communications** (email, text, Microsoft Teams, written correspondence) between any combination of [[mayor-smith|Mayor Paige Smith]], [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]], [[clayton-buchner|Buchner]], and any Trustee, regarding Pete McCarthy, Pete's flyer, or the Board's written response.
2. **All drafts, revisions, and tracked changes** of the March 30 response letter.
3. **All legal opinions, memoranda, or written advice** from Buchner regarding use of "libelous," "libel," or "defamation" in connection with Pete McCarthy, his flyer, or any citizen communication.

## What Was NOT Produced

### No emails calling or scheduling the March 30 special meeting

Under Colorado's open-meetings law ([C.R.S. 24-6-402](https://leg.colorado.gov/)), a municipality must post notice of a special meeting at least **24 hours** in advance. The [[2026-03-30-special-meeting|March 30 special meeting]] therefore had to be scheduled by at least March 29. Someone on the board coordinated that scheduling. Someone drafted the notice. Someone posted it to the Town website.

**Not a single email about that process was produced.** No Trustee-to-Trustee scheduling email. No email from or to Vetter about posting the notice. No email about the agenda. No email from the Mayor to any other Trustee about calling the meeting.

If those communications happened by email, they were withheld or not searched. If they happened any other way (phone, in person, text, Teams), the request explicitly asked for text and Teams messages. None were produced.

### No draft or tracked-changes version of the letter

The request asked for "all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes" of the March 30 letter. The response points to two already-publicly-posted versions:

- The final agenda packet version: https://paoniaco.portal.civicclerk.com/event/562/files/agenda/1320
- The revised public version dated March 31: https://townofpaonia.colorado.gov/sites/townofpaonia/files/documents/Letter%20to%20Paonia%20Citizens%20-%2003312026.pdf

No draft with tracked changes. No Word version with revision history. No email circulating a draft for comment. A seven-Trustee board does not produce a joint written statement without somebody drafting it, circulating it, and collecting edits. None of that internal product was produced.

### No responses from any other Trustee

Pete's 3/29–3/30 correspondence was addressed to all seven board members. The response produces one reply (Lucy Hunter, individual capacity). **The other six trustees and the Mayor are absent from the production.** Either no one else responded in any form during those six days (implausible given they convened a special meeting about him), or those responses were withheld.

### All attorney communications withheld as privileged

Item 3 — Buchner's legal advice about use of "libelous" — was withheld entirely. The clerk cites:

- C.R.S. § 24-72-204(1)(a) — CORA exemption for records required to be kept confidential by state law
- C.R.S. § 13-90-107(1)(b) — attorney-client privilege evidentiary rule
- C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)(a)(IV) — specific CORA exemption for records of attorney-client communications

**No privilege log** was provided. There is no way for the requester or a reviewing court to assess the scope of what was withheld. Colorado courts have held that blanket assertions of privilege without a privilege log are disfavored; the custodian must identify each withheld record by date, author, recipient, and general subject-matter.

## Significance

This response is the central record showing the Town is **either withholding or not preserving** internal deliberations around the decision to convene a special meeting about a citizen and publish a letter accusing him of libel.

Specifically, either:

1. **Material exists and was withheld in violation of CORA.** The attorney-client privilege claim covers the Buchner advice. It does not cover trustee-to-trustee communications. It does not cover emails from the Mayor to Trustees about scheduling a special meeting. If those records exist and were not produced, that is an unlawful denial.

2. **Material exists on non-email channels that were not searched.** The request included text messages, Microsoft Teams messages, and written correspondence. If the Board coordinated the special meeting and the public letter through Teams, personal email, phone, or in-person meetings, CORA still covers those records (or documents them as not existing in searchable form).

3. **Material does not exist because coordination happened off the record.** If seven elected officials coordinated a special meeting about a citizen without any written communication about scheduling, agenda, or response drafting, that is itself a process problem — a board that operates outside of records law is a board that cannot be held accountable through records law.

Each of the three possibilities supports [[pattern-of-cora-obstruction|the same conclusion]]: the Board's March 30 action against Pete was developed through channels that are invisible to the public.
