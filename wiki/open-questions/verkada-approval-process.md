# How Was the Verkada Surveillance System Approved?

**Question:** What was the procurement and approval process for the approximately $50,000 Verkada surveillance camera deployment on public property in Paonia?

## What the Record Shows

- The town deployed approximately $52,000 in Verkada surveillance cameras. [[verkada-10-things|source]]
- Cameras were discussed at budget meetings between October 2024 and December 2025. [[hunter-rebuttal-flyer|source]]
- The Nov 12, 2024 budget work session included a camera presentation, but discussion was about locations and cost, not surveillance policy or vendor capabilities. [[meeting-2024-11-12|source]]
- Board approved unanimously on 2025-08-12 with zero public comment. [[hunter-rebuttal-flyer|source]]
- Hunter's rebuttal acknowledges no public comment occurred.
- No surveillance policy exists governing access, retention, sharing, or oversight. [[verkada-10-things|source]]
- Verkada cameras include facial recognition, AI-powered "People Analytics," photo-based person search, and Axon Evidence integration. It is unclear whether these capabilities were evaluated or disclosed during the approval process. [[verkada-10-things|source]]

## What the Record Does Not Show

- Who selected Verkada as the vendor and why
- Whether the AI surveillance capabilities (facial recognition, People Analytics) were presented to the Board
- Whether alternative vendors were considered
- Whether Verkada's history (employee harassment, 150K camera breach, FTC fine) was disclosed
- Whether a surveillance policy was considered before or alongside the purchase

## What Could Answer This

- Board meeting minutes from the "at least six meetings" where cameras were discussed
- Purchase orders or contracts with Verkada
- Any internal communications about vendor selection
- CORA request for Verkada-related communications and procurement documents

## Status

**Open.** Partially illuminated by the Nov 12 budget work session transcript and Hunter's rebuttal, but the key questions about vendor selection and capability disclosure remain unanswered.
