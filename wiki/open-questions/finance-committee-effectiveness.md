# Has the Finance Committee Ever Flagged Anything?

**Question:** The board cites the Finance Committee (Mayor + one Trustee) as a key internal control. Has this committee ever flagged a disbursement, questioned a spending decision, or rejected a payment?

## What the Record Shows

- The Finance Committee consists of the Mayor and a Trustee. They review every invoice and ask questions on expenditures and budget allocations. "These questions must be answered to the Committee's satisfaction before an invoice can be included on the list of disbursements to be paid." [[original-draft-with-libelous|source]]
- Committee members both sign each check. [[original-draft-with-libelous|source]]
- The board describes this as a "robust set of internal controls." [[original-draft-with-libelous|source]]

## What the Record Does Not Show

- Whether the committee has ever actually flagged, delayed, or rejected a disbursement
- How often the committee meets and for how long
- Whether the committee has independent access to financial records or relies on what the Administrator presents
- Whether committee members have any financial expertise
- What happens if the committee has questions -- do they get answered before or after payment?
- Minutes or records of Finance Committee deliberations

## Suggested CORA Requests

**Request 1: Finance Committee Records**
> Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request all minutes, notes, agendas, and records of Finance Committee meetings for the period July 2023 to present.

**Request 2: Flagged Disbursements**
> Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request records of any disbursements that were flagged, questioned, delayed, or rejected by the Finance Committee for the period July 2023 to present, including any written communications between committee members and town staff regarding questioned expenditures.

**Request 3: Committee Composition and Authority**
> Under C.R.S. 24-72-201 et seq., I request any resolution, ordinance, or policy document establishing the Finance Committee, defining its authority, membership, meeting schedule, and procedures.

## Why This Matters

An oversight mechanism is only meaningful if it actually provides oversight. If the Finance Committee has approved every disbursement without question since Wynn's appointment, that is a different kind of "control" than the board describes. The committee's track record is a factual question with a factual answer.
