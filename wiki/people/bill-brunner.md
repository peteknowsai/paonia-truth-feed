# Bill Brunner

**Role:** Trustee, Town of Paonia (former)
**Elected:** April 2024, 227 votes (67.7%)
**Removed:** 2024-08-15 by 4-1 vote

## Timeline

- **2024-04**: Elected to Board of Trustees with 227 votes (67.7%).
- **2024 (summer)**: Began exercising oversight. Filed CORA requests, asked questions, criticized administration.
- **2024-08-01**: [[stefen-wynn|Stefen Wynn]] submitted conditional resignation, indicating he would stay if Brunner was removed. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-08-13 to 2024-08-15**: Removal hearing. [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]] served as prosecutor. Charges: "harassment and abuse of position," defined as "harassing and bullying town staff to include statements of incompetence and assertions of criminal behavior." [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-08-15**: Removed by 4-1 vote. For removal: Stelter, Czech, Valentine, [[lucy-hunter|Hunter]]. Against: Swartz. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
- **2024-08-30**: Announced plans to file lawsuit. (KVNF)

## Ongoing Litigation

**Brunner v. Town of Paonia** -- Active lawsuit, Delta County District Court. Challenges his removal from the Board of Trustees.

## Significance

The Brunner removal established the conduct standard that the board letter argues should be applied consistently. The charges were broad ("statements of incompetence," "assertions of criminal behavior") and no written definition of harassment or code of conduct was cited.

Pete McCarthy testified at the hearing that the harassment dynamic was reversed, that it was Wynn harassing Brunner, not the other way around. No board member followed up. [[board-letter-2026-03-30|source]]
