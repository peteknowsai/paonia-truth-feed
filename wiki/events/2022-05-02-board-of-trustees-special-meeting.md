## Summary

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda amended** to add two discussion items: 211½ Niagara Alley tree work reimbursement and Water Treatment Plant habitat project — passed unanimously
- **Amended agenda approved** — passed unanimously
- **Ad hoc committee recommendation on meeting policy/procedure deferred** to next meeting when Jeff Thompson can attend — passed (Thomas Markle voted against)
- **Quarterly budget-to-actual summaries** directed to be provided to the board — passed unanimously
- **Special meeting on water/sewer spending** — motion withdrawn by Trustee Smith after agreeing to review invoices directly with staff
- **Motion to add 211½ Niagara Alley tree removal to May 12th agenda** — passed
- **Insurance statement on offsite vehicle storage** — staff directed to obtain statement from insurer regarding public works vehicles parked on private property — passed
- **Ad hoc IT committee formed** to review existing technology solutions, subscriptions, and assets — passed (members: Markle, Valentine)
- **Purchasing policy review** scheduled for first meeting in June — passed
- **Staff directed to use electronic originals** in packets rather than printing and scanning documents — passed
- **Advisory water committee plan for 1 million gallon plant** — motion failed for lack of a second
- **Public Works status report continued** to next meeting — passed
- **Five-minute recess** — passed

### Discussion Highlights

- **Budget-to-actual report**: Lengthy discussion on how to read the quarterly budget tables. Finance officer Cindy Jones explained the modified accrual accounting method, the columns, and why numbers are "subject to change" (manual entries incomplete, audit in progress). Trustee Smith identified multiple discrepancies between the approved budget and figures in the report.
- **Water/sewer spending concerns**: Trustee Smith flagged that $24,000 appeared in water fund Contract Services despite zero being budgeted, likely SSG emergency work. Debate over whether unbudgeted emergency expenditures require board action to reallocate funds. Smith volunteered to review invoices in person with the finance officer.
- **Audit timeline**: Finance officer stated the audit is her top priority and she's working 8am-8pm. No firm date for a draft audit; final expected around June/July.
- **Meeting civility and length**: Multiple trustees and outgoing board members cited meeting length and lack of civility as the primary reason people don't want to serve. Jeff Thompson's meeting procedure proposal seen as important but deferred.
- **Mayor's role in debate**: Trustee Markle raised a point of order that the mayor/chair should remain neutral during discussion and not indicate how the board should vote. The point of order was voted down.
- **Police chief search**: Mayor reported working with recruiter Chris Low from GPS Government Solutions. Three strong candidates identified; may need board approval for travel expenses exceeding $5,000.
- **Board retreat controversy**: Mayor disclosed she was personally funding a retreat in Grand Junction. Multiple trustees objected — no board vote occurred, no input was sought, and concerns raised about gift/ethics statutes (>$50 gift to officials). Tabled for future agenda.
- **Administrator's report rebuttal section**: Trustee Markle called it inappropriate for the administrator to use the public packet to rebut public comments. Community member Watson disputed the administrator's characterization of her words, citing the YouTube recording as proof. Board punted the issue to the ad hoc meeting procedures discussion.
- **Document accessibility**: Extended discussion about the police report being scanned as an image rather than included as a searchable electronic file. Board directed staff to always use electronic originals.
- **Grant updates**: Quick Win grant funded for 5 trees ($5,000 limit); ARPA and CDOT grants still pending. Congressional Appropriations request of ~$969,000 submitted for spring box rebuilds — won't hear back until March next year.

### Public Comment

- **Bill Bruner** raised concerns about the audio system — no amplification for the in-person audience; also questioned the legality of the mayor gifting a retreat to trustees under the $50 gift statute
- **Christina Patterson** noted the camera placement doesn't show the audience or podium speakers anymore
- **Miss Watson** asked about making trustee emails publicly accessible (cited Estes Park's Google Group model); questioned police presence at meetings; disputed the administrator's characterization of her public comments using YouTube timestamp evidence
- **Michelle Patterson** (via Zoom) questioned the order of operations on the park bathroom grant (board hasn't set priorities); supported public email transparency citing Estes Park; argued factual corrections in packets should cite sources (timestamps, statutes)

### Notable Quotes

- **Trustee Markle** on budget oversight: "We are kind of flying blind here in a monetary sense because we don't know the numbers."
- **Trustee Markle** on the chair's neutrality: "It is so important for a chair of a meeting to be neutral on matters of discussion because their opinion holds so much more sway. I find it an abuse of the station of the chair to indicate that we should vote a certain way."
- **Trustee Smith** on spending transparency: "Every time we get a packet there are more things that have to be purchased. I have no idea how we're paying for it. I feel completely in the dark about how much is being spent."
