# Brunner Removal Hearing and Vote

**Date:** 2024-08-13 to 2024-08-15
**Type:** Quasi-judicial hearing, Board of Trustees
**Location:** Town of Paonia

## Background

[[bill-brunner|Bill Brunner]] was elected in April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7%). After he began exercising oversight (asking questions, filing CORA requests, criticizing the administration), [[stefen-wynn|Town Administrator Wynn]] submitted a conditional resignation on 2024-08-01, stating he would stay only if Brunner was removed.

## What Happened

- [[mayor-smith|Mayor Smith]] brought the charges and served as prosecutor. She could not vote.
- Charges: "harassment and abuse of position," defined as "harassing and bullying town staff to include statements of incompetence and assertions of criminal behavior."
- No written definition of harassment was cited. No code of conduct was referenced. No specific incidents were documented publicly.
- Pete McCarthy testified that the harassment dynamic was reversed, that it was Wynn harassing Brunner. No board member followed up.

## Vote

| Trustee | Vote |
|---------|------|
| Stelter | For removal |
| Czech | For removal |
| Valentine | For removal |
| [[lucy-hunter|Hunter]] | For removal |
| Swartz | Against removal |

**Result:** 4-1, Brunner removed.

## Aftermath

- 2024-08-30: Brunner announced plans to file lawsuit (KVNF).
- **Brunner v. Town of Paonia** is active in Delta County District Court.

## Significance

This hearing established a conduct standard that the [[board-letter-2026-03-30|board complaint letter]] argues should be applied consistently. The charges were broad enough that asking questions and filing records requests qualified as "harassment." That standard has not been applied to town staff.
