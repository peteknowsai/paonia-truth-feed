## Summary

## Town of Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** February 2021 (likely February 9 or nearby, based on context clues)
**Format:** Zoom (virtual due to COVID restrictions)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Motion to have Treasurer King investigate sales tax on franchise fees with Town Attorney Nerlin (general, not DMEA-specific):** Passed, with one nay (Dave Knutson). An earlier, narrower motion specific to DMEA died for lack of a second.
- **Disbursements approved:** Including $150,000 transfer to Summit, $54,094.18 accounts payable, $74,750 AMCO bond payment, $3,932 Chase credit card charges, payroll and payroll taxes, and internal grant transfers. Passed unanimously.
- **North Fork EMS Balance and Fitness Program grant letter of support:** Passed unanimously.
- **Western Slope Conservation Center letter to Senators Hickenlooper and Bennett** supporting permanent protections against oil and gas development in the North Fork Valley: Passed with one nay (Bill Bear).
- **Consent agenda:** Approved unanimously.
- **Meeting format restructuring (work session / business meeting split):** Tabled until in-person meetings resume.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Franchise fee / sales tax calculation:** Treasurer King reported that DMEA's treatment of franchise fees within gross revenue for sales tax purposes is standard accounting, confirmed by checking San Miguel REA. Trustee Bill Bear pushed back, arguing the franchise fee is non-tangible and shouldn't be subject to sales tax. The board ultimately directed the treasurer and town attorney to analyze whether the town's ordinance permits collecting sales tax on franchise fees across all franchise holders (DMEA, Black Hills, Elevate/TDS).

- **Disbursement details:** Trustee Michelle asked about the Ellen Hansen Center kitchen remodel (grant-funded with town match), elevated vehicle expenses ($3,000 in truck/police vehicle repairs), park Wi-Fi installation (antenna on Town Hall for Paolis Park, pandemic-era initiative), Ranger repair parts (back-ordered from November), PayPal/eBay charges (police lights and sirens), a defensive tactics instructor class in Littleton, and Amazon Business switching to invoicing.

- **Conservation letter:** Tanya from Western Slope Conservation Center requested the town send a letter to Colorado senators urging permanent protections against oil and gas, noting the Biden pause on new leases is temporary and multiple RMP lawsuits are pending.

- **Meeting format:** Michelle proposed splitting meetings into a discussion/work session and a business meeting each month. Mayor Bachran opposed it, arguing it doubles workload and slows government action. Tabled per Town Administrator Corinne's recommendation to wait for in-person meetings.

- **Water Advisory Committee:** Thomas requested anonymized water billing/meter data for the committee's analysis and asked the board to articulate long-term population and density goals to inform water planning. Tammy reported the committee meets the fourth Wednesday at 6 PM and is building a priority list.

- **Tree Board:** State Forest Service inventory and assessment draft received. The board is looking at Grand Junction's certified arborist model. Tree City USA online presentations being attended. Discussion of replacement tree sources (DMEA, Arbor Day Foundation, Forest Service).

- **Committee meetings and public access:** Christina Patterson asked that all committee meetings be posted on the town calendar. Corinne agreed to work on it.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson** spoke on the franchise fee issue, citing Ordinance 5-2-50, arguing the franchise fee is collected on the town's behalf and is not part of DMEA's gross revenue, therefore shouldn't be taxed. She stated she would file a complaint with the state if the issue isn't resolved.
- **Thomas** criticized the board for not sufficiently valuing public participation and expressed disappointment in the mayor's responsiveness, saying it made him question his vote.
- **Paige Smith** (215 Delta) complimented the tree board's online resources and asked about public access to tree board meetings.
- **James** asked about replacement trees after the county removed his Dutch elms.
- **Christina Patterson** thanked the water advisory committee and asked that all committee meetings be posted publicly.
- **Suzanne Watson** (second comment) suggested the Arbor Day Foundation as a source of free trees and jokingly suggested adopting the meeting style of towns where nobody shows up.

---

### Notable Quotes

**Bill Bear, on the franchise fee:** "How do we legitimately charge a tax on a fee? The franchise fee is collected from the citizens on their purchase of electricity... and on top of that we get taxed for it. We're getting taxed twice."

**Mayor Bachran, on the meeting format proposal:** "Every agenda item will have to be discussed twice, which means you will get half as much work done in twice as much time... Nobody else does this. I have searched, I have looked at local towns, and nobody does this."

**Dave Knutson, on public engagement:** "One thing I feel restrained on is when a person from the public makes a comment, I cannot at least respond in an active listening way to show that I've understood what they said... I think one of the reasons you get into this back and forth so much with people is that they don't feel heard."
