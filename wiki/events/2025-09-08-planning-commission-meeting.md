## Summary

## Paonia Planning Commission Meeting -- September 8, 2025

**Type:** Public Hearing
**Subject:** Rezoning Application RES 25-01, 2011 Boxelder Avenue

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- unanimous voice vote
- **Minutes approved** (August 4, 2025) -- passed, with Commissioner Bachan recusing (not present at that meeting)
- **Rezoning RES 25-01 recommended for conditional approval** -- motion to recommend the Board of Trustees approve rezoning from C2 (Community Commercial) to R3 (Higher Density Residential), with the condition that the property owner comply with all provisions of the town's utility code for water, sewer, and trash. Roll call: Chair Watson (aye), Vice Chair H. (aye), Commissioner Bachan (aye), Commissioner Smith (aye), Commissioner Hutchkins (aye), Commissioner Stelter (absent). One commissioner abstained. **Motion carried.**

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Property background:** 2011 Boxelder Ave is a 0.35-acre parcel (15,236 sq ft) currently used as a 4-unit apartment building. The structure allegedly originated as a church/parsonage, was converted to a single-family residence, then expanded to four units over time. Zoning approvals were never secured, creating a non-conforming use under C2 zoning.
- **Rezoning rationale:** R3 zoning permits multifamily dwellings (up to 6 units on lots of 10,000+ sq ft). The rezoning would bring the existing use into conformity, align with the 2025 comprehensive plan's housing goals (affordability, infill development, housing diversity), and serve as a buffer between the commercial corridor (Second Street) and the R1 residential neighborhood to the north.
- **Water/utility billing:** The property currently pays only one water base rate and two sewer base rates despite having four units. Staff recommended requiring compliance with the utility code (either four meters or four base rates). This does not increase water consumption -- it corrects the billing structure. The property averages ~11,565 gallons/month.
- **Potential fifth unit:** The applicant's representative disclosed in rebuttal that the basement has an exterior door and could potentially be finished as a fifth unit. R3 zoning would allow up to six units by right. However, adding a unit would require a new water tap (blocked by the current water tap moratorium), a building permit, and would likely trigger special use review and parking requirements.
- **Parking:** Staff showed aerial photos confirming ample off-street parking for the existing four units. Commissioners discussed parking requirements under Section 16-6-10, concluding that the parking table applies to new construction, additions, or changes of use requiring special review -- not to the current rezoning of an existing use.
- **Rezoning tied to property sale:** The applicant intends to sell the property; the town's code requires non-conforming uses to be brought into conformity before transfer.

---

### Public Comment

- **Ryan Rosco, 212 Boxelder** -- neighbor who received the mailed notice. Came wanting to understand why the rezoning was happening. His main concern was whether it would lead to more units and more traffic. Said his concerns were largely addressed by the staff report and the transparency about the potential fifth unit.

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Liz Hedrick (applicant's representative, Needle Rock Realty):** "I don't think it was the intent of Lora Rolf to be in any sort of violation, but as with growing pains of any community, sometimes things get slipped through the cracks."

- **Town Administrator (staff):** "You can't make a non-conformity worse. And our code also says that if you're going to transfer property, you should probably make that non-conformity conforming before that property transfers. That's the reason for this rezoning."

- **Commissioner (during deliberation):** "It's in a commercial zoning but the existing use is multifamily residential, and so to me the rezoning makes sense. It provides something that this town can't beat, and it's a building that's there."
