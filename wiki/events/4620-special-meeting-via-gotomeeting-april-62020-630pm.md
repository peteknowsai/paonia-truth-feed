## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Regular Meeting — April 14, 2020 (Virtual via GoToMeeting)

**Present:** Mayor (presiding), Trustees Baccarini, Bear, Budinger, Deal, Kinnison, Patterson. Amanda on phone. Town Attorney Nolan present.

---

### Key Decisions

- **Motion to add emergency mask ordinance to next meeting agenda:** Passed (5-1, Baccarini voted nay)
- **Approval of tonight's agenda:** Passed unanimously
- **Ordinance 2020-TBD (Modifications to Mayor's Duties):** Passed unanimously. Mayor no longer counts toward quorum (quorum = majority of 6 trustees, i.e., 4). Mayor's veto can be overridden with 4 trustee votes.
- **Motion to adjourn:** Passed unanimously

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Mask ordinance:** Trustee (likely Baccarini) raised a citizen request for an emergency ordinance requiring masks in town. Mayor ruled it couldn't be acted on since it wasn't on the agenda, but allowed a motion to add it to the next meeting (April 21). Mayor expressed reservations, suggesting the town should take direction from county/state/federal authorities rather than impose its own mandate. Trustee Kinnison argued the situation was fast-moving and supported putting it on the agenda.
- **Mayor's quorum/veto ordinance:** Extensive discussion on removing the mayor from quorum calculation. Key concern: three trustees could block business by not showing up (no quorum without 4 of 6). Trustee Bear argued the trade-off was worth it to prevent the mayor from having outsized veto power. Town Clerk noted quorum issues were rare during regular meetings but more common during special/budget meetings. Mayor raised a legal question about whether a 4-3 override is meaningful veto power since it's the same simple majority -- Town Attorney Nolan argued the statute allows the board to define the governing body, so the two-thirds threshold would be met. Trustee Deal reported community support for excluding the mayor from quorum. Trustee Kinnison confirmed the practical implication: if only 3 trustees show up, a veto cannot be overridden.
- **Mask donations:** Trustee (Mary) announced the hospital had enough cloth masks; surplus materials would be available at the town hall for anyone to take and sew their own.
- **Little Free Library:** Trustee Kinnison announced a sanitized book library at 401 Vista Drive.

### Public Comment

- No public comments were offered on any agenda item (opportunities were provided before each vote).

### Notable Quotes

- **Trustee Bear on mayor's power:** "I think the board should hold the power and not the mayor, and so I would actually take my chances on the quorum and not have the mayor have that much power."
- **Trustee Kinnison on masks downtown:** "I was one of few people wearing a face mask. I had to do errands in order to get food and I was really disappointed to see that the person behind the counter was wearing a face mask, but a lot of the customers were not. My wife's mother is 95 years old -- for me it's really important that people are in this together trying to protect each other."
- **Trustee Deal on quorum logic:** "If the mayor's not going to have a vote on everything then he shouldn't count for the quorum, because he's never going to vote to overturn his own veto."
