## Summary: Joint Work Session -- Short-Term Rentals & Accessory Dwelling Units

**Date:** March 19, 2026 (Wednesday)
**Facilitator:** Shay Gober, Urban Rural Continuum
**Format:** Presentation followed by public input and board/commission comments

---

### Key Decisions

- No votes taken -- this was the first of three planned workshops
- Written comments accepted through end of day March 26
- Next joint work session targeted for April 8 or 11 (Monday or Thursday), 5-7pm; doodle poll sent to board/commission members
- Third and final meeting intended to present a draft ordinance for board consideration

---

### Discussion Highlights

**Short-Term Rentals (STRs):**
- Proposed definition: rental of all or portion of a dwelling unit for 30 or fewer days (excludes hotels, motels, B&Bs, hostels)
- Proposed cap of 30 licenses (approximating current count); strong public sentiment to lower it (15-20 suggested repeatedly)
- Licenses would be non-transferable (don't convey with property sale), require annual or biennial renewal
- License fee would cover administrative costs plus enforcement software
- Required: parking per existing code, smoke/CO detectors, fire extinguishers, trash service, posted address
- Owner or designated agent must reside/conduct business within 15 miles
- Proposed in all zoning districts (cap controls total number rather than zone-by-zone restrictions)
- Taxing STRs as commercial property discussed but deferred -- state legislation may address this; statutory town has limited taxing authority
- Renting a single room in owner-occupied home: multiple speakers supported treating this differently (not counting toward cap but still licensing/tracking)
- Banning STRs outright described as "questionably legal" by facilitator

**Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs):**
- Proposed: one ADU per single-family lot, max 800 sq ft, must comply with existing setbacks/height/building code
- Could be freestanding, attached, or within principal dwelling
- One additional off-street parking space required
- Design must complement principal dwelling
- ADU could be built before main house (allows affordable entry to housing market)
- Owner-occupancy requirement debated -- task force was split; some want flexibility to rent both units
- Water: would share physical tap with main house; no new service line extensions until moratorium lifts
- Tap fees and monthly water/sewer charges for ADUs unresolved -- some want full fees, some want incentives
- Existing non-conforming ADUs: strong concern about health/safety (extension cords under rugs, missing egress windows); consensus that existing ADUs need inspection, not blanket amnesty
- Lot coverage, setback preservation, and tree protection raised as concerns
- Construction cost reality check: ~$200,000 for 800 sq ft at $250/ft, translating to $1,400-1,500/month mortgage

**Water Moratorium:**
- Town Administrator clarified: no water service line extensions will be processed until moratorium conditions are met, regardless of ADU regulations adopted
- Multiple speakers urged not waiting 4 years for the water study to plan for outcomes

---

### Public Comment

- **Peter McCarthy** -- Cap of 30 preserves a housing crisis; suggested 15 as compromise to return units to long-term market
- **Jeff Thompson** -- Raised vacant/second-home ownership problem; grandfathering concerns for old structures that may not meet code; urged incentivizing long-term rentals, possibly via STR tax funding affordable housing assistance
- **Sky** -- Supported lower cap; suggested licensing first to see actual demand, then reduce over time; asked that action plan documents be posted online
- **Sam Cal** -- Questioned why requiring local ownership is too hard but 15-mile agent requirement isn't; suggested forgiveness program for existing illegal ADUs to get them regulated and on the books
- **Marlene** -- Wants "within 15 miles" tightened to truly local
- **Patty Peach** -- Opposes STRs in single-family residential neighborhoods (noise, cars); wants timeline on ADU implementation; raised uninspected existing ADUs as safety concern
- **Molly** -- Wants lower cap excluding owner-occupied room rentals; supports affordable housing tax; opposes requiring owner occupancy for ADUs (both units should be rentable)
- **Jess Derin Aran** -- Supports "be local" campaign incentivizing long-term rentals to locals; suggested applying 15-mile agent rule to non-owner-occupied ADU properties
- **Aaron Watson (Dark Sky Paonia)** -- Concerned about density impact on lighting ordinance lumen caps; wants STR regulations to reference lighting ordinance; educate visitors on dark sky culture
- **Michelle Patterson (online)** -- Noted town code lacks hotel definition; recommended adopting International Building Code definitions
- **David Mer** -- Warned against restricting STRs too much; tourism (Cherry Days, BMW rally, CESI, concerts) depends on STR capacity; sales tax is major town revenue
- **Lori Merrick** -- STR co-host; echoed tourism concerns; reported no neighbor complaints in her experience; guests come for weddings, reunions, wineries
- **Alice Elliott** -- Exempt single-room rentals from regulation; lower cap; prioritize residents over tourism; wants lot coverage limits for ADUs
- **Laura Carr** -- Supports cap but lower; community can't function without workers who can afford to live here; described problematic neighboring STR renting multiple rooms to strangers simultaneously
- **Travis** -- Supports incentivizing long-term rental investment by community members; ADU feasibility naturally limited by lot constraints
- **Mary (Second Street)** -- Appreciates the process; supports proposals as fair first step; 30 cap seems high but acknowledges property owners' investment rights; wants all STRs on the books paying taxes
- **Unnamed speaker** -- Pro-ADU, anti-STR; wants minimal regulation of private property
- **Bill (Second Street)** -- Opposes blanket amnesty for non-compliant uses; suggests lottery for licenses with 3-5 year sunset; wants explicit acknowledgment of water system near/over firm yield; urges planning for water study outcomes now
- **Town Administrator (Corinne, as resident)** -- No water line extensions during moratorium; annual license fee needed; cautions against grandfathering setback encroachments as ADUs; lives near STR, experienced sketchy police-involved incident
- **Paige (Delta Avenue)** -- Owns historic carriage house ADU; counted 16 current in-town STRs on platforms (not 30); supports lower cap; wants ADUs by special review not by-right; emphasizes design standards and neighbor impact
- **Lynn (Minnesota Avenue)** -- Concerned about lot coverage and setbacks; supports dark sky protections; enforcement is critical but difficult; opposes grandfathering unsafe structures

**Board/Commission Comments:**
- **Steve (Trustee McInnis?)** -- Played devil's advocate: 30 STRs won't dramatically change housing; ADU construction costs may limit affordability impact; but more supply generally helps
- **Rick (Trustee Stelter?)** -- Major concern is health/safety of existing ADUs; wants every ADU inspected; insists on water/sewer tap fees for ADUs; supports STR licensing and cap
- **Suzanne Watson (Commissioner)** -- Wants clarification on downtown dwelling unit rules (STRs vs. long-term, storefront vs. upstairs); concerned about lot coverage, tree canopy, microclimate; warns ADUs may increase property values and worsen affordability
- **Dave (Mayor Betts?)** -- Suggested scoring criteria for license allocation; curious about capping ADU permits per year; thanked housing task force
- **Kathy (Trustee Smith?)** -- Supports lower STR cap but wants flexibility for room rentals that keep owners in their homes; wants ADU policy to promote affordable long-term rentals
- **John Valentine (Trustee, online)** -- Appreciates congruency between proposals and public comment; noted limiting STRs and promoting tourism are not opposed
- **Shay (Trustee Schwarz?)** -- Doesn't think STRs should be allowed in ADUs; curious about setting cap organically via initial licensing window with lottery if over 30; wants existing ADUs brought to code before allowing new ones; concerned about single individuals holding multiple licenses
