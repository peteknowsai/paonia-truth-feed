# STR Citizen Initiative Approved for Circulation

**Date:** 2026-04-13
**Type:** Citizen initiative approval by Town Clerk
**Petition:** Paonia Short-Term Lodging Policy (v5 PRINT)
**Proponents:** Pete McCarthy and Melayna Rosa, 119 ½ Dorris Ave

## Summary

[[samira-vetter|Town Clerk Samira Vetter, CMC]] approved for form the Short-Term Lodging Policy citizen initiative petition submitted by Pete McCarthy and Melayna Rosa. This is the first citizen initiative Vetter has approved -- after rejecting the prior STR initiative in May 2025 and rejecting the surveillance initiative in April 2026. See [[initiative-process]].

## Timeline

- **2025-05-21:** Original STR citizen initiative submitted.
- **2025-05-28:** Vetter rejects the original STR initiative. [[2025-05-28-str-initiative-rejection|event]]
- **2026-03-30:** v4 Final Draft completed with revised land-use framing.
- **2026-04-06:** v5 PRINT version prepared -- leaner structural provisions designed to clear the Clerk's "administrative function" objections that had rejected prior petitions.
- **2026-04-07:** v5 submitted to the Town Clerk.
- **2026-04-13:** Vetter approves the petition for form.

## What the Approval Letter Says

Samira Vetter's letter (dated 2026-04-13, on Town of Paonia letterhead) states:

> "I received your submission of a Citizen Initiative Petition for 'Paonia Short-Term Lodging Policy' on April 7, 2026.
>
> I hereby approve your petition for form exactly as it is. If you make any changes, even just grammatic, you must bring in the new version to be approved for form before circulation. Active registered voters as of April 7, 2026 is 1,143, which means you need to collect 57 signatures of in town active voters.
>
> Please continue to refer to Title 31, Article 11, which clearly details the requirements of circulation and collection of signatures, as well as the verification and protest process."

Signature: Samira M Vetter, CMC, Paonia Town Clerk.

## Requirements for Circulation

- **Signatures required:** 57 (5% of 1,143 active in-town registered voters as of 2026-04-07)
- **No edits permitted.** Any change to the approved text -- even grammatical -- requires re-approval before circulation.
- **Governing law:** Title 31, Article 11, C.R.S. (circulation, signature verification, protest process).

## What Changed Between v4 and the Approved v5

The v4 Final Draft contained provisions the Town Clerk had previously rejected in other initiatives as "administrative" or "judicial" -- explicit "no license or fee" language, an "unlawful to operate, advertise, or offer" clause, a conflicts-with-existing-law section, citizen enforcement with attorney fees, and an amendment-and-repeal clause requiring voter approval to change.

v5 removes all of those. What remains is the pure land-use core: principal residence hosting is a permitted use in every zoning district with no discretionary approval required; non-resident short-term rentals are prohibited in residential zoning districts and permitted in non-residential zoning districts; general building, fire, noise, nuisance, and health regulations are preserved; severability; effective thirty days after certification of election results.

The lean framing -- zoning classification only, with no enforcement mechanics, no voter-lock, no contract voiding -- is what cleared the Clerk's prior objections.

## Significance

First citizen initiative the current Town Clerk has approved. Establishes that a narrowly-scoped zoning-classification ordinance, stripped of enforcement and amendment provisions, can survive the "administrative function" framework that has defeated prior citizen initiatives in Paonia.
