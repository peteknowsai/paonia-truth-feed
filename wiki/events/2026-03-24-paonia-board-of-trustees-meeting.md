## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** April 7, 2026 (based on context clues)
**Present:** Mayor Sn[yder], Trustees Czech, Hec[k], Hunter, M[a]gado, Carnell
**Absent:** Pro Tem Stelter (excused), Town Clerk Vetter (sick), Town Attorney Clay

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda reordered** to move STR staff report (item 6) before items 4 and 5. Passed unanimously.
- **Public comment added to agenda** (had been accidentally omitted). Passed unanimously.
- **Consent agenda approved** (March 10 minutes, March 24 disbursements). Passed unanimously.
- **Work session scheduled for April 14 at 5:00 PM** to discuss Ellen Hansen Smith Community Center operations agreement. Passed unanimously.
- **Lease purchase agreement approved** for new street sweeper (with Timco), authorizing town administrator and attorney to execute documents. Passed unanimously.
- **Change order #2 for KLM Engineering approved** in the amount of $32,928, for a project total not to exceed $163,723 (tank relining inspection). Passed unanimously.
- **STR committee tasks reaffirmed** -- committee is limited to seven policy/legislative topics assigned Feb 24; administrative functions (fees, licensing, code enforcement) are outside their scope. Passed unanimously.
- **Resolution 04-26 adopted** extending the short-term rental moratorium through December 31, 2026 (unless sooner repealed). Passed unanimously.
- **Scott Brown selected as facilitator** for the STR ad hoc committee; staff directed to finalize agreement. Passed unanimously.
- **Cleanup days program postponed** to a future year due to public works capacity constraints. Passed 3-2.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Ellen Hansen Smith Community Center:** Staff clarified the facility is no longer called the "teen center." Electrical, plumbing, and contractor repairs are underway for state code compliance. The town is gathering information from Red Feather Bowman and the center's board to draft an operations agreement for the April 14 work session. Some confusion about whether outgoing trustees would still serve on the 14th -- confirmed they remain trustees until new ones are sworn in.

- **Street sweeper lease:** Standard lease restrictions (can't sublease to another town). Attorney Clay may need to adjust language around an attached resolution requirement. A follow-up resolution may come at the next meeting.

- **Tank relining project (MPC/KLM):** The project is under the original $2.31M engineer's estimate at ~$1.83M total despite change orders. MPC missed the March 24 substantial completion date. Administrator Wyn refused to grant additional days because the contractor voluntarily demobilized for months. Liquidated damages of $700/day begin immediately; $1,500/day after 30 days if final inspection isn't passed. MPC says they need until April 27. After the completion deadline, MPC must pay for KLM's inspection services.

- **STR committee scope:** Mayor presented a memo distinguishing legislative (policy) functions from administrative functions. The Colorado Constitution prohibits citizens initiatives on administrative matters -- this is why the prior citizens initiative was rejected. The committee was not tasked to write an ordinance, create a phased approach, or scrutinize alternative ordinance content beyond the seven assigned policy topics.

- **STR facilitator selection:** Two candidates (Scott Brown and "Dev") were considered equally qualified by the committee. The differentiator: only Brown responded to a follow-up question about providing executive summaries between meetings. Price difference was ~$10/hour but total cost was roughly equivalent given different time commitments.

- **Cleanup days program:** County offered to waive $650 in tipping fees (~10 tons). Staff proposed options: single collection point or curbside pickup after the townwide garage sale. Concerns raised about staff capacity, outside dumping, co-mingling with household trash, and logistics. Public works director heard of it for the first time at this meeting. Discussion touched on the upcoming yard waste collection program and fire mitigation concerns. Ultimately postponed 3-2 with sympathy for the idea but recognition of public works being overextended.

---

### Public Comment

- **Sheree (in-town resident):** Objected to advice given by town attorney Clay at a recent meeting, saying Clay emphasized that trustees should "follow along" and watch their words, which she found insulting to residents and amounted to muzzling trustees who disagree with the group.
- **Jen (in-town, works for the Spotlight):** Commented that the three administrative items (licensing fees, door dimensions, local representative) were key reasons the last STR ordinance was defeated, and the committee understood that and tried to address it.
