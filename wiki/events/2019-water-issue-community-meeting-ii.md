## Summary

## Town of Paonia Community Meeting -- Water Emergency Update

**Date:** Approximately late February / early March (likely 2018, based on context of Feb 17-18 emergency timeline)
**Location:** Community meeting hall, Paonia, CO
**Presiding:** Mayor Charles Stewart; moderated by Tracy Truelove (CDOT Regional Communications Manager / PIO for Southwest Incident Management Team)

---

### Key Decisions
- **Boil water notice remains in effect** for all affected areas until bacteriological testing confirms safety -- no piecemeal lifting; it will be lifted town-wide or not at all
- **Bacteriological samples to be pulled Thursday**, lab results expected Friday (approximately end of that week)
- **Water conservation to continue** well into the year, likely with Board of Trustees direction, until drought risk is fully assessed
- **After-action report** to be produced by the Southwest Incident Management Team; will be made publicly available
- **Future Board of Trustees agenda items** announced: in-town tap moratorium, out-of-town tap sales policy (currently capped at 5/year to water companies), metering at springs vs. plants, conservation incentives, additional storage options

### Discussion Highlights
- **Water system status:** ~1.126 million gallons in storage across two tanks, up from roughly 1 foot of water in each tank days earlier. Lamborn plant processing 280-300 gal/min; Clock plant processing reservoir water. Goal is 3 million gallons within weeks.
- **Chlorine levels** at 3.5 (legal limit is 4; normal is 0.5-1.2). Safe for bathing -- below public pool levels (~5.0). Water usable for domestic purposes (laundry, showers) but not irrigation or non-domestic use.
- **Cause of crisis:** Major line breaks depressurized the system. A leak at the elementary school was estimated at ~200,000 gallons/day -- once fixed, tanks immediately began refilling. A second major leak was found behind the library.
- **Spring recharge concerns:** Springs currently producing about half of normal for this time of year due to severe drought the prior year. Springs may not recharge predictably.
- **Infrastructure context:** $6 million spent on water system over prior 6 years -- filtration upgrades, 21,000 feet of pipeline replaced (some 100+ years old), 1-million-gallon tank refurbished and brought online just before the emergency. System draws from 32 springs across 20+ miles of lines serving ~25 square miles.
- **Metering proposal:** Town considering dual metering (at springs and at plants) to detect water loss in the 20+ miles of transmission lines between source and treatment.
- **Tap inventory:** Roughly half of taps are inside town, half outside. Water companies have master meters; individual home metering varies by company.
- **Demand vs. production:** Normal consumption ~200-300 gal/min (~135,000-148,000 gal/day). During crisis, production was pushed to ~200,000 gal/day but still fell short until the school leak was found.
- **Colorado 133 confirmed open** (had apparently been closed during the emergency).

### Public Comment
- **Resident (conservation question):** Asked what concrete measures the town will take to incentivize conservation, citing California drought programs with rebates for efficient appliances. Mayor responded that work sessions and community meetings would follow.
- **Resident (spring monitoring):** Asked whether springs are regularly checked in winter, noting the town seemed "caught flat-footed." Ken acknowledged new protocols including dual metering are being considered.
- **Resident (recharge rate):** Asked about tank refill timeline and capacity projections.
- **Resident (water taps/growth):** Asked about tap counts inside vs. outside town and future development policy. Ken confirmed this would be a Board decision.
- **Resident (demand):** Asked about daily consumption relative to tank capacity; Travis confirmed ~135,000-148,000 gal/day normal production.
- **Civil Air Patrol representative:** Thanked the town for including cadets, noted they'd trained two years for this kind of emergency response.
- **Resident:** Thanked town for recycling bins for bottles and hand sanitizer; asked about potable water truck (National Park Service recalled it, expected back Friday).

### Notable Quotes

**Mayor Charles Stewart:**
> "During the five years that I've been on the Town Board, one of my greatest concerns was that the town could not respond adequately to an emergency -- that the town, the county, and the state would not be able to work together effectively. I no longer have those concerns."

**Ken (Town Administrator), on conservation and future planning:**
> "When we really start talking substantively about the growth of your community, I hope we can have turnouts like this -- that we may have to move out of the community room into a bigger room to get everybody involved. Because when everybody is involved, then we all have buy-in."

**Mayor Stewart, on the Lamborn Ranch water agreement:**
> "We sat down with Mark and Steve, told them what we wanted to do. Their response was 'Let's do it.' There was no lengthy discussion about all the various factors -- it was just simply 'Let's get it done.'"
