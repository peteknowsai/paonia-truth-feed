## Summary: Paonia Board of Trustees Budget Training Work Session

**Date:** Not explicitly stated in transcript (context suggests 2023 based on references to 2020 audits as most recent)
**Type:** Work session (not a regular meeting -- no quorum required)
**Present:** Trustees Valentine, Marshall, Slaughter (partial roll call); not a full quorum noted

---

### Key Decisions

- None. This was a training/work session, not a decision-making meeting. No motions or votes occurred.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Budget Law Overview (Mia Gonzalez, DOLA Division of Local Government):** Walked the board through Colorado budget law (Title 29, Article 1), including the budget message, budget schedules, adoption/appropriation resolutions, mill levy certification, and key deadlines (Oct 15 budget presentation, Dec 15 mill levy certification, Jan 31 filing).

- **Budget Amendments:** Covered three types -- transfer amendments (moving money between funds), supplemental amendments (unanticipated revenue), and revision amendments (rare, for significant revenue drops). Emergency contingency spending requires attorney consultation since "emergency" is not defined the same way as under TABOR.

- **Enterprise Fund Transfers:** A recurring question throughout the session. Whether money can be transferred between enterprise funds (water/sewer) and the general fund was not definitively answered. Dana (DOLA regional manager) strongly recommended against borrowing from enterprise funds, citing 40 years of experience watching governments forget to repay those internal loans.

- **Legal Limits on Revenue:** Five limits discussed -- TABOR debt limit, fiscal year spending limit, mill levy limit, property tax revenue limit, and the pre-TABOR 5.5% property tax revenue limit. The town has "de-Bruced" (waived certain TABOR limits). Whichever limit produces the lowest revenue amount is the binding constraint.

- **Financial Trends Analysis (Marcellus Briscoe, DOLA):** Presented 7-8 years of financial data from audits through 2020:
  - **Population:** Steady, no significant growth or decline
  - **Retail sales:** Slowly increasing since 2016-2017, supporting sales tax revenue
  - **Revenue diversification:** Reasonably diversified, though sales tax + intergovernmental combined exceed 50% (both volatile sources)
  - **General fund health:** Revenues generally exceed expenditures; unrestricted fund balance has been over 60% of expenditures in recent years (GFOA best practice is 2-3 months / ~25%)
  - **Water fund:** Operating revenues (charges) generally cover operating costs, but total expenditures have exceeded total revenues; reserves declining. A large 2017 capital fee spike was explained as a rate study rebooking.
  - **Sewer fund:** Operating expenditures have more often exceeded operating revenues, suggesting a potential need for a sewer rate study. Reserves more stable than water fund due to less capital activity.
  - **Capital asset condition:** Steady at 60-70% across funds, indicating ongoing capital investment

- **Capital Planning & Financing (Dana, DOLA):** Urged the board to think long-term about capital needs, particularly water treatment plant upgrades. Outlined a potential financing path: Energy Impact grant (up to $750K, application window Aug 1 - Sep 1) combined with State Revolving Fund loan (Paonia likely qualifies as disadvantaged community, potentially 49% loan forgiveness). Emphasized the board needs to understand bonding capacity, overlay multiple capital project debts, and communicate infrastructure needs to the community.

- **2027 Sewer Treatment Standards:** New enforcement standards coming in 2027 will require capital investment in the sewer plant. Failure to meet standards could result in fines from the state. James (staff/consultant) is doing preliminary analysis.

- **Auditor Consistency:** Board member asked about impact of switching auditors over the years. Briscoe acknowledged different auditors may classify items differently but said DOLA's compendium model tries to normalize the data.

---

### Public Comment

- No formal public comment period (work session format). Town staff member Cindy confirmed the town has not borrowed from enterprise funds during her tenure.

---

### Notable Quotes

**Dana (DOLA Regional Manager), on enterprise fund borrowing:**
> "Please, please, please stay away from if at all possible borrowing from your enterprise funds. I've seen many governments over 40 years say 'well, you know, taxes are low, we've got great reserves in our enterprise funds, let's borrow' -- and then this debt sits out there and four or five election cycles later everyone kind of forgets about the debt."

**Mia Gonzalez, on the purpose of budgets:**
> "A budget is really more than numbers. It's a tool to communicate with your community about your financial health, priorities, and how you're planning to get there."

**Dana, on infrastructure financing urgency:**
> "In 2027 you've got new treatment standards coming down on your sewer plant. If you don't have the capital to do what's going to be required to meet those treatment standards, you're not only not going to be able to do enough -- you're going to start being fined."
