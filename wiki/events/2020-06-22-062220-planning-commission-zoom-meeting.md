### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approval** -- passed unanimously
- **Minutes from February 27** -- tabled to next meeting (only two members eligible to vote on them; insufficient votes)
- **Chair appointment** -- deferred to next meeting; current chair (Barb) continuing temporarily due to health concerns
- **Spiegel fence variance** -- motion to recommend approval to Board of Trustees passed unanimously (no opposition). Discussed in context of a fence ordinance vote scheduled for the following evening's Town Board meeting.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Fence variance vs. fence ordinance timing:** Corinne explained the variance hearing proceeded despite a pending fence ordinance change at the next night's Town Board meeting, because repeated continuances and 15-day certified letter posting requirements would further delay Debra Spiegel's case. The new fence ordinance would be less restrictive than what the variance requests, potentially making the variance moot.
- **Sight triangle clarification:** A commissioner asked about the corner cut-off requirement. Corinne clarified the rule requires fencing within 30 feet of a corner to be no taller than three feet -- a fence can physically exist there, it just has a height limit.
- **Master Plan update (Evan, town intern):** Evan has been working on the master plan update for over a year. Key points:
  - Public input meetings and surveys completed; data crunched and analyzed
  - Goals and objectives distributed covering: utilities, transportation, housing, community sustainability (five pillars: social, economic, governmental, environmental, and one other)
  - Using a brochure-style format (20-25 pages) modeled after Bennett, Colorado's plan -- designed to be concise and cheaply updatable in-house ($2,500-$5,000 vs. hiring consultants)
  - Chapter drafts expected next month for Planning Commission review
  - Land use section will come last, involving 3-4 growth scenarios for residents to vote on
  - Three-mile annexation plan required by state statute will be included in land use section
  - Capital Improvement Plan and asset inventory preliminary reports expected late fall (delayed several months due to earlier-year events)
  - Housing section covers both affordability and property maintenance/code enforcement
  - Governmental pillar focuses on increasing trust between government and citizens
  - Planning Commission must approve the master plan before it goes to the Board of Trustees
- **Ditch companies and mapping:** A commissioner asked about water/ditch company maps within town. Corinne noted there are 27 water companies (out of town), but only about two ditches run through town, with individualized lateral entities branching off. The Stewart Ditch was named. One ditch company has a map but hasn't provided a copy yet. Only one out-of-town water company has provided a map of water lines.
