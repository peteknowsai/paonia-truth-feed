## Summary

### Key Decisions
- **Agenda approval** -- Motion to approve, passed unanimously
- **Street closure for two Saturdays (July 4th and following Saturday), 1pm-6pm** -- Motion to close Main Street for five hours, extended from one Saturday to two at Trustee Johnson's suggestion. Passed unanimously.
- **Fee waiver ($125/event) for those two Saturdays** -- Motion to waive the $25/hour street closure permit fees for both events. Passed unanimously.
- **Administrative approval for closures beyond 4 hours** -- Explicitly NOT approved. Board agreed this needs further discussion at the next regular meeting.

### Discussion Highlights
- Amy (applicant) visited Main Street businesses; all were in favor of the Saturday closures. The broader request is for closures every Saturday through end of September (13 total Saturdays), but unanswered questions -- particularly about whether businesses' insurance covers outdoor areas beyond their brick-and-mortar footprint -- limited this approval to two weeks only.
- Multiple trustees expressed reluctance about waiving fees long-term. The $25/hour fee exists for a reason: overtime for police, Public Works staff time for barricades, cleanup.
- Trustee Mech requested staff provide an actual cost accounting of the first two events (overtime, Public Works time, phone calls, cleanup) to inform future decisions.
- Town Administrator Corinne noted that closures of 4 hours or less have always been approved administratively; only the 5-hour extension triggers board approval.
- Discussion of whether waived fees could be reimbursed through CVRF (Coronavirus Relief Fund). Corinne said it's unclear -- may only apply to businesses that were required to close, not those that simply lost revenue. She has a meeting scheduled with a grants advisor and may have answers by next meeting.
- A DOLA grant application (forwarded by Suzie Lo) was mentioned as another possible funding source for the events.

### Public Comment
- No public comments were offered at either opportunity.

### Notable Quotes
- **Trustee Johnson:** "I would suggest we alter the motion to allow this to be for two Saturdays and that will get us into our next meeting. I think there's a lot of unanswered questions both from the applicant and maybe of the businesses."
- **Trustee (likely Budinger or Patterson):** "I hate to waive fees but I think we just make sure that they understand that we're doing this because of COVID-19, you know, we make that very clear and then go from there."
- **Trustee Mech:** "It would be helpful to make the decision going forward if staff could provide an accounting of the actual cost associated with the event -- actual overtime required, the time Public Works needs to close the street, any phone calls you're fielding related to that, and any cleanup that's required after."
