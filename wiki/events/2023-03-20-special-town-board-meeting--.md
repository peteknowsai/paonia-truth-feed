## Summary

## Executive Session: Town Administrator Candidate Interviews

**Note:** This is an executive session held pursuant to C.R.S. 24-6-402(4)(e) and (g) for interviewing non-finalist applicants for the Town Administrator position. Board members present: Mayor Bachran, Trustees Paige Smith, Dave Kudron (partial), Rick Stelter (partial), Dave Weber (partial), Thomas Markle. Interim Town Administrator Leslie Clement also present.

---

### Candidates Interviewed

Three candidates were interviewed: **Colton Burke** (planner, Town of Eagle, CO), **David Lynch** (former mayor of Euclid, OH; former city manager of Newton Falls, OH), and **Miles Viness** (economic development director, Central City, CO; retired Air Force Lt. Colonel).

---

### Key Decisions

- No formal votes were taken (executive session rules prohibit formal action)
- Board planned to deliberate and select finalists the following morning at 10:00 AM
- A previously scheduled candidate (Quinn) withdrew the morning of the interviews
- A candidate named Dennis Start was previously excluded from interviews due to undisclosed issues in Nevada

### Discussion Highlights

**Colton Burke Interview:**
- Currently a planner in Eagle County, CO; holds MS in planning
- Attracted to Paonia after visiting; originally from small-town Nebraska
- Supervisory experience is primarily project management of land use files and customer service coordination, not direct staff supervision
- Described handling a noticing error on a controversial aggregate recycling facility -- owned the mistake, rescheduled, maintained applicant trust
- Water/sewer experience is adjacent: coordinated with public works and town engineers on infrastructure capacity for development review; observed a water treatment plant expansion process including rate discussions and bonding
- Grant experience mostly from time as recycling coordinator at University of Nebraska-Lincoln (equipment grants, environmental/sustainability)
- Budget experience limited: undergraduate accounting degree, worked at Deloitte as advisory consultant, participated in audits, managed community engagement program budgets
- Emphasized community engagement as infrastructure; advocated listening before prescribing solutions
- On development after water moratorium lifts: stressed long-range planning, preserving community identity, learning from Eagle's rapid 1990s growth

**Board's post-interview assessment of Burke:**
- Impressed by his demeanor -- described as humble, thoughtful, a good listener
- Recognized he lacks direct administrative experience but acknowledged "chicken and egg" problem -- first-time managers typically start at towns Paonia's size
- Leslie Clement noted she'd looked up another candidate (Eric Osterberg) who appeared very strong but speculated he might want Paonia for a slower pace
- Concern about Burke's lack of water/sewer and direct management experience
- Positive that his land use skills would address Paonia's code issues
- Leslie noted federal grant writing might require outside help but state grants would be within his capability

**David Lynch Interview:**
- 37 years practicing law; former mayor of Euclid, OH (~70,000 pop.); former city manager of Newton Falls, OH (~5,000 pop.)
- Emphasized collaborative management style; described authoritarian approach as counterproductive
- Claimed record as Ohio's #1 community administrator for per-capita grant acquisition; all projects came in under budget and on time
- Described managing regional sewer system (~$200M service area) and water system in Newton Falls; emphasized hands-on EPA relationship management
- Referenced Inflation Reduction Act as major infrastructure funding opportunity
- Noted Paonia is eligible for GFOA (Government Finance Officers Association) award but hasn't applied recently
- Discussed Economic Development focus; suggested subscribing to databases of companies seeking locations

**Controversial background on Lynch:**
- Fired from Newton Falls per July 2021 Tribune Chronicle report; Lynch explained it as political faction shift (3-2 vote flipped when mayor appointed replacement council member); resulted in voluntary separation agreement
- Lawsuit filed by Jose Morales over comments Lynch made at a council meeting, repeating what he characterized as Morales's own "inside joke" about working on his green card; lawsuit was dismissed with no payment
- Board member (likely Paige Smith) found letters to the editor accusing Lynch of homophobic statements in newspaper columns
- Board member researched and described him as "racist and sexist" based on internet findings

**Board's post-interview assessment of Lynch:**
- Leslie Clement: "The word that came to me was glib"; didn't think he was a fit; concerned he couldn't adjust from being "in charge" to an administrator role; put off by the Morales incident explanation
- Board concerned about his enthusiasm for economic development incentives (buying property and giving it to developers, tax abatements) -- "you just can't do that kind of thing"
- Consensus he was not a cultural fit for Paonia
- Board preferred taking a chance on a younger, less experienced candidate over Lynch

**Miles Viness Interview:**
- Retired Air Force Lt. Colonel (pilot, instructor); current economic development director in Central City, CO
- Grew up in small-town Nebraska; father was public works superintendent, mother was city clerk
- Informal role as deputy city administrator in Central City though no official title
- Executive director of 21-member Development Corporation board
- Grant writing experience: USDA REDLG grants, state downtown redevelopment grants, currently writing million-dollar housing grants
- Water/sewer experience from Central City: managed wells with nitrate/uranium issues; handled major water main break and emergency response
- Worked on Central City's 2012 comprehensive plan and major downtown renovation (four city blocks including state highway)
- Inherited Development Corporation $1.7M in debt; worked to sell/split properties and pay down loans
- Budget experience limited to Development Corporation budgeting and working alongside city administrator
- Could not provide an example of a serious error he'd made
- On Paonia's needs: identified water/tap moratorium as primary constraint; suggested capitalizing on festival tourism revenue

**Board's post-interview assessment of Viness:**
- Concern about military command style not fitting Paonia's culture -- "he likes to make the decisions"
- Development Corporation focus seemed misaligned with Paonia's priorities (water infrastructure, not growth)
- Leslie Clement: "sharp and pretty organized" but lacking direct municipal management experience; would likely "rely on military background as a basis for management"
- Seen as potentially capable if stronger candidates withdrew; acknowledged municipal experience and grant writing as assets
- Described as "lowkey" compared to other candidates
- Leslie indicated the board had "four really strong candidates" overall

### Board Context Shared With Candidates

All three candidates were told about:
- Prior strife caused by a single person serving as both administrator and clerk, leading to code misinterpretation and poor trustee-citizen relations
- Position has been separated; new town clerk already hired
- Leslie Clement working as interim to clean up chain of command issues (committees/citizen groups had been directly supervising staff)
- Water infrastructure is the dominant challenge: $23M water grant and $8M sewer grant recently submitted; 50+ year old clay sewer lines; one of two water treatment plants not running; tank needs relining
- Water tap moratorium in place, expected to last 2-3 more years
- 2019 water crisis: town without water ~5 days, some out-of-town users without water for weeks
- Embezzlement ~10 years prior eroded public trust
- Town lacks current financial reports (Leslie working on this)
- Land use code has inconsistencies across chapters and needs wholesale review
- Community is mix of agricultural/ranching heritage, remote workers, and tourism
- Board cohesion has improved significantly through the hiring process

### Public Comment

- No public comment (executive session, closed to public)

### Notable Quotes

**Colton Burke:** "I try not to be the person that comes in and says here's this and this and this that needs to be done... I'm not someone who wants to just say what it should be without really being that familiar with what people want."

**Leslie Clement** (on Lynch): "The word that came to me was glib. I don't think he's a fit. I think he's been living large and been in charge and it's going to be hard for him to adjust to an administrator role where he has to take a back seat."

**Mayor Bachran** (on Paonia's identity): "We are an agricultural community and I always say our number one crop is kids."

**Leslie Clement** (on what the new administrator needs): "The code be followed by the Town Administrator. It's not make a decision and then go back and check and make sure it's okay with the code -- and that was being done to some extent."
