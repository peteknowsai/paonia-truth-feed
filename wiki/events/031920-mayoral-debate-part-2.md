## Summary

## Town Board Candidate Forum — Paonia, CO

*Note: This appears to be a candidate forum/discussion between two board candidates, not a regular board meeting. The transcript is auto-captioned with significant errors throughout.*

### Key Decisions
- No motions or votes — this is a candidate forum, not a business meeting.

### Discussion Highlights
- **Space to Create (affordable housing/artist live-work project):** Dominated the discussion. One candidate supports the concept but criticized the lack of specifics in the contract — no clear costs, timelines, funding sources, or fallback plans. Called it "cheeky" to say details can't be discussed until a site is designated. The other candidate defended the pre-development process, noting you can't price a building without architectural plans. Said the project uses CHFA guidelines ($57K income threshold), is not intended to change the town's character, and would house existing residents, not newcomers. Referenced sociological research suggesting reduced housing costs free up income for business investment.
- **Water system:** Both candidates agreed the system has been neglected for decades. Discussion of the need for a reservoir (known since at least the 2000/2004 water studies). Disagreement over tap counts — one candidate claimed the 2004 study double-counted out-of-town taps. The town reportedly grew from ~1,130 to ~1,660 taps. A new water study (RFP mid-July) will analyze springs, treatment, storage, raw water availability, system capacity, staffing, and new storage potential. Mention of the 2-million-gallon tank needing realignment, and a 2015 project that was supposed to include a second tank but didn't.
- **Reservoir options:** One candidate favored Roper Reservoir (already functional, cheaper to expand). The other referenced multiple studies evaluating options including a reservoir at the base of Creek Springs, enlarging Todd Reservoir, and other sites.
- **Zoning:** Both agreed zoning is outdated. Adopted hastily during mining boom to prevent man-camps. Industrial zone permits almost nothing by right, leading to uneven/selective enforcement. Commercial-to-residential conversions downtown are cannibalizing retail space and sales tax base. One candidate argued the industrial zone should have the same live-work rights as commercial zones.
- **Home rule vs. statutory town:** One candidate floated expanding participation through home rule designation. The other cautioned strongly against it, saying the town struggles to follow existing state law and shouldn't be writing its own. Suggested instead making all committees three-person (with the mayor on each), which would trigger open meeting requirements and increase public access.
- **Water utility governance:** One candidate proposed the water utility board could eventually be elected at-large from all water customers, not just town residents — giving out-of-town users a voice.
- **Budget/fiscal priorities:** One candidate pushed for cutting non-essential spending and directing savings toward water system debt and improvements. Referenced a $7,000 budget overrun and ~$300K spent on a water crisis and trash truck replacement.
- **Marijuana shops:** Brief mention — one candidate compared them to bars/liquor stores, cited Mancos earning $5-10K from three shops. Noted Paonia's disadvantage vs. Mancos (highway location between Durango and Telluride).
- **Master/comprehensive plan:** Both agreed the existing plan is outdated and a new one is desperately needed to guide zoning and growth decisions.

### Public Comment
- No formal public comment period — this was a candidate forum.

### Notable Quotes
