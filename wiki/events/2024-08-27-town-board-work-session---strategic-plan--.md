## Summary: Paonia Board of Trustees Work Session — Strategic Planning / SWOT Analysis

*Note: This appears to be a work session (not a regular board meeting), so the format is less formal. The transcript is auto-captioned with significant garbling in places.*

---

### Key Decisions

- No motions or votes were taken. This was a strategic planning work session.
- The board was assigned **homework**: review mission/vision/values examples from Durango, Rifle, and Bayfield, plus the draft comprehensive plan's existing values, and return with notes for a future workshop.
- The board was also asked to prepare **SWOT analysis items for Finance & Administration** before the next session.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Mission/Vision/Values**: Town Administrator Stephan distributed a spreadsheet of other communities' mission/vision/values statements. He highlighted Rifle as the best example ("succinct and impactful") and recommended reviewing the draft comprehensive plan's existing value statements, which already received public feedback. Public Works (Nikki) was praised for producing an outstanding departmental values draft.

- **Budget & Strategic Plan Context**: The entire exercise is driven by the need for a strategic plan to qualify for a **GFOA (Government Finance Officers Association) budget award**. The fiscal year 2024 budget submission was rejected for lacking goals/objectives tied to a strategic plan.

- **Administration/Finance Goals** (prepared by Kaja): Build confidence in utility billing; build transparency in town accounting; digitize records using the Caselle ERP system; standardize filing for utility/accounting documents; create a safe working environment for office staff; invest in continued staff training.

- **Meter Audit**: Public Works has been systematically correcting a historic bad practice of using endpoint numbers as serial numbers for meters, encoders, and endpoints. Work began in January and is ongoing.

- **SWOT Analysis — Water**:
  - *Strengths*: Skilled/dedicated employees, really good water quality, three strong engineering firms, knowledge of system needs
  - *Weaknesses*: Understaffed, funding shortfalls, scale of needed improvements, aging infrastructure
  - *Opportunities*: Grants/supplemental funding, local contractors, consecutive water systems collaboration with CDPHE
  - *Threats*: CDPHE state regulations, misinformation/social media, fire risk, stigma/reputational issues making recruitment difficult, pay competitiveness

- **SWOT Analysis — Wastewater/Sewer**:
  - *Strengths*: Additional treatment capacity available
  - *Weaknesses*: At discharge capacity (nitrogen/phosphorus limits in summer low-flow), potentially insufficient land for future treatment needs, aging infrastructure (possibly worse than water)
  - *Opportunities*: Land application to change discharge permit, polishing pond, Rural Water assistance, ditch company wetlands projects downstream, cheaper flood-plain land acquisition
  - *Threats*: CDPHE/EPA regulations, NPDES permit requirements, sludge removal timeline (possibly pushed to 2030), PFAS/heavy metals contamination concerns for biosolids composting, lab/sampling reliability issues (labs failing to deliver reports on time, limited options nearby)

- **SWOT Analysis — Trash**:
  - *Strengths*: Dedicated crew
  - *Weaknesses*: Single trash truck with no backup (the spare is shot), customer education gap on pickup rules, no system for tracking excess-can charges, employee safety (wasp stings from dumpsters, injuries from overgrown alleyways), scheduling (three days/week with 10 employees and one truck)
  - *Opportunities*: Recycling/composting grants (brochures picked up at CML conference), code rewrite for clearer rules, technology for scheduling
  - *Threats*: Landfill capacity, skyrocketing material costs

- **SWOT Analysis — Streets, Parks & Recreation**:
  - *Strengths*: Well-loved parks, accessible to everyone, volunteer network (flower programs), tree board knowledge, strong public-private partnerships (Western Slope Conservation Center at River Park, Nature Connection on trails)
  - *Weaknesses*: No seasonal maintenance schedule, parks get least attention (not life-safety critical), potholes, storm water, sidewalks, accessibility gaps (ADA), unpermitted public events disrupting maintenance, underutilized spaces (football field), tree conflicts with infrastructure
  - *Opportunities*: Grants (federal highway, state), internal scheduling via new intranet system being built by Reuben, park reservation calendar on website, public-private partnerships, legacy events
  - *Threats*: Skyrocketing material costs (Fifth & Grand project went from $1M to $2.9M), trees damaging infrastructure, overuse wearing down parks

- **SWOT Analysis — Police**:
  - *Strengths*: Fully staffed, community-oriented policing approach, strong social media presence, inter-agency cooperation (police help Public Works and vice versa)
  - *Weaknesses*: Reliance on only three mobile/on-call officers, retention difficulty due to isolation, officers not required to live in town
  - *Opportunities*: School district partnership (SRO program extending to youth athletics, skate park, library), Reserve Officer program (unpaid officers maintaining POST certification), four officers from other agencies living within 10 minutes
  - *Threats*: Geographic isolation from other law enforcement (Sheriff, Delta PD far away and busy), state mandates (body cameras cited as expensive unfunded mandate requiring perpetual cloud storage), recruitment challenges (spouse employment, daycare, cost of living)

- **Cross-Department Theme**: Inter-agency cooperation within town departments was highlighted as a significant strength — police assist with water breaks, Public Works helps across departments, etc.

- **Intranet Project**: Reuben (staff) has been quietly building an internal intranet for inter-department information sharing, including a maintenance calendar system. The board was not previously aware of this project.

- **Purchasing Policy**: A new purchasing policy manual with forms is nearly ready for board approval, addressing the current weakness in procurement processes.
