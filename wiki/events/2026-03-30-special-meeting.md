# Special Meeting - Board Response to 10 Things Flyer

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Type:** Special meeting, Board of Trustees
**Called in response to:** Pete McCarthy's "10 Things" flyer distributed 2026-03-25

## Background

Pete distributed a flyer on 2026-03-25 citing adopted Town budgets, published news coverage, court records, and publicly available documents. Within 5 days, the Board called a special meeting to approve a public response.

For comparison: Pete's formal complaint sent 2024-11-18, documenting specific statutory violations with dates and quotes, received no response from any board member for 16 months.

## Agenda Items

- Approval of a "Statement of Fact" rebutting the flyer
- Proposed paid display ads in three newspapers (Merchant Herald, High Country Shopper, Delta County Independent) using taxpayer funds

## Mayor's Report

- Called the flyer "libelous"
- Referred to the "anonymous author(s)" despite knowledge that Pete had publicly claimed the flyer
- Stated concern that public criticism "can place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies"
- Noted [[lucy-hunter|Trustee Hunter]]'s counter-flyer was coordinated with [[stefen-wynn|Wynn]] and [[clayton-buchner|Buchner]]

## Draft "Statement of Fact"

- Called the flyer "erroneous and libelous"
- Referred to "a few ill-willed players in Town harassing, bullying, and defaming Town Administrators"
- Did not dispute the sources cited in the flyer (budgets, court records, news coverage)
- Reframed editorial conclusions as factual claims

## Post-Vote

- The Board discussed editing the statement to remove "assertions and characterizations that aren't necessary" before publication. [[fb-02-they-didnt-edit-a-word|source]]
- The version published was identical to the unedited draft. "Erroneous and libelous" remained.
- Meeting video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DEe3Jw6u4VE

## Discrepancies

- Board's response characterized Pete's opinions (supported by cited facts) as "misinformation" and "blatant falsehoods" without disputing the underlying sources
- The board stated that the claim "anyone who asks questions gets pushed out" is "blatantly false and the subject of ongoing litigation," simultaneously denying the pattern and acknowledging they are being sued over it
