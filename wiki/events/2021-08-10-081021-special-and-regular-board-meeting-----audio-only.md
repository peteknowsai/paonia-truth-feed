## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

*Includes Work Session and Regular Meeting*

---

### Key Decisions

- **Meeting structure change (work session):** Motion passed to restructure public comment -- public comments on non-agenda items during visitors/guests, then separate public comment periods before unfinished business and new business, plus a public comment period at end of meeting on process/decisions. Staff directed to write up the new rules for the next meeting.
- **Special meeting scheduled for August 24 at 5:00 PM** to discuss committee structure, mayor's role, and organizational structure. Passed unanimously.
- **Street striping at Main & Second:** Motion passed to add striping (crosswalks, stop lines) at the new four-way stop intersection to the upcoming street striping project.
- **Ordinance 2021-06 (Alcoholic Beverage Licensing):** First reading passed. Cleans up distance requirements from schools/childcare.
- **Ordinance 2021-07 (Marijuana code cleanup):** First reading passed. Reflects passage of ballot issues 2C/2D and prior licensing ordinance.
- **Learning Council letter of support:** Approved. Letter supports grant fundraising for the Learning Council to purchase the Ali's building as a community center.
- **Redistricting letters:** Approved with amendment -- replaced "gerrymandering" language with "not adequately representing communities of interest." Letters oppose splitting the North Fork Valley watershed between legislative districts.
- **Main Avenue fence dispute:** Tabled for the evening due to ongoing legal dispute. Staff directed to provide a written report on the process failures.
- **Airport advisory committee:** Motion passed not to appoint a liaison to county commissioners pending more information. Mayor Bocker appointed to the airport advisory committee. (Later flagged as potentially not meeting county requirements -- to be revisited.)
- **Reserve fund balances:** Board requested current reserve balances for water, sewer, and trash funds in simplified format by August 24 meeting. Discussion of debt/reserves tabled until then.
- **Disbursements approved:** $31,752.32 in accounts payable plus payroll, credit card, and retirement payments.
- **Consent agenda (July 27 minutes):** Approved as amended with corrections to Dave Knutson's leave request and an ADA compliance note from public comment.

### Discussion Highlights

- **Meeting length and public comment structure** dominated the work session. Trustees debated whether to limit public comment to designated periods rather than allowing comment on each agenda item individually. Trustee Knutson argued strongly for the change, citing 11+ PM meetings and unproductive back-and-forth. Trustee Bear consistently defended the public's right to comment on each item before votes. The compromise: public comment in blocks before each business section, with time limits.
- **Robert's Rules vs. alternatives:** Board acknowledged they follow Robert's Rules poorly and debated adopting a modified process. Attorney Merlin advised getting any new rules written down before implementing.
- **Civility in public comment:** Mayor Bocker and Trustee Knutson raised concerns about "vicious" public attacks on staff. Trustee Patterson pushed back, arguing most perceived hostility stems from people not feeling heard and not receiving responses to their concerns.
- **Staff direction authority:** Trustee Knutson raised concern that individual trustees and committee members were directing staff work without full board approval, overloading staff. Board discussed requiring formal motions for any staff assignments.
- **Committee structure and mayor's role:** Trustee Patterson outlined three options for committees (keep with public members added, eliminate and create ad-hoc, or assign individual trustee portfolios) and raised the weak mayor/strong mayor distinction, noting Paonia sits awkwardly between the two.
- **Trustee Bettinger's statement on financial controls:** Detailed the town's history of a $400K embezzlement over 10 years ago and the strict financial controls implemented since, pushing back against current accusations of malfeasance over ~$100/month coffee purchases.
- **Short-term rentals:** Board reviewed survey results from the Governmental Affairs & Public Safety committee. Discussion covered taxation (occupational tax would require voter approval; sales tax already applies but may not be collected), registration/licensing, caps on number of STRs (Ridgeway model), owner-occupancy requirements, and fair competition with commercial lodging. Trustee Patterson recused from voting due to conflict of interest but remained to answer questions.
- **Water system update:** 1 million gallon tank is online and in service. Springs are producing well with spillover. Board requested notification when springs stop spilling.
- **Publicly shared emails:** Trustee Patterson advocated for publishing all board correspondence on the town website for transparency, citing other Colorado communities that do this.

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne** (multiple comments): Argued the public can't comment meaningfully on items they haven't been briefed on; urged using planning commission more to reduce board workload (cited dark skies ordinance as example); cautioned against Bob's Rules (doesn't allow explaining votes); said proposed meeting changes sounded confusing.
- **Paige Smith, 215 Delta Ave:** Emphasized that emails to the board are public records but there's no repository for public access; defended the value of public comment based on citizens' life experience and expertise.
- **Thomas Markle, 302 Second St:** Reminded board they serve the public, not vice versa; defended First Amendment rights including angry speech; questioned where meeting time is actually spent; suggested board members arrive in a calm mindset. Later spoke extensively on short-term rentals from his experience as a lodging operator, explaining that Airbnb/VRBO handle tax collection automatically and advocating for equitable treatment between commercial lodging and residential STRs.
- **Jim Lang, High Country Printing:** Attended regarding the Main Avenue fence issue; was told the discussion would focus on process, not the neighbor dispute.
- **Ashley Sargent** (fence dispute party): Requested tabling the fence discussion due to ongoing legal dispute.
- **Chris Kendall, Minnesota Creek Rd:** Argued the fence issue should have been addressed months ago and shouldn't be tabled now.
- **Christina Patterson, Rio Grande:** Urged the fence issue not be tabled; asked about trail design in river park; raised concerns about Red Feather Bowmen being excluded from trail planning.
- **Mary DeFranco, 7th St:** Wanted fence discussion focused on process, not litigation; opposed bringing lawyers into it.
- **Alicia Michelson, Orchard Ave:** Presented the Learning Council's plan to purchase the Ali's building for a community center with teen business programming and ice cream.
- **Natasha Leger, Citizens for a Healthy Community:** Presented on state redistricting, arguing the proposed maps split the North Fork Valley watershed and should keep Delta County aligned with Gunnison County.
- **Ms. Watson** (multiple comments): Raised concerns about fence permit enforcement quality; suggested getting the airport MOU in strike-and-underline format; asked about STR tax rules from Colorado DOR.

### Notable Quotes

**Trustee Bettinger (on financial controls):** "I cannot stand by and let false accusations of malfeasance and corruption in the town be made without a challenge. These charges are without merit and more questionable when made within minutes of the outside auditor's report on the soundness of our financials and the accuracy of our controls... These current charges are about coffee -- less than a hundred dollars a month."

**Trustee Patterson (on public comment and being heard):** "I really think a lot of what is perceived as vicious commenting or attacks comes from someone bringing something up time and time again and not feeling heard on that subject. If that happens a couple of times you start to get angry."

**Thomas Markle (on First Amendment at meetings):** "Anyone in America can come to this podium and say anything they want. That's really cool. That's a First Amendment right... Sometimes people's livelihoods and homes and businesses are at stake. Those decisions get made here and you cannot expect someone to sit here and be calm and cheerful and polite at every interaction, and they have no obligation to."
