## Summary

This is not a regular town board meeting -- it's a **CIRSA risk management training for the Paonia Planning Commission** presented by Nick Vas (CIRSA representative). No motions or votes were taken.

---

### Key Decisions
- None. This was a training session, not a decision-making meeting.

### Discussion Highlights
- **Scope of authority:** Planning Commission's authority comes only from state statutes and local ordinances. The commission is advisory to the Board of Trustees and has no supervisory role over staff.
- **Governmental immunity:** Commissioners are protected under the Colorado Governmental Immunity Act for acts within scope of employment, but willful/wanton conduct can result in personal liability and loss of CIRSA insurance coverage.
- **Quasi-judicial vs. legislative roles:** Extensive discussion distinguishing legislative functions (general, forward-looking policy) from quasi-judicial ones (specific applications affecting individual property rights). Quasi-judicial decisions require: pre-existing criteria, evidence developed at hearing, and a fair/impartial decision maker.
- **Ex parte contacts:** Commissioners must avoid substantive discussions about pending applications outside hearings. If inadvertent contact occurs, disclose it at the hearing. Social media posts about upcoming hearings are problematic.
- **Code conflicts:** Significant back-and-forth (led by Suzanne) about what happens when staff reports contain errors or the code has internal conflicts. Nick advised raising inconsistencies during the hearing rather than pre-hearing with staff, and that the Board of Trustees must ultimately resolve code conflicts.
- **2018 zoning code restructuring:** Suzanne described how zoning ordinances were moved into the building code, which she voted against, causing years of conflicts still being resolved.
- **Dissenting opinions:** A commissioner who loses a vote cannot write a separate letter to trustees or actively undermine the commission's recommendation. The body speaks as one.
- **Continuances:** Planning Commission can open and continue a public hearing without re-noticing, which addresses concerns about being pressured to decide before the Board of Trustees hearing already scheduled.
- **Criteria gaps:** Multiple commissioners noted Paonia's code lacks clear criteria for rezoning and special reviews, with some criteria appearing only on staff-created application forms with no code citation.
- **Recusal standards:** If your financial interest exceeds that of an ordinary person in the same situation, you should disclose, recuse, and leave the room. Leaving the room is recommended (not strictly required) to avoid even nonverbal influence.
- **Privacy at public hearings:** Discussion about requiring speakers to state name and address on the record, especially given YouTube recordings. Sign-in sheets suggested as alternative. Colorado's anti-doxing law mentioned.
- **Rule 106 challenges:** Decisions will be upheld if based on code criteria with any competent evidence in the record. The "rule of why" -- keep asking commissioners why until deliberations build a defensible record.
- **Variances:** The term "variance" should be reserved for zoning/building code contexts involving topographical hardship. Using it as a catch-all for exceptions to any code provision is problematic.

### Public Comment
- No formal public comment period. This was a training session with interactive Q&A among commissioners and two trustees present.

### Notable Quotes
- **Nick Vas:** "The best claim is no claim at all, right, from an insurance perspective."
- **Nick Vas (on deliberations):** "It's math class -- you can have the right answer because you looked over at your neighbor's paper, but show your work. You'll get zero points if you don't."
- **Nick Vas (on the "grand bargain" of zoning):** "People's property rights came first and zoning came second. You were granted this power in exchange for granting folks due process."
