## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Regular Meeting Summary

**Date:** October 2022 (exact date not stated; references suggest mid-October)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda modifications passed:** Flag policy moved from new business to old business; property/casualty renewal and workers' comp moved to consent agenda
- **Motion to remove public censure of Trustee Markle from agenda failed** for lack of a second
- **Consent agenda approved** (including CIRSA insurance renewal, meeting minutes, property/casualty, workers' comp)
- **Flag policy:** Referred back to Streets committee for a noticed meeting; attorney to provide draft language with viewpoint criteria
- **Censure of Trustee Markle:** Motion by Trustee Smith to "drop this, have a reset, and discuss Resolution 2017-6 at a future meeting" -- vote unclear from transcript but appears to have passed; no further censure motion carried
- **General contract engineering firm selection deferred** to next meeting pending fee schedule comparisons and follow-up questions (due to board by Monday)
- **Town Clerk job description approved with modifications:** Remove first two "board support" bullets; minimum qualification changed to deputy clerk experience; preferred qualification is previous clerk or municipal clerk certification; bachelor's degree in relevant field; salary range $56,160-$72,800 retained
- **Master Plan RFP approved** with changes: remove electronic submission requirements, add thumb drive option, remove intent-to-bid requirement; Planning Commission to review proposals and recommend to board
- **Trustee Stelter appointed as One Delta County representative** (unanimous)
- **Accessory Dwelling Unit ordinance continued** to next meeting
- **Motion passed to invite library district representative** to next meeting regarding threats to local library

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Flag policy (Section B):** New town attorney Nick Cotton-Baez gave detailed analysis of *Shurtleff v. City of Boston* (2022). He warned the current draft blurs the line between government speech and public expression by inviting the public to request ceremonial flags without clear criteria for approval/denial. Recommended adding either permissive or prohibitive viewpoint criteria to make the policy legally defensible. Board discussed whether to use permissive language (listing what's allowed) vs. prohibitive (listing what's banned). Safety concerns raised about private citizens hanging flags on town infrastructure (OSHA/insurance issues). Definition of "street flag" needs refinement to exclude flags on private property extending into right-of-way.

- **Censure debate:** Trustee Kenson presented a memo proposing censure of Trustee Markle for stating the board "enjoyed" raising utility rates. Markle gave an extended rebuttal arguing: his actual words were misquoted (he said "exciting for some people," not "enjoyed"); he named no individuals; Resolution 2017-6 was never repealed but a motion to enforce it failed 5-1 on Aug 17, 2022; his examples of Colorado censure cases (Judge Chase, Aurora's Jurinsky, Denver School Board's Tate Anderson) involved far more serious conduct. Attorney Cotton-Baez confirmed: the 2017 resolution remains binding (never repealed by subsequent resolution); censure is lawful under implied municipal authority even without specific statutory reference; removal requires finding of "malfeasance or serious misconduct" per 1899 case law. Extensive public comment followed, sharply divided.

- **Contract engineering firms:** Four proposals reviewed (KLJ, Westwater, SGM, JDS). Board frustrated by missing fee schedules from two firms. SGM noted for broad capabilities and grant-writing track record but possibly responsible for inaccurate asset inventory of the 1-million-gallon plant. KLJ praised for grant writer, Montrose proximity, and public engagement plan. Westwater familiar with Paonia's water system (six local projects). JDS limited to water engineering. Karen to reach out to local engineers for independent review of proposals.

- **Town Clerk hiring:** Detailed discussion of salary range ($56K-$73K), advertising outlets (CML, Clerks Association, DCI, High Country Shopper, Montrose Daily Press, Durango paper, Western Slope papers), application process (require town application + resume + cover letter), residency requirements (decided not to require in-town residency due to housing costs/availability), and benefits (90% of employee-only health premium).

- **Administrator transition:** Town Administrator Karen Ferguson emotional about ongoing public criticism of staff. Warned she would modify her resignation to be effective immediately if mistreatment continued. Board discussed interim options; decided to accelerate the existing hiring process rather than hire an interim. Personnel committee working with Steve Barwick and Bill Crank as screening committee; seeking third screener (retired Crawford resident with county/municipal experience suggested).

- **Family Medical Leave Act (FAMLI):** Colorado's new family leave program requires towns to opt out by year-end or contribute 4.5 cents per employee. Attorney confirmed the 180-day lag won't apply if town opts out before December 31. Employees can still voluntarily participate. Written notice to employees required before board vote; resolution to be on upcoming agenda.

- **Police report:** Department conducted emergency preparedness meeting with school staff; coordinating driver training with Hotchkiss PD; received Narcan shipment (officer carry at discretion pending policy); established co-responder program and mobile crisis response unit for mental health calls (used several times in past 30 days); received 50 donated traffic cones from CDOT.

- **Public Works:** 31 of 100 new remote-read water meters installed since Wednesday (of ~191 total needed). Exploring cost savings by reducing trash runs to twice weekly. Board member raised concern about diesel price increases and hot ashes in trash during heating season.

- **Library district:** Trustee Markle raised concerns about threats to the local library from the Delta County library system; board voted to invite a library representative to the next meeting.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson** (Second Street): Criticized the "be kind, be fair" sign as an unagendized, ambiguous policy statement now being used to justify censure; argued laws and ordinances, not slogans, provide fairness and consistency; asked the board to take it down
- **Susy Palis** (Harding Road): Thanked the board and Public Works (especially Corey) for a successful Harvest Fest weekend
- **Margaret Hollander** (Third Street): Expressed long-standing frustration with zoning changes, inconsistent building permit enforcement, unresolved water loss from five years ago, and rate increases without accountability
- **Michelle Addison** (Second Street): Noted CIRSA insurance renewal was late again despite board requesting early quotes the prior year; criticized lack of cost comparison
- **Multiple speakers on censure:** Watson, Tammy (Orchard Ave), Bill Bruner, Chris Kendall (Creek Road), Dave, Jeff Steels (born Avenue, presented 56 signatures supporting removal/option 3), Margaret Hollander, Susy Palis all spoke -- sharply divided between those defending Markle's right to speak freely and those supporting consequences for his conduct
- **Patterson:** Asked about lack of jaws of life and stretchers at a fatal accident scene; EMS explained budgetary constraints ($40K for jaws of life) and that the second ambulance was down with a bad injector
- **Watson on Public Works:** Suggested using "back the badge" money for a permanent animal control/dog facility
- **Michelle on trash:** Suggested recycling program as cost-saving measure to reduce landfill trips
- **Watson on master plan RFP:** Cited CRS 31-23-205 noting the Planning Commission has statutory authority to appoint its own employees and urged commissioners to read and understand their statutory powers

---

### Notable Quotes

**Trustee Markle (on censure):**
> "What's happening here is an attempt to coerce me into retracting my honest and generalized opinion by substituting your words for mine, distorting my opinion into a personal attack, and making demands under threat of censure and threat of removal from the board at the discretion of a single board member. That is not nice and it is not fair, and I shall not submit to your threats."

**Trustee Smith (urging the board to move on):**
> "We all have had our moments. To censure Thomas is so over the top. Let's get past this -- this is what makes us the town of Paonia. The drama that we continue to have has to stop. We have so much to do."

**Town Administrator Karen Ferguson (on staff morale):**
> "I am tired of sitting in meetings having to listen to myself being degraded and disparaging comments being made by community members. Staff is completely devastated and torn apart and upset and concerned about having to continue to work for the community and the board with the way that people are treated. If this continues, I will modify my resignation effective immediately."
