## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** October 28, 2025 (based on context clues: consent agenda references Oct 14 minutes, Oct 28 disbursements, Nov 5 deadlines, Nov 10 rescheduled meeting)

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- unanimous
- **Consent agenda approved** (Oct 14 minutes, liquor license renewal for Ed Food Yo LLC DBA Neos, Oct 28 disbursements) -- unanimous
- **Thank you letters approved** to Christina Patterson, the Garden Club, and Shane (Mayor Smith's husband) for planter donations and maintenance -- unanimous
- **Sole source contract approved** for Rundle Construction to repair the Pan-American/Stewart Ditch crossing -- unanimous. Project total estimated at ~$173,000 (pipe already purchased ~$60K, Rundle quote $72,500, plus concrete/headwall work TBD). Will require a budget amendment before year-end.
- **IT managed services contract approved** -- board authorized administrator to negotiate and execute a 3-year agreement with Astonish (formerly "Ice Astonish"), effective December 1, 2025, with option for two 1-year renewals. Unanimous.
- **Planning commission appointment deferred** -- sole applicant Carrie Sodto withdrew due to potential recusal conflicts. Position remains open.

### Discussion Highlights

- **Outdoor instrument donation:** Pia Sharpenberg offered to donate Cascade Bells (outdoor instrument by Freenote Harmony) to the town. Staff will put it on a future agenda with full information including a video demo. Trustees want outreach to nearby business owners and residents about noise/location concerns before deciding. Possible locations: Poulos Park area, River Park, Town Park.
- **Administrator's report (Stefan):** Departmental scorecard updated; ADA transition plan survey active (including targeted outreach to mobility-impaired residents); meter audit ongoing with a 16-month non-functional meter on Grand Avenue replaced with radio-read; code rewrite contract amendment coming (budget jumping from $38K to $75K, not yet ready for board); lead service line inventory had to restart because surveys asked residents about the wrong location (house entry vs. 3 feet from meter pit), and the state lost funding for pot-holing so the town will do it themselves with a newly acquired vac trailer; proposed new full-time front desk position to free up existing staff for utility billing and permit coordination.
- **Public works report (Nikki):** Town running 100% off temporary water tank while 2 million gallon tank is stripped and rehabbed (hole cut, interior work underway). Lambert Mesa Drive water line realignment at 90% design; homeowner meeting went well, easement concerns and irrigation line conflicts being worked through. Water leak on Omega Road from pinholing pipe (line scheduled for replacement). New curb at White Bank completed by in-house crew learning concrete work. All Fourth Street school crosswalks repainted before cold weather. Three staff now certified flaggers. Park cameras installed (town hall, park, water/wastewater plants); vandalism down at gazebo since art installation and timed lighting. Playground chips completed at Lee's trailer park. Yard waste: meeting with company to potentially eliminate multiple weekly trips to Austin. Stewart Ditch patched but needs permanent repair (covered under sole source agenda item).
- **Rightwater Engineers hydrogeological study presentation (Jonathan and Claire/Clara Brad):** Phase 1 findings on the town's spring-fed water supply. Key points:
  - The core problem is seasonality: springs produce enough water annually but peak flow doesn't align with peak demand, and there's no way to capture/store the surplus.
  - Three spring types identified: fracture-controlled (best for town -- natural underground storage, flat hydrograph), contact springs, and gravity springs.
  - Lake Fork Spring behaves like a surface stream (peaky, no attenuation) and is legally limited -- town gets only 1/8 of first 4 CFS, further reduced by consumptive use to as low as 0.17-0.21 CFS depending on month.
  - Old Original Spring is the most promising: nearly flat year-round flow (~0.9 CFS), likely fracture-controlled, but has a tiny surface drainage basin suggesting significant subsurface contribution.
  - German Creek Springs have the most development potential -- 4 CFS cumulative legal right, currently collecting far less. Geophysical survey found shallow saturated zone near German Creek Spring #2 where no spring box currently exists.
  - 2D resistivity survey completed (6 of 9 planned transects; 3 couldn't be done due to brush, standing water, terrain). Results show promising shallow water at German Creek, limited potential at Lake Fork.
  - All current spring boxes and survey transects are within 500 ft of decreed points of diversion (important for legal compliance).
  - Strong recommendation: legal water supply analysis is critical for Phase 2 -- water rights are complex and will be more strictly administered as demand grows. The 80/20 splitter box arrangement further reduces available supply.
  - Transit losses in pipelines unknown (could be 5% or 50%) -- Phase 2 should assess infrastructure condition and hydraulic capacity.
  - Climate change impact on spring yield is a real concern, especially if water tables drop below spring box capture depth.
  - Trustees and staff discussed need for weather station(s) near actual collection basins rather than relying on distant stations; existing Snowtel stations miss lower-elevation snow loss.
- **IT RFQ process:** 10 qualified proposals received (most in 15+ years). Scoring panel included technically capable staff across departments. Astonish scored highest, Silicon Plains second. Astonish has CJIS certification (current provider lacks this for police systems). Astonish representatives introduced themselves: CEO Annette Kintana, client partner Clinton, and local contact Andy Russell (based in Parachute).
- **Short-term rental ad hoc committee:** Update deferred; committee waiting for Mayor Pro Tem Schwarz to return to lead.
- **Zoning Board of Adjustments** still has an open position; applicants don't need to live in town. Applications due Nov 5 to town clerk.

### Public Comment

- **Pia Sharpenberg** (Hotchkiss resident, seasonal farmer): Offered to donate Cascade Bells outdoor instrument to the town. Has the permanent-mount version, friends willing to install on a concrete slab. Open to any location the town prefers.

### Notable Quotes

- **Jonathan (Rightwater Engineers):** "The legal supply is as important as the physical supply. If you don't have both, you're not going to get water delivered to your customers."
- **Jonathan on infrastructure:** "What does it matter if you gain an additional 10% in supply if you're losing 30% of it trying to deliver it to your treatment plant?"
- **Mayor Smith:** "The little town of Paonia has the most complicated water system in the west and we are tackling it the best we can and getting good information... it's a geologic conundrum indeed."
