## Summary

### Key Decisions
- **Approved change order for Solid Solution Geosciences (SSG) LLC** -- Paonia Water Project #01800-00100_CO1A, adding Task 50 (observation of repairs) for $7,200. Motion passed unanimously (no opposed votes heard).

### Discussion Highlights
- **Water plant conditions at takeover:** When SSG (Brianna and Josh) took over operations, the Lamborn plant had a foot of water in the back room, numerous unidentifiable leaks, all alarms disabled, and chlorine set to an illegal level by the previous operator. The chlorine violation will appear on the January monitoring report.
- **Thiosulfate pump failure:** The sodium thiosulfate pump (which neutralizes chlorine before discharge to the river) failed, resulting in a chlorine discharge to the river -- a daily maximum permit violation, also appearing on the January report. SSG repaired it and reported to the state.
- **Scope creep on repairs:** SSG's original contract did not include repairs, but urgent repair calls kept coming. SSG emphasized they are operators, not electricians, excavators, or pipefitters, and requested the $7,200 change order to cover out-of-scope repair observation work already performed.
- **Filter Tech involvement:** Filter Tech, the firm that designed the plants, had two people on-site for a full day (~$1,800 including parts). They are reviewing SSG's repair matrix to provide cost estimates. Billing rates around $105/person.
- **Staffing crisis acknowledged:** Multiple speakers noted JDS Hydro had warned months ago that at least three people were needed for water operations alone. The board acknowledged culpability for not providing adequate staffing. One person was never going to be sufficient to manage three plants.
- **Previous operator (Travis):** Had reportedly been failing his ORC-B certification for three years. Multiple speakers suggested accountability but Brianna cautioned against blaming one person given the impossible workload. She suggested leaving any licensing questions to the state.
- **Clark Plant:** State records show it as offline and non-functional, not approved to distribute water. SSG's scope does not include bringing it back online. A resident complained that being on the upper (Lamborn) plant instead of the lower (Clark) plant causes dangerously high water pressure (85-90 psi) in town.
- **Costs context:** Trustee Smith noted Travis's total compensation was $113,000/year and asked how close current contractor spending is to that amount. Town administrator Corinne noted JDS and SGM have been providing advice at no additional cost given the severity of the situation.

### Public Comment
- **Thomas Mark** (advisory water committee member): Asked pointed questions about chlorination dangers and plant failures affecting public and environmental health. Demanded to know what the board intends to do about town management, stating failures were "entirely preventable" and caused by "bad management."
- **Suzanne Watson**: Said the public had been warning about these problems for years, particularly Travis failing his tests. Accused the board of protecting Travis and being "blind" to the issues. Called for scrutiny of the town administrator's qualifications. Also raised concerns about Clark Plant being offline and high water pressure in town.
- **Michelle Patterson**: Called the situation "infuriating" and demanded full transparency -- "I want to see all the numbers, I want to see the emails, I want to see reports back and forth to the state" -- saying it's the only way to rebuild public trust.
- **Bill Bruno**: Said the board has "willingly suspended critical thought" regarding town management and has been "actively deceived." Asked why the Clark Plant was excluded from the contract.

### Notable Quotes
- **Brianna (SSG):** "When we finally got in there, the chlorine level had been turned down to an illegal level by the previous operator. I don't know why -- I don't know if it was short on chlorine or an accident."
- **Brianna (SSG):** "There is no way one person or two people even could manage three different plants. It's not possible."
- **Trustee Dave Balkan:** "We as a board have not actually physically been able to go to these plants and see... We are culpable as a board about not providing the adequate staffing."
- **Michelle Patterson:** "I want to see all the numbers. I want to see the emails. I want to see reports back and forth to the state. I think all of that needs to be made public if you have any hope of winning back any trust from your community at this point."
