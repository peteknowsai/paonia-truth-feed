## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Regular Meeting — January 12, 2021 (Virtual)

### Key Decisions
- **Agenda approved** — motion carried unanimously
- **Disbursements approved (12/11/20–12/30/20)** — $48,833.85 accounts payable plus payroll transfers; motion carried unanimously
- **Disbursements approved (12/30/20–1/08/21)** — $140,589.04 accounts payable, $95,000 summit-to-operating transfer, $86,918.95 water loan payment; motion carried unanimously
- **Resolution 2021-01 (Public Posting)** — Designated town hall display box and Delta County Independent as official posting locations; passed unanimously
- **Tree Board appointments** — Paula Martin, Greg Hodginger, and Mariah Woolery appointed; passed unanimously (Roger Barrel not reappointed)
- **Water Advisory Committee term limits** — Public Works Committee (Tammy and Bill) to draw random 1/2/3-year staggered terms, terms to begin/end in August, results reported at next meeting; passed unanimously
- **Dark Sky lighting ordinance** — Referred back to committee (Dark Skies committee with Karen, Dave, police, public works) for further drafting; passed unanimously
- **Consent agenda approved** — Three sets of minutes and one liquor license renewal; passed unanimously
- **Executive session entered** — For town administrator six-month review (personnel, CRS 24-6-402-4f) and attorney conference re: Eric Pace v. Town of Paonia (CRS 24-6-402-4b)

### Discussion Highlights
- **Public comment policy debate:** Multiple speakers (Charles Markle, Paige Smith, Suzanne Watson) pushed back on the one-comment-per-agenda-item rule. Paige cited Resolution 2017-10 Section 8, which requires the mayor to open the floor for public comment after board discussion of each item, contradicting Mayor Bachran's earlier claim that state law only requires comment at the start. Suzanne Watson advocated for "round robin" polling of board members before motions are made, citing Jurassic Parliament methodology.
- **Agenda format:** Paige Smith noted the resolution-mandated "Unfinished Business" and "New Business" categories had been replaced with "Regular Business" without formal board action.
- **COVID update:** Delta County still in orange/high risk. Total deaths rose to 35 (4 reported the previous day). Two-week positive count up 6.4% with 385 positive results.
- **Health plan loss:** Rocky Mountain health plan will not continue; town actively seeking quotes from governmental health agencies, United, Humana.
- **Dark Sky ordinance (first read):** Adapted from Ridgway's certified ordinance. Key unresolved issues: enforcement mechanism, security lighting exemptions (banks have federal requirements), grandfathering/10-year compliance timeline, property transfer rules, financial assistance fund for compliance. Dave noted it could be a tourism driver.
- **Skating rink proposal:** New resident Joan Hunt (220 N Fork Ave, moved from Santa Fe) proposed a simple community skating rink for winter recreation, especially for kids/teens.
- **Wastewater issues:** Civil penalty assessed for failure to timely report sample results; corrective actions taken. Ammonia levels rising in cold weather due to dropping nitrification and rising sludge levels (28-30% capacity).
- **Infrastructure updates:** Brown Hill completing programming at 1M gallon water plant (remote access); bulk water fill station shipped; high school meter installation pushed to February; Poulos Park info sign installation imminent (CDOT grant funded).
- **Police report:** New vehicle ordered after special meeting, expected next week. Officers doing increased traffic enforcement and specialized training. Sidewalk snow removal enforcement shifting from mailed letters to direct red-tag notices for faster compliance. No penalty assessment system yet — violations currently require court summons. Michelle suggested fines fund town sidewalk clearing.
- **CDOT grant success:** Mayor presented town's street painting project to CDOT/Downtown Colorado Inc. as representative of small communities statewide. Grant also funding wayfinding sign, benches, Poulos Park upgrades. New CDOT grant with potential sidewalk funding being investigated.
- **Tree City USA:** Paonia declared Tree City USA for the 14th consecutive year.
- **Committee updates:** Government Affairs/Public Safety hosting "Let's Talk STRs" (short-term rentals) zoom Thursday at 6pm. Still working on accessory dwelling unit ordinance with attorney. Public Works meeting scheduled for Jan 20.

### Public Comment
- **Charles Markle** — Called for transparency over trust as the 2021 guiding principle, citing the Kristen Chesnik situation. Praised Neil (police chief) as a model of working transparently via body/car cameras. Asked board to reconsider public input restrictions.
- **Paige Smith (215 Delta Ave)** — Cited Resolution 2017-10 to challenge the one-comment rule. Requested return to "Unfinished/New Business" agenda format. Praised inclusion of written staff reports and agenda links.
- **Joan Hunt (220 N Fork Ave)** — Proposed a community skating rink. Directed to email Karen with formal proposal.
- **Suzanne Watson (903 2nd St)** — Criticized the practice of trustees making motions immediately on agenda items before discussion (specifically the water committee appointments). Advocated round-robin discussion before motions. Referenced Jurassic Parliament (Ann McFarlane) as a resource.
- **Thomas (Charles Markle?)** — Provided Ridgway's dark sky enforcement details (building official, nuisance classification, building permit denial for non-compliance). Also flagged that the 72-hour vehicle parking limit may only apply to restricted vehicles, not regular cars. Noted July 14 and July 28, 2020 minutes on the website contain identical content.

### Notable Quotes

> "I'm asking that in 2021 our goal be transparency rather than trust. That's what was given to Kristen Chesnik and we know exactly what happened with that level of trust." — Charles Markle

> "This person just grabbed the ball and ran for the goal and everybody was like... it's really startling and a little off-putting. It doesn't give people a chance to find out where we're all at before these motions are made." — Suzanne Watson, on premature motions

> "If you want to see the difference between downtown and a really dark street, come over to the 300 block of Orchard at night. We have no street lights and we have spectacular sky viewing. We do not want to lose that." — Trustee Tammy, on dark sky preservation
