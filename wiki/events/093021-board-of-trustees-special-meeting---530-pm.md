## Summary

### Key Decisions
- **Motion to approve letter of support for The Nature Connection**: Passed unanimously (no opposed votes recorded). Letter includes $1,500/year cash contribution (2022 and 2023, $3,000 total) and in-kind support for hazardous tree removal on town property sections of the trail.

### Discussion Highlights
- **Herbicide language removed**: A line stating the town would apply herbicides was deleted from the letter because the town does not have a licensed person to do that work.
- **$1,500 commitment sourced from parks budget**: The cash contribution will come from the existing parks budget.
- **Letter personalization**: Ben Graves noted the letter was a template used for multiple towns/cities and needed to be personalized for Paonia. Tammy did copy editing (commas, punctuation, sentence order -- no content changes). References to "city" needed to be changed to "town."
- **Blank in the letter**: Bill flagged a blank space in the letter. Corin (likely the administrator) suggested filling it with "parks, fields, and playgrounds." Ben's edited version used "many parks, fields, and playgrounds." Board had no strong preference on exact wording.
- **In-kind support (TBD)**: The in-kind contribution was left as "TBD" because it covers hazardous tree removal on town-owned trail sections -- costs can't be predicted in advance.
- **Grant contingency**: The contribution is contingent on Colorado Parks and Wildlife board approving the grant. If approved, The Nature Connection would receive ~$5,000/week (~$15,000/year) for trail work on the Paonia section, River Park, Western Colorado Conservation Center section, and school district section. If not approved, Ben indicated he'd return to the board to request the funds for a smaller trail crew.
- **Multi-year budget commitment question**: Michelle asked whether the board can commit funds across multiple years. Corin confirmed it's within the board's discretion and that $1,500 falls within administrator approval authority, so it would remain as a line item for both 2022 and 2023.
- **Michelle requested staff compile a list** of all in-kind and monetary support the board has committed to going into 2022. Staff agreed.

### Public Comment
- No formal public comment period recorded in this transcript. (This appears to have been a special meeting with a single agenda item.)

### Notable Quotes
- **Ben Graves** (The Nature Connection): "I was using this as a template for a bunch of the other towns and cities we're working with and so I was hoping that whatever we end up submitting was personalized to Paonia."
- **Corin** (on in-kind support): "I put TBD because we don't know if a hazard tree falls and that's a contribution that would be to be determined, but just stating that the town is comfortable with that responsibility."
- **Michelle**: "Would it be possible for staff to put together a list of the in-kind and monetary support that the board has committed to going into 2022, just so we can see where we're at overall?"

**Note**: The recording started late -- the beginning of the meeting was not captured. The mayor provided a brief verbal summary of the missed discussion at the start of the recording. This appears to be a special meeting (single agenda item) likely from late 2021 or early 2022.
