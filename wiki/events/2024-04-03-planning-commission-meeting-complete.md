## Summary

## Paonia Planning Commission Meeting Summary

**Date:** April 5, 2024 (based on context -- two weeks before April 17 comment deadline, April 23 Board of Trustees meeting)

**Commissioners Present:** Chair Neilson, Vice Chair Bachran, Secretary How, Commissioner Suzette, Commissioner Watson

---

### Key Decisions

- **Approval of March 13, 2024 Planning Commission minutes** -- passed unanimously
- **Rezoning application RZ 24-01 (215 North Fork Ave)** -- Motion to recommend approval of rezoning three lots from Light Industrial (I-1) and Community Commercial (C2) to Low Density Residential (R-1), conditioned on favorable determination from Board of Trustees. Passed with one dissent (Commissioner Suzette/How opposed, preferring C2 designation). Board of Trustees hearing scheduled April 23.
- **Special Review SRV 24-01 (130 Grand Ave / "The Hive")** -- Motion to recommend approval of change of use from tenant community space to cocktail lounge/bar ("Fiona"), with conditions: off-street parking agreement with town, future non-by-right uses require separate special review, parking calculations adjusted for future tenants, improvements completed by October 23, 2024, and favorable Board of Trustees determination. Passed unanimously by roll call (Bachran aye, How aye, Watson aye, Neilson aye).
- **No formal action on comprehensive plan elements** -- Economic Development and Governance/Community Participation draft elements presented for public comment; written comments accepted through April 17, 2024.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Comprehensive Plan -- Economic Development element:** Presented by Marissa (Phoenix Rising Resources). Vision centers on resilient, localized economy emphasizing local food production, health/wellbeing, creative industries, and tourism that supports locals. Key data: median household income below state/national levels, 1 in 5 households housing-cost burdened, 48% of population 55+. Recommendations include local economic development strategy, downtown beautification, improved food truck/popup regulations, potential annexation along Highway 133, and housing/short-term rental regulation.
- **CSU Extension presentation (Libby Christon via Zoom):** Highlighted state resources for food-based entrepreneurship, resilient food system infrastructure grants through Colorado Dept of Agriculture, Regional Food Business Development Center, and READY Institute for economic impact analysis.
- **Creative Coalition comment (Jess Dervan-Arriman):** North Fork Valley Creative Coalition applied for OEDIT business preservation funding -- scored well but not selected. Noted $500K in unused Just Transition funds available for coal-transitioning communities (Delta County is Tier 2 eligible, no applications in two years).
- **Highway 133 annexation concerns:** David Merrick cautioned against allowing commercial development on 133 that would compete with and hollow out the downtown core, referencing prior 133 overlay committee work.
- **Commissioner concerns on economic development:** Commissioner How raised concerns about density vs. rural/agricultural character preservation within town limits, potential ageism in "younger workforce" language, and questioned whether arts/agriculture economy is realistic long-term. Chair Neilson noted nonprofits as an underrepresented economic sector and suggested prioritization of action items.
- **Governance element:** Short section focused on building public trust, implementing adopted plans, transparent budgeting, volunteerism, and regional cooperation. Chair Neilson emphasized the importance of intergovernmental relations, noting past reputation problems ("What's wrong with Paonia?") and praising recent improvements like TextMyGov notifications.
- **Rezoning debate (215 North Fork Ave):** Staff recommended approval -- property is former bulk fuel storage site (cleaned up under Colorado Cleanup Act), mid-block on North Fork Ave surrounded by residential uses. Commissioner Suzette/How argued the lots should stay C2 to preserve commercial/live-work potential and avoid shrinking the town's commercial inventory. Staff and other commissioners countered that the actual use of the street is residential and the rezoning conforms to the 1996 comprehensive plan.
- **Bar/cocktail lounge debate (130 Grand Ave):** Extensive public support for the bar. Multiple speakers described downtown as "dead" on evenings and weekends. Primary sticking point was off-street parking requirements -- town code has restrictive requirements with a broken fee-in-lieu provision. Staff recommended a parking agreement similar to one used for Blue Sage. Commissioner How advocated eliminating parking requirements entirely for the downtown commercial district, citing a calculation that full compliance would require 2.5 acres of parking.

---

### Public Comment

- **Jess Dervan-Arriman** (Creative Coalition): Urged economic development collaboration between town and nonprofits; identified untapped state funding for coal transition communities
- **David Merrick:** Warned against Highway 133 commercial development that would compete with downtown businesses, citing prior overlay committee work
- **Sharon Kim** (227 North Fork Ave, adjacent to rezoning property): Testified about noise nuisance from laundromat dryer vents running until 11pm, lint smell, and high-pitched whistling -- questioned whether anyone would want to build a residence that close
- **Jeff Thompson** (315 Buck Selder): Supported rezoning as consistent with housing goals discussed at prior meeting
- **Robin Arthur** (124 Grand Ave gallery owner): Strongly supported the bar -- described downtown as shuttered and dead on weekends; said tourists constantly ask where to get a cocktail and she has to say "no, no, and no"
- **Karen Goodwin** (Bark & Bargains board): Supported the bar; argued parking enforcement is inconsistently applied and the town should provide public parking rather than penalizing new businesses
- **Julie Bennett** (co-owner, applicant): Said they were told by former town staff that parking would not be an issue before purchasing the property; did not get it in writing
- **Lori Mitchell:** Praised the building renovation, supported the bar as job creation and an outlet for local wineries
- **Lori American** (55 Pan American Ave): Vouched for the applicants' credibility based on Root & Vine's success

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Robin Arthur** (gallery owner on Grand Ave): "I cannot tell you how many disappointed tourists and locals come into my lonely little shop on Saturday afternoon and say 'where can we get a cocktail, is there anything to do?' and I have to say no, no, and no."

- **Commissioner How** on parking reform: "A very respected resident did some computations and she said if every business in town complied with the parking ordinance we would need two and a half acres of parking. We don't have that. I think that's one of the biggest ordinances we need to change in order to bring more economic advantage to town."

- **Chair Neilson** on intergovernmental relations: "I sat down at a table with our County Commissioners and some state representatives and out of their mouths the first thing they said is 'What's wrong with Paonia?' So there was an impression that we were very dysfunctional, that we didn't work well with others."
