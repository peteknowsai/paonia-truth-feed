## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** February 2021 (exact date not stated; references suggest late February based on context about March 6 vaccination and Feb 25 Paonia in Motion session)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved with Item 11 (Resolution 2021-02, amending 2020 budget) removed** -- passed unanimously
- **Disbursements approved** -- accounts payable $22,131.49, payroll taxes $1,692.92, payroll direct deposit $21,263.55 -- passed unanimously
- **Skid steer broom attachment purchased** from Hanan Equipment for $98.17 (appears to be partial figure) -- passed unanimously
- **Demolition of decommissioned 500,000-gallon water tank tabled** to next meeting pending water reserve fund analysis -- passed unanimously
- **Marijuana ordinance: one shop per block (both sides of street), retail and/or medicinal** -- passed (vote count not specified)
- **Marijuana ordinance: delivery allowed** with same regulations as liquor delivery -- passed
- **Marijuana ordinance overall: additional readings required** -- consensus to bring back revised version rather than vote on first reading
- **ADU ordinance tabled** to next meeting due to time constraints
- **Resolution 2021-03 (liquor license encroachment modification)** approved with minor language fix -- passed unanimously
- **Consent agenda approved** -- passed unanimously

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **COVID-19:** Delta County planning mass vaccination of 1,000 people for March 6 (registration required). County moved to "blue" on COVID dial, meaning in-person meetings may resume. Mayor called for moment of silence marking 500,000 U.S. deaths.
- **Town job posting:** Suzanne Watson noted a town employment listing was advertised in print but missing from the town website. Administrator Corinne Ferguson said a linking issue was corrected that morning.
- **Lead in drinking water:** Six of 20 December samples exceeded lead levels. Key distinction: samples were from private exterior taps after water sat in pipes, not from the public distribution system. Town is resampling; anticipates no exceedance. Three blocks have possible lead pigtail service lines (200 Main, 200 and 300 Orchard) -- all tested below detectable limits. Notification language follows CDPHE-mandated templates.
- **DMEA franchise fee:** Treasurer Ross King and attorney Bo Nerlin recommended no immediate action on taxing franchise fees as part of gross revenue. The 2001 franchise agreement (20-year term) is expiring, providing an opportunity to renegotiate and redefine "gross revenue" to potentially exclude franchise fees. Suzanne Watson argued the town should define gross revenue in its ordinance to exclude franchise fees, citing Fruita's model.
- **Marijuana ordinance (major discussion):**
  - Working group (Geraldo, Dale Thiesel, Tom Jacobs, Tom Scudder, Corinne, Chief Ferguson) presented draft
  - Extensive debate on limiting number of shops -- moved from a flat cap of 6 to "one per block" after working group members argued the market would self-limit to 1-2 survivors
  - License transfer policy: mirrors liquor license process; new owner must apply and be vetted
  - Distance from schools reduced to 500 feet (matching liquor)
  - Delivery approved with same rules as liquor
  - Residency requirement discussed but not adopted (state already requires 1-year Colorado residency)
  - Public commenters raised concerns about "establishment" vs. "store" language (ballot said stores; ordinance used broader "establishment" terminology)
  - $5/transaction excise tax criticized as unusually high; working group member Jer acknowledged it "needs to be revisited"
  - Board consensus: needs more public review, will have additional readings
- **Riverbank subdivision:** Moving toward Board hearing (earliest April). Applicant aware no certificates of occupancy until water tap moratorium lifted. Between 7 and 15 units proposed.
- **Accessory dwelling units:** Attorney presented draft ADU ordinance; flagged connection to potential short-term rental regulations. Tabled.
- **School meter installation:** Line not where as-built drawings indicated; may need to wait until school's out if line is under asphalt.
- **Short-term rental survey:** Board asked to review and send comments; goal is to publish via SurveyMonkey with 10 or fewer questions.
- **Dark Skies ordinance:** Clarification that the citizens committee (not internal staff) is responsible for implementing suggestions and bringing revised ordinance back to the board.
- **Trustee letter-signing policy:** Attorney advised trustees can use their title on letters as individuals, but should avoid signing on issues likely to come before the board. No formal policy recommended; suggested incorporating into board training instead.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson:** Criticized limited advertising for town job posting; argued franchise fees should be excluded from gross revenue definition (cited Fruita's ordinance language); raised multiple concerns about marijuana ordinance including zoning code placement, "establishment" vs. "store" language, and notification requirements
- **Christina Patterson:** Asked about Riverbank subdivision status -- confirmed still 7-15 units, going to planning commission per timeline in packet
- **Sharon Kind:** Complained about noise from laundromat dryer vents on Second Street; directed to call Delta County dispatch
- **Paige (last name unclear):** Pointed out ballot language said "stores" but ordinance uses "establishments," which under Colorado law includes cultivation, testing, and manufacturing facilities -- broader than what voters approved
- **Stephen Keenan:** Urged the town to use marijuana legalization as a PR/branding opportunity; referenced Paonia's appearance in High Times in the 1970s; warned about Boulder's experience with homelessness following legalization
- **Skyler (last name unclear):** Strongly recommended limiting number of licenses based on experience in cannabis industry across multiple states; also criticized the $5/transaction excise tax as excessively high and likely to hamper sales

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Mayor (on COVID deaths):** "This is more people who have died from this than everyone who was killed in World War I, World War II, and in the Vietnam War together. In one year we have passed this mark."

- **Jer (working group member, on shop limits):** "You only get to be the town of Paonia opening up for dispensaries one time. You only get to do that once."

- **Bill Bettinger (on unlimited shops):** "Six licenses in a C1 and C2 district in Paonia has the ability to basically gut Main Street. You put six marijuana businesses on Main Street, what are you going to have left?"
