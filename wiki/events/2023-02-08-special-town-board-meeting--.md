## Summary: Special Town Board Meeting — February 8, 2023

### Key Decisions
- **Approval of agenda:** Passed unanimously (voice vote)
- **Motion to enter executive session** pursuant to CRS 24-6-402(4)(b) and (4)(f) — legal advice and personnel matter: Passed unanimously
- **Motion to grant interim administrator authority to hire and terminate, and to instruct the attorney to amend the contract accordingly:** Passed with one abstention (Trustee who cited difficulty hearing on Zoom and lack of access to the personnel manual)

### Discussion Highlights
- Meeting called to order with Pledge of Allegiance, roll call confirming all trustees present
- The board moved almost immediately into **executive session** for two purposes: (1) conferencing with the town attorney for legal advice on specific legal questions, and (2) discussing a personnel matter concerning a town employee who had not requested the matter be discussed in open meeting
- Town Attorney Nick Bates advised that the discussion constituted **privileged attorney-client communication** and recommended no further record be kept beyond the required recording
- Executive session lasted approximately from 5:02 PM to 5:19 PM
- Upon return to open session, the board voted to **expand the interim town administrator's authority** to include hiring and firing, and directed the attorney to amend the administrator's contract accordingly
- A trustee clarified that the authority was **effective immediately**, not contingent on the contract amendment being completed first

### Public Comment
- No public comment period is discernible in this transcript

### Notable Quotes
- **Trustee (abstaining):** "My abstension was because it was very difficult to hear on Zoom and I don't have a copy of a personnel manual and I'm somewhat jaundiced about the defense of that... if there is an avenue for the employee to be able to appeal and I don't have the document in front of me, so I abstained."
- **Town Attorney (on executive session recording):** "As Town Attorney it's my opinion that the discussion of the matter announced in the motion to go into executive session constitutes a privileged attorney-client communication. I am therefore recommending that no further record be kept of this executive session."
