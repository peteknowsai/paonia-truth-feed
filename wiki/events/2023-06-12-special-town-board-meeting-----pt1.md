## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees -- June 12, 2023

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- motion passed unanimously
- **Executive session entered** -- pursuant to C.R.S. 24-6-402(4)(e) and (g), for interviews and discussion of town administrator candidates. Passed unanimously.
- **All four finalist candidates to be brought for in-person interviews** -- Kevin, Stephan, Angela, and Patrick. Board reached consensus (no formal vote in executive session).
- **Tentative interview schedule set** -- candidates fly in June 19, tours/activities June 20, open house evening of June 20, board interviews June 21
- **Citizen panel format changed** -- instead of a selected committee, the public will be invited to submit questions in advance; an MC (Leslie, the interim administrator) will consolidate and ask them at a community open house
- **Staff to handle logistics** -- arranging rooms, coordinating travel for candidates

### Discussion Highlights

- **Second-round interview with Patrick Marsh** -- the board conducted a lengthy Zoom interview covering grant writing (claims ~75 grants, ~70% success rate, including a $25M Biden infrastructure grant for Fernley, NV), emergency management (NIMS-certified, served as emergency management director in three communities), department head management, law enforcement relations, affordable/workforce housing, organizational change, water/wastewater experience, and professional accomplishments
- **Candidate assessments** -- Patrick was the clear frontrunner among board members. Angela "fell down" in her second interview. Stephan was praised for doing extensive research on Paonia and Delta County. Kevin was seen as worth including to keep the field broad.
- **Concern about Patrick's personality fit** -- one trustee noted Patrick has the right experience but wondered if his personality would mesh with current staff
- **Leslie (interim administrator) advised bringing all four** -- warned that candidates drop out and urged the board not to "cheap out" after investing so much time
- **Board's top priorities for new administrator**: water/sewer system (millions in needed improvements, grants in progress, one water plant not operating), a ~$1M CDOT grant that needs someone to push the state forward, and professional leadership/honest advice to the board
- **Citizen panel controversy** -- Trustee Rick pushed back hard against hand-selecting a citizen committee, citing transparency concerns and referencing a problematic housing task force appointment process. The board pivoted to an open question-submission format.
- **Previous citizen panel issues** -- last time, an influential community member reportedly dominated the citizen panel discussion, making it "a useless exercise"
- **Scheduling difficulties** -- CML conference (June 25-28), trustee travel conflicts, and the July 4th holiday complicated scheduling. The board settled on June 19-21 as the target with July 5-7 as backup.
- **Budget critique** -- Leslie bluntly called Paonia's 2022 budget "one of the worst budgets I've ever seen," suggesting the interview exercise should ask candidates how they'd restructure it as an informational document

### Public Comment

- No public comment period in this session (executive session plus working discussion)

### Notable Quotes

- **Leslie (interim administrator) on the budget**: "Your budget is awful. It's one of the worst budgets I've ever seen."
- **Trustee Rick on what he needs in an administrator**: "I want to be told when I'm screwing up because I don't have the expertise. The professional advice is the biggest thing for me."
- **Patrick Marsh on honesty with elected officials**: "Sometimes you're not going to like the things I tell you, and that's fine. At the end of the day we're not going to be any worse off for it, but I'm gonna tell you things factually and I'm gonna give you my best advice."
- **Leslie on why to bring all four candidates**: "You're not going to run out of money. You've got plenty of reserves. Why cheap out on it?"
