## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees & Planning Commission Joint Work Session

**Date:** Early 2021 (references to March 11 upcoming, 2018 building codes, 2020 ordinances)
**Format:** Zoom
**Presiding:** Mary (Mayor Bachran)

---

### Key Decisions

- No formal votes taken -- this was a work session only
- **Consensus** to skip page-by-page review and focus on major issues for the building code update
- **Consensus/buy-in** on the five-part structure of the master plan update (outlined on page 10 of the master plan document)
- **Consensus** that Tammy would do an editorial pass on the master plan draft to clean up factual errors, grammar, and readability

---

### Discussion Highlights

**Building Code Update (Chapter 18 -- Municipal Code)**

- Dan (building code consultant) walked through appendix chapters of the 2018 IBC and IRC, recommending which to adopt and which to skip
- **IBC appendices recommended to adopt:** B (board of appeals, with modifications for small-town qualifications), C (agricultural buildings -- allows larger greenhouses without sprinklers), I (patio covers), J (grading/site development)
- **IBC appendices recommended NOT to adopt:** A (employee qualifications -- would prevent hiring less-credentialed building officials), D (fire districts), F (rodent proofing), G (floodplain -- already covered in municipal code), K (administrative provisions), L (earthquake instrumentation), M (tsunami), N (replicable buildings -- town has formula business ordinance)
- **IBC appendix left to board discretion:** E (supplementary accessibility/ADA requirements) and H (signs)
- **IRC appendices recommended to adopt:** B (venting for boilers/furnaces), F (radon gas mitigation -- get components in place during construction), H (patio covers), J (existing buildings), K (sound transmission for duplexes), M (home day care -- prevents onerous state requirements like mandatory sprinklers), Q (tiny homes), R (light straw clay construction), S (straw bale construction)
- **IRC appendix left to board discretion:** T (solar ready provisions -- requires roof capacity for PV and conduit pathways; Lucy noted the town's own Chapter 18 already has solar access protection language)
- Michelle proposed replacing specific dollar amounts for fines/fees with "set by resolution" language throughout, so the board can adjust without amending the code
- Michelle flagged that the 600 sq ft minimum home size (Section 18-550-B) would conflict with ADU plans and should be removed
- Lucy raised concerns about site plan requirements (items 7-9) being excessive for small projects like sheds over 200 sq ft
- Lucy recommended specifying a minimum 25 psf roof snow load in addition to the 33 psf ground snow load
- Dave flagged that requiring engineered foundations for all habitable structures could be burdensome for ADUs
- Dan noted the town has never had an appeals board meeting in 20 years of Delta County experience; suggested a regional appeals board drawing from multiple jurisdictions
- Town attorney is drafting board of appeals language, expected as early as the next meeting

**Recurring theme -- items misplaced in Chapter 18:**
- Suzanne repeatedly argued that signs, steep slope development, solar access protection, site plan review, and fences/hedges/walls were moved from the zoning code (Chapter 16) into the building code (Chapter 18) roughly four years ago and don't belong there
- She recommended restoring them to Chapter 16 (land use) where proper due process protections (public notice, planning commission review) apply
- Multiple board members encountered confusion from this mixing, supporting Suzanne's point

**Master Plan Update**

- Corinne (Town Administrator) presented the draft, modeled after Ridgway's master plan
- Looking for buy-in on five-part structure and proposed timeline (page 54 of packet)
- Process uses data from community meetings hosted by Evan Bolt the prior year plus 10 years of prior planning work
- Karen noted the draft omitted mining history and Tree City USA designation from early chapters
- Tammy found ~25 factual/editorial issues: incorrect claim that Utes were the only indigenous people in Colorado (Cheyenne, Arapahoe, Shoshone also), "council" used instead of "board of trustees" in five places, capitalization errors
- Monica advocated for housing equality language given proximity to Aspen/Telluride and affordability pressures
- Dave pushed for a climate change section in the plan
- Lucy felt the vision statement was too narrowly focused on "creatives" given the town's broader identity
- Barb objected to the word "quirkiness" to describe residents and opposed fruit trees on Grand Avenue (pest management concerns)
- Suzanne cited CRS 31-23-206 and 31-23-207, urging inclusion of water supply, fire safety, emergency planning, and population distribution elements
- Karen countered that those statutes apply to towns of 10,000+ and that the Delta County report covers some of those elements

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne** (participated throughout): Argued extensively that zoning provisions were improperly moved into the building code by a previous administrator (Ken Knight) and should be restored to Chapter 16. Cited state statutes on master plan requirements. Expressed frustration that her repeated input on these issues has been "ignored." Also raised concerns about fire safety/emergency planning and questioned the $76,000 trails grant spending priorities.

---

### Notable Quotes

**Dan (building code consultant), on employee qualifications appendix:**
> "When you send me down the road, which is hopeful, you want to be able to hire someone that doesn't have all those years of experience and certifications already under their belt. That's how I started -- when I started with Delta I had no certifications at all. I just had a lot of experience."

**Monica, on housing equity:**
> "We're sort of sandwiched in between Aspen and Telluride and we're seeing those communities really struggle with providing equal housing for workers. Just a space for everyone that is in the community instead of just people who have money."

**Corinne (Town Administrator), on the master plan's purpose:**
> "I don't ever want to be in a place where we're reacting or modifying the way that we run the town based on whatever voice is the loudest, and that we're really functioning from a place of what's in the best interest of the town."

---

**Attendance notes:** Bill was out of town and unable to attend. Mick joined late. Barb had technical difficulties connecting. Roll call included planning commission members (Peck, Buttinger, Fogues, Hunter, Mayor Bachran) and trustees (Bear absent, Johnson absent, Knutson, Mech, Pattison). No quorum requirement for work session.
