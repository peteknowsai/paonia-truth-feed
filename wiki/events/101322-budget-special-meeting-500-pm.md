## Summary

## Summary: Paonia Board of Trustees Budget Work Session

**Date:** Appears to be late 2022 (discussing 2023 budget)
**Type:** Budget work session

---

### Key Decisions
- **Agenda approved** -- motion passed, voice vote (all ayes)
- **No final budget votes taken** -- this was a work session; public hearing scheduled for first meeting in November, final approval in December after assessor's numbers arrive
- **Direction given to staff:** Prepare a rate comparison table/spreadsheet showing revenue generated at different base rate and usage rate levels, so trustees and public can see trade-offs

---

### Discussion Highlights
- **Water fund shortfall:** Cindy (finance) reported a projected **$112,000 shortfall** in the 2023 water fund budget. The 2022 shortfall was driven primarily by lower-than-expected usage revenue -- usage came in at ~$216K vs. ~$700K budgeted.
- **Rate increase structure debate:** Trustees debated whether to increase the base rate vs. usage tiers. One trustee argued the 0-1,000 gallon tier effectively functions as a base rate increase (everyone pays it) and should be moved into the actual base rate for transparency. Adding $2.50/month to all ~1,800 users would generate ~$45,000/year -- leaving ~$67,000 still to find.
- **75/25 base-to-usage ratio:** Referenced a prior analysis (Dick Knutson / AWC) recommending 75% of water revenue come from base rates and 25% from usage. The town is currently around 50/50. Grant agencies look at this ratio unfavorably.
- **Usage rate risk:** When rates go up, usage goes down -- so relying on usage increases to close the gap is unreliable.
- **Water company billing:** Discussion about how the water companies (with ~28 service connections) are billed on a master meter, with usage prorated per customer rather than billed tier-by-tier per individual user. This may have contributed to revenue shortfalls.
- **1 million gallon tank / Brownsville plant:** The plant has been offline but the town continues paying ~$20,000/year for a Browns Hill software/hardware subscription. Staff noted the system is still monitoring incoming water and collecting data. Bringing the plant back online is needed before the 2 million gallon tank can be relined.
- **Sewer plant future:** The town must convert from an open lagoon system to an enclosed system (federal requirement). Discussion of artificial marsh/natural treatment as a cheaper alternative to chemical-driven systems. Tertiary treatment will be required for nitrogen, phosphorus, and potentially PFAS ("forever chemicals"). Estimated cost in the millions. Timeline roughly 2027.
- **Staff certification contracts:** Trustee raised concern about protecting the town's investment in employee water/wastewater licensing -- suggested 3-4 year retention agreements with payback clauses.
- **2022 budget reconciliation:** Extensive back-and-forth about why 2022 actual expenditures ($1.23M) were so much lower than budgeted ($2.56M). Answer: the 2 million gallon tank relining project ($1.28M) was budgeted but not executed, and grant revenue that was budgeted was not received. Net result is roughly break-even for 2022 -- no surplus to carry forward.

---

### Public Comment
- **Chris (unidentified last name):** Argued that rate increase justification needs to be broader -- the public needs to see where *all* the money goes, not just water. Trust is gone. Need transparency on whether new trucks or equipment are truly necessary. Called for a five-year and ten-year plan.
- **Unidentified woman:** Said she never hears the board talk about saving money, only about increasing revenue. Asked the board to consider being more frugal. Also noted past rate increases were never followed up with public accounting of what the increases actually generated.
- **Christine:** Asked whether the town is fully utilizing Colorado Rural Water Association (free resource). Asked about a comprehensive inventory of water/sewer system assets. Noted an RFP for operations came back at ~$140K and suggested reconfiguring staff assignments to reduce costs.
- **Mr. Robert:** Identified two potential savings: (1) the Browns Hill software subscription (~$20K/year) for the offline 1M gallon plant, and (2) switching from 55-gallon drums to 2.5-gallon totes for chemicals.
- **Mr. Soldier (phonetic):** Cautioned that canceling the Browns Hill subscription would mean a large up-charge later when equipment needs replacing, and the 1M gallon tank is essential for taking the 2M gallon tank offline for relining.
