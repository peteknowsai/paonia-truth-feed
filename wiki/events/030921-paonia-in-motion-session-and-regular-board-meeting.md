## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** March 9, 2021 (based on context clues: COVID vaccination rollout, moratorium references, 2021 resolutions)
**Format:** Zoom (with discussion of returning to in-person meetings)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Western Slope Conservation Center letter of support:** Motion to sign on to letter supporting conservation priorities in the state budget (long bill). **Passed unanimously.**
- **Resolution 2021-03** (amending 2020 budget, general fund -- appropriating COVID/CDOT grant funds): **Passed unanimously.**
- **Resolution 2021-04** (capital improvement fund -- sales tax increase, $500 donation, audit reserves): **Passed unanimously.**
- **Disbursements approved:** $49,211.21 accounts payable, $5,389.41 credit card, $1,680 Norris retirement, $174.98 and $961.63 Amazon, $24,014.26 payroll taxes, $22,213.50 payroll direct deposit. **Passed unanimously.**
- **Consent agenda** (minutes): **Passed unanimously** with minor corrections noted (typos, skid steer amount correction to $9,817).
- **Marijuana ordinance work session** scheduled for Tuesday, March 16 at 5:00 PM, in person.
- **ADU ordinance** referred to Planning Commission for review (no formal vote required).
- **Decommissioned water tank:** No action taken; deferred to future date pending reserve numbers and potential grant funding.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Paonia in Motion (Parks, Recreation & Trails Master Plan):** Matt and Davis Fair of Western Slope Consulting presented project overview. Three phases: Phase 1 (current conditions, wrapping up end of March), Phase 2 (community vision, design workshop end of April, concluding June), Phase 3 (action/implementation plan, concluding August). Green Play doing inventory work; Jim Leggett doing illustration/site plans. Community Action Team guiding outreach. Roughly 60-70 individuals have participated so far.
- **Astro park concept** emerged from community input -- a dedicated stargazing platform away from light pollution. Mayor Bachran called it "novel and creative."
- **School consolidation impact:** Discussion about what happens to the football field in Town Park, the vocational/tech building, and broader community effects of losing the high school. Trustee Tammy Mack raised concerns about economic impact on rural communities losing schools. Board agreed to invite school district representatives to a future meeting, likely summer when plans are more concrete.
- **AARP Community Challenge Grant:** Mayor Bachran writing grant for Town Park upgrades -- sound system, lights, stage upgrades, electrical, Wi-Fi, shaded table by play area, handicapped accessible bathroom. Due April 14.
- **ADU regulations:** Gaps committee recommended 500 sq ft minimum and no parking requirements; draft ordinance had 800 sq ft and one off-street parking space. Board debated both. Dave Knutson suggested capping at 10 units first year as pilot. Discussion about interaction with water tap moratorium -- legal interpretation needed on whether extending existing water lines to convert garages constitutes a moratorium violation.
- **Food truck regulations:** GAPS committee presented recommendations. Key debates: fees (no current business license in town, but food trucks on public property could warrant fees), allowing trucks on Grand Avenue during special events, and ensuring regulations don't undercut brick-and-mortar restaurants. Restaurant owners largely reported being fine with food truck competition.
- **Advisory Water Committee priorities:** Thomas Markle presented top 10 priorities developed via survey of 22 topics. Not ranked by timeline but by importance, as topics are interconnected. Mayor emphasized tap moratorium review and raw water storage as top priorities.
- **Hybrid meeting format:** Michelle Patterson pushed for continued remote participation options even as in-person meetings resume, citing unvaccinated residents. Corrine noted technical challenges of running hybrid meetings with limited staff.
- **Hotchkiss police assistance:** Paonia PD helping Hotchkiss with patrol and traffic enforcement due to short staffing. IGA being drafted by town attorney; will come before board next meeting. Officers volunteering on days off. Hotchkiss will reimburse for officer time and vehicle wear.
- **Tree removal controversy:** A private property owner removed a tree, causing public outcry (many thought the town did it). Tree board meeting Monday to discuss creating an application/permitting process for tree removal and planting, and sending municipal code to tree service companies across the Western Slope.
- **Short-term rental survey:** Finalized, opening April 1, going out with water bills. Data expected back for first May meeting.
- **P Hill access, ditch trail opportunities:** Board discussed converting piped ditches into walking/biking trails (citing Glenwood Springs as precedent). Ditch company outreach planned but president is away.
- **Vaccination clinic announced** for Friday in Hotchkiss, open to eligible groups. Waitlist available for extras from no-shows.

---

### Public Comment

- **Dave Knutson** (trustee, but commenting during work session): Noted no dirt bike/four-wheeler users represented in Paonia in Motion outreach, despite being significant Jumbo Mountain users.
- **Ross King** (town treasurer): Urged consultants to reach out to Pan American Property Owners Association, Vista Creek HOA, and Hawks Haven HOA, all impacted by Jumbo Mountain planning.
- **Suzanne Watson:** Questioned timeline for building department reform, calling current fees "illegal" and "unreasonable." Asked about transparency of attorney's report not in public packet. Asked about food truck fee distinctions between private vs. public property. Asked about ADU process and public comment opportunities.
- **Paige Smith (215 Delta Ave):** Requested staff wear masks properly at in-person meetings. Noted typo in Resolution 2021-03. Requested actual signed/dated bids be included in packets for transparency. Noted incomplete cross-references in ADU ordinance.
- **Thomas Markle:** Argued that the water moratorium explicitly covers extension of water delivery pipes, restricting ADUs. Disagreed with characterization of 500 sq ft as too small, citing personal experience living in 120 sq ft for four years.
- **Ben Katz (Western Slope Conservation Center):** Presented letter of support for state conservation budget priorities including wildlife corridor funding and wildfire/forest health investments. Noted Highway 133 is on the state's wildlife corridor watch list. Mentioned upcoming fence removal pilot project for elk/deer migration.
- **Steve (unclear last name):** Raised safety concerns about school buses on Highway 92 when Little Blue Canyon construction diverts Highway 50 traffic -- speed limits reach 65 mph on winding road.

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Mayor Bachran** on vaccinations: "Please, please register. We want every single one of those vaccines to actually go into people's arms."

- **Matt Fair** on planning: "I worked on a couple plans where you had all these great ideas and it was this great fancy document and then it just didn't go anywhere. What's exciting for me is actually seeing things happen in the community."

- **Trustee Tammy Mack** on the school consolidation: "What happens to a community when it loses its high school? Paonia has lost a lot of jobs, a lot of income through the mines. We've basically pretty much lost the mines and that's been a big blow. We're looking at a drought, we're looking at all kinds of problems."

- **Mayor Bachran** on the CDOT closure opportunity: "This is an opportunity for Paonia to snag some tourists. If there is a way that we can do some outreach while people are driving on that road, then this is the time to do it."
