## Summary

## Paonia Water Advisory Committee -- First Meeting Summary

*Date: Late January 2021 (exact date unclear from transcript)*

### Key Decisions
- **Motion to establish regular meeting schedule:** Fourth Wednesday of each month at 6:00 PM. Passed unanimously.
- **Next meeting set for February 24th**, with agenda focused on prioritizing committee members' topic lists.

### Discussion Highlights
- **Committee member introductions and backgrounds:**
  - **Philip** -- Former physician turned farmer, ran water on Stucker Mesa for ~10 years, built a water treatment plant there, ORC-certified, strong engineering/CDPHE relationships. Focus: supply side, engineering, water flow modeling, GIS mapping.
  - **Mike** -- Stuart Mesa Water Company board member (10-12 years), aerospace engineering master's, 35 years working on complex systems. Focus: instrumentation, SCADA, system monitoring improvements.
  - **Thomas** -- Mechanical engineering degree, hands-on water system operations experience, owns the Local Nomad hostel. Focus: billing equity (feels current structure is weighted against out-of-town users and multi-business locations), greywater ordinances, rainwater catchment programs, demand-side conservation.

- **Water supply concerns:** Small North Fork water systems are struggling with dwindling supplies. Drought impact growing. Temperature up and precipitation down over past 30-40 years. Raw water storage needs identified but scope unclear.

- **Out-of-town water companies:** ~27 "water companies" exist, many not formally incorporated. Limited documentation on why they were created. Town attorney reviewing existing agreements. Stuart Mesa's engineering survey found they're at tap capacity (79 taps, 76 in use).

- **Tap moratorium:** Out-of-town tap moratorium has been in place 20+ years (modified several times). Lifting it depends on infrastructure analysis and system mapping results.

- **SCADA systems:** Lower plant (1 million gallon treatment) SCADA nearly complete with Allen Bradley units; remote access being secured. Upper plant also has SCADA with remote capability.

- **Metering:** Radio-read meters being installed system-wide, expected complete by end of year. Smart meters (cloud-connected, continuous monitoring) explored with National Meter but stalled due to COVID remote-work disruptions.

- **Historical studies discussed:** Wheeler feasibility report (two versions, one accepted, one not), crude study of 2016, 2003 water loss analysis, county-wide water availability study. Committee agreed to review all available studies.

- **Action items:** Each member to compile a priority/wish list within two weeks and send to Tammy for distribution. Corrine to arrange a plant tour with David Jacobson (tentatively early February). SGM mapping and study data expected within 1-2 weeks.

- **Public communication:** Committee agreed their work should be publicly noticed and well-advertised, same as regular board meetings.

### Public Comment
- **Philip (during discussion)** raised that Pickin Mesa's fill station gets overwhelmed when the town shuts down water -- 95,000 gallons drawn in under 24 hours. Requested cooperation/communication between the town and Pickin Mesa during shutdowns.
- **Travis** responded that a new fill station was received two days ago and should be online within a couple weeks.
- **Smith (215 Delta Avenue)** -- Expressed appreciation for the committee's formation and the depth of knowledge represented. Looks forward to participating.

### Notable Quotes
- **Philip:** "Water systems are all the small systems in the North Fork that I deal with and a number over in Crawford -- they're all struggling right now. A lot of water supplies are dwindling. The drought is beginning to take a huge impact."

- **Thomas:** "My real motivation for being on this board is to hopefully bring justice and equitability to the billing and use structure of the water system, because I feel that the billing structure is weighted heavily against out-of-town users."

- **Mike:** "The engineering analysis came back and said you can't issue any more taps, because if you do, somebody's going to flush the toilet and somebody else ain't going to have water in their shower."
