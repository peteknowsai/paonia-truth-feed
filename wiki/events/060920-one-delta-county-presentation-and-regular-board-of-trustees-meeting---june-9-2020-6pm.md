## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** June 9, 2020 (based on context clues: COVID timeline, references to fall 2019 discussions, Cherry Days cancellation, variance requests)
**Format:** Zoom (virtual meeting due to COVID-19)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- unanimous
- **Consent agenda approved** -- unanimous
- **Accounts payable approved** -- disbursements of $19,313.86, payroll transfer of $19,445.60, payroll taxes of $7,824.20, direct deposit payroll of $19,445.60, Chase credit card of $3,011.97
- **One Delta County participation** -- motion carried to place on next meeting's agenda for formal consideration, with the Town Administrator to prepare dollar amounts and funding source recommendations
- **Town Administrator contract approved** -- unanimous. Corinne formally appointed as Town Administrator (previously interim)
- **Minnesota Creek sewer line mitigation bid awarded** -- Tribble and Sons LLC, $21,600 (low bid of three received). Unanimous
- **Resolution 2020-11 approved** -- local disaster declaration extended to June 25th. Unanimous
- **Resolution 2020-12 approved** -- administrative modification of liquor licenses to facilitate outdoor dining within town right-of-way during disaster declaration
- **Personnel handbook modification approved** -- addition of protection from retaliation language. Unanimous
- **Planning Commission reappointments** -- Mayor reappointed Barb and Lucy Hunter (incumbents), passing over Suzanne Watson despite board members advocating for Watson's code knowledge
- **Personnel Liaison Group** -- Elaine Brett appointed (mayoral appointment). Evan confirmed as employee representative; Dave as Personnel Committee lead, Karen as backup

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **One Delta County economic development proposal:** Elyse (County), Larry (Mayor of Hotchkiss), and Cami (Cedar Edge) presented the plan to replace the struggling DSED with a new countywide economic development entity. Key elements: county employs a "project manager" position with benefits; costs split among municipalities by population formula (same as shared sales tax); Paonia's year-one share is just over $1,100; three-year commitment subject to annual appropriation; each municipality gets a board seat. Paonia had declined to participate in November 2019 but the Town Administrator strongly advocated for joining. Board agreed to put formal approval on next agenda.

- **Opportunity Zone progress:** Consultant hired in February, delayed by COVID but still advancing. Stakeholder meetings held in Paonia in March. Consultant scheduled to return mid-July with list of most viable projects. Downtown Paonia redevelopment identified as "low-hanging fruit."

- **Town Administrator/Police Chief relationship:** Trustee Knutson raised concern about nepotism given Corinne (Administrator) and Neal (Police Chief) are married. Mayor reported an MOU is being drafted establishing the Mayor as supervisor of the Police Chief with no supervisory role for the Town Administrator over the police department.

- **Town Administrator evaluation:** Finance/Personnel Committee proposed a simplified one-page evaluation form with 10-point scale. Mayor suggested the list of objectives was too long and should be narrowed to 3-4 big items. Michelle suggested adding a self-evaluation component.

- **Return to in-person meetings:** Board generally agreed to move toward hybrid in-person/Zoom meetings once the county's variance allowing gatherings of up to 50 people is approved. Discussion of logistics: airflow, masks, microphone placement, ADA compliance, sanitization of podium.

- **Outdoor dining/liquor license expansion:** Resolution allows administrative modification of liquor licenses so restaurants can extend service into the right-of-way for outdoor dining during the disaster declaration. Concern raised about sidewalk width and pedestrian distancing; Corinne clarified tables would likely go in modified road space, not on sidewalks.

- **Committee meeting transparency:** Suzanne Watson raised concern that committee meetings are not consistently publicized or open to the public. Corinne clarified committees are not subject to open meeting requirements and each committee decides individually whether to allow public attendance. Mayor suggested this as a retreat topic.

- **Infrastructure updates:** Streetlight delivery expected mid-to-late June. JDS and SGM working on water system mapping and pressure analysis, preliminary drafts expected late summer/fall. Poulos Park improvements progressing (lights installed, wall repairs, playground disinfected). Parks now on regular watering schedule. Police department lost an officer (Officer Vassal); actively recruiting. Municipal judge search narrowed to two candidates from four applicants.

- **Dry Gulch water line breaks:** Trustee Bear requested a cost summary of the rash of water line breaks and PRV valve repairs, noting supplies alone exceeded $4,000. Travis agreed to compile for next meeting.

- **Main Street closure:** Due to a culvert failure on Paonia Ditch. Costs and liability (town vs. ditch company) still being determined by town attorney.

- **Tree Board:** Sick Tree Day tentatively set for July 11. Working with state forester (remote due to COVID) and entomologist Jim Leezar. Planning tree planting at elementary school for fall.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson** -- Raised multiple issues: officer appointment timing under state statute (questioned whether reappointment is needed every two years rather than four); concerns about the word "community" being too vague in committee qualification language; ongoing concerns about the Administrator/Police Chief spousal relationship and nepotism policy enforcement; code enforcement conflicts of interest; questioned the municipal judge selection process transparency; requested committee meeting schedules be made public
- **Deborah Spiegel** -- Asked about the fence ordinance timeline; echoed support for Suzanne Watson's Planning Commission appointment
- **Paige Smith (215 Delta Ave)** -- Requested a copy of the personnel policy (told she could email for it); raised concerns about outdoor dining resolution not adequately protecting pedestrians if tables are on sidewalks with cars still parked at curb
- **Stephen Keenan** -- Attempted to raise federal food security funding opportunities but was redirected as discussion was limited to open meetings topic
- **Chris Kowski** -- Business perspective on One Delta County; had to locate his business in Montrose because he couldn't find space in the North Fork valley

### Notable Quotes

- **Larry (Mayor of Hotchkiss) on One Delta County:** "The overwhelming question that I was asked while campaigning was what are you gonna do about economic development. I said I don't know but I'll find out."

- **Corinne (Town Administrator) on joining One Delta County:** "You're not one Delta County if you're not one Delta County. We need to be part of that. We need to have that seat at the table."

- **Dave (Trustee) on evaluation objectives:** "There's way way way too many. I think it needs to be much smaller and more concise. Instead of dumping everything into the bucket, I think it needs to be maybe three, maybe four big objectives."
