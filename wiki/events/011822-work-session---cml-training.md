## Summary

### Key Decisions
- No motions or votes were taken. This was a training session, not a regular business meeting.

### Discussion Highlights
- **Kevin Bommer, Executive Director of Colorado Municipal League (CML)**, presented remotely to the Paonia Board of Trustees on municipal elected office roles and best practices
- CML is a nonpartisan organization of 270 member municipalities (every incorporated municipality in Colorado except two), funded ~90% by member dues, with a staff of 14 full-time and 2 part-time employees
- Paonia is a **statutory municipality** operating under a council-manager form of government (technically "board-administrator" since statutory towns use those terms rather than "council" and "manager")
- Key roles of elected officials covered: setting public policy, being professional, being accountable to all constituents (not just supporters), big-picture thinking, stewardship of public resources, advocacy, community building, decision-making, oversight, and ethics
- Emphasized the **chain of command**: trustees should not individually direct department staff; policy direction flows from the board as a body to the administrator, then to department heads
- Stressed that trustees must **vote yes or no** on all matters -- no "maybe" votes -- and should justify decisions concisely without filibustering
- Recommended trustees establish a **singular set of goals and priorities** as a board, then hand implementation to staff
- Referenced the CIRSA handbook "Ethics, Liability and Best Practices for Elected Officials" (2nd edition, 2019) as assigned reading for all trustees
- CML's 100th annual conference planned for Breckenridge; newly elected training planned after April elections
- CML database needs updating for current Paonia board members; Corinne (town clerk) to send updated roster

### Public Comment
- No public comments were made. The mayor opened the floor but no one spoke.

### Notable Quotes
- **Kevin Bommer:** "You represent everyone, not just the people that supported you and your candidacy. Even those who say 'I'll never vote for you, I don't like you' -- you still represent them."
- **Kevin Bommer:** "If you think that things are a mess policy-wise, you got to look in the mirror as a town board, because the town board's responsible for establishing policy."
- **Kevin Bommer:** "There isn't any championship team where every player agreed on everything all the time. But the important thing is that in spite of differences, with a good coach, they were all rowing in the same direction."
