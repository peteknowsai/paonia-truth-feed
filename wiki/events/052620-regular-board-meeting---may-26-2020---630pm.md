## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** Late May 2020 (exact date unclear; references Cherry Days cancellation, COVID restrictions, June deadlines)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda amendments:** Removed item 9 (personnel handbook) and made fence ordinance (item 8) discussion-only. Passed by roll call vote, unanimous.
- **Executive session:** Entered executive session for legal advice (re: a case referencing "very fast-paced versus California") and personnel matters regarding Town Administrator Corinne Ferguson's employment. Passed by roll call.
- **Consent agenda:** Approved unanimously.
- **Disbursements approved:** Accounts payable $37,003.44, payroll transfer $19,459.43, payroll taxes $17,772.06, transfer of $33,041.88 (bond refinancing excess) to ColoTrust reserves. Passed unanimously.
- **1 million gallon water treatment plant SCADA system upgrade:** Motion to accept the lease agreement for SCADA equipment. Passed 4-2 (Bear, Bettinger, Johnson, Mac in favor; Knutson and Patterson opposed).
- **Tree board ordinance (Ordinance 2020-05), second reading:** Approved unanimously. Tree board agreed with all changes; shrub/vegetation language already stricken.
- **Town Administrator contract:** Motion to approve a one-year contract for Corinne Ferguson, with the town paying half (~$137/pay period, described as 95% of health insurance), contract to be posted publicly and approved at first meeting in June. Passed unanimously.
- **Interim contract extension:** Corinne Ferguson to continue under current interim contract through June 10, 2020. Passed unanimously.
- **Intergovernmental agreement -- shared victims advocate:** MOU with Hotchkiss and Cedaredge for VALE grant (victims assistance). Passed unanimously.
- **Meeting time extension:** 15-minute extension approved.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Bond refinancing results:** The town refinanced a $1.65M bond. USDA payoff was ~$1.588M, leaving $33,041.88 excess going to reserves. Gross savings: $401,411.96. Net savings (after debt service reserve): $300,661.96. Trustee Knutson acknowledged he was initially skeptical but praised the outcome.
- **COVID-19 update:** Delta County at 67 total cases, 62 recovered, 38 positive tests, 29 probable cases from contact tracing, 1 death. 956 tests administered, 59 pending.
- **Cherry Days cancelled for 2020:** The 73rd annual event (longest-running outdoor event in Colorado) cancelled due to gathering restrictions. BMW rally expected to follow.
- **Restaurant outdoor dining:** Discussion of allowing restaurants to extend outdoor seating, possibly into streets, following Breckenridge's model. Ridgway already passed a resolution. Concerns raised about handicap parking access and street closures. Board consensus to signal willingness and let restaurants propose plans.
- **Water line breaks:** An 8-inch line on Old Road broke -- pipe "exploded into 30 pieces," likely a PRV (pressure reducing valve) malfunction. Travis working on repair parts; scheduled line shutdown anticipated next week. Mayor commended public works crew for working 8:30 PM to 2 AM on a previous leak.
- **Police department:** Losing Officer Andrew Castle (last day June 6). Seeking replacement; academy graduates same day. Kennel arrangement nearly finalized for stray/quarantine dogs. Reminder that complaints must be in writing for PD to investigate. Municipal judge interviews starting the 26th with four qualified applicants.
- **Fence ordinance:** Packet version differed from committee recommendation; committee meeting the next day to review. Trustee Patterson requested waiving a variance fee for a resident waiting on the ordinance change; town attorney advised it was premature.
- **Retail/medical marijuana ballot measure:** Extensive discussion. Master plan survey showed ~82% of in-town respondents would vote in favor. Previous ballot measures narrowly defeated. Respondents preferred tax revenue go to infrastructure. Bill Bear spoke against, citing community character concerns. Others (Tammy Mac, Dave Knutson, others) favored putting it to voters. GAPS committee directed to continue investigation; Planning Commission to be involved. July deadline for ballot language noted.
- **Resuming in-person meetings:** Government exemption exists for gathering limits, but logistics are challenging -- sound system issues, distancing requirements, hybrid meeting complexity. Ideas floated: outdoor meetings at Town Park, Paradise Theatre, high school auditorium. No resolution; will revisit in two weeks.
- **Committee reports:** GAPS meeting the next day at 2 PM (closed to public due to COVID, may offer Zoom in future). Public Works meeting scheduled June 1 at 9:15 AM. Tree board visited trees on 3rd and Box Elder related to sidewalk replacement. Dave Knutson wrote a letter to the Air Quality Commission as an individual elected official re: oil and gas, following previous board direction. Board directed GAPS committee to meet with Suzanne Watson regarding zoning and building code suggestions.

---

### Public Comment

- **Thomas Michael (333 2nd Street):** Researched concrete scarifiers -- a gas-powered grinder (~$6,000 new) that could level sidewalk heaves up to 2 inches, potentially saving money vs. the $30,000+ budgeted for concrete replacement.
- **Paige Smith (915 Delta Avenue):** Attempted to comment on fence ordinance; directed to next meeting. Asked about committee meeting time.
- **Christina Patterson:** Urged delaying the administrator contract until in-person meetings resume so public can participate meaningfully.
- **Thomas Michael (second comment):** Reminded board that Corinne's interim contract was meant to be temporary until the new board could hire a permanent administrator. Called the renewal "a cop-out" and urged opening applications.
- **Deborah (last name unclear):** Supported marijuana measure, citing positive experience in a previous town. Also supported typed-in participation for meetings.
- **Evan (master plan surveyor):** Presented survey data: 308 respondents, 85% in-town, 82% would vote to legalize recreational marijuana. Top preference for tax revenue: infrastructure.

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Trustee Dave Knutson on bond refinancing:** "I was a skeptic about this process when we started out and was very doubtful about whether it was a good deal for the town, and I have to admit I was very wrong."

- **Corinne Ferguson on Cherry Days cancellation:** "While an event like the coronavirus doesn't take away your long-standing history -- this would have been the 73rd annual Cherry Days, the longest-running outdoor event in the state of Colorado -- they don't lose that designation, but it still has a different feel to it when you've had to skip a year."

- **Trustee Tammy Mac on marijuana:** "Paonia has been known for its cannabis for decades, and I would just like to see us as a board and as a town open up that discussion again now that a lot of the data has been compiled and looked at and a lot of towns are profiting from this."
