## Summary

## Summary: "Let's Talk Short-Term Rentals" Listening Session

**Type:** Public listening session hosted by the Governmental Affairs (GAPS) Committee
**Format:** Zoom call with phone-in option
**Facilitated by:** Mick (committee member) and Michelle (staff/moderator)

---

### Key Decisions

- No decisions or votes were taken. This was an information-gathering session only.
- The GAPS committee was tasked by the board with researching whether STR code updates are needed.
- The town of Nederland, Colorado's STR code was identified as a reference model (similar-sized town, recently adopted).

### Discussion Highlights

- **Current code is outdated:** The existing municipal code addressing STRs and ADUs dates to the 1980s and says almost nothing substantive. The committee wants to update it.
- **Definition proposed as starting point:** Nederland's definition was read aloud -- rental of a dwelling or portion thereof for less than 30 days, accessory to primary residential use, excluding hotels/motels.
- **Owner-occupied vs. whole-house rentals:** Multiple speakers drew a sharp distinction between renting rooms in a home you live in versus renting out a vacant second property. There was no consensus on whether the code should treat these differently.
- **Resistance to over-regulation:** Nearly every speaker pushed back on regulating preemptively. Common refrain: "What problem are we trying to solve?"
- **Noise and quiet hours:** 10pm quiet time was the most broadly supported concrete regulation. Multiple speakers referenced noise/party issues.
- **Parking:** Raised as a potential concern, especially for properties with multiple guests or ADUs.
- **Economic necessity:** Several speakers emphasized that STR income helps residents survive in a town with few jobs and rising costs. Suzanne Watson made a pointed argument about single-person households subsidizing infrastructure costs.
- **Best practices over regulation:** Susan (STR operator, ~3 months experience) offered to help form a mentorship/best practices group for new STR hosts. Lulu also volunteered. Mick encouraged them to contact Town Administrator Corrine Ferguson.
- **Airbnb's built-in tools:** Susan described using Airbnb's review requirements (4+ positive reviews) and house rules to self-regulate guest quality.
- **Enforcement concerns:** Lulu asked how the town would even know about or monitor informal room rentals (e.g., to solar energy students).
- **Planning commission involvement:** Suzanne Watson noted this is fundamentally a land use/zoning issue and would likely need to go through the planning commission process.
- **Origin of the effort:** Christina recalled the issue originated ~2 years prior when residents complained to then-committee members Samara Hart and Dave Knutson about neighborhood STR problems (parking, noise, strangers, rental market impact).

### Public Comment

- **Rio Grande Ave resident (218):** Questioned the distinction between STRs and hotels/motels, and whether regulation is needed at all -- called it "government interference."
- **Deborah (in-town, operates rooms in her home via Airbnb):** Distinguished her situation from absentee whole-house rentals. Supported quiet hours. Opposed being told what to do with her own home.
- **Lulu (1003 Third Street):** Rents rooms to solar energy students at low rates. Asked what problem the town is trying to solve. Suggested looking at other communities' approaches. Volunteered for a best practices group.
- **Suzanne Watson (903 Second Street):** Argued single-person households shouldn't face additional restrictions for renting rooms. Questioned whether regulation is needed absent clear problems. Advocated "carrots instead of sticks."
- **Susan (new to valley, ~June; operates part-of-house Airbnb):** Shared practical experience with Airbnb house rules and review requirements. Offered to mentor new hosts. Suggested Airbnb Experiences as economic opportunity for the town.
- **Christina:** Recalled the issue's origin from neighborhood complaints. Recommended starting with voluntary guidelines rather than an ordinance.

### Notable Quotes

- **Suzanne Watson:** "Why would I have to be charged or billed because I want to use some of the other rooms that I have? I think I should have a right to utilize my house and rent out rooms if I choose."

- **Lulu:** "It's almost like you're trying to solve a problem that hasn't presented itself yet... What exactly is it that you're trying to achieve?"

- **Suzanne Watson:** "I'd like to see it happen with carrots instead of sticks, and then if issues seem to come out of it, at that point you say, oh, this seems to be kind of a problem, so let's start creating some regulation."
