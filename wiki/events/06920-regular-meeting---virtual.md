## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** June 9, 2020 (based on context clues: COVID timeline, references to fall 2019 discussions, May 2020 dates)

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- unanimous voice vote
- **Consent agenda approved** -- unanimous voice vote
- **One Delta County participation** -- Motion to place on next meeting's agenda with administrator's funding recommendations. Passed unanimously. No commitment to join yet.
- **Town Administrator contract approved** -- Corinne confirmed as town administrator (was interim). Passed unanimously. Salary unchanged from budget.
- **Personnel Liaison Group** -- Mayor appointed Elaine Brett. Dave (finance/personnel committee) appointed as committee rep, Karen as backup. Evan confirmed as employee representative.
- **Planning Commission vacancy** -- Mayor appointed Barb Heck and Lucy Hunter (incumbents) over Suzanne Watson, despite board members vocally supporting Watson's code knowledge.
- **Personnel handbook modification** -- Anti-retaliation language added. Passed unanimously.
- **Minnesota Creek sewer line bid awarded** -- Triple S and Sons LLC, low bid at $21,600. Passed unanimously.
- **Resolution 2020-11** -- Local disaster declaration extended to August 25. Passed unanimously. Late fees remain waived, no disconnects for delinquent accounts.
- **Resolution 2020-12** -- Administrative modification of liquor licenses and encroachment permits for outdoor dining approved. Passed unanimously.
- **Town Administrator evaluation process** -- Finance/personnel committee presented simplified one-page evaluation form. Board asked to review and provide feedback by next meeting. No vote taken.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **One Delta County (economic development):** Elise (county), Larry (Mayor of Hotchkiss), and Cami (Cedar Edge) presented the proposal to replace DSAT with a new county-wide 501(c)(3) focused on business retention/expansion/recruitment. Funding split by population formula (same as sales tax distribution). Paonia's Year 1 cost: ~$1,100. County would employ a "project manager" with benefits to reduce turnover. Three-year commitment subject to annual appropriation. Each municipality gets a board seat. Paonia had declined in November; administrator Corinne strongly advocated joining. Chris Caskey noted he had to locate his business in Montrose because no space existed in the North Fork.

- **Administrator/Police Chief relationship:** Tammy raised concerns about Corinne supervising the police department given her marriage to Police Chief Neil. An MOU is being drafted (approved by town attorney) designating the mayor as police chief supervisor, removing administrator oversight of PD. Suzanne Watson pressed on practical conflicts (code enforcement, budget preparation, going home together).

- **Open meetings/COVID:** Board agreed to pursue hybrid in-person/Zoom meetings once county variance allows gatherings of 50. Discussion of airflow (ceiling fans, box fans, continuous HVAC vent), mask requirements, microphone logistics, and ADA compliance. Tammy requested masks due to living with her 90-year-old mother.

- **Outdoor dining/liquor licenses:** Resolution enables restaurants to extend liquor license premises into the right-of-way. Paige Smith raised concerns about sidewalk width and pedestrian social distancing. Corinne clarified tables would likely go into modified street/parking areas, not on sidewalks. One restaurant owner declined to participate. Michelle suggested making street closures permanent beyond COVID.

- **Officer appointments debate:** Suzanne Watson questioned whether state statute requires reappointment of clerk, treasurer, and attorney after each trustee election (every 2 years) vs. every 4 years. Town attorney Bo said the interpretation is every 4 years based on term of office; last done in 2018.

- **Municipal judge search:** Committee (Michelle, Dave, Neil) interviewed 4 candidates, narrowed to 2, plan to present recommendation at next meeting. Suzanne Watson questioned whether the committee should present all 4 to the full board.

- **Public Works updates:** Street light delivery expected mid-to-late June. Poole's Park cleanup progressing with new lighting and wall repairs. Playground equipment disinfected. Water fill station card system needs future replacement. ~$200K available for street repairs. Main Street closure due to Paonia Ditch culvert failure -- cost liability being determined. Dry Gulch water line break costs to be summarized for next meeting. Infrastructure analysis (JVA Hydro + SGM) on track, preliminary drafts possibly delayed to late fall due to pandemic.

- **Police report:** Department lost Officer Vassell; 3 applications received, none POST-certified. Marijuana compliance checks underway (12 plant max, must be locked/enclosed).

- **Committee access:** Paige Smith expressed frustration about not knowing when committees meet or whether they're open to the public. Corinne clarified committee meetings don't fall under open meeting requirements -- each committee decides independently whether to allow public attendance.

---

### Public Comment

- **Chris Caskey** -- Local resident, One Delta County transition team member. Had to locate his business in Montrose due to lack of space in the North Fork; supports One Delta County.
- **Suzanne Watson** -- Raised multiple issues: Zoom participation instructions inadequate (unmute instructions missing); officer appointment timing under state statute; planning commission interest (was not appointed); personnel handbook concerns; questioned judge search committee process; urged reopening in-person meetings while virus levels are low.
- **Deborah Spiegel** -- Asked about fence ordinance status (delayed pending in-person meetings); supported Suzanne Watson for planning commission.
- **Paige Smith** -- Questioned outdoor dining logistics and pedestrian safety on narrow sidewalks; requested committee meeting schedules be published and accessible.
- **Stephen** (last name unclear) -- Began asking about federal food security funding but was redirected as off-topic for the open meetings discussion item.
