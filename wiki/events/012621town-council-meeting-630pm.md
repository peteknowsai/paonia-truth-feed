## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Regular Meeting

**Date:** February 9, 2021 (based on context clues: January meetings referenced as past, February planning commission mentioned as upcoming)

**Present:** Mayor Bachran, Trustee Bear, Trustee Bettinger, Trustee Johnson, Trustee Knudsen, Trustee Mack (absent/no response at roll call), Trustee Paterson. Also: Administrator Corinne Ferguson, Attorney Bo (Blumenfeld), Treasurer, Finance Director Cindy, Police Chief Neil, Public Works Director Travis.

---

### Key Decisions

- **Agenda approved** -- unanimous
- **Franchise fee/sales tax discussion added to next meeting agenda** -- motion by Trustee (Phil), passed unanimously. Treasurer to prepare research with finance director.
- **Meeting format discussion added to next meeting agenda** -- motion by Trustee Dave Paterson, passed unanimously. Attorney to provide examples of jurisdictions using workshop/business meeting alternating format.
- **Trustee letter-signing policy added to next meeting agenda** -- motion by Trustee Paterson, passed unanimously. Addresses whether trustees can sign letters using their official title without board approval.
- **Disbursements approved** -- accounts payable $63,878.99, Norris retirement payment $1,680, payroll transfer $2,014.15, payroll taxes $9,556.33 (reading unclear), ops transfer $23,014.15, plus payroll direct deposit. Passed unanimously.
- **2020 sanitary survey follow-up accepted** -- two items outstanding, on track for Feb 5 deadline. Consumer confidence report and public notices to be released in March with plain-language cover letters.
- **Administrator evaluation accepted** -- motion by Trustee Paterson, passed unanimously. Evaluation covered final three months of 2020; public input was solicited on at least two occasions.
- **Administrator contract amendment approved** -- two-year extension retroactive to Jan 1, 2021. Salary set at $6,413.34/month (up from $6,153.34), though Ferguson and Chief Neil have voluntarily deferred the raise. Breach/termination notice extended from 10 to 30 days; severance increased from 2 to 3 months. Minor wording corrections made. Passed unanimously.
- **Consent agenda (revised minutes) approved** -- with several corrections noted. Passed unanimously.
- **Executive session entered** -- for legal advice re: Eric Pace v. Town of Paonia and procedural matters (CRS 24-6-402).

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Franchise fee taxation:** Suzanne Watson raised that DMEA appears to charge town sales tax on top of the franchise fee on electric bills, effectively taxing a fee that is remitted back to the town. Finance Director Cindy argued this is a Department of Revenue matter, not the town's. Board voted to agendize it anyway; treasurer will research.
- **COVID update:** 21 new cases in 24 hours, 9 hospitalizations, 230 active cases, 48 deaths in Delta County. Vaccinations underway for 70+ age group and frontline workers.
- **Parks, Trails & Recreation Master Plan:** Website (paoniainmotion.com) soft-launched; meeting schedule to be published within a week. Public can sign up as key stakeholders.
- **Water production data:** Thomas Markle asked about December showing more water sold than produced; Travis explained the 2-million-gallon storage tank accounts for the difference.
- **Police patrol car:** New vehicle arriving from dealership, expected on road within 1-1.5 weeks after decals and wiring.
- **Vaping ordinance:** Chief Neil reported significant vaping problems at schools. Current town code lacks a charge for underage vaping (only covers tobacco). Working on drafting new ordinance; currently sending cases to county.
- **Public comment policy:** Attorney confirmed public comment is allowed for each agenda item; mayor can limit to one comment per person on non-quasi-judicial matters.
- **Sewer plant ammonia problem:** Public Works committee stressed urgency of finding a long-term engineering solution. Staff directed to get engineering help and report back. Trustee Bear emphasized the plant is aging and expensive to replace.
- **Infrastructure projects:** Third & Grand intersection expansion under review; county agreed to pave Meadowbrook streets; school water meter installation still pending; town ditch to park being culverted before irrigation season; new street signs and 5 mph alley speed signs being installed.
- **Trustee signing letters:** Attorney's position is that trustees may sign letters in individual capacity but not with their official title unless the board votes to approve the letter as a board statement. Referenced a letter circulated by Trustee Knudsen.
- **Tree Board report:** New members welcomed; working on Arbor Day event (April 30), potential second "Sick Tree Day," exploring tree-trimming licensing requirements modeled on Grand Junction.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson** -- Raised franchise fee/sales tax issue on electric bills; later questioned why the administrator contract doesn't require ICMA membership (as previous managers' contracts did); argued the membership includes a code of conduct that holds managers to a higher standard.
- **Thomas Markle** -- Congratulated staff on the Paonia in Motion website launch; asked about water production vs. sales discrepancy; asked whether the administrator's cost-of-living adjustment applied to all town employees (told there were no COLA increases, only performance-based).
- **Suzanne Watson (second appearance)** -- Asked about the deferred raise, questioning whether the December announcement was "a publicity stunt"; was told the raise is contractual but Ferguson and Neil voluntarily deferred taking it. Also asked about urgency of the irrigation ditch repair due to neighbor's water seepage.

---

### Notable Quotes

**Trustee Dave Paterson on meeting format:**
> "I kind of like the informal nature of some of our work sessions where we can have more of a discussion back and forth and it doesn't feel quite so stilted. I'm wondering if every single meeting has to be a huge agenda with decisions being made."

**Trustee Bill Bear on sewer plant:**
> "The sewer plant is an integral part of our community and it's a very high cost ticket to work on and replace. This sewer plant issue is something that needs to be delved into quickly. The plant is a mature plant and we have to get it to last a long, long time yet."

**Trustee Paterson on public water notices:**
> "As a member of the public I got one of those notices years ago in Paonia and I was confused by it because it was very bureaucratic and wasn't well explained. When we send these notices to the public, there should be like an executive summary that actually explains what it means, because the way the state requires those notices to go out, you have to have some kind of engineering degree or a degree in political science to understand them."
