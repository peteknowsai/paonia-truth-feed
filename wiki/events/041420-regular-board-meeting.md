## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Summary

**Date:** April 14, 2020 (based on context clues: COVID cases, April 2nd/6th special meeting minutes, April 7 election referenced as "last Tuesday")

---

### Key Decisions

- **Motion to table Ordinance creating Advisory Water Committee (Item 10):** Passed unanimously (6-0). Trustee Bear moved to table as not time-sensitive given remote meeting difficulties.
- **Agenda approved as amended:** Passed unanimously.
- **Consent agenda approved with three corrections to March 10 minutes:** Passed unanimously. Corrections: (1) Ferguson check-signing authority was one-time only, (2) Trustee Deal's vote added to a recorded motion, (3) Clark Alley sewer resolution approval noted as contingent on Rupe Excavating acceptance of release agreement.
- **AAMCO Municipal Bond Sale -- Bernardi Securities bid accepted:** Passed unanimously. Resolution adopted authorizing issuance of $1,650,000 sewer revenue refunding bonds, Series 2020. Refinances USDA loan from 4.375% to ~2.98% weighted average, saving ~$300,000 over bond life.
- **North Fork Gunnison River West Vein Repair -- Contract awarded to Rupe Excavating for $31,600:** Passed unanimously. Contingent on Rupe signing/dating the proposal and receipt of Army Corps of Engineers permit.
- **Resolution encouraging community to wear face coverings in public:** Passed 5-1 (Trustee Deal voted nay).
- **Ordinance mandating face coverings (Item 9):** Tabled unanimously, deemed moot after resolution passed.

---

### Discussion Highlights

- **Remote meeting logistics:** Multiple speakers noted difficulty hearing/understanding over phone. Suzanne Watson supported tabling items until better communication possible.
- **BLM Resource Management Plan:** Trustee Bear reported the BLM's final plan ignored 10+ years of town input. He proposed the board consider writing a protest letter to BLM and Senator Bennett, with help from the Conservation Center (NCHS).
- **Meeting protocols and First Amendment concerns:** Dan Watson raised concerns that the "Meeting Protocol and Standards of Conduct" sheet in the packet goes beyond Resolution 2017-10, potentially infringing First Amendment rights by banning "belligerent, accusatory, impertinent, slanderous" comments. He also questioned which Colorado Revised Statutes authorize removal from meetings.
- **Dog issue follow-up:** A resident asked about timeline for the dog resolution discussed at a prior meeting; Mayor said hard to predict given COVID prioritization.
- **COVID-19 update:** Delta County reported 12 total cases (up from 7), 168 tests performed, one hospitalization, one death. Mesa and Montrose counties had community spread.
- **Town Hall electricity savings:** Light repairs reduced electricity use so dramatically that DMEA contacted the town concerned about low usage. Town's solar panels now generating net electricity for DMEA; meter being upgraded to net meter.
- **Bond refinancing process:** Finance Committee pushed bid date from April 9 to April 13 due to market volatility. Four bids received (DA Davidson, Northland, Bernardi Securities, Robert W. Baird). Committee chose to go straight to bids rather than use an agent.
- **Sewer pipe repair:** Exposed sewer pipe in the North Fork river near Samuel Wade bridge. Five contractors solicited, three bids received. Suzanne Watson raised concerns about Rupe Excavating's failure to sign and date their bid form.
- **Face coverings debate:** Extended public comment (15+ speakers). Supporters cited Czech Republic success, asymptomatic spread, limited local hospital capacity (6 ICU beds). Opponents cited personal liberty, questioned cloth mask effectiveness vs. N95s, and argued existing state/federal guidance was sufficient. Two nurses spoke -- one cautioned about mask material quality, the other affirmed masks reduce spread. Trustee Deal opposed even the resolution as unnecessary. Trustee Knutson made a personal appeal about protecting his 95-year-old mother-in-law.
- **New hires:** Police/Public Works clerk Joanne Katzer hired and started. Four letters of interest received for municipal judge position; virtual interviews planned for May.
- **Arbor Day:** Tree board set May 1 at noon, Apple Valley Park. Tree planted in honor of Jordan Chevigny, limited to 9 attendees with social distancing.
- **Sidewalk grant program:** Dan Watson learned about the town's 20+ year sidewalk reimbursement program (50% of concrete cost, up to $500). He also asked about reimbursement for $2,000 spent pruning a right-of-way tree -- told no current program exists.

---

### Public Comment

- **Suzanne Watson:** Supported tabling water committee item due to communication issues; questioned town employee mask policy; asked about Second & Grand project design details; raised concerns about Rupe Excavating's incomplete bid form.
- **Pamela/Willa Jackson:** Agreed with Watson on tabling; asked about water bill drop box location.
- **Dan Watson:** Raised First Amendment concerns about meeting conduct standards; asked about dog resolution timeline; questioned sidewalk grant and right-of-way tree reimbursement; asked if electricity savings were from Town Hall closure (told no).
- **Face covering public comment (~15 speakers):** Split between support for resolution and opposition to any mandate. Notable speakers included Patrick McCarney (cited Czech Republic), Matt Neal (RN, cautioned on mask material quality), a Naval Reserve member (supported resolution, opposed mandate on legal grounds), Tamara Hardesty (favored ordinance for enforcement teeth), Dee Milhoan (Mask Up the North Fork group, 200 members), Melanie Wolf (nurse, supported masks), and several residents opposing mandates on personal liberty grounds.

---

### Notable Quotes

- **Trustee Knutson** (on masks): "I have a 95-year-old mother-in-law that lives down in Delta and she is extremely vulnerable during these times. It bothers me going into the stores... people don't wear masks, they're not being considerate. I think it's important that the town steps up."

- **Dan Watson** (on meeting conduct rules): "I looked some of these words up in the dictionary... what do you call impertinent? Other definitions are cheeky, sassy, or saucy. So you know, when people are commenting, sometimes things come out, and especially in the public comment period people do have a right to express views by the First Amendment."

- **Trustee Deal** (dissenting on mask resolution): "I think there are plenty of recommendations from the state, federal government, everybody else. The community knows that it's recommended. I really think that this is a waste of time, money, and effort for the board."
