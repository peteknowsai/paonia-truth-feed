## Summary

## Paonia Planning Commission Meeting Summary

*Note: This appears to be a Planning Commission meeting, not a Board of Trustees meeting. Audio quality is poor throughout; some portions are garbled.*

### Key Decisions
- **Agenda approved** — unanimous voice vote
- **Minutes approved** — unanimous voice vote
- **Neighborhood boundaries for liquor licensing tabled** to next meeting (moved, seconded, carried unanimously) — item deferred until clerk Sam can be present to explain
- **No CAFOs in the IGA** — motion carried unanimously that the Planning Commission will recommend to the Board of Trustees that no Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations be allowed within the Intergovernmental Agreement area

### Discussion Highlights

**Master Plan Update (led by consultant, name unclear — possibly "D" or similar)**
- Kickoff meeting held the prior Thursday; Lynn and Mary attended
- Timeline: community engagement sessions April 27 (5:30-7:30pm, pizza reception) and June 10 (Saturday 10am-noon); draft plan to town by July 31; two rounds of town revisions (substantive by Aug 18, final by Nov 14); public review Sept 25-Oct 9; final plan by Dec 18, with contract extended to end of February as buffer
- Planning Commission asked to do first substantive revisions before trustees review
- Commissioners who aren't trustees asked to volunteer as facilitators for small-group breakout sessions
- Plan covers: culture/character, recreation/tourism, economic development, water/infrastructure, growth framework, risks/resilience/emergency preparedness
- Final document target: 30-40 pages
- Consultant reviewing ~2,000 pages of background materials
- Dedicated email for public comments; phased release of draft sections planned
- Promotion materials expected this week; Easter weekend identified as opportunity for church outreach

**Growth Framework Discussion (all commissioners weighed in)**
- Strong consensus against becoming another Aspen/Moab — preserve agricultural character and small-town identity
- Water moratorium creating de facto growth limit; concern it favors wealthy buyers (taps costing hundreds of thousands of dollars)
- Support for urban density over sprawl; walkability and bikeability prioritized
- Affordable/workforce housing identified as the top issue — concern that service workers can't afford to live in town
- Strong interest in a trade school or technical college to attract younger families and preserve skilled trades
- Desire for diversified, recession-proof economy — not tourism-dependent
- Light manufacturing that fits the valley's character encouraged
- Business corridor development suggested along Highway 133
- Year-round business viability emphasized over seasonal tourism income
- Nonprofit sector recognized as significant employer
- Arts community highlighted as major asset — visual arts, healing arts, fiber arts, makers

**Infrastructure and Environment**
- Sewer system upgrade needed within 5 years due to changing Colorado law; green alternatives to mechanical plant preferred
- Gray water catchment encouraged (legal in Colorado but poorly written regulations)
- Xeriscaping for new construction instead of lawns
- ADA/mobility accessibility on sidewalks and streets flagged as poor — wheelchairs and scooters regularly in the road
- Third Street conditions described as terrible for bikes, pedestrians, and accessibility
- Parking: current zoning requirements hamstringing some businesses; diagonal parking may need to shift to parallel due to road width; satellite parking lot near river bridge suggested with small shuttle vehicle

**Community Engagement Concerns**
- Commissioner Dave concerned that 2-hour sessions with sticky notes won't be substantive enough; pushed for 3 hours and focused key questions
- Consultant noted June 10 session could be longer; considering partnership with Urban Rural Continuum group
- Missing voices identified: 20-30 year olds, school children, church communities, Indigenous/Ute history
- Previous plans failed due to lack of trustee buy-in; commissioners urged regular 5-minute updates at board meetings to keep trustees engaged
- Paonia in Motion plan criticized as too long and not actionable; strong desire this plan be concise and usable

**IGA (Intergovernmental Agreement) Review**
- Several sections flagged as needing clearer timelines and notification requirements: C3a, C(exact), E3, F2, H2a, H4
- Half-mile buffer from town boundary for feeding operations considered too close
- Discussion of difference between IGA area and Growth Management Area — annexation of agricultural properties on town water system raised as master plan consideration

### Public Comment
- No formal public comment period identified in the transcript

### Notable Quotes

- **Commissioner Dave (on growth fears):** "A lot of people here are concerned about unfettered growth happening and that all of a sudden it becomes an attractive, desirable community and we California-gate... See it as it is, not as you wish it to be, and what it is is really, really beautiful."

- **Commissioner Steve (on trade school):** "I'm getting too old to be doing this... I would like to have a trade school here and teach people. If you had a place as beautiful as this to come to, to learn how to do something, that'd be charming."

- **Commissioner Monica (on economic priorities):** "Where does the money go? If we ask where does the money go, it really builds — does the money stay in the community or does the money go out?"

- **Commissioner Dave (on Indigenous history):** "In the history there was no recognition of the fact that we live on stolen land. This is Ute land, and that part of the history should start with the Ute, not with when the white settlers got here."
