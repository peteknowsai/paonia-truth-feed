## Summary

## Paonia Board of Trustees Regular Meeting -- December 2020

### Key Decisions
- **Executive session tabled** to first meeting in January 2021 due to Zoom webinar platform limitations preventing breakout rooms (passed unanimously)
- **Disbursements approved**: $108,905.70 accounts payable, $1,700 COVID grant check, $6,766.79 Chase credit card, $19,679.91 payroll transfer, $8,488.52 payroll taxes, $19,679.91 direct deposit payroll (passed unanimously)
- **End-of-year disbursement pre-authorization** granted to finance committee, treasurer, and staff (passed unanimously)
- **Water Advisory Committee appointments**:
  - Trustee Tammy Meck appointed as board representative (unanimous)
  - Thomas Markle appointed as in-town water user (unanimous; Trustee Michelle recused)
  - Mike Drake appointed as out-of-town water user (unanimous)
  - Phil Ceriani appointed as water company representative (unanimous)
  - Staff seat left open for either Travis or Corinne depending on agenda
- **Snow/sand spreader purchase approved**: Meyer PolyHawk PB spreader from Tractor Supply, $7,999 (passed unanimously)
- **Resolution 2020-22**: Coordinated election results accepted (Back the Badge, medicinal/retail marijuana sales, $5 transaction fee) (passed unanimously)
- **Resolution 2020-23**: Mill levy certification approved (passed unanimously)
- **Resolution 2020-24**: 2021 budget adopted (passed unanimously)
- **Resolution 2020-25**: Local disaster declaration extended through end of March 2021 (passed unanimously)
- **Accessory Dwelling Units**: Board directed GAPS committee to draft an ordinance based on their recommendations (passed 5-1, Bill Bettinger voting nay -- wanted planning commission review first)
- **Consent agenda** approved (passed unanimously)

### Discussion Highlights
- **COVID update**: Delta County at 39 new cases in 24 hours, 9 hospitalizations, 19 deaths, 13.49% two-week positivity rate. Still in orange but red status a strong possibility. County and municipalities working on measures to keep restaurants and non-essential businesses open if red status hits.
- **Water system leaks**: Six leaks on Lamborn Mesa in two weeks, attributed to cold weather and ground shifting. Mix of pinhole corrosion, failed plastic fittings, and corroded brass tap saddles. Leaks ranged 2-25 gallons/minute.
- **Wastewater ammonia levels**: Elevated but not yet out of compliance. Sludge levels at 20-30% capacity causing off-gassing. Can likely be controlled with nitrification additives rather than a major sludge removal project.
- **Police staffing**: Now at full staff with six full-time officers thanks to Back the Badge. Officer Andrew Vassell rehired. Shifting to four 10-hour days for better morning/night coverage.
- **Remote restraining orders**: New process instituted so residents can file restraining orders remotely via phone/fax rather than traveling to Delta -- noted as a real benefit during COVID.
- **SB 19-166 (police accountability)**: Thomas asked Chief Ferguson about first application of the law. Ferguson noted six officers had POST certification revoked. Plans to work with town attorney Bo on full compliance.
- **Riverbank subdivision**: Developer met with staff; application substantially complete but requires significant variances. Developer voluntarily redoing schematics; planning commission hearing expected Q1 2021.
- **Building department crisis**: Colorado Code Consulting not responding to calls/emails about contract renewal or modification. Active permits mostly near completion. Corinne exploring county-wide building department shared among municipalities. Paonia must adopt 2018 building code to participate.
- **Sidewalk shoveling enforcement**: Police will begin red-tagging unshoveled sidewalks; citations to follow. Michelle suggested a program to shovel for residents unable to do so and bill them via utility, rather than just issuing fines.
- **2021 budget**: ~25% higher than previous year per Thomas Markle's public comment. Corinne and Chief Ferguson voluntarily deferred their salary increases. Treasurer King and staff praised for comprehensive ClearGov document.
- **Disbursement oversight**: Dave and Karen announced they review credit card charges (itemized receipts) in detail monthly. Proposed that staff explain each disbursement directly to trustees going forward.
- **ADU recommendations**: No short-term rental use allowed for ADUs; zoning coverage requirements to be relaxed to make ADUs feasible on more lots.

### Public Comment
- **Suzanne Watson**: Asked about mag chloride application on town streets (locations, form, frequency). Later opposed building department defunding in a written letter. Noted state statute process for adopting secondary building codes. Suggested differentiating in-town business vs. residential water users on advisory committee.
- **Christina Patterson**: Asked about Riverbank subdivision variances (Bo declined to detail since application not final). Urged expanding water advisory committee membership. Asked about meeting attendance count.
- **Thomas Markle**: Detailed his water system experience (mechanical engineering degree, farm/ranch water systems, seasonal system maintenance). Noted 2021 budget is ~25% higher than 2020. Asked about SB 19-166 police accountability law.
- **Paige Smith**: Asked about COVID relief disbursement to Simpler Time BnB ($1,700). Asked about water/trash rate effective dates (confirmed December 1, 2020).
- **Chris Kendall**: Confirmed water rate information hadn't yet been communicated to water companies; told rates effective December 1 will appear on January billing.
- **"Doug"**: Asked about Body Intuitive disbursement ($2,875 -- COVID relief fund) and whether Amazon/Walmart/Dollar General purchases are reviewed beyond staff level.
