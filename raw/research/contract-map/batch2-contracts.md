## (4) Purchasing policy — transcribed key thresholds and rules

Source: **DRAFT Purchasing Policy Manual, v.7.22.2024**, attached to Resolution 2024-12 (DRAFT) in packets 2024-08-13-evt263 (p.171–196) and 2024-08-15-evt93. Drafted by Stefen Wynn, M.P.A., ICMA-CM, Town Administrator/Treasurer. NOTE: presented for review only in this batch; adoption not confirmed within these 13 packets.

**Procurement thresholds (Manual §2.5):**
- **Commodity purchases/contracts to $999:** No purchase requisition or PO required. Purchasing Card/Credit Card encouraged. "Shop around" encouraged but **competitive bidding NOT required.**
- **$1,000 to $5,000:** Formal PO required. Must attach **at least three (3) documented quotes** to the electronic requisition. One (1) "no bid" from a qualified vendor can count as one of the three.
- **$5,001 to $9,999:** Requires **prior approval from the Town Administrator/Treasurer.** Minimum **three (3) written quotations** (one "no bid" allowed). Informal bidding process required. The **Town Administrator/Treasurer may waive the written-quote requirement** if "not practical or advantageous," via a written determination with justification; such proposals must then go to the Town Board for approval before purchase.
- **$10,000 and greater (commodity, service & construction):** **Formal, public bid process required.** Solicitation document developed with Finance; posted on a procurement website for an agreed timeframe, **"typically no less than thirty (30) days."**
- **Consulting services:** "may require a formal contract to be completed and signed by the Mayor, the Town Administrator, the Town Attorney, and the Town Clerk."

**Formal Solicitation Policy (Manual §5.0):** A formal solicitation is required for purchases of goods and services valued **$10,000 or greater.** Formal bidding requires adoption of plans/specs/scope, **formal advertising in a general-circulation newspaper for "typically not less than (10) days,"** sealed submissions, evaluation, award to the lowest responsive/responsible bidder, notice of award, contract documentation, and a notice to proceed (for grant-funded work, the granting agency's notice to proceed must be received first).
(NOTE the internal inconsistency: §2.5 says the ≥$10,000 posting is "typically no less than thirty (30) days" while §5.0 says formal advertising is "typically not less than (10) days.")

**Public construction (Manual §3.3(a)):** The IFB/RFQ method "is also used for public construction projects valued at $50,000 and above."

**RFP requirement for professional services (Manual §3.4(a)):** "RFPs are **required** to be used when it is necessary to obtain the services of professional consultants, contractors, architects, engineers, and design firms when the contract for services is **expected to exceed $50,000.**"

**Emergency Purchasing (Manual §2.6):** Permitted for unforeseen circumstances posing a substantial hazard to life/health/welfare, imminent threat to property, serious interruption to a Town department, or necessary repair of Town/heavy equipment. Departments must work through the Town Administrator/Treasurer. After-hours, a Department Head may authorize, after notifying the Administrator/Treasurer. **Authorization must be documented**, and the Town Administrator/Treasurer must make "an immediate report … to the Board of Trustees detailing the purchase and the cause of the emergency."

**Change Order Management (Manual §2.7):** "If a change order results in a total project value surpassing the formal bid threshold, Town management approval must be secured. In addition, a project previously issued with a value of **$50,000 or greater, that has a change order value increase of 10% or greater, also requires approval of the Town Administrator/Treasurer.**" The Administrator/Treasurer + Department Head work with the Town Attorney to update accompanying contract changes.

**Sole Source / Request to Proceed (Manual §3.5):** Applies to purchases **$10,000 and greater** where a competitive solicitation will not be used. "Sole Source justifications or Requests to Proceed are strongly discouraged and **must be signed by the Town Administrator/Treasurer at any dollar amount.**" Approval ladder: "**The Town Administrator/Treasurer is authorized to approve sole source requests valued up to $5,000. Any Sole Source requests over that amount must be approved by the Board of Trustees** and must have received a favorable recommendation from the Town Administrator/Treasurer prior to being placed on an agenda."

**Anti-splitting (Manual §2.3(a) and §4.0(b)):** "Departments are forbidden to artificially divide purchase requisitions to circumvent any competitive bidding requirement." And: "Contracts shall not be intentionally split to avoid approval or procurement requirements."

**Exceptions to competitive process (Manual §2.4):** No competitive bid required for, among others: any good/service under $1,000; any good/service on a Purchase Card under $1,500; anything bought during the normal course of an already-competitively-bid contract; advertising; books/periodicals; Town & County shared services (operating expenses); training/conference fees; debt/benefit/insurance/utility payments; lodging/meals/travel; memberships; **temporary agency services and contracted employees**; grant pass-through payments.

**Inter-agency purchasing (Manual §2.3(b)):** Administration may purchase from other public agencies and use cooperative purchasing programs without competitive bids, "however, the prices paid must be competitive with comparable products offered in the marketplace."

**Local preference (Manual §2.9):** Preference for Delta County-local vendors — within 10% of non-local bids for non-capital purchases; within 2% for capital purchases. "Local" = place of business in Delta County ≥12 months, ≥50% Delta County-resident employees, ≥50% of fleet registered in Delta County.

**Contract term (Manual §4.0(a)):** "Generally, the Town does not enter into contracts for longer than one (1) fiscal year." Commodity/equipment contracts may renew annually but must be reviewed at least every 5 years.

**Software (Manual §4.3):** Software (incl. SaaS) may only be purchased via an "IT Agreement for Software and Services," reviewed by the Town's IT provider and approved by the Town Administrator/Treasurer and Town Attorney before going to the Board.

**Ethics (Manual §1.1–1.4):** Standard COI provisions — employees must not participate in procurement where they or an immediate family member has a financial interest; must withdraw and notify their Department Head/Town Administrator on discovering an actual or potential COI; the Town Administrator consults the Town Attorney on appearance-of-conflict questions. Purchases made contrary to the Manual/CRS "shall be void and of no effect."

**Authority structure:** "The Town Administrator/Treasurer and each Department Head are designated as the Town's Purchasing Agents, with respect to the limits and thresholds … established … by the Board of Trustees." The Manual says it follows the Colorado Revised Statutes, and "If there is a conflict with this Manual, the CRS are the superseding rules."
