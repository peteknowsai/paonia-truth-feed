# Town of Paonia Contract/Spending Evidence Map — Batch 1

**Corpus:** 13 Board packets, 2024-01-09 through 2024-05-14 (extracted text).
**Scope:** Distinct contracting/spending actions. Routine bi-weekly Payment Approval
Report disbursement registers excluded by design (tracked only as present/absent in §5).
**Rigor note:** Facts extracted and patterns flagged. No conclusion of corruption,
fraud, or pay-for-play is drawn or implied — incompetence and steering are
indistinguishable in any single document; this map gathers the data that would
distinguish them.

**Standing context:** Stefen Wynn holds the combined title "Town Administrator /
Treasurer" (confirmed in 2024-04-23 packet, Resolution 2024-05 staff report). He is
the presenter/recommender on the large majority of money items below. There is an
unwritten **Town Administrator approval limit of ~$5,000** (inferred — see §3, USGS
item) and an internal **three-vendor/three-quote requirement** for purchases (inferred
— Police Chief asked the Board to "waive the three-vendor requirement" for vests,
2024-01-09). No written purchasing/procurement policy document appears in any of the
13 packets (see §4).

## 3. Red Flags — consolidated bullet list

- **Wynn is presenter/recommender on nearly every money item.** Of ~14 distinct spending actions before the Board in this batch, Wynn personally presented or signed the staff report for at least 9 (WWE PSA, USGS, City of Delta IGA, Kelly PC amendment, URC Amendment #2, SGM CO, RESPEC task order, Caselle module, RFP 2024-01). He is also one of the three RFP scorers on both competitive procurements (Code Revision, Town Attorney). Concentration of procurement control in the combined Administrator/Treasurer role.
- **Insider contract:** 2024-01-09 — Town signed a services contract with its own **Police Chief** (Matt Laiminger / Paonia Car Wash). Recusal noted; no competitive process described; dollar amount omitted from the minutes.
- **Competitive-quote rule waived:** 2024-01-09 — Board explicitly "waived the three-vendor requirement" for the bulletproof-vest purchase. Confirms a 3-quote rule exists and that it is waivable by simple motion.
- **Approval-limit revealed but no written policy:** the USGS staff report (2024-01-23) states the agreement is "over the approval limit by $1,868" on a $5,000 base — pinning the Town Administrator's spending authority at ≈$5,000. Yet **no written purchasing/procurement policy appears in any of the 13 packets** (see §4).
- **iWorQ — sole-source + anti-comparison clause + out-of-area:** Logan, Utah vendor; selected as a "sister business" discount of incumbent TextMyGov with no competing quotes and no written sole-source justification; the proposal itself states it **"cannot be disclosed or used to compete with other companies."** Year-1 cost $17,600.
- **Caselle — sole-source to incumbent, $21,610, in the bid-threshold band:** Community Development module added to the incumbent ERP with no procurement method stated; funded by quietly **reallocating a "contract labor" budget line to "data processing"** to avoid a budget amendment.
- **Cluster of contracts/task-orders deliberately landing in the $15k–$25k range** (below Colorado's $25,000 published-competitive-bid threshold for public-improvement contracts): iWorQ $17,600; RESPEC task order $21,600; Caselle $21,610. Each is a services/software buy rather than a "public improvement," so the $25k construction-bid rule may not strictly apply — but the **consistent just-under-$25k sizing across three separate no-bid buys is a pattern worth quantifying** against the Town's (unproduced) policy.
- **Change orders enlarging existing contracts without fresh competition:** Phoenix Rising (+30.3%, paper→full plan), URC (Amendment #2 scope admittedly reverse-engineered to a grant balance), SGM (added survey/SUE/design). None went back out to bid.
- **SGM change-order documentation is internally inconsistent:** "#2" vs "1" numbering, and three different dollar figures ($32,500 / $35,200 / $40,000) across agenda, attached memo, and scorecard.
- **Contracts signed before Board approval:** VoTech purchase offer signed by the Mayor before Board action (later terminated by the Board); WWE PSA dated the same day as Board approval with the Board pre-authorizing Wynn to "finalize" it.
- **Missing documentation for a Board action:** the SCADA contract termination (2024-03-26) has no staff report, no contract, and no dollar figure anywhere in the packet, despite a public commenter raising "the amount."
- **Blank staff report:** the 2024-05-14 SBA Towers cell-tower lease amendment (a multi-decade agreement) was presented with a completely empty STAFF REPORT form — no scope, budget, recommendation, or attachment listed.
- **Recurring vendors:** RESPEC appears as water-moratorium consultant, construction-standards task-order holder, AND technical advisor inside the WWE hydrogeology RFQ scoring process. SGM appears as a finalist in the same RFQ and as the holder of the 5th & Grand engineering contract. Caselle is the incumbent ERP vendor receiving an add-on. iWorQ rides in on the TextMyGov relationship.
- **Out-of-area vendors:** iWorQ (Logan, UT); SBA Towers (Boca Raton, FL); SGM (Glenwood Springs); RESPEC (Colorado Springs); Wright Water (Denver metro). Most professional-services work flows to firms outside the North Fork Valley — expected for specialized engineering, noted for completeness.

**Counter-evidence / things done by the book (for balance):**
- Wright Water ($197,974) and Sustainable Futures (Code Revision) were selected through real competitive processes — RFQ 2023-04 and RFP 2024-01 — each with multiple responders and three independent scorers; the Code Revision RFP was published on Bidnet with a recorded pre-proposal meeting.
- The Town Attorney RFP 2024-03 ran a full multi-meeting interview process.
- The Board itself imposed guardrails (e.g., the $5,000 re-approval trigger on the City of Delta IGA; rejecting auto-renewal) and showed friction — the VoTech offer was terminated, and Trustee Valentine dissented on the Phoenix Rising change order.

## 4. Purchasing / Procurement Policy

**No written purchasing or procurement policy document is contained in any of the 13 packets in this batch.** Cannot transcribe thresholds verbatim — none are in the corpus.

What the packets *do* reveal indirectly about Town procurement rules:
- **Town Administrator approval limit ≈ $5,000.** Source: 2024-01-23 USGS staff report (evt171) — "In the past the agreement has been within the approval limits of the Town Administrator, but this agreement is over the approval limit by $1,868" on a $5,000 base agreement ($6,868 total). The Board also treated $5,000 as a meaningful line when it required the City of Delta IGA to return for re-approval if projected expenses "exceeds $5,000."
- **Three-vendor / three-quote requirement exists for purchases.** Source: 2024-01-09 minutes (evt308) — Police Chief "asks the Board to waive the three-vendor requirement" for the bulletproof-vest purchase, and the Board's motion explicitly includes "waiving the three-vendor requirement." The rule is waivable by ordinary Board motion.
- Multiple 2023-era Departmental Scorecard lines (carried in the Jan/Feb packets) note that "procurement policies need[] updates" and that the Cardholder policy "will be updated with updated procurement policy" — indicating that as of this period the Town's procurement policy was acknowledged by staff to be outdated / under revision.
- Colorado statutory-town baseline (external, not from packets, for analyst reference): public-improvement contracts over **$25,000** generally require published competitive bidding. Several no-bid software/services buys in this batch land just under that figure (§3).

**Recommendation for the evidence map:** file a CORA request for the Town of Paonia's current written Purchasing/Procurement Policy and any version in effect 2024–2026, plus the Administrator's signing-authority resolution — needed to test the "just-under-$25k" and sole-source patterns against the actual rules.
