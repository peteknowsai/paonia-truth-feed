# Contract & Spending Extraction — Town of Paonia BOT Packet, April 28, 2026

**Source:** `raw/meeting-agendas/2026-04-28/regular-packet.txt` (extracted text of the 614-page April 28, 2026 Board of Trustees regular meeting packet) + `regular-agenda.pdf`.
**Extracted:** 2026-05-14.
**Scope:** Every instance of money leaving the Town to an outside party that appears in the packet text.

**RIGOR NOTE:** This document extracts facts and flags patterns. It does **not** conclude or allege corruption, fraud, or pay-for-play. Where a red flag is noted, incompetence/process-gaps and deliberate steering are *both* live explanations; the data here is not sufficient to distinguish them.

## 2. Disbursements / Check Register

**The April 28, 2026 Disbursements register is NOT present in extractable form in this packet.**

- The agenda lists it as Consent Agenda item **E3, "April 28, 2026 Disbursements."**
- The Town's Rules of Procedure (Section 4(j)) provide for a "Disbursements (15 minutes)" agenda slot, and historically the register is reviewed "by the Finance Committee with the Town's Finance Officer" before approval.
- In the packet text, the pages where the disbursements register and supporting check detail would normally sit (the consent-agenda backup region, and several other ranges) rendered as **bare "Page N of 614" lines with no content** — i.e. **scanned images that the OCR/text extraction did not capture.** Affected ranges include approximately pp. 14–19, 31–44, 51–78, and scattered pages in 95–138.
- **Therefore no vendor-by-vendor check register could be transcribed from this source.** To obtain it, pull the native PDF pages (the 97 MB `regular-packet.pdf`) for the consent-agenda region, or file a CORA request for the April 28, 2026 disbursements/check register and the April 14, 2026 minutes.

The only check numbers visible anywhere in the extracted text are incidental: **Check #6181** ($250.00, Rio Bravo liquor renewal fee — inbound) and a receipt #359666 (illegible amount, inbound). Neither is a Town disbursement.

## 3. Red Flags / Patterns to Watch

> Reminder: each item below is a *flag for follow-up*, not a finding. Process gaps and deliberate steering produce identical-looking documents.

### 3.1 — KLM Engineering: a contract that has nearly doubled via serial change orders
- **Original NTE: $97,860.00. After CO#1 (+$32,935) + CO#2 (+$32,928) + CO#3 (+$26,700): $190,423.00.**
- That is **+$92,563 over original, or +94.6%** — the contract has essentially doubled.
- **And it is not done:** the staff report states a **4th/final change order is already anticipated** for May 12, 2026. The running total will exceed $190,423.
- Every change order is the *same* line item: "additional week(s) of AMPP coating inspection... daily rate $1,568.00." CO#1 = 3 weeks, CO#2 = 1 week, CO#3 = 1 week.
- Stated cause: "project delays and extended construction duration associated with the **contractor's** performance" — i.e. the Town attributes the overrun to the *relining contractor* (not named in this packet), not to KLM. **Follow-up:** who is the 2MG tank relining contractor, what is *that* contract's value and change-order history, and is the Town recovering these inspection-overrun costs from that contractor or absorbing them?
- All three KLM change orders were submitted to the Board by **Stefen Wynn**, who is also the authorized executor of each.
- **No competitive bid documentation for the original KLM agreement appears in this packet.** Cannot confirm from this source whether KLM was competitively selected.

### 3.2 — KLM and SGM: out-of-state / out-of-county engineering vendors, both routed through Wynn
- **KLM Engineering is in Woodbury, Minnesota.** **SGM is in Glenwood Springs, Colorado** (Garfield County — out of Delta County).
- Both water-project engineering items on the April 28 agenda (H10, H11) were **submitted by Stefen Wynn** and both recommended motions name **the Town Administrator** as the executing signatory.
- SGM is referenced elsewhere in the packet as the Town's standing consultant: "the Town's Consultant Brandyn Bair, SGM, Engineers" (p. ~98, in Silver Leaf correspondence). So SGM recurs across the Town's water work. **Pattern to track:** how much total has SGM been paid across all task orders during the Wynn period?

### 3.3 — SGM Task Order: no-bid, and a "streamlined / relatively unchecked" framing
- The $10,000 SGM task order has **no competing proposal** in the packet. It is presented as a logical repurposing of existing infrastructure — a plausible operational call — but there is **no written sole-source justification** in the staff report beyond the convenience narrative.
- The SGM letter itself contains language worth noting verbatim: *"CDPHE doesn't have any design criteria for raw water storage tanks allowing the Town to proceed relatively unchecked."* and describes the CDPHE filing as a *"courtesy" submittal* to avoid *"red flags during sanitary surveys."* This is the vendor's framing, not the Town's, but it signals a low-oversight pathway.
- The same staff report attaches a **much larger related number — $212,300 to "purchase temporary tank"** (budget line 60-50-70) — flagged as a "Decision Later." **Follow-up:** the $10,000 design task and the $212,300 tank purchase are two slices of one initiative; track whether the larger purchase later comes back with or without competitive procurement, and who the $212,300 is paid to.

### 3.4 — Amounts clustered just under thresholds — and a possible split
- **SGM task order = exactly $10,000.00** ("not to exceed $10,000"). Round-number NTE figures at exactly $10k warrant noting.
- **The three KLM change orders are $32,935 / $32,928 / $26,700** — each individually well under a $25k... actually *over* a $25k public-improvement-bid threshold, but each is structured as a *change order to an existing contract*, which is the standard way extra work avoids fresh competitive bidding. The cumulative $190,423 is the number that matters; no single approval ever exposed the full figure to a bid process. **This is the textbook change-order-creep pattern** — flag, not finding.
- **Possible scope-splitting on the Mini-Grant signage:** Task 2 (public parking-lot signs, Houseweart) and Task 3 (Welcome sign refurbishment + Alcon lights) and the Street Name Sign Replacement (Brimar) are **three separate sign/signage procurements** moving in parallel in the same Feb–April 2026 window, under different staff (Rodarte vs. Byrge) and different funding (grant vs. operating). Individually small ($3k–$8k each). Aggregate signage spend is in the ~$25k+ range. Likely just separate programs — but worth confirming they were not deliberately kept separate to stay sub-threshold.

### 3.5 — Mini-Grant: two of three task vendors appear sole-sourced
- **Task 1 (banners)** used a genuine open competition (community art contest, 5 entrants, public survey) — good.
- **Task 2 (parking-lot signs)** went to **Ira Houseweart** via "an interview was established to commission" — single artist, **no competition shown**.
- **Task 3 lights** went to **Alcon Lighting** and banners to **Craftsmen Industries** with **no quotes shown**.
- For grant-funded work the grantor (Region 10) may impose its own procurement rules; **follow-up:** what does the Region 10 Community Development Mini-Grant agreement require for vendor selection, and was it followed?
- Note the internal-controls angle: a **Town employee (Ashley Rodarte, Payroll & Accounting Specialist)** both **authored the grant progress/accounting report** and **personally performed in-kind work on the project** ("volunteered to make these features at no cost"). Mixing the accounting role with the doing role is a control weakness to flag — even though "at no cost" is the benign reading.

### 3.6 — Verkada: dollar figure withheld; "state bid" asserted but not documented
- The TA memo's **"Financial Justification" section was asked point-blank "Is the expense... justified?" and "Could a less expensive alternative have been considered?" — and answered without ever stating the system's cost.** The dollar figure for the Town's largest known surveillance investment is **not disclosed anywhere in this 614-page packet.**
- Procurement defense is **"Verkada has been vetted through a state bid process"** / "Max Swensen with Verkada is providing state bid contract." Cooperative/state-bid purchasing is legitimate, but **the actual contract, the state-bid reference number, and the price are not attached.** Assertion without documentation.
- **Follow-up:** CORA the Verkada purchase orders/contract, the total spend to date, the cited state bid award, and which budget lines ("enterprise funds and capital allocations") were charged.

### 3.7 — Authorized Signatories resolution: Wynn is one of only two "Position 1" signers on all new bank/credit accounts
- **Resolution 26-06** sets the signature scheme for opening or changing any Town bank/credit account: **(1) Stefen Wynn OR Samira Vetter; AND (2) the Mayor or Mayor Pro-Tem, OR (3) Finance Committee member Walter Czech.**
- This is a reasonable dual-control structure on its face. Noted here only because it confirms Wynn's central position in Town financial authority — combined with his role as the submitting/executing officer on the KLM and SGM items, he is the recurring signature across the Town's contracting and banking.
