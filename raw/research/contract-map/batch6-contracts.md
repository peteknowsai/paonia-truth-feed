2025-12-30-evt549, 2026-01-13-evt495, 2026-01-27-evt508, 2026-02-10-evt496, 2026-02-24-evt509, 2026-03-10-evt497, 2026-03-24-evt510, 2026-03-30-evt562, 2026-04-14-evt498, 2026-04-28-evt511, 2026-05-12-evt499.
**Extracted:** 2026-05-14.

**RIGOR NOTE:** This document extracts facts and flags patterns. It does **not** conclude or allege corruption, fraud, or pay-for-play. Where a red flag is noted, incompetence/process-gaps and deliberate steering are *both* live explanations; the data here is not sufficient to distinguish them.

**Note on the April 28, 2026 packet (evt511):** that 614-page packet was extracted separately and in detail in `raw/research/contract-map/april28-extraction.md`. Its actions are summarized in the table below (rows tagged "2026-04-28") and folded into the change-order threads; see that file for full backup.

**Note on procedure:** Many staff-report field labels render as mojibake in the extracted text (`'E/dD͗`=ITEM/AGENDA ITEM, `^hD/ddz͗`=SUBMITTED BY, `<'ZKhE͗`=BACKGROUND, `h'd͗`=BUDGET, `ZKDDEd/KE͗`=RECOMMENDATION, `dd,DEd͗`=ATTACHMENT). Content under them is readable. Several change-order EJCDC forms and some H-item staff reports came through as blank/garbled pages; where a figure could not be read off the source form it was taken from the corresponding staff report or the minutes of the next meeting and is flagged as such.

## 3. Red Flags / Patterns

> Each item is a *flag for follow-up*, not a finding. Process gaps and deliberate steering produce identical-looking documents.

### 3.1 — Serial change orders, two parallel on one project
- **KLM Engineering: $97,860 → $200,713 across 5 change orders (+105%).** A "final" change order (CO#4) was itself followed by another change order (CO#5), both $5,145. No single approval ever exposed the doubled figure to a bid process — the textbook change-order-creep pattern. **No competitive-bid record for the original KLM agreement exists in batch-6.**
- **MPC: $916,250 → ~$1,003,417 across 3 change orders (+9.5%).** CO#1's cause was the *Town's own* delay; CO#2 inserted a brand-new $15,000 subcontractor (Roop Excavating) on a single quote.
- The two threads run in parallel on the same project, both routed through Wynn, both blessed by the same reviewing engineer (SGM).

### 3.2 — Out-of-area concentration on the large-dollar work; routed through the Town Administrator
- **KLM Engineering** — Woodbury, **Minnesota**. **SGM** — Glenwood Springs, CO (Garfield County). **Wright Water Engineers** — Glenwood Springs/Denver, CO. **Consor** — Colorado (out of Delta County). **TYMCO** — Waco, TX, via **Intermountain Sweeper** — West Jordan, UT. **Verkada** — Salt Lake City, UT. **Rehrig Pacific**, **Brimar/SafetySign**, **Axon** — all out of state.
- Every six-figure engineering/infrastructure item in batch-6 (KLM, SGM, WWE $511,876, Consor $367,432, TYMCO $394,500) is out of Delta County, and **submitted/presented and/or executed by Stefen Wynn**.
- The one large item *not* routed through Wynn — the street-sign quote ($3,182, PW Supervisor Byrge → Brimar, low bid) — is also the one that's small and cleanly competitive.
- Benign reading: specialized water/security/sweeper work has no local provider. The pattern to keep building: the *concentration of large-dollar, out-of-area awards under one officer*.

### 3.3 — Documented sole-source / "suspend the rules" procurement on big-ticket items
- **Verkada/Mitchell cameras ($50,288):** the 8/12/2025 motion **expressly waived Code Section 4-6-80** "allowing for a sole source bid." This is the clearest documented instance — a code waiver to sole-source a ~$50k system. Now under discussion for termination.
- **TYMCO street sweeper (~$394,500):** Board "**suspended purchasing rules, answered 8 sole-source questions in the affirmative**" (3/10/2026) on a ~$394k capital lease-purchase. The "comparison" was to two other cities buying *the same* sole-source vendor — not competing brands.
- **Rehrig Pacific carts ($47,141.61):** sole-source from "the Town's existing cart vendor"; justification is a questionnaire, not a market test. Staff recommended the *over-budget* option ($51,993.56); the Board chose the in-budget one.
- **Net2Phone, Istonish firewall:** both bought with no competitive process — both **explicitly invoked the "under $10,001" threshold** to skip it (see §4).

### 3.4 — Amounts clustered at / just under thresholds; scope-splitting signals
- **The recurring round number is $10,000:** Urban Rural Continuum SB24-174 addendum = NTE **$10,000.00**; SGM temp-tank task order = NTE **$10,000.00**. Net2Phone and the Istonish firewall ($9,290) were both justified as "**less than $10,001**." Several items sit exactly at or just under the $10,001 line where the Town's competitive process and sole-source-justification requirement *both* switch off.
- **SGM $10,000 design task + $212,300 temp-tank purchase:** one initiative (repurpose the temp tank) presented as a small sub-threshold design task now, with the **$212,300 purchase** flagged as a "Decision Later." Track whether the $212,300 comes back competitively bid.
- **Smith Center "up to $50,000" bucket:** a single authorization covering **three** scopes (mold remediation + electrical + general contractor) under different vendors. Round-cap, multi-scope buckets can let sub-components dodge individual competitive bidding. Mesa Restoration's mold piece ($17,861.67) shows no competing quote.
- **Change orders as the bid-avoidance mechanism:** KLM's individual COs ($32,935 / $32,928 / $26,700 / $5,145 / $5,145) and MPC's ($44,895 / $15,000 / $27,270.75) are each "a change order to an existing contract" — the standard way extra work avoids fresh competitive bidding. The cumulative figures ($200,713 KLM; ~$1.0M MPC) are what matter.

### 3.5 — Contracts/awards moving before the Board acts, and award authority delegated to the Administrator
- **Rundle Construction:** Notice of Award issued 12/15/2025 — *before* the Board's 12/30/2025 presentation and 1/13/2026 ratification.
- **City of Delta inspection contract:** ratified 2/10/2026, *after* the prior contract had already lapsed on 2/1/2026.
- **Caselle cloud amendment:** approved under "the Town Administrator's authority," then *ratified* after the fact.
- **Smith Center electrical:** Board authorized **Wynn to select the lowest bidder and sign** before competing quotes were in.
- **Police officer employment/training contract:** handled **entirely under the Administrator's approval authority with no Board vote.**
- Recurring motion language across the big items: "authorize **the Town Administrator** to execute all necessary documents" (KLM COs, SGM task order, TYMCO lease, Istonish firewall, Smith Center electrical).

### 3.6 — Verkada: "state bid" asserted in April, "waived 4-6-80 sole source" documented in May
- The April-28 TA memo (april28-extraction.md) said Verkada was "vetted through a **state bid process**" and declined to state the system's cost.

## 4. Purchasing Policy

**No batch-6 packet transcribes the Town's purchasing/procurement policy verbatim.** The full policy lives in **Paonia Municipal Code Chapter 4-6** (referenced as "Code Section 4-6-80"). What batch-6 *does* reveal about its rules, from staff reports and motions:

- **Code Section 4-6-80** is the provision that governs **sole-source procurement** — the 8/12/2025 Verkada motion "**waive[d] the procurement and purchasing under Code Section 4-6-80, allowing for a sole source bid**" (evt499 p.73, staff report by Town Clerk Vetter). I.e. 4-6-80 can be *waived by Board motion* to permit a sole-source purchase.
- **Competitive process / sole-source threshold = $10,001.** Verbatim from the Net2Phone staff report (evt496 p.249, Town Clerk Vetter): "**This doesn't fall under the competitive purchasing process and Sole Source Justification Policy since it is less than $10,001.** However, we are requesting your approval since it is a multi-year agreement." The Istonish firewall purchase ($9,290) was treated the same way. **Below $10,001, neither a competitive process nor a sole-source justification is required**; multi-year agreements still go to the Board.
- **Sole-Source Questionnaire / "Sole Source Justification Policy."** For sole-source buys at/above the threshold the Town uses a written **Sole Source Questionnaire** (used for Rehrig Pacific, evt549) and, for capital equipment, a set of "**8 sole-source questions**" the Board "answer[s] in the affirmative" (used for the TYMCO sweeper, evt510). The motion language is "find that the responses of the sole source questionnaire **satisfy the purchasing policy of the Town of Paonia**."
- **Town Administrator/Treasurer approval authority.** Multiple staff reports reference a dollar level "**within the Administrator/Treasurer approval authority limits**" below which the Administrator contracts without a Board vote (police-officer employment/training contract, evt496 p.234; Caselle amendment "within the Town Administrator's authority," evt496 minutes). **The exact dollar value of that authority is not stated in batch-6** — follow-up target.
- **Statutory backdrop.** As a Colorado **statutory town**, Paonia is bound by CRS Title 31; public-improvement contracts over **$25,000** generally require published competitive bidding. Several batch-6 actions exceed $25,000 (KLM cumulative, MPC, TYMCO $394,500, Rundle $140,850, City of Delta $27,600/yr, Rehrig $47,141, WWE $511,876, Consor $367,432) — of these, Rundle was competitively bid; TYMCO and Verkada were sole-sourced by suspending/waiving the rules; KLM and MPC reached their totals through change orders; WWE and Consor were reformats of pre-existing (RFQ-era) contracts.
- evt562 (Mayor's rebuttal) confirms the policy's origin: "through our Town Administrator's own initiative, the Town's Municipal Code now requires the use of a fully defined purchasing policy for any procurement of goods or services."

**Follow-up:** CORA the full text of Paonia Municipal Code Chapter 4-6 (esp. 4-6-80 and any section setting the Town Administrator's spending-authority dollar limit), and the Sole Source Questionnaire / "8 sole-source questions" form.
