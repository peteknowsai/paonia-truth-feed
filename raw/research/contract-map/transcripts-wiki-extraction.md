| 2023-05-16 | Wynn files whistleblower retaliation lawsuit vs. Neptune Beach (Case 23-CA-8801, Duval County) | master-timeline.md |
| **2023-07-11** | **Town of Paonia appoints Wynn as BOTH Town Administrator AND Town Treasurer.** Done via **Resolution 2023-07 and Resolution 2023-08** — the two resolutions that consolidated the two roles into one person. | citation-sheet.md, master-timeline.md, town-spending.md, who-controls-the-money.md |

**Process gap on the public record:** The wiki dates the appointment to **2023-07-11** by Resolutions 2023-07 / 2023-08. None of the sources reviewed contain a competitive-search record, candidate pool, interview process, or selection-committee documentation for the Administrator hire. The Hunter rebuttal flyer and Board letter defend the *structure* ("Administrator/Trustee form of government," "the Board sets the salary," "the Board conducts performance reviews") but **do not dispute** that both roles are held by one person, that Wynn prepares the budget containing his own compensation, or describe how he was selected. Paonia hired him roughly six months after the unanimous Neptune Beach firing (ten-things-flyer.md item #1).

**Roles consolidated — confirmed undisputed:** who-controls-the-money.md states three of the four underlying facts are "not contested anywhere in the Board's response": (1) dual Administrator + Treasurer role; (2) the Administrator prepares the annual budget and Wynn's compensation is a line item in it; (3) the 2007–2012 embezzlement of ~$480,000 happened under a similar concentration of financial authority *with* controls in place. Hunter corrects only one framing point: the Board, not Wynn, *approves* the salary.

# SECTION C — RED FLAGS & PROCESS CONTEXT

> Each item is a **flag for follow-up**, not a finding. Process gaps and deliberate steering produce identical-looking documents.

## C.1 — The Town's purchasing policy and bid thresholds (what's on the record)

- A **"new purchasing policy"** existed by **November 2024** — the PD sticker-vendor change report states "The new purchasing policy was followed and the Town Administrator was consulted during the purchasing process" (paonia-nov26.en.srt). The policy text itself is **not in any reviewed source.**
- The internal **trustee-approval threshold is ~$10,000**: staff stated on May 12, 2026 that purchases "under [a] certain amount… for staff it was [$]10,000" can be done without coming to the Board. Consistent with this, the Smith Center plumbing ($1,600) was "approved without the board because the number was [low enough]."
- Pete McCarthy asserted on the record (May 12) a **Colorado public-improvement competitive-bid threshold of $25,000** for "a town this size." The Town did not contest the $25,000 figure; instead staff/Mayor cited an **"emergency statute"** exception for safety work.
- **Flag:** the SGM task order is set at **exactly $10,000** (the internal trustee threshold). The KLM change orders are each individually *over* $25,000 (CO#1 $32,935 / CO#2 $32,928) — but structured as change orders to an existing agreement, which is the standard way added work avoids fresh competitive bidding. **No single Board vote ever exposed the full KLM figure ($200,713) to a competitive process.**

## C.2 — Board members and public raising concerns about no-bid contracts and overruns

- **KLM serial change orders — the clearest overrun on the record.** Original NTE $97,860 → **$200,713 final (+105%)** across five change orders, with the staff report itself anticipating each next one. The Town's consistent attribution is "the contractor's [MPC's] performance," and the Town does hold a performance bond + withheld final payment + $50,250 in liquidated damages against MPC. **But:** (a) on April 28 the Board was told the project "would still remain under the original estimated budget," and Pete McCarthy noted on May 12 that the picture had "materially changed in 14 days"; (b) no independent written financial analysis accompanies the change orders; (c) Trustee comments on May 12 ("it's just hard to get up to speed and know how this sub contract relates to the overall project budget… not being on the finance committee") show even sitting trustees could not trace the numbers.
- **No original-agreement bid documentation** for KLM appears in any reviewed packet — cannot confirm KLM was competitively selected.
- **SGM task order is explicitly no-bid** ("repurposing existing infrastructure"; no competing proposals; no written sole-source justification beyond the convenience narrative).
- **Rundle Construction (Oct 28, 2025) is explicitly a sole-source contract** (~$173,000) — approved unanimously, with the pipe (~$60K) already purchased before the contract vote.
- **Smith Center $50,000 (May 12, 2026):** the Board authorized up to $50,000 with competitive bids in the packet for **only** the ~$17–18K mold-remediation slice; the ~$25–32K electrical + general-contractor balance was authorized as a discretionary administrative bucket. Three speakers (Pete McCarthy, Bill Brunner, Susie Calvin) objected on the record that this bypasses the purchasing policy / public-improvement bid statute; trustees pushed back partway, adding co-oversight by Trustee Fischer and a commitment to report figures back, but did **not** split the electrical/GC scopes out for separate competitive bids.
- **Verkada dollar figure withheld:** the April 28 Wynn memo's "Financial Justification" section answered "Is the expense justified?" and "Could a less expensive alternative have been considered?" **without ever stating the system's cost.** The $50,288 figure is known only from the FY2026 budget and the signed Mitchell quote — not from the procurement narrative.

## C.3 — Structure of who controls procurement

- **Wynn holds both Town Administrator and Town Treasurer** (Resolutions 2023-07, 2023-08). He **prepares the budget** that contains his own compensation. He is the **recurring submitting officer and authorized executor** on the water-engineering contracts: every KLM change order and the SGM task order was submitted by Wynn, with the recommended motion naming the Town Administrator (or Administrator & Treasurer) as the signatory. He **personally signed the Verkada/Mitchell quote.** He holds **Organization Admin** (highest permission) in the Verkada system.
- **Resolution 26-06 (Authorized Signatories)** sets the bank/credit-account signature scheme: (1) **Stefen Wynn OR Samira Vetter**; AND (2) the Mayor or Mayor Pro-Tem, OR (3) Finance Committee member Walter Czech. A reasonable dual-control structure — noted because it confirms Wynn is one of only two "Position 1" signers on all Town accounts.
- **Counter-example worth recording:** not everything routes through Wynn. The **street-sign procurement (April 28)** was handled by Public Works Supervisor Rodney Byrge with 3 competitive quotes and a low-bid recommendation, and **no Wynn involvement noted**. The **Astonish IT contract** went through a 10-proposal competitive RFQ with a staff scoring panel. The **Grit and Grace** sticker change was a PD staff report (Wynn "consulted," not driver). So the pattern is **not** "Wynn touches everything" — it is concentrated on the **large water-infrastructure engineering contracts and the Verkada system**, which is where the no-bid / change-order-creep flags cluster.

## C.4 — The Finance Committee's role (and what the record now shows it actually does)

- Per town-spending.md and who-controls-the-money.md, the Town cites a **Finance Committee (Mayor + one Trustee) that "reviews every invoice"** as one of four internal controls — and the wiki's standing critique was that **no Finance Committee minutes or invoice-review log have been published**, so the control rested on assertion (open question: `finance-committee-effectiveness`).
- **New on the May 12, 2026 record:** the Finance Committee (Mayor Smith + Trustee Czech) is shown **functioning in real time** — they caught a mis-coded **$2,952 Fulcrum** disbursement and a mis-allocated **United Business/Merchants Bank** entry during consent-agenda review, and described their process: "we look at everything that's being purchased, ask questions if we don't understand what it is, but we also… double check that every purchase is being assigned to the proper budget line item… we get down to that level."
- **But the gap the wiki flagged remains:** that is **line-item coding review**, not contract-procurement review. On the same night, multiple trustees said they could **not** trace how the KLM sub-contract related to the overall project budget ("hard to get up to speed… not being on the finance committee… I frankly don't see how to do it in this moment with this information"). And Pete McCarthy's point stands on the record unrebutted: "in a town with a functioning finance committee posting minutes, agendas, and review logs [a 105% overrun] would already have produced a written analysis, but there aren't [any]." **Flag:** the Finance Committee catches *coding errors* but there is still no published evidence it reviews *procurement method* or *change-order justification*, and it has never been shown to have flagged or rejected a disbursement on substance.
