## (4) Purchasing Policy — verbatim key thresholds and rules

**Source:** The Purchasing Policy Manual + **Ordinance No. 2024-03** ("Purchasing and Procurement,"
Article 6 of Chapter 4 of the Paonia Municipal Code) are reproduced in full in the **09-23
packet (evt384, ~p.165 onward)** and again in the **12-09 packet (evt374, ~p.230 onward)**.
(The 12-09 packet item is Ord. 25-09 re-adopting / memorializing the policy.)

### Procurement thresholds (Manual §2.5) — verbatim:
- **Commodity purchases/contracts to $1,000:** "Departments are authorized to make purchases up to $1,000 without a requirement for a purchase requisition or purchase order... competitive bidding is not required."
- **$1,001 to $5,000:** "Departments must attach to the electronic requisition at least three (3) documented quotes... One (1) 'no bid' from a qualified vendor can be used to satisfy (1) of the three (3) required bids."
- **$5,001 to $10,000:** "Departments are authorized to solicit suppliers at this level **with prior approval from the Town Administrator/Treasurer**. A minimum of three (3) written quotations are required... Informal Bidding Process Required... The Town Administrator/Treasurer may waive written quotation requirements if it is determined that it is not practical or advantageous... Such a determination shall be made in writing with justification... Proposals in this range must be submitted to the Town Board for approval before the purchase can be made."
- **$10,001 and Greater (Commodity, Service, & Construction):** "Purchases of goods and services $10,001 and greater require a formal, public bid process... the solicitation will be posted on a website used by the Town for procurement purposes for an agreed upon timeframe, which is typically no less than thirty (30) days."
- **Consulting Services:** "Any purchase of consulting services may require a formal contract to be completed and signed by the Mayor, the Town Administrator, the Town Attorney, and the Town Clerk."

(Condensed restatement appearing elsewhere in the 09-23 packet: "Up to $1,000: no formal
competition required. $1,001–$5,000: at least three documented quotes required. $5,001–$10,000:
three written quotations and Town Administrator approval required. $10,001 or greater: formal

### Competitive Purchasing — anti-splitting rule (Manual §2.3(a)) — verbatim:
"The Town is committed to a program of purchasing competitively and wisely. **Departments are
forbidden to artificially divide purchase requisitions to circumvent any competitive bidding
requirement.** In addition, no department or employee shall draft or cause to be drafted any
specifications in such a manner as to limit the bidding directly or indirectly to any one
specific concern, or any specific brand, product, thing, or service."

### Sole Source / Request to Proceed (Manual §3.5) — verbatim highlights:

- "Sole Source justifications or Requests to Proceed are **strongly discouraged** and must
  [be]... [justified] in sufficient [detail]..."
- "**The Town Administrator/Treasurer is authorized to approve sole source requests valued up to

- A list of factors that "if verified, may justify sole source purchases" follows; "Sole source

- The requesting "department shall complete the **Sole Source Justification form** (See Section

### Change Order Management (Manual §2.7) — verbatim:
"Change orders to projects that have previously been approved is commonplace. **If a change
order results in a total project value surpassing the formal bid threshold, Town management
approval must be secured. In addition, a project previously issued with a value of $50,000 or
greater, that has a change order value increase of 10% or greater, also requires approval of
the Town Administrator/Treasurer.** The Town Administrator/Treasurer and the Department Head
shall work with the Town Attorney's Office to update, as appropriate, any accompanying contract
changes resulting from the change order for the project."

### Emergency Purchasing (Manual §2.6) — verbatim highlight:

purchase to avoid a substantial hazard to life, health, and welfare... An immediate report
shall be made to the Board of Trustees by the Town Administrator/Treasurer detailing the
purchase and the cause of the emergency."
