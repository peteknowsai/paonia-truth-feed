# Batch 3 Contract Map — Town of Paonia Board Packets, Sep 2024 – Jan 2025

**Corpus:** 13 extracted-text Board packets, 2024-09-10 through 2025-01-28.
**Scope:** distinct contracting/spending actions; routine bi-weekly disbursement registers excluded.
**Rigor note:** Facts extracted, patterns flagged. No conclusions of corruption/fraud/pay-for-play drawn — incompetence and steering are not distinguishable from any single document.

A recurring data quirk: many of these packets carry the **prior meeting's minutes** in the consent agenda, so several contracting motions surface in a packet dated *after* the meeting where they were voted. Each row's "Packet & page" notes where the text was found; the "Meeting date" is the meeting that actually acted.

- **Precision Concrete Cutting — sole-source >$25k, no written justification form.** $42,830.63 sidewalk grinding declared sole source on a single staff sentence ("staff aren't aware of any other companies in Colorado") — no Sole Source Justification (SSJ) memo attached, which the Town's own new Purchasing Manual (§3.5) requires for purchases $10,001+. Out-of-area vendor (Denver). Exceeds the $25k public-improvement competitive-bid line for Colorado statutory towns.
- **Vac-Trailer "three quotes" are all the same vendor/brand.** ~$110,000 purchase backed by Vermeer Colorado quotes #1/#2/#3 — three configurations from **one vendor (Vermeer)**, not three competing vendors. No genuine price competition for a six-figure buy. Vendor location stated two different ways (Commerce City on the quote vs. "Grand Junction" in the approving motion).
- **RESPEC task-order concentration.** At least two PNAs ($40k and ~$15k) plus CIP Phase I design plus 2-MG tank scopes routed to RESPEC via task orders/amendments rather than separate bids. The umbrella agreement that would justify this is not in the packets.
- **Change orders without disclosed dollar amounts.** Waterline Design CO#1 (evt185) and Waterline Design Amendment CO#1 — motions approved with **no dollar figure in the packet text**. 5th & Grand is already on **CO#3** (cumulative NTE $197,734) with CO#1/CO#2 amounts not in this corpus.
- **Hemlock Consulting brush-clearing award — winning amount not in packet.** RFP 2024-04 was properly competed and administered by Wynn, but the extracted packet contains the RFP and Q&A addendum, **not the bid tabulation or the winning dollar figure**. Cannot confirm number of bids received.
- **Scope-splitting / use of the Administrator's individual approval authority.** Park bathroom remodel: the **electrician (AB Electric) was pre-approved by the Administrator under his own approval limit**, separately from the $35k Dependable Lumber materials request and the rejected $100k+ contractor quotes — a larger remodel effort handled as in-house labor + sub-threshold pieces. - **Xpress Bill Pay — informal 2-vendor comparison for a 36-month, $10k+ contract; explicitly the more expensive option.** Staff scoring sheets, not a formal RFP, for a contract value in the Manual's "$10,001 and greater = formal public bid" range.
- **Stefen Wynn is the recommender/presenter/signer across a large share of contracting items** — RESPEC task order (directs execution), NFVA geotech (directed to engage), water-tank land purchase (background + co-signs), Wright Water extension (submitter), Mary Bachran PSA (submitter/presenter), bathroom electrician (approver), RFP 2024-04 administration, HMP presentation. Wynn is also the official who "approves" the Purchasing Policy Manual itself and holds sole-source approval authority up to $5,000. **This is concentration of procurement control, not by itself evidence of wrongdoing** — but it is the single most consistent structural pattern in the batch.
- **Out-of-area vendors recur** on the professional/specialty work: Precision Concrete Cutting (Denver), Wright Water (Denver), WSP (Denver), SGM (Glenwood Springs), Earthworx (Montrose). Local vendors appear on small buys (Dependable Lumber, Mary Bachran). Consistent with a small town lacking local specialty firms — note, don't allege.
- **Recurring vendors:** RESPEC (≥3 scopes), SGM (≥3 project billings + 2 bid recommendations), Wright Water (≥3 contracts/grants), Phoenix Rising Resources (≥2 mentions), Vermeer. None improper on its face; flagged for the evidence map.

## 4. Purchasing Policy (verbatim key thresholds & rules)

The **2024-09-17 packet (evt77)** contains the full new policy: **Ordinance 2024-03** ("Establishing a Finance Purchasing & Procurement Policy," enacting Chapter 4 Article 6) and **Resolution 2024-14** adopting the **Purchasing Policy Manual** (Exhibit A, dated 9.10.2024). It **repeals the prior Purchasing Policy adopted March 14, 2017.** The Manual states it is "approved by the Town Administrator and [the Board]." (The 2017 policy is also reproduced in the same packet for reference.)

### §2.5 Procurement Thresholds (verbatim)
- **(1) Commodity Purchases/Contracts to $1,000** — Departments may purchase up to $1,000 without a requisition or PO. Purchasing/Credit Card encouraged. "competitive bidding is not required."
- **(2) Commodity Purchases/Contracts $1,001 to $5,000** — Formal POs required. "Departments must attach to the electronic requisition at least three (3) documented quotes ... One (1) 'no bid' from a qualified vendor can be used to satisfy (1) of the three (3) required bids."
- **(3) Commodity Purchases/Contracts $5,001 to $10,000** — Requires "prior approval from the Town Administrator/Treasurer. A minimum of three (3) written quotations are required ... One (1) 'no bid' ... can be used to satisfy (1) of the three (3)." **Informal Bidding Process Required.** "The Town Administrator/Treasurer may waive written quotation requirements if it is determined that it is not practical or advantageous ... Such a determination shall be made in writing with justification from the Town Administrator/Treasurer. Proposals in this range must be submitted to the Town Board for approval before the purchase can be made."
- **(4) Commodity, Service, & Construction Purchases/Contracts $10,001 and Greater** — "require a formal, public bid process ... the solicitation will be posted on a website used by the Town for procurement purposes for an agreed upon timeframe, which is typically no less than thirty (30) days."
- **(5) Consulting Services** — "Any purchase of consulting services may require a formal contract to be completed and signed by the Mayor, the Town Administrator, the Town Attorney, and the Town Clerk."

### §2.6 Emergency Purchasing (verbatim summary)
Emergency POs allowed for "unforeseen circumstances [requiring] an immediate purchase to avoid a substantial hazard to life, health, and welfare ... or there is an imminent threat to property or threat of serious interruption to the operation of a Town Department, or the necessary repair of Town equipment." Departments work with the Town Administrator/Treasurer. After-hours, a Department Head may authorize and then notify the Administrator/Treasurer. "An immediate report shall be made to the Board of Trustees by the Town Administrator/Treasurer detailing the purchase and the cause of the emergency."

### §2.7 Change Order Management (verbatim)
"Change orders to projects that have previously been approved is commonplace. If a change order results in a total project value surpassing the formal bid threshold, Town management approval must be secured. In addition, a project previously issued with a value of $50,000 or greater, that has a change order value increase of 10% or greater, also requires approval of the Town Administrator/Treasurer."

### §3.5 Sole Source / Request to Proceed (verbatim key points)
"The Sole Source/Request to Proceed policy should be followed for purchases **$10,001 and greater** where a competitive solicitation process will not be used. ... Sole Source justifications or Requests to Proceed are **strongly discouraged** and must [be justified]. ... Sole source requests should be documented **through a memorandum** outlining the [basis]. ... **The Town Administrator/Treasurer is authorized to approve sole source requests valued up to $5,000. Any Sole Source requests over that amount must be approved by [the Board].**" Sole Source Justification (SSJ) form referenced (§3.5 / used in §6 department responsibilities).

### Other notable Manual provisions
- **Conflict of interest:** "Town Employees shall not participate directly or indirectly in procurement when [a] personal financial interest [or] ... arrangement concerning employment is involved in procurement."
- **Anti-splitting:** Contract procurement policies state requirements may not be "split to avoid approval or procurement requirements."
- **Local preference:** 2% local preference for vendors doing business in Delta County ≥12 months prior to bid date, at Board discretion.
- **Definitions** include Change Order, Competitive Sealed Bidding, Formal/Informal Bidding, Sole Source Purchase, Professional Services, RFP/RFQ/RFB.

> **Note:** The Manual's thresholds are the Town's *internal* rule. Separately, Colorado statutory-town law requires published competitive bidding for **public-improvement contracts over $25,000** — relevant to the Precision Concrete Cutting ($42.8k) item and any construction-type work; equipment/rolling-stock purchases (vac-trailer, UTVs) are not public-improvement contracts.
