## 3. Red Flags — Bullet List

**Sole-source / no-bid without adequate written justification:**
- **Temporary water storage tank (2025-04-08):** sole-source recommendation with **no vendor named, no price, and attachment field = "None."** Justification is generic boilerplate ("complexity," "prior design investment," "previously approved CDPHE submittals"). Board approved proceeding with an unnamed "proposed sole source contractor." This is the weakest-documented procurement in the batch.
- **New Focus HR, LLC (2025-06-10):** sole-source — *does* have a written 8-point justification, but: (1) out-of-state vendor (Indianapolis); (2) the sole-source email thread reveals it began while **Wynn was in Indianapolis for the Indy 500**, with the vendor referencing attending the race — a personal-proximity context worth investigating (how/why was an Indiana HR firm chosen for a Colorado statutory town?); (3) agreement signed by Wynn; (4) the "under $10,000" rationale only covers the $7,500 handbook — the open-ended $175/hr consulting + $150/yr subscription were never tested against a threshold.
- **AXON body-worn camera renewal (2025-03-25):** 5-year proprietary sole-source ($36,534.40 through 2030), no written sole-source justification in this batch; equipment "received and in use" around the time of board action per the Departmental Scorecard.
- **Laserfiche / CivicPlus (2025-02-11):** Clerk admits she could not get apples-to-apples competing quotes; effectively a single-source selection of a ~$11k/yr-then-$7.7k/yr recurring system.
- **Town Attorney employment agreement (2025-02-11)** and **Wright Water extension (2025-02-25)** and **Butler Snow (2025-04-08)** and **SGM CM scope (2025-05-13)**: all incumbent/referral engagements with no competitive process described. Legal and engineering services repeatedly go no-bid (a prior council member had already flagged "why no RFP for a water attorney").

**Change orders enlarging a prior contract:**
- Earthworx 2MG Tank Pad civil contract: ≥6 COs; ≥+$28,839 visible in-batch (+8.6%) on $336,278.15, CO3/CO4 amounts still needed. CO#1, CO#2 effective before board approval; CO#5 invoiced before board approval.

## 4. Purchasing Policy

**The Town's full Purchasing/Procurement Policy is NOT contained in any of the 13 packets in this batch.** Only an oral reference appears:

- **evt381, 2025-06-10 minutes (New Focus HR discussion):** *"The Board also addressed the question of whether the Purchasing Policy was followed given there was no RFP. Since it was under $10,000, it fell under that requirement – Paragraph 2-5, sub-paragraph 4 of the Town's Purchasing Policy Manual."*
  - This establishes that in mid-2025 the Town operated under a **"Purchasing Policy Manual"** with a section numbered **2-5**, and that **sub-paragraph 4 of 2-5 allowed a no-RFP purchase under $10,000.** The manual's full thresholds are not in this batch.
- Departmental Scorecard entries (recurring across packets) note a "Purchasing Policy" item was in progress as far back as a 7/23/2024 agenda — i.e., the policy was being revised/finalized during this period.
- For reference: the **full, codified Purchasing Policy Manual appears in the 2025-12-09 packet (evt374)** — adopted as Chapter 4 of the Town Code in Dec 2025, requiring three documented written quotes attached to a requisition above a stated threshold. That is outside this batch; flagged here so a later batch transcribes it verbatim.

**Bottom line:** During Feb–Jul 2025, purchasing decisions in this batch were governed by an internal "Purchasing Policy Manual" (Section 2-5 et seq.) that was *not published in board packets* and whose only on-record threshold is the "$10,000 → no RFP required" rule cited for New Focus HR. The policy was not codified into the Town Code until December 2025.
