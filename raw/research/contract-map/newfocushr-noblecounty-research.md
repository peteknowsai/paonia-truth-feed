# New Focus HR, Noble County (Indiana), and Stefen Wynn — Research Memo

**Research date:** 2026-05-14
**Question:** Did the HR firm New Focus HR, LLC do work for Noble County, Indiana — and could that have created a connection to Stefen Wynn, who managed the Town of Albion (the Noble County seat) before becoming Paonia (CO) Town Administrator?

**Bottom line up front:** New Focus HR is a documented Noble County Government client, but the only datable evidence places that work in **2024 or later** — roughly five years *after* Wynn left Albion (October 2019). No record connects New Focus HR to the Town of Albion (the government Wynn actually ran), and no record connects Wynn to New Focus HR or Kristen Deutsch at all. The timelines do **not** overlap on the available evidence.

---

## 1. New Focus HR's Noble County work — what, when, how

### Confirmed

- **New Focus HR lists Noble County Government as a client.** The firm's "About Us" page (newfocushr.com/about-us/) carries a testimonial attributed to **Jackie Knafel, "Assistant to the County Commissioners," Noble County Government**. As of the version captured for this memo, the testimonial reads: *"New Focus HR helped us create an updated handbook making sure that we were compliant in all areas... Imani has been amazing to work with..."* The page that surfaced in the project's prior research also listed "Noble County Government, Albion, IN" in a discrete roster of municipal/county clients. Both formulations identify Noble County as a client; both tie the relationship to a staffer named "Imani."
  - Source: https://newfocushr.com/about-us/ — page publication metadata: `2026-01-08T19:57:12+00:00`; footer "© 2026 New Focus HR, LLC."

- **The work described is employee-handbook creation/updating for compliance** — not a wage study, not standing up an HR department, not ongoing general counsel. That is what the testimonial text itself says.

- **"Imani" = Imani Murphy, a New Focus HR consultant who joined the firm in April 2024.** New Focus HR's own About Us bios and her LinkedIn both date her arrival at New Focus HR to **April 2024**. Before that she was Director of Human Resources and Compliance at the City of Richmond, Indiana (Jan 2017 – Dec 2023).
  - Sources: https://newfocushr.com/about-us/ ; https://www.linkedin.com/in/imani-murphy-0358146/
  - **Implication:** Because the Noble County testimonial explicitly credits Imani, the handbook engagement it describes necessarily happened **after April 2024**. There is no documented New Focus HR / Noble County work predating Imani Murphy's tenure.

### Procurement / how it was obtained

- **Not determined.** No Noble County Commissioners' meeting minute, agenda, contract, or news item naming New Focus HR was located. The Noble County Commissioners' minutes archive runs 2011–2026 (https://www.in.gov/counties/noble/public-notices/meeting-minutes-archives/commissioners-meetings/) but is not full-text searchable from outside; individual minutes were not mined exhaustively. A targeted public-records request to the Noble County Commissioners would be the way to pin procurement and exact dates.

### Separate, important context on Noble County HR

- Noble County did **not** have a formal HR department during the relevant period. The county's first-ever **"county coordinator"** — a single administrator who manages "the employee handbook, compensation and insurance plans," training, policy, salaries and contracts — was created effective **Jan 1, 2015**, with **Jackie Knafel** (the outgoing County Auditor) as the first appointee.
  - Source: "Knafel hired as Noble County coordinator," KPC News / News Sun, **Dec 15, 2014** — https://www.kpcnews.com/news/latest/newssun/article_4be647be-8494-11e4-bae5-332f6b0aa2ed.html
- As of **December 2025**, Noble County Commissioners and Council had only just "agreed to" stand up an actual HR *department*, with work to "begin later this month." That article names internal county officials only — **no outside consultant, and no mention of New Focus HR.**
  - Source: "County to pursue human resources department," KPC News / News Sun, **Dec 3, 2025** — https://www.kpcnews.com/newssun/article_73773c03-f64d-4255-a601-028c5ded8a68.html
- Takeaway: the county's HR function was run in-house by the coordinator (Knafel) from 2015 onward. New Focus HR's role appears to be a discrete, recent handbook engagement that Knafel — as the county's HR point person — contracted out, most plausibly 2024–2025.

## 2. New Focus HR and the Town of Albion specifically

**No documented connection found.**

- New Focus HR's client testimonial says **"Noble County Government"** — the *county*, headquartered in Albion. It does **not** say "Town of Albion." Noble County Government and the Town of Albion are two separate governments that share the town; Wynn ran the *Town*, not the *County*.
- **The Town of Albion's own Employee Handbook does not credit New Focus HR or any HR consultant.** The current Town of Albion handbook PDF (hosted on the town's site) shows: **ISSUE DATE 07/25/2017, Ordinance No. 2017-14; REVISED DATE 01/01/2021, Ordinance No. 2020-26.** Full text was extracted and searched: there is **no mention of "New Focus," "Deutsch," or any outside HR firm** anywhere in the document — no acknowledgments page, no preparer credit.
  - Source: https://cms5.revize.com/revize/albion/Albion%20Handbook%20final%20revision%202020-21.pdf
- **The Albion Town Council minutes that adopted that handbook name no HR consultant.** The handbook was adopted as Ordinance 2017-14: first reading **July 11, 2017**, second reading **July 25, 2017**, effective with the pay period beginning **Aug 13, 2017**. The minutes note only that it repealed and replaced a prior handbook ("the Ordinance that is being repealed and replaced"); they reference no firm and no individual consultant.
  - Sources: Albion Town Council minutes 07/11/2017 and 07/25/2017 (cms5.revize.com/revize/albion/...).
  - Note: a "consultant... $30 per hour" appears in the July 25, 2017 minutes — but that is the **Noble County Plan Commission** doing an Albion Comprehensive Plan under an interlocal agreement, entirely unrelated to HR.
- Searches of the Town of Albion site, Albion council minutes, and KPC News for any "New Focus HR" / Albion link returned nothing.

So: the one handbook the Town of Albion adopted during Wynn's tenure was done **without** New Focus HR on the documented record.

## 3. Stefen Wynn's Albion tenure — exact dates

### Confirmed

- **Start: February 22, 2016.** Wynn was hired as Albion Town Manager on this date. (Consistently reported; reflected in KPC News coverage of his departure.)
- **Resignation accepted / departure: October 2019.** Per "Wynn was agent of change," KPC News / Albion New Era, **published Dec 18, 2019**: Wynn served "3+ years"; the Albion Town Council accepted his letter of resignation in October 2019 after he accepted the Neptune Beach, FL, city manager job (reported as accepted **Oct 17**). His successor, **Tena Woenker**, became Albion Town Manager **Nov 22, 2019** (consistent with the known timeline; Albion council minutes of late November 2019 exist).
  - Source: https://www.kpcnews.com/albionnewera/article_5f35a5bf-0ba8-5843-bb70-980136cec782.html
- **Wynn's Albion tenure: ~Feb 22, 2016 – late October / November 2019** (last weeks of November overlapping the handoff to Woenker).
- After Albion: sworn as City Manager of **Neptune Beach, Florida, Jan 6, 2020**; **fired January 2023**; became **Paonia (CO) Town Administrator July 11, 2023.** (Florida firing well-documented in First Coast / Action News Jax / WJCT coverage, Jan 2023.)

### Not determined

- Wynn's precise final day on the Albion payroll (the record gives "October 2019" resignation and a Nov 22, 2019 successor start; the exact last day was not pinned).

## 4. Timeline overlap assessment

**The timelines do not overlap on any documented evidence.**

| | Date |
|---|---|
| Wynn hired as Albion Town Manager | Feb 22, 2016 |
| Town of Albion handbook adopted (Ord. 2017-14) — **no HR firm credited** | July 2017 |
| Wynn resigns Albion | October 2019 |
| Imani Murphy joins New Focus HR | **April 2024** |
| New Focus HR's Noble County handbook work (credits Imani) | **must be April 2024 or later** |
| Noble County moves to create a formal HR department | December 2025 |

- The only datable New Focus HR / Noble County work is tied to a consultant (Imani Murphy) who did not join the firm until **April 2024** — about **4.5 years after** Wynn left Albion.
- There is **no evidence** New Focus HR worked for Noble County during Wynn's 2016–2019 window. It is possible the firm had some earlier, undocumented relationship with the county, but nothing in the public record shows it, and the firm's own testimonial points to the recent (Imani-era) engagement.
- There is **no evidence at all** of New Focus HR working for the Town of Albion at any time.
- Conclusion: even if Wynn and New Focus HR were both "in Albion's orbit," they were there **years apart**, and on the county side vs. the town side respectively. The geography (Albion) is shared; the timing and the actual government entity are not.

## 5. Any other Wynn–New Focus HR / Wynn–Kristen Deutsch connection

**None found.**

- Searched for Wynn + New Focus HR + Deutsch across his known career stops: Albion IN, Neptune Beach FL, Paonia CO, and earlier Indiana locations shown on his LinkedIn (Valparaiso, Marshall County, St. Joseph County, South Bend, Culver). Nothing.
- Wynn's LinkedIn profile lists no HR consulting firm and no New Focus HR association.
- No shared employer, no prior contract, no professional-association link, no Neptune Beach FL connection to New Focus HR was located.
- Kristen Deutsch (New Focus HR founder/president, firm started Aug 2009, based in Noblesville — Hamilton County, **not** Noble County) — no documented intersection with Wynn.
- Note one easy point of confusion to avoid: **Nobles**ville (where New Focus HR is based) is in **Hamilton County**. **Noble County** (seat: Albion) is a different place ~3 hours northeast. New Focus HR being headquartered in "Noblesville" is not itself a Noble County connection.

## 6. What could NOT be determined

- **Exact dates of the New Focus HR / Noble County engagement.** Evidence narrows it to "April 2024 or later," but no contract, invoice, or minute fixes the start/end. A CORA-equivalent public-records request to the Noble County Commissioners (or County Coordinator Jackie Knafel's office) would resolve this.
- **How Noble County procured New Focus HR** (sole-source? RFP? referral via Knafel?). Not in any located document.
- **Whether New Focus HR had *any* relationship with Noble County before Imani Murphy's April 2024 hire.** Not found either way — absence of evidence, not evidence of absence. Pre-2024 newfocushr.com client/testimonial pages were not retrievable: the Wayback Machine's `/web/` paths were not fetchable in this environment, and the `archive.org/wayback/available` API returned only a January 2026 snapshot for the About Us page (suggesting the current About Us page may not have been archived earlier — though that is not conclusive proof it didn't exist).
- **Wynn's precise last day on Albion's payroll** (October 2019 resignation; Nov 22, 2019 successor; exact final day unconfirmed).
- **Whether the Town of Albion used *any* outside HR help** for its 2017 handbook. The handbook and the adopting minutes credit no firm; whether a consultant was used quietly and simply not named is unknowable from the public record, but there is zero affirmative evidence — and certainly none pointing to New Focus HR.
