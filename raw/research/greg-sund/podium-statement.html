Public comment · Paonia Board of Trustees · May 29, 2026

I looked carefully into the person being considered tonight. I'll tell you what I found, and then ask a few questions.

Greg Sund's last permanent job was city manager of Trinidad. In September of 2019, he resigned — to avoid being fired. Every position since has been a short-term interim: Walsenburg, then Telluride, then Mt. Crested Butte, then Georgetown. In Walsenburg, he resigned in the middle of a council meeting — the same night that council voted against keeping him. Seven years, and no permanent post.

In Trinidad, one of his own department heads gave the council a written complaint. It states that Mr. Sund told the town's finance director, and I quote, “This is not a democracy.”

And this churn isn't unusual — across small Colorado towns, it's the norm. Ouray's council said out loud that it hoped to end a “merry-go-round of chief executive officers.” Our own Delta County Independent once called Paonia “the fifth administrator in six years.” So I have real questions about this candidate — and about hiring this way at all.

But here is what concerns me most: the speed of it.

I understand the seat has to be filled — Mr. Wynn leaves in about six weeks. And to their credit, the board did tell us, last week, that he is leaving. But they have known since May 8th. That was three weeks to begin an open, careful search for the most important position this town fills. Instead, here we are at a special meeting with a single candidate in front of us — no slate, no field, no real public process. For a decision this important, that is simply too fast.

And even now, we have not been told plainly why Mr. Wynn is actually leaving.

So I'll ask the obvious questions. Why the rush? And why is there only one name?

I'll end with this. The people of Paonia deserve a real say in who leads this town. So I am announcing today that I am filing a petition to recall Mayor Paige Smith. And I am not going to simply criticize from this microphone — a recall has to name someone willing to step in and serve, so I am putting my own name forward. I'm ready to answer to the people of Paonia, in the open. Thank you.
