Hi Nancy,

I have two primary objections regarding the new water meters.

The first is the cost. Beyond the higher unit and installation prices,
likely an extra $200,000 for the town, each meter carries a $36 annual
cellular data connection fee in perpetuity. This cost is subject to
increase if cellular rates rise over time.

The second objection is privacy. It is not specifically about knowing when
someone takes a shower; it is about the ability to track when residents are
home. These meters read data every 15 minutes rather than once a month.
This data is stored on the vendor's cloud (Badger's BEACON platform),
making it a target for breaches. This happened in 2024 when Veolia's water
division was hit by ransomware, exposing customer information. Furthermore,
during California's drought, household water use was deemed public record
and released under records requests. Water-utility data is already being
shared with ICE through commercial databases. Once this data leaves town
hall, we lose control of it.

Much like the surveillance cameras, the Board is moving forward without
proposing any data privacy or opt-out policies. There are currently no
requirements for a warrant to share data with law enforcement, and no clear
policy on who actually owns the data.

Best,
Pete
