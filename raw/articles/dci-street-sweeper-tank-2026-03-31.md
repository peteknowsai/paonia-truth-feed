# Paonia Trustees Approve Street Sweeper Purchase, Advance Tank Project

**Author:** Justin Shaw | justin.shaw@deltacountyindependent.com
**Date:** March 26, 2026 (Updated March 31, 2026)
**Source:** Delta County Independent
**URL:** https://www.deltacountyindependent.com/news/paonia-trustees-approve-street-sweeper-purchase-advance-tank-project/article_0aac4b06-8f21-4a02-97cc-f55aab93efbd.html

---

The Paonia Board of Trustees unanimously approved a lease-purchase agreement for a new street sweeper at their March 24 meeting while also authorizing a second change order for the town's water tank rehabilitation project.

## Street Sweeper Purchase

The board approved leasing a TYMCO Model 600BAH regenerative air street sweeper, with the existing unit traded in as a $60,000 down payment. The total equipment cost is $124,887, financed over five years at 3.30% interest. Annual payments of approximately $23,786 will begin in April 2027 and continue through 2031, requiring no budget amendment for the current fiscal year.

One outstanding issue remains: a lease clause requires an attached resolution and legal counsel opinion from Town Attorney Clay Buchner, who was absent from the meeting. Town Administrator Stefen Wynn noted the language may need revision rather than requiring a full resolution, with any necessary follow-up action deferred to the next meeting.

## Water Tank Project Update

The board unanimously approved change order number two for KLM Engineering, the inspection firm overseeing water tank relining work performed by contractor NPC. The change order totaled $22,986, increasing KLM's not-to-exceed contract value from $120,586 to $143,572.

The engineer's preliminary construction cost estimate was $2,121,200. After all change orders, the projected total currently stands at approximately $1,761,670, roughly $360,000 under the original estimate. KLM has been paid approximately $97,661, with $54,331 remaining after the new change order.

March 24 was NPC's substantial completion date, though work remained unfinished as of the meeting. The town will begin charging liquidated damages: $300 daily through final inspection deadline and $1,000 daily thereafter if NPC fails to pass final inspection within 60 days.

According to Wynn: "We are giving no more days."
