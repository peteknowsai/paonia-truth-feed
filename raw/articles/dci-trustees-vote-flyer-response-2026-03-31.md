# Paonia Trustees Vote to Publish Edited Response to Critical Flyer, but Back Away from Paid Newspaper Ads

**Author:** Justin Shaw | justin.shaw@deltacountyindependent.com
**Date:** March 31, 2026
**Source:** Delta County Independent
**URL:** https://www.deltacountyindependent.com/news/paonia-trustees-vote-to-publish-edited-response-to-critical-flyer-but-back-away-from-paid/article_fdf6f3d8-d18d-4fa5-9960-86bcb5c2bf3f.html

---

The Paonia Board of Trustees convened for a special Monday meeting to address a widely distributed flyer criticizing Town Administrator Stefen Wynn. The document, titled "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator," was attributed to Pete McCarthy, a former planning commissioner who publicly claimed authorship on social media.

Mayor Paige Smith initiated the session due to concerns the flyer contained potentially damaging misinformation that could jeopardize the town's standing with grant and lending agencies. Smith stated that leaving such claims unanswered could transform "perception becomes reality" regarding Wynn's administration.

## Board Actions

The trustees voted 4-1, with Stelter dissenting, to:

- Edit and publish a response removing personal characterizations and opinions while preserving factual rebuttals
- Distribute via town channels rather than through paid newspaper display advertisements
- Use official platforms including the website and social media instead

## Key Opposition and Concerns

Several board members and residents objected to spending taxpayer funds on newspaper ads, viewing this approach as ineffective and potentially inappropriate. Trustee Karen Tarnow recommended removing unnecessary assertions before publication. Board members noted the agenda lacked proper public notification, having been posted Friday afternoon with no announcement on official channels or text alerts.

## Notable Quotes

Resident Benni Hebrew emphasized: "A personal rebuttal requires using personal funds" rather than town resources.

McCarthy indicated he would not respond during the meeting and suggested five days sufficed for addressing formal complaints.

The edited statement is expected to be published through official town channels in coming days.
