Dear Ms. Vetter,

Thank you for your May 26, 2026 response to CORA request C 26-23. I appreciate the prompt turnaround within the statutory window.

I want to acknowledge what your response addressed before raising what it did not. Your letter directed me to the Board meeting packets posted on the Town website and, specifically, to the **Disbursements** entry in the Consent Agenda for each meeting. I have reviewed those packets. They contain a "Payment Approval Report – Board of Trustees Disbursement Approval" generated by the Town's accounting system, with handwritten initials in the upper-right corner (e.g., "WC PS" on the April 24, 2026 report) and small handwritten marks alongside line items. I understand the Town's position to be that this initialed report is the record of the Finance Committee's review.

That is helpful as far as it goes. It is also not what I requested.

My request was for:

> *"All records of Town of Paonia Finance Committee meetings from January 1, 2024 through May 9, 2026, including meeting minutes, agendas, sign-in sheets, and any log, journal, or tracking spreadsheet the Committee produces or maintains."*

An initialed payment approval report is, at most, evidence that two members signed off on a list of invoices. It is not a meeting minute, an agenda, a sign-in sheet, or a deliberative record. It contains no record of what was discussed, what was questioned, what was flagged, what was delayed, or what (if anything) was rejected.

My request also included this preemptive clause:

> *"If no records responsive to this request exist for the specified period, please so confirm in writing pursuant to C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)."*

Your May 26 response did not invoke § 24-72-204(3), nor did it state that any category of record I requested does not exist. It simply redirected to a different record (the Payment Approval Report) and closed the request. Under CORA, the custodian's obligation is either to produce responsive records or to state in writing the grounds on which production is being denied — including non-existence. I am asking you now to fulfill that obligation.

Accordingly, please confirm in writing — for each of the categories below, separately and individually — whether records exist for the period **January 1, 2024 through May 9, 2026**:

1. **Finance Committee meeting minutes** — any written record of any Finance Committee meeting, by any name (minutes, notes, summaries).
2. **Finance Committee agendas** — any document prepared in advance of, or used during, a Finance Committee meeting to organize its work.
3. **Sign-in sheets, attendance lists, or any record of who attended Finance Committee meetings.**
4. **Logs, journals, or tracking spreadsheets the Committee produces or maintains** — including but not limited to any record of invoices questioned, flagged, delayed, or rejected; any record of follow-up items between meetings; any running list of recurring concerns.
5. **Written communications between Finance Committee members regarding Committee business** — including emails, text messages, or letters between the Mayor and any Trustee or Committee member discussing the Committee's review of invoices or disbursements, for the same period. (Note: I understand this category may overlap with separately requestable correspondence; I include it here to ensure completeness.)
6. **Any resolution, ordinance, policy, or written charter** establishing the Finance Committee, defining its membership, defining its authority, defining its meeting schedule, or defining its procedures.

If responsive records exist for any of these categories, please produce them in electronic format (PDF or native format) by email.

If no responsive records exist for any of these categories, please so confirm in writing pursuant to **C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)**, identifying each category individually so the record is clear about which records exist and which do not.

I am addressing this to you as Custodian, and copying the full Board of Trustees, because the question of whether the Finance Committee — described by the Board in past correspondence as a "robust internal control" — actually keeps records of its work is a question the Board itself should want answered for its own purposes. I would be happy to discuss any of the above in person or by phone, and to clarify scope if any item is ambiguous.

Thank you for your continued work on these requests.
