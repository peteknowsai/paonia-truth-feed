### Records that exist — and a note on grounds for withholding

These records exist, and the Town's own files identify them **by filename**. The Town's Microsoft 365 unified audit log — which the Town produced to me under CORA matter C 26-12 (cure response of May 27, 2026) — records the file **`TownofPaonia_EmployeeHandbook_DRAFT_02012026.pdf`** being accessed, previewed, and downloaded within the Town's own SharePoint/OneDrive by named Town staff on multiple dates between February and April 2026, including a download on **April 27, 2026**. The same log records the working file **"Changes for the Employee Handbook 4-2026"** being edited and uploaded on **April 27–28, 2026**, and New Focus HR correspondence within the Town's mailboxes through **late April 2026**. I am not speculating that these records exist; the Town's own logs name them. Accordingly, a certification of non-existence under C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3) is not available for these records.

An employee handbook is a public record under C.R.S. § 24-72-202(6)(a)(I): it is the set of policies governing the Town's public employees, paid for with public funds. A working draft does not change that. The narrow "work product" exclusion (C.R.S. § 24-72-202(6.5)) reaches the deliberative materials of elected officials and the advice of the Town Attorney — it does not reach a policy deliverable the Town commissioned and paid an outside consultant $7,500 to prepare. If you intend to withhold or redact any responsive record, in whole or in part, I request that you state the specific statutory basis for each withholding, identifying the record and the subsection relied upon, as required by C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3) and (4).
