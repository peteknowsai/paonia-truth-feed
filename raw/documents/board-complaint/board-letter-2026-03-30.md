Dear Board of Trustees,

On November 18, 2024, I sent each of you a formal complaint documenting a pattern of statutory violations, retaliatory conduct, and hostile behavior by Town Administrator Stefen Wynn. That letter detailed specific incidents with dates, quoted Mr. Wynn's own words, and requested nine specific actions including an investigation and review of M365 audit logs.

Not one of you replied. Not an acknowledgment. Not a question. Not even a form response.

On December 2, 2024, I resigned from the Planning Commission, citing your silence and the impossible environment created by Mr. Wynn's conduct. That letter was also sent to each of you individually.

Again, not one of you replied.

I am now resurfacing this complaint because the situation has gotten worse, the pattern has expanded, and two current board members, Trustees Tarnow and Mejorado, were not serving when the original complaint was sent. They deserve the chance to respond that the rest of you declined.

Both original letters are attached in full.

## What Has Happened Since My Complaint

Since November 2024, the pattern I warned you about has continued. Four separate individuals have now challenged or raised concerns about Town Administrator Stefen Wynn. Every single one has been punished, silenced, or ignored:

**1. Bill Brunner, Elected Trustee**, Elected in April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7%). Began exercising oversight. Mr. Wynn responded by submitting a conditional resignation: he would stay only if Brunner was removed. The Board complied, removing Brunner in a 4-1 vote on August 15, 2024 after a hearing where the Mayor served as prosecutor. The charges: "harassment and abuse of position", defined as making "statements of incompetence" and "assertions of criminal behavior" about staff.

**2. Pete McCarthy, Planning Commissioner**, Testified at the Brunner hearing that the harassment dynamic was reversed, that it was Wynn harassing Brunner, not the other way around. No board member followed up. Refused Wynn's attempt to recruit me against Brunner (Wynn told me Brunner had "nefarious ends"). Was then subjected to a hostile environment from town staff. Submitted formal written complaint to all board members November 18, 2024. Zero response. Resigned December 2, 2024. Zero response.

**3. Cory Heiniger, Public Works Director**, Resigned and included accusations against Mr. Wynn in his resignation letter. The Board did not investigate.

**4. Kaja Bowman, Deputy Treasurer**, At the request of several town employees, Ms. Bowman approached Mayor Smith on September 16, 2025 to ask whether there was a process for making a complaint to the Board involving the Town Administrator and HR. Ms. Bowman had not stated she was making a complaint. But the Mayor contacted Mr. Wynn, identified Ms. Bowman by name, and characterized the inquiry as a complaint before Ms. Bowman ever said it was one. Twenty-three days later, on October 9, 2025, Ms. Bowman was terminated.

Four people. Four different roles. Four different time periods. One common factor: Stefen Wynn. And one common response from this Board: nothing.

## The Standard You Set

This brings me to the core of this letter.

On August 15, 2024, this Board defined "harassment" broadly enough to remove an elected trustee. The charges against Bill Brunner were "harassment and abuse of position", which in practice meant asking questions, filing CORA requests, and criticizing the administration. No written definition of harassment was cited. No code of conduct was referenced. No specific incidents were documented publicly. The standard was subjective, and it was low.

That is now the standard. You set it. I am asking you to apply it consistently.

**By the Brunner standard, Stefen Wynn has engaged in harassment:**
- Called my work as Planning Commissioner "a waste of Town resources" (statements disparaging a public official performing duties, the same category as Brunner's "statements of incompetence")
- Told me that Trustee Brunner had "nefarious ends" (characterizing an elected official, worse than anything Brunner was charged with)
- Threatened to abandon his duties: "I will not attend the December 2, 2024 meeting unless required to by the Board of Trustees" (dereliction, not oversight)
- Threatened to resign unless an elected official was removed (coercion and abuse of position)
- Repeatedly forwarded my private emails to all commissioners and trustees with hostile editorial framing, a pattern of public humiliation. On September 30, 2024, when I asked Mr. Wynn to cite sources for 28 new housing policies he had introduced, he forwarded my critique to the full Board and Planning Commission and defended himself by listing his credentials rather than providing the citations I requested. On October 31, he forwarded another of my emails to everyone, publicly framing my comments as contradictory. On November 14, he forwarded his most hostile reply, in which he called my participation "a waste of Town resources," used his children to guilt me for a meeting I missed due to work travel (despite documented advance notice), and announced he would refuse to attend future meetings, to all trustees and commissioners. This was not transparency. It was a deliberate pattern of taking private exchanges, adding hostile editorial framing, and broadcasting them to my colleagues to isolate and discredit me.
- Created a hostile workplace environment witnessed by multiple individuals, including Ms. Bowman who observed Wynn and staff openly disparaging me in town offices on numerous occasions

**By the Brunner standard, Town Clerk Samira Vetter has engaged in harassment:**
- Openly expressed negative, inappropriate, and disparaging views about me in the workplace, witnessed by Ms. Bowman on multiple occasions. This is literally "harassing and bullying" by the standard applied to Brunner.
- Blocked my CORA request for email records using the false pretext that records were "held by a third party." Those email records would have proven that Mr. Wynn made a false statement on the public record about when he accessed my email. The Town of Paonia has already been judicially sanctioned for CORA bad faith, Judge Schultz found the town "failed to exercise reasonable diligence" in the Brunner CORA case and ordered the town to pay his legal fees.
- Rejected my citizen initiative petition on May 28, 2025, with reasoning that lacked the specificity required by law. The Town Attorney's detailed legal analysis existed at the time but was withheld from me for nearly ten months. I was never given the opportunity to review the reasoning, respond, or amend the petition. The analysis was finally released just last week. Obstruct first, justify later.

**By the Brunner standard, this Board has failed in its duty:**
- Received my written complaint (November 18, 2024) documenting specific statutory violations and retaliatory conduct, took no action
- Received my written resignation citing harassment and retaliation (December 2, 2024), took no action
- Received Cory Heiniger's resignation with accusations against Wynn, did not investigate
- Mayor Smith disclosed a complainant's identity to the subject of the complaint, directly precipitating that employee's termination
- Ignored testimony at the Brunner hearing that contradicted the basis for removal

If asking questions and filing records requests is "harassment" when a trustee does it, then what is it when a Town Administrator calls a commissioner's work a waste of resources, threatens to skip meetings, tries to recruit allies against elected officials, and creates a workplace environment so hostile that staff members witness it daily?

You cannot set the bar at "asking questions" for an elected official and then set it at "nothing is enough" for an appointed administrator.

## Today's Meeting Proves My Point

I am sending this letter on March 30, 2026, the same day this Board is holding a special meeting to approve a public "Statement of Fact" rebutting a flyer I distributed last week. I want to put two timelines side by side:

**My flyer (sourced from public records, adopted budgets, and published news coverage):**
- Distributed: March 25, 2026
- Board response: Special meeting called within 5 days. Three-page official statement drafted. Paid display ads proposed in three newspapers using taxpayer funds. Trustee Hunter posted counter-flyers around town within 24 hours, coordinated with Mr. Wynn and Mr. Buchner.

**My formal complaint (signed, sent to every board member individually, documenting specific statutory violations with dates and quotes):**
- Sent: November 18, 2024
- Board response: Nothing. Sixteen months. Zero.

An anonymous flyer gets a special meeting, a unified public statement, and taxpayer-funded newspaper ads. A signed complaint from a sitting Planning Commissioner gets silence. That is the double standard I am asking you to explain.

Three additional points about today's agenda:

**First, the Mayor's report calls my flyer "libelous."** Every claim in that flyer is sourced from adopted Town budgets, published news coverage, court records, and publicly available documents, all cited at the bottom. Truth is an absolute defense to defamation. When a governing body officially labels a citizen's sourced, factual criticism as "libelous" in a public agenda packet, that is not a statement of fact, it is an act of intimidation. It sends a message to every citizen in Paonia: criticize this administration and we will threaten you with legal consequences. That is the same chilling effect that removed Bill Brunner and silenced every person who has tried to hold this administration accountable.

**Second, I am not anonymous.** I publicly claimed that flyer online last week and engaged directly with Trustee Hunter about it. The Mayor's agenda packet continues to refer to the "anonymous author(s)" despite the Board being fully aware that I am the author. Characterizing documented, publicly claimed criticism as "anonymous" is a rhetorical choice designed to suggest I am unwilling to stand behind my words. I am standing behind them right now, with my name on this letter, as I did sixteen months ago when none of you replied.

**Third, Trustee Hunter's rebuttal flyer was also unsigned**, yet the Mayor's report treats it as a legitimate, authorized response, noting it was coordinated with the Town Administrator and Town Attorney. An unsigned trustee flyer vetted by the subject of the criticism is an official act. A sourced, publicly claimed citizen flyer is "libelous." The double standard speaks for itself.

**Fourth, the Board's response avoids the substance of what I published.** My flyer cited adopted Town budgets, FY2025 and FY2026 budget documents, published news coverage from News4Jax, WOKV, KVNF, the Delta County Independent, and the High Country Spotlight, court filings, and public meeting minutes, all listed at the bottom of the page. The Board's "Statement of Fact" does not dispute those sources. Instead, it reframes my editorial conclusions, clearly labeled as my opinions, each supported by cited facts, as "misinformation" and "blatant falsehoods." There is a difference between disputing a fact and disagreeing with an opinion about what that fact means. The Board is doing the latter while claiming to do the former.

For example: I never opposed pursuing grants or loans for water infrastructure. My concern, clearly stated, is that the town is spending aggressively on equipment and staffing while depending on external funding for 69% of its budget. The Board's response reframes this as "the author believes grants and loans are not the correct path forward." That is not what I said. The Mayor's own agenda packet reveals her actual concern: not that my claims are false, but that public criticism "can place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies." If the town's financial decisions are sound, they can withstand public scrutiny. If they cannot, that is not a problem with the criticism, it is a problem with the decisions. Every citizen of Paonia should be concerned that their Mayor's first instinct when faced with public scrutiny is not to address the substance, but to worry about what the lenders might think.

I also note that the Board proposes to spend taxpayer money on display ads defending the Town Administrator, while never spending a single minute investigating formal complaints about him. The citizens of Paonia are paying for the defense but not getting the oversight.
