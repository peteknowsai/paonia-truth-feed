Members of the Board,

I'm writing to you directly. What I found is more than a paperwork problem with the Clerk's office, and I think you need to see it.

**Why this mattered.** On March 30, the Board put out a public letter about a flyer I had handed out on the Town's spending and the Town Administrator. That letter called my flyer "erroneous and libelous" and a "smear campaign." Libelous is a legal word. It means the Town was publicly saying I broke the law, and the Board had its attorney involved. The same night, the Mayor's official report called me an "anonymous" author, even though Town staff had already identified me by name days earlier. So my own Town government accused me of libel and got basic facts about me wrong. I wanted to understand how that happened.

**So I asked for the records behind it.** On April 6, I filed records request C 26-11. I asked for three things about that letter:

1. the emails among the Mayor, the Administrator, the Town Attorney, and the trustees about my flyer and the response, in the week before March 30;
2. all drafts and revisions of the response letter; and
3. the Town Attorney's written advice about calling my flyer "libelous."

I wanted to see how the Town decided to call a resident's flyer libelous. Who was involved, what was said, and what the attorney actually advised.

**What I got back.** Four documents. Then the request was closed as complete. Two of the four were my own email: my complaint, and the Mayor forwarding my complaint. The other two were from one trustee. On the drafts, I was told the only ones that existed were the two versions already posted on the Town website. I asked for the inside record of how that letter came together, and I was told there wasn't one.

**I didn't take that at face value.** I asked for the Town's Microsoft 365 email logs. Those are the Town's own record of what was actually sent and received. I wanted to check whether the answer was really complete. But the first set of logs left out the period that mattered. The records for late March, the week the letter was written, were not in it. I had to object and keep pushing. Weeks later, the Town finally gave me the complete logs.

**The complete logs made it clear. I was not given anything close to the full record.** For that same week, the Town's own log shows real emails, sent and received among Town officials, that I was never given and that were never listed as withheld. For example:

- **"Being Handed Out In Town by McCarthy."** An internal alert passed around Town leadership the day my flyer appeared. The title names me. This is the same week the Mayor's report called me "anonymous."
- **"Response to the Citizen's 10 things flyer. I need comments no later than 2:00 tomorrow."** A thread the Mayor started, sending a draft of the response to the trustees to edit on a deadline.
- **"Draft letter from Trustee."** Sent and received among the Mayor, the Administrator, and the Clerk.
- **"Draft Mayor's report for the special meeting."** A thread the Clerk herself replied to.
- **"Revised Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens, including the agreed-upon changes approved by the Board at the 3/30 Special Meeting."** The final revision of the letter I asked about.

These were not rough notes. The Town's log shows each one was actually sent and received among officials. Most have no attorney on them at all. They are trustees and staff editing a public letter, so attorney-client privilege does not cover them.

Here is the part I can't get past. **The Clerk herself was on several of these emails.** She received them. On the "Mayor's report" thread, she sent a reply of her own. She was part of the conversation she later told me did not exist beyond the two published versions. She would have known these records were there.

This is exactly why I asked for these records. It is also exactly the kind of thing that should not have been left out. You don't have to take my word for any of it. Every email I named is in the Town's own log, by subject, date, and mailbox.

**What I'm asking the Board to do.** This is all I'm asking:

1. Have someone other than the Clerk compare what I was given against the Town's own email log.
2. Give me the records I asked for, or a plain written explanation of why each one is being held back.
3. Preserve these records while you do that.

I would rather be wrong about this. The way to show me I'm wrong is to produce the records. I'm bringing it to you directly, as the people responsible for this Town, because it needs to be looked at honestly and made right.

Respectfully,

Pete McCarthy
