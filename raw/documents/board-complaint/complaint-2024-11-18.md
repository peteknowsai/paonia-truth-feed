Dear Board of Trustees,

I am writing to formally document a pattern of conduct that fundamentally
undermines the integrity of our town governance and poses a direct threat
to the proper functioning of the Planning Commission. While the culminating
incident involves a particularly troubling communication from Town
Administrator Stefen Wynn dated Nov 12, 2024, these issues represent
systemic failures that demand immediate Board intervention.

The gravity of this situation is best demonstrated by Mr. Wynn's own words
in his recent email, which exemplifies the hostile and retaliatory
environment that has become normalized in our town operations. Mr. Wynn
wrote:

"Quite frankly, I will not attend the December 2, 2024, meeting unless
required to by the Board of Trustees. I have done what has been asked of me
from a majority vote of the Board of Trustees and gone above and beyond to
accommodate you... it's becoming a waste of Town resources."

This statement alone demonstrates multiple serious issues:
- A senior administrator threatening to abandon his duties
- Characterization of basic job requirements as "accommodation"
- Dismissal of statutory obligations as "waste of Town resources"
- Clear retaliatory intent against legitimate oversight

The documented issues fall into several concerning categories:

1. Statutory Violations:
* Systematic displacement of Planning Commission authority in violation of
C.R.S. § 31-23-306
* Bypassing required Planning Commission review of the STR ordinance
* Exclusion of legally required Housing Element from the Comprehensive Plan
* Deviation from Board directive regarding Housing Needs Assessment
incorporation

2. Professional Misconduct:
* Demonstrably false statements, including Mr. Wynn's claim that "I spent a
14-hour day ready to go over what you asked, and you couldn't give me the
courtesy of letting me know that you wouldn't attend" - despite documented
email records showing 5-hour advance notification
* Use of personal circumstances for professional intimidation: "I too have
children, three of them in fact"
* Direct threats to withhold performance of duties unless "required by the
Board of Trustees"
* Pattern of hostile and retaliatory communication
* Deliberate misrepresentation of facts regarding meeting notification

3. Meeting Management Violations:
* Commissioners being muted during official proceedings
* Improper staff intervention in parliamentary procedure
* Insufficient notice periods for meetings
* Deliberate exclusion of commissioner input

4. Administrative Irregularities:
* Introduction of 28 new housing policies without documentation or
explanation
* Dismissal of highest-voted community priority (STR regulation)
* Characterization of statutory compliance as "above and beyond
accommodation"
* Refusal to provide written explanations for significant policy decisions

The parallels between these documented behaviors and the recent removal of
a trustee for alleged procedural violations are particularly troubling.
They suggest an inconsistent application of professional standards that
appears to protect administrative overreach while punishing proper
oversight attempts.

This pattern creates an untenable situation where commissioners are
actively prevented from fulfilling their statutory duties through a
combination of intimidation, procedural obstruction, and outright
hostility. Mr. Wynn's email perfectly encapsulates this dynamic -
attempting to shame a commissioner for family obligations while
simultaneously using his own family circumstances as justification for
professional discourtesy.

I maintain comprehensive documentation of the incidents mentioned above,
including:
* Email exchanges with complete headers
* Meeting recordings and minutes
* Public engagement voting records
* Timeline of irregular meeting schedules and notifications

  - Exact time my email was sent
  - When/if it was accessed
  - Through which client/device it was accessed
  - All subsequent access patterns

These logs would definitively prove or disprove Mr. Wynn's claim about lack
of notification and his multiple statements that he was unaware of my

Given the severity of these issues, I formally request that the Board:
1. Initiate an immediate formal investigation into these patterns of conduct
2. Review all documentation of statutory violations
3. Establish clear protocols for staff-commissioner interactions
4. Address the retaliatory behavior and intimidating environment
5. Ensure compliance with state law regarding Planning Commission authority
6. Create accountability measures for professional conduct standards
7. Review all email communications between staff and commissioners
8. Establish clear consequences for professional misconduct
9. Obtain and review Microsoft 365 audit logs to verify the accuracy of Mr.
Wynn's statements regarding email notifications

The integrity of our planning process and the trust of our community depend
