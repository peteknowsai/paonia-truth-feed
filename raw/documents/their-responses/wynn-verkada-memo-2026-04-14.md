# Wynn Memorandum: Verkada Security Camera System (2026-04-14)

**Source:** Submitted by Town Administrator Stefen Wynn at the April 14, 2026 Paonia Board of Trustees regular meeting during agenda item G.1 (Review and Discussion of Draft Ordinance for Regulation of Security Cameras).

**Transcription from screenshots provided by Pete McCarthy on 2026-04-15.** Text reproduced in full.

---

**TO:** Mayor & Board of Trustees
**FROM:** Stefen Wynn, Town Administrator & Treasurer
**DATE:** 4/14/2026
**SUBJECT:** Verification and Clarification of Claims Regarding the Verkada Security Camera Systems

This memorandum presents my professional review of recent claims concerning the Town of Paonia's Verkada security camera system. Based on records released pursuant to Colorado Open Records Act (CORA) Request C 26-09, audit logs, system documentation, and direct administrative verification, it is my belief that this analysis provides a factual and objective assessment of the matters raised. It is further my belief that the allegations are either unsupported, misleading, or based on misinterpretations of system capabilities and data. In my professional judgment, the Verkada system has been implemented and utilized for legitimate municipal purposes, including public safety, asset protection, and administrative oversight.

Additionally, it is my belief that the ordinance being presented at the April 14, 2026 meeting concerning regulations on the use of video surveillance is appropriate, prudent, and I highly encourage adopting an Ordinance as a means of promoting transparency, accountability, and public trust.

## BACKGROUND

On March 25, 2026, the Town received a CORA request seeking records related to the procurement, configuration, and use of the Verkada security camera system. The Clerk's Office provided responsive documentation, including contracts, data retention information, and redacted audit logs.

It is my understanding and belief that the Town disclosed all non-exempt records necessary to ensure transparency while safeguarding sensitive technical details.

The Verkada system was installed to enhance security at Town Hall, public parks, and critical infrastructure, including water and wastewater facilities.

According to the Town Clerk, the only information redacted from the Verkada audit logs consisted of MAC addresses, IP addresses, device identification numbers, and serial numbers. These elements constitute sensitive technical security information that, if disclosed, could expose or make the Town's camera system vulnerable to unauthorized access or cyber attack.

It is my belief that these redactions were narrowly tailored and applied in compliance with the Colorado Open Records Act (CORA) to protect the integrity of the Town's security infrastructure.

To the best of my knowledge, no unilateral operational data were withheld.

## The released records related information necessary for public oversight, including:

- Dates and times of system access;
- Types of actions performed, such as live viewing, exporting footage, and system checks;
- Camera locations or zones involved; and
- User roles or classifications associated with system activity.

It is my belief that these disclosures ensure transparency while safeguarding sensitive technical details.

On April 14, 2026, I personally reviewed the Verkada administrator dashboard to verify the configuration and operational status of the system. During this review, I accessed the platform's help documentation regarding People Analytics and related features to better understand the distinctions between standard detection capabilities and more advanced analytics functions.

Based on this review and the Verkada documentation, it is my belief that the following findings are accurate:

- **People Detection (Default Functionality):** All Verkada cameras perform basic people detection "out of the box," meaning they identify when a person is present within the camera's field of view without any additional configuration. This function is a standard feature and does not identify or recognize specific individuals.

- **People Analytics and Face Detection (Configurable Features):** More advanced analytics, including person attributes, face detection, and the ability to search for individuals, require enabling the *People History Feature* at both the organizational level and an individual cameras. This is not enabled by default and requires administrative action through the system's Feature Manager and camera-level settings.

- **Facial Recognition Related Functionality:** The ability to search for faces, create profiles, or identify individuals across cameras is dependent on enabling People History and associated features such as face detection. This requires deliberate administrator configuration, including toggling features on and, where applicable, uploading or selecting images to create identifiable profiles.

- **Verification of System Settings:** Upon reviewing the administrator dashboard, it is my belief that the People History feature and associated advanced analytics, including face detection and any functionality that would enable identification of individuals, were disabled. As a result, the system is operating only with its default people detection capability and not with any advanced or identification-based analytics.

To the best of my knowledge and based on this review, the system is not configured in a manner that would allow for facial recognition or identification of individuals.

## CLAIM 1: FACIAL RECOGNITION IS ENABLED

**Finding: Not Substantiated**

It is my belief that there is no evidence indicating facial recognition has been activated or used by the Town. Administrator verification conducted on April 14, 2026, confirmed that these features were disabled. While People detection is enabled by default, it does not constitute facial recognition.

Two isolated audit log entries out of 32,339 separate log entries, referencing a "person_of_interests_id" appear to coincide with system installation and testing in October 2025 and do not demonstrate ongoing or operational use.

## CLAIM 2: CAMERAS ARE VIEWED APPROXIMATELY 200 TIMES PER DAY

**Finding: Misleading/Unsupported**

It is my belief that this claim misinterprets how Verkada systems record activity. Each login, camera selection, or page refresh generates a separate log entry. Consequently, a single user session may produce multiple "views."

The audit logs document system interactions rather than continuous surveillance.

## CLAIM 3: ONE EMPLOYEE ACCOUNTS FOR MORE THAN HALF OF ALL ACTIVITY

**Finding: Unverified and Contextually Misleading**

Based on my review, it is my belief that this claim lacks sufficient context. Administrative staff responsible for record management, compliance, and facility oversight would reasonably generate a higher number of system interactions. I found no evidence suggesting misuse or improper surveillance by any individual.

## CLAIM 4: THE MOST VIEWED CAMERA IS THE COMMUNITY ROOM

**Finding: Partially Supported but Operationally Justified**

It is my understanding that the Community Room camera has been accessed for legitimate municipal purposes, including:

- Clarifying meeting minutes;
- Monitoring facility rentals;
- Verifying cleaning services; and
- Protecting public property from vandalism or theft.

Interior cameras at Town Hall were installed as part of the approved security system. It is my belief that such uses are consistent with standard governmental practices.

## CLAIM 5: CAMERAS ARE USED TO ACTIVELY SURVEIL TOWN STAFF AND CREATE A HOSTILE WORK ENVIRONMENT

**Finding: Unsupported**

Based on the records reviewed, it is my belief that there is no evidence indicating the Verkada system has been used to surveil employees improperly or to create a hostile work environment. The audit logs document routine administrative functions such as viewing footage, exporting recordings, and conducting system checks.

Saved recordings of specific incidents appear consistent with standard municipal security practices and do not constitute employee surveillance.

## ACKNOWLEDGEMENT OF PROPOSED VIDEO SURVEILLANCE ORDINANCE

It is my professional belief that the ordinance being presented at the April 14, 2026, Board of Trustees meeting concerning regulations on the use of video surveillance is appropriate, prudent, and highly encouraged. Establishing formal policies governing surveillance technologies reinforces transparency, accountability, and responsible governance.

In my judgment, adoption of the ordinance will:

- Provide clear standards for the ethical and lawful use of video surveillance;
- Strengthen public confidence in municipal operations;
- Ensure compliance with privacy and security best practices;
- Clarify permissible uses and limitations of surveillance technology; and
- Promote transparency and consistency in administrative procedures.

Proactively codifying these standards reflects the Town's commitment to safeguarding civil liberties while protecting public assets and infrastructure.

Further, it is my understanding that the Verkada security camera system was procured to enhance public safety, protect municipal assets, and improve operational oversight. The system includes cloud-based monitoring protected by authorized user access requiring two-factor authentication to access the system and a standard 30-day video retention period. Cameras are installed at Town Hall, Town Park, and critical utility facilities to safeguard public infrastructure and ensure accountability.
