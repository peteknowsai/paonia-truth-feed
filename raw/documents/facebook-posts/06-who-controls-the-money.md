The Board called one of my flyer claims "blatantly false." Let's look at it.

Item #3 on the "10 Things" flyer said Stefen Wynn "controls all the money, alone."

The response, from Lucy Hunter's rebuttal flyer and the Board's official letter, was clear: "This is blatantly false." They named four controls:

1. A Finance Committee (Mayor + one Trustee) that "reviews every invoice"
2. Caselle accounting software
3. Professional Management Solutions, a third-party firm
4. The Board (not the Administrator) sets his salary

I went and looked at each of those controls. Here is what I found.

**What they don't dispute:** Wynn holds both Town Administrator and Town Treasurer (Resolutions 2023-07 and 2023-08). He prepares the budget his salary sits inside. The last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person, $480,000 was embezzled over five years from 2007 to 2012. That happened while the Town had internal controls in place at the time.

**What "blatantly false" would actually require:**

- **Finance Committee reviews every invoice.** Where are the minutes? Where is the invoice-review log? The Town has not published any. Has the Committee ever flagged or rejected a disbursement? Not on the public record.

- **Caselle as a control.** Caselle is a control only if its audit logs are preserved, reviewable by someone other than the person being audited, and actually reviewed. I have filed three Colorado Open Records Act requests targeting exactly this — April 1 (Caselle user access report), April 12 (security audit log and audit trail for 2022-2024), and April 19 (user permission changes and all communications about them since October 2024). The April 1 deadline has already passed without production. Until these are produced, "Caselle is a control" is an assertion, not evidence.

- **Professional Management Solutions.** What does PMS actually do for the Town? Audit the books? Prepare them? Who reviews what, and when? None of this is documented on the public record. The April 19 CORA seeks those communications directly.

- **The Board sets the salary.** True. That one is documented.

Three of the four "controls" have no public evidence they actually function. The fourth does.

The Town is calling a characterization "blatantly false" while refusing, so far, to produce the records that would prove the characterization wrong. A Town confident in its controls should welcome the production of its Caselle audit logs, its Finance Committee minutes, and its PMS communications. All of that is public information under CORA. None of it has been released.

"Blatantly false" is a claim that requires evidence. So does "controls all the money, alone." The pending Caselle CORA requests are the mechanism that will show which characterization the record actually supports.
