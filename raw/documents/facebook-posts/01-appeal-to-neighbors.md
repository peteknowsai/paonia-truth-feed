Paonia, I need to talk to you.

Last week I distributed a flyer called "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator." Every claim in it was sourced from adopted Town budgets, published news articles, court records, and public meeting minutes. I cited my sources at the bottom because I knew exactly what would happen next.

I was right.

Today the Board of Trustees is holding a special meeting to approve a taxpayer-funded "Statement of Fact" rebutting my flyer. They want to run paid display ads in three local newspapers, with your money, to defend the Town Administrator. The Mayor's agenda packet calls my flyer "libelous," "misinformation," and a "smear campaign."

It is none of those things. It is public records, cited and sourced, with my opinions clearly stated alongside the facts that support them.

But here's what I really need you to understand:

In November 2024, when I was still a Planning Commissioner, I sent a formal, signed complaint to every member of the Board documenting specific misconduct by the Town Administrator, with dates, quotes, and evidence. I asked for an investigation. Not one board member replied. Not one. I resigned two weeks later, citing their silence. Not one board member replied to that either.

Sixteen months of silence on a signed, formal complaint.

Five days to respond to a flyer.

They didn't call a special meeting when I reported statutory violations. They didn't draft a public statement when I documented hostile and retaliatory conduct with the Administrator's own words. They didn't propose spending a dime when I asked for accountability.

But they'll spend your tax dollars on newspaper ads the moment someone tells the public what's in the budget they approved.

The Mayor's report says the quiet part out loud: she's worried my flyer could "place us in jeopardy with our current granting and lending agencies." She's not worried that what I said is wrong. She's worried that the people who loan the town money might start asking the same questions I've been asking.

I cited every claim because I know who I'm dealing with. I knew they would try to retaliate. Let me tell you why.

In August 2024, this Board removed elected Trustee Bill Brunner, who had won 67.7% of the vote just three months earlier, after Town Administrator Stefen Wynn threatened to resign unless Brunner was removed. Brunner's crime: asking questions and filing records requests. The Board chose the appointed administrator over the elected trustee. Brunner is now suing for reinstatement.

I testified at that hearing that the harassment was actually coming from Wynn, not Brunner. No board member followed up.

Then there's Cory Heiniger, the former Public Works Director, who resigned and included accusations against Wynn in his resignation letter. The Board did not investigate.

And then there's Kaja Bowman, the former Deputy Treasurer. At the request of several town employees, Ms. Bowman approached Mayor Smith on September 16, 2025 to ask a simple question: is there a process for making a complaint to the Board involving the Town Administrator and HR? She had not even stated that she was making a complaint. But before hearing what the concerns were, the Mayor contacted Wynn and told him that Kaja Bowman was making a complaint. Twenty-three days later, on October 9, 2025, Kaja Bowman was fired.

Let that sink in. Multiple employees asked their Deputy Treasurer to find out how to file a complaint. She went to the Mayor to ask about the process. The Mayor gave Wynn her name and characterized it as a complaint before Kaja ever said it was one. And the messenger was terminated within weeks.

That is four people: an elected trustee removed, a planning commissioner silenced, a public works director who resigned with complaints, and a deputy treasurer fired for asking on behalf of her colleagues how to file a complaint. Four different roles, four different time periods. One common factor.

Today I sent a formal letter to every member of the Board of Trustees laying out the full timeline, the pattern, the evidence, and six specific requests including a formal investigation. That letter, with 27 citations, is linked below.

Now, let's talk about the word "libelous."

That's not a casual word. It's a legal term. It means someone published a false statement that damaged another person's reputation, and it's the basis for a lawsuit. When your town government puts that word in an official agenda packet about a citizen's speech, that is a threat. Maybe they'll sue, maybe they won't. But the message to every person in this town is crystal clear: speak up and we will come after you.

Here's what they're not telling you: that threat has no legal foundation.

Stefen Wynn is a public official, a Town Administrator managing millions in public funds. Under the landmark Supreme Court case New York Times v. Sullivan, a public official can only win a libel claim by proving "actual malice," meaning the speaker knew the statement was false or acted with reckless disregard for the truth. That is the highest bar in American defamation law, and it exists specifically to protect citizens who criticize their government.

My flyer cites adopted Town budgets, published news coverage from News4Jax, WOKV, KVNF, the Delta County Independent, and the High Country Spotlight, court records, and public meeting minutes. Every source is listed at the bottom. I invited people to file their own CORA requests and verify independently. That is the opposite of reckless disregard. It's the behavior of someone who did their homework.

And look at the Board's own rebuttal. They don't dispute a single source I cited. Not one. They dispute my opinions about what the facts mean. "The savings are disappearing": they say that's "responsible planning." "He grew the bureaucracy": they say those hires were needed. "This isn't governing, it's shopping": they say it's "good governance." Those are policy disagreements. Disagreeing with my interpretation of a budget number is not libel. It's democracy.

Their rebuttal on trees cites $40K in "Right-of-Way Maintenance" line items. But Right-of-Way Maintenance is a Public Works category that covers sidewalks, curbs, and general maintenance, not the dedicated tree care, planting, and urban forestry program the Tree Board was asking for. The Comprehensive Plan the town adopted in July 2025 explicitly calls for urban forest investment. The budget allocates $0 to it. Meanwhile, the Board suspended the Tree Board entirely. Rebranding a sidewalk maintenance line as "tree funding" doesn't change what the Tree Board members said on the record: they felt unheard, their liaison called himself "the town's spy," and they resigned in frustration.

And calling for a public employee's firing? That's political advocacy. "Fire Stefen Wynn" is no more actionable than "vote out the mayor." It's a citizen petitioning their elected officials about someone who serves at those officials' pleasure. That is as core First Amendment as it gets.

Colorado also has anti-SLAPP protections, laws designed to stop powerful parties from using frivolous lawsuits to silence public participation. If the Town actually sued me over this, they could end up paying my legal fees.

So why use the word "libelous" at all? Because it's not meant as a legal claim. It's meant as a threat. The Mayor's report also calls my flyer a "smear campaign" and accuses me of "deliberately damaging" statements. Their draft letter calls it "erroneous and libelous." These aren't words you use in good-faith disagreement. These are words you use to intimidate.

And it's working exactly as they intended. Because the next person who thinks about questioning this administration, maybe a town employee, maybe a volunteer, maybe your neighbor, is going to remember what happened to Bill Brunner when he asked questions, what happened to me when I filed a complaint, what happened to the deputy treasurer when she approached the Mayor, and what the Board just called my flyer. And they're going to stay quiet.

That is the point. That has always been the point.

You cannot libel someone with their own budget. You cannot defame a public official by reading his own words back to him. And a government that uses official documents to threaten citizens for citing public records should concern every person in Paonia, regardless of how you feel about the flyer itself.

I'm not anonymous. I put my name on this the day I posted it. Trustee Hunter and I discussed it publicly online last week. The Mayor's packet still calls me "anonymous" because it's easier to dismiss unnamed critics than to explain why they ignored a named one for sixteen months.

I'm asking my neighbors to stand with me. Not because you have to agree with everything on my flyer. But because a government that spends taxpayer money to silence criticism instead of answering it is a government that has stopped working for you.

Show up to the meeting today if you can. Ask why a formal complaint from a Planning Commissioner got sixteen months of silence while a flyer got five days of panic. Ask why they're spending your money on newspaper ads instead of investigating the complaints they've received. Ask why the word "libelous" appears in an official town document about a citizen quoting their own budget back to them.
