🏛️ Paonia is hiring the most powerful job in town government right now, and almost nobody knows it.

Town Administrator Stefen Wynn is leaving in about six weeks. On Friday the Board held a special meeting (the kind that's easy to miss) to hire an interim replacement. Here's what happened:

→ There was ONE candidate. Just one. He's an interim manager from Trinidad whose last permanent job ended when he resigned to avoid being fired, and who has done nothing but short-term interim stints since.

→ It wasn't really a search. Staff called an outside managers' association, which handed over a single name and said there were "no other candidates." Nobody looked for someone local. The contract was posted to the public the day before the vote.

→ The cost: about $72.50 an hour, roughly $140,000+ a year. On top of that, the Town pays half his housing. In the middle of our housing shortage, we'd be relocating and housing an out-of-towner for a few months instead of training a neighbor who's already here.

The good news? It didn't pass. One trustee refused to hire anyone within 24 hours and without checking references, and the motion died for lack of a second.

And before anyone says "but we HAVE to hire someone": yes, we do, and there are good, doable ways to do that. A short interim with a firm end date. Asking the same network for more than one name. Checking references before the vote instead of after. Using the gap to design the real, permanent search properly and in the open. The choice isn't "this one stranger today, or chaos." That's a false choice, and it's being used to rush this.

So this isn't over. The Board meets AGAIN this Wednesday, June 3, at 5:30 p.m. to decide. If you care who runs this town, that's the room to be in.
