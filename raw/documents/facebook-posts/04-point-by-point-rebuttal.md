Pete McCarthy, March 31, 2026

**PETE:** Great. I feel like the FY2026 Adopted Budget says what it says. I feel like six news outlets reported what they reported. Feelings aren't facts. My flyer has 19 pages of citations. Your response has zero. Let's see who did their homework.

Also worth noting: the first version of this letter, which you voted on Sunday and published before editing, called my work "erroneous and libelous," a "smear campaign," and accused "ill-willed players" of "harassing, bullying, and defaming" the administrator. You scrubbed all of that after the meeting. Good instinct. Bad that you published it in the first place. The internet remembers.

**BOARD:** "Statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position. Mr. Wynn was interviewed by the Trustees publicly and disclosed that he had filed a Whistle Blower Discrimination Lawsuit against his previous employer."

**PETE:** This is my favorite part. You're "addressing" my claim by... confirming it. You can't file a whistleblower lawsuit against a former employer unless that employer fired you. Here's what I said: Neptune Beach fired Wynn unanimously in January 2023 for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city." Sources: News4Jax, WJCT News 89.9, WOKV 104.5, First Coast News, Jacksonville.com, Florida Times-Union. January 2023.

Here's what you said: He filed a lawsuit. Yes. He did. After he was fired. For the reasons I cited. Thank you for confirming my claim while pretending to rebut it.

You also left out that the lawsuit is going to trial in August 2026. Duval County Circuit Court, Case No. 23-CA-8801. That's a public record too.

**BOARD:** "Paonia's Form of Government: The overarching theme of the postings around Town and social media campaign is that the Administrator is making all the decisions for the Town without any direction or oversight from the elected body..."

**PETE:** I never said the board doesn't exist. I said the Administrator holds both the Administrator and Treasurer roles, prepares the budget that determines his own salary, and allocates admin costs across four separate enterprise fund pages. That's from Page 8 of your own FY2026 Adopted Budget. You don't dispute any of those facts. You just explain how government works. Thanks for the civics lesson. Now explain why one person holds both roles when the last time Paonia did that, $480,000 was embezzled. (9News Denver, Denver Post, Oct 31, 2012; KVNF, Oct 28, 2015)

---

**BOARD:** "Grant and Loan Funding for Infrastructure... the author(s) believe that the Town being awarded grants and low-interest loans to address the Town's aging drinking water infrastructure is not the correct path forward."

**PETE:** I never said that. Not once. Not in the flyer. Not online. Not anywhere. I said 69% of the budget comes from external funding and that creates a fiscal vulnerability. The FY2026 budget is $16.16M. Only ~$4.6M is your own revenue. That's from Pages 4-5 of your adopted budget. I also said the town took on a $9.74M water infrastructure loan adding $263K/year in debt payments through 2045. Your own budget says water rates "will need to continue to increase over time to support repayment of existing and future debt" (Page 5).

You built a straw man, called it my argument, and knocked it down. That's not a rebuttal. That's a magic trick for people who don't read budgets. Fortunately, I do.

---

**BOARD:** "Staffing: The posting includes a statement that our Administrator 'grew the bureaucracy.' This is not accurate."

**PETE:** FY2024 Adopted Budget: 22 budgeted FTE. FY2026 Adopted Budget (Page 44): 25 regular full-time positions plus 2 seasonal Parks positions plus 1 part-time Municipal Judge, approximately 25.7 FTE. That's a 35% increase. Admin positions grew 57%. Population grew 1.1% (U.S. Census QuickFacts). Personnel costs rose over $530K (+28%), calculated by comparing Personnel Services line items across all funds between FY2024 and FY2026 adopted budgets.

You say "the Trustees approve staffing levels." I never said they didn't. I said the bureaucracy grew 6x faster than the population. That's math. Your rebuttal is "we needed them." That's an opinion. See the difference?

---

**BOARD:** "Town Finances and Accountability: Beginning approximately 19 years ago the Town of Paonia had almost $500,000 stolen by a single employee over a period of six years..."

**PETE:** I appreciate you bringing up the embezzlement. I cited it too. (9News, Denver Post, KVNF.) My point was that concentrating financial authority in one person is how it happened last time, and you've done it again by consolidating the Administrator and Treasurer into one role. Your response describes internal controls. Great. I never said Caselle software doesn't exist. I said the structural risk is the same: one person at the center of the money. Your controls are processes. Processes can be overridden by the person who implemented them. That was the lesson from 2007-2012. You apparently didn't learn it.

**BOARD:** "Capital Improvement Planning... This posting refers to this long-term planning as 'shopping,' it is not. It's responsible planning..."

**PETE:** Here's what I listed, all from your FY2026 Adopted Budget, Pages 68-76 and 144: $1,024,542 backup generators. $500,000 cellular meters. $250,000 squad cars. $196,000 vehicle replacement. $85,000 sidewalk/ADA. $50,000+ security cameras. $35,018 street repaving. $0 dedicated to trees.

You say "it's responsible planning." I say look at the list and decide for yourself whether the priorities make sense. That's what citizens are supposed to do. You just don't like it when they actually do it.

**BOARD:** "Trees: The posting asserts that the 2026 budget does not include funding for tree maintenance and that the Administrator told the citizen Tree Board to 'pound sand.' Again, neither are true. Two new budget line items for trees totaling $40,000..."

**PETE:** Streets 10-45-71 "Right-of-Way Maintenance" and Parks 10-46-71 "Right-of-Way Maintenance" are Public Works line items that cover sidewalks, curbs, and general right-of-way maintenance. They are not dedicated tree care, tree planting, or urban forestry funding. The Tree Board spent years asking for exactly that. They didn't get it.

Your own Comprehensive Plan, adopted July 16, 2025, INFRA GOAL 10 (Page 42): "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees." The budget allocates $0 to that goal.

You suspended the Tree Board entirely via Resolution 03-26. Your own Tree Board members resigned. The Delta County Independent (October 9, 2025) reported they felt "unheard for the past five years," that their town liaison called himself "the town's spy," and that Public Works stopped meeting with them after the previous director left.

But sure. $40K in sidewalk maintenance. Problem solved.

Six meetings where cameras were "discussed" is not the same as a public hearing with citizen input on surveillance policy. You know the difference.
