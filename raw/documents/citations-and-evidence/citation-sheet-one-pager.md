# CITED FACTS: "10 Things Paonia Should Know About Its Town Administrator"

All facts sourced from public records, adopted budgets, published news reports, and government filings. Pete McCarthy, March 30, 2026.

---

**Neptune Beach, Florida fired Stefen Wynn by unanimous City Council vote in January 2023** for "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city.": *News4Jax, Jan 18, 2023; WJCT News 89.9, Jan 18, 2023; WOKV 104.5, Jan 19, 2023; First Coast News, Jan 2023*

**Paonia hired Wynn six months later**, appointing him Town Administrator and Town Treasurer on July 11, 2023 - *Town of Paonia Official Website, Resolutions 2023-07 & 2023-08*

**Neptune Beach council members accused Wynn of bullying staff**, using profanity toward employees, storming out of offices during meetings, and turning off public comments on the city's Facebook page - *Action News Jax / Yahoo News, Sep 20, 2022; WOKV 104.5, Jan 18, 2023*

**Wynn filed a whistleblower retaliation lawsuit** against Neptune Beach in June 2023. Trial is scheduled for the week of August 17, 2026 - *Jacksonville.com / Florida Times-Union, Jun 29, 2023; Duval County Circuit Court Case No. 23-CA-8801*

**Wynn holds both the Town Administrator and Town Treasurer roles.** He prepares the budget that determines his own salary. $106K in administrative costs are allocated across enterprise funds, spread across four separate budget pages - *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Page 8; Town of Paonia Official Website*

**The last time Paonia concentrated financial authority in one person, $480,000 was embezzled** over five years (2007-2012) - *9News Denver, Oct 31, 2012; Denver Post, Oct 31, 2012; KVNF, Oct 28, 2015*

**The FY2026 adopted budget projects fund balances dropping 23% in a single year** ($5.4M to $4.1M). The Sales Tax CIP fund is projected to drop from $513K to $447K. Bridge Reserve projected down 73.5% - *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Page 5 (Budget Message); Fund Summary Pages*

**The FY2026 adopted budget is $16.16M. Only ~$4.6M is the town's own revenue.** The rest is grants and loans. Wynn took on a $9.74M water infrastructure loan adding $263K/year in debt payments through 2045 - *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Pages 4-5; High Country Spotlight, Mar 2025*

**The budget states** water rates "will need to continue to increase over time to support repayment of existing and future debt.": *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Page 5*

**Staff went from 19 to 25.7 FTE (+35%)** with a 57% increase in admin positions. Population grew 1.1% during the same period. Personnel costs rose over $530K (+28%) - *Town of Paonia FY-2024, FY-2025, FY-2026 Adopted Budgets, Page 44; U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts*

**$50,288 spent on Verkada cloud cameras** installed in Town Park and town facilities. Verkada was fined $2.95M by the FTC for CAN-SPAM violations and data security failures. In 2021, hackers accessed video from 150,000 Verkada cameras including hospitals, schools, and police departments - *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Page 7; FTC Press Release, Aug 2024; High Country Spotlight, Aug 18, 2025*

**Cameras approved unanimously with zero public comment.** No surveillance policy exists - *Town of Paonia Board of Trustees Meeting Minutes, Aug 12, 2025*

**Tree Board members resigned, citing frustration with town leadership.** Board member called the town's own liaison "the town's spy." Public Works stopped meeting with the Tree Board after the previous director left. Members said they "felt unheard for the past five years." Trees "planted about 100 years ago" need replacement in 10-20 years - *Delta County Independent, Oct 9, 2025 (updated Nov 15, 2025)*

**The Comprehensive Plan, adopted July 16, 2025, explicitly calls for urban forest investment** (INFRA GOAL 10, Page 42: "Monitor, properly care for, and carefully replace street and park trees"). The budget allocates $0 to dedicated tree care, tree planting, or urban forest management - *Town of Paonia Comprehensive Plan, Page 42; Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget (full 246-page review)*

**Capital expenditures from the FY-2026 adopted budget:** $1,024,542 backup generators (CIP Page 144), $500,000 cellular meters (Page 75), $250,000 squad cars replacement (Page 69), $196,000 vehicle replacement (Page 69), $85,000 sidewalk/ADA (Page 73), $50,000+ security cameras (Page 71), $35,018 street repaving (Streets division). $0 dedicated to trees - *Town of Paonia FY-2026 Adopted Budget, Pages 68-76, 144*

**Trustee Bill Brunner was elected in April 2024 with 227 votes (67.7%).** Three months later, Wynn submitted a conditional resignation: he would stay only if Brunner was removed - *KVNF, Aug 6, 2024: "Wynn has handed in his resignation as of the end this month, but has indicated he will rescind it if Brunner is removed from the Board."*

**The Board voted 4-1 to remove Brunner** on charges of "harassment of town staff and abuse of position" and "personal interest and bias." Wynn rescinded his resignation the same month - *KVNF, Aug 20, 2024; Delta County Independent, Jan 2, 2025; High Country Spotlight, Aug 5, 2024*

**Brunner announced he would file suit** to fight for reinstatement. That lawsuit is active - *KVNF, Aug 30, 2024; Delta County Independent, Aug 27, 2024*
