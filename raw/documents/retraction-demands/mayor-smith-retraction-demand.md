Re: Demand for Retraction of Defamatory Statement Published March 30, 2026

Dear Mayor Smith and Members of the Board of Trustees,

On March 30, 2026, the Board of Trustees approved by a 5-1 vote and published a "Letter to Town of Paonia Citizens" containing the following statement:

"Erroneous and libelous statements were made about the reasons for our Administrator leaving his previous position."

This statement is directed at claims I made in a publicly distributed flyer. The claims in question are:

1. That Stefen Wynn was fired by unanimous City Council vote from his position as City Manager of Neptune Beach, Florida in January 2023.

2. That the stated grounds for his termination were "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct which substantially impairs or damages the interests or reputation of the city."

3. That the Town of Paonia hired him approximately six months later.

Every one of these statements is true and verifiable from published sources:

- News4Jax (WJXT Jacksonville), January 18, 2023: "Neptune Beach city manager fired citing 'flagrant neglect of duty' & 'willful misconduct'"
- WJCT News 89.9 (NPR Jacksonville), January 18, 2023: "Neptune Beach city manager fired"
- WOKV 104.5 (Jacksonville), January 19, 2023: "Neptune Beach City Council voted unanimously to fire its city manager, Stefen Wynn"
- First Coast News (WTLV/WJXX), January 2023: "Neptune Beach city council votes to fire city manager"
- Jacksonville.com (Florida Times-Union), June 29, 2023: "The council cited 'flagrant neglect of duty' and 'willful misconduct' by Wynn when it fired him in January"
- Town of Paonia Official Website, Resolutions 2023-07 and 2023-08: Wynn appointed Town Administrator and Town Treasurer on July 11, 2023

Your own "Statement of Fact" does not dispute the firing. Trustee Hunter's rebuttal flyer does not dispute the firing. Your published letter acknowledges Mr. Wynn "filed a Whistle Blower Discrimination Lawsuit against his previous employer," which presupposes the termination occurred. No party to this matter disputes that Stefen Wynn was fired unanimously from Neptune Beach.

Despite this, the Board approved by a 5-1 vote and published a statement calling my accurate, sourced reporting of these events "erroneous and libelous." The word "libelous" is a legal term of art meaning a published false statement that damages another person's reputation. By using this word in an official government document to describe my truthful, sourced statements, the Board has:

1. Made a false statement of fact (that my claims are "erroneous and libelous" when they are demonstrably true).
2. Published that false statement to the citizens of Paonia.
3. Done so in the Board's official capacity, lending the weight of government authority to a false characterization of my speech.
4. Damaged my reputation by publicly branding me as someone who publishes libelous falsehoods, when I published verified facts sourced from six independent news outlets and the Town's own records.

I also note that during the March 30 meeting, the Board discussed editing the statement to remove "assertions and characterizations that aren't necessary" before publication. The motion that passed 5-1 specifically contemplated these edits. The version that was actually published is identical to the unedited draft, with "erroneous and libelous" still intact. The Board either failed to follow through on its own motion or deliberately chose to retain the defamatory language after voting to remove it. Either way, the word "libelous" remains in the published document as of today.

I am formally demanding the following:

1. **Retraction.** The Board must publicly retract the statement that my claims about Mr. Wynn's departure from Neptune Beach are "erroneous and libelous." The retraction must be published through the same channels used to distribute the original statement.

2. **Correction.** The Board must publicly acknowledge that the following facts are true: (a) Mr. Wynn was terminated by unanimous vote of the Neptune Beach City Council in January 2023; (b) the stated grounds were "flagrant neglect of duty" and "willful misconduct"; and (c) these facts are documented in published news coverage from multiple independent outlets.

3. **Removal.** The word "libelous" must be removed from any version of the statement that remains on the Town's website, social media, or any other public platform.

I am requesting that these actions be completed within ten (10) business days of the date of this letter.

I want to be clear about what I am not asking for. I am not asking the Board to agree with my opinions about what these facts mean for the Town of Paonia. I am not asking you to stop defending Mr. Wynn's job performance. I am asking you to stop calling true statements "libelous" in official government documents. That is not governance. That is defamation.

If the Board does not retract and correct the statement within the time specified, I reserve all legal rights and remedies available to me under Colorado law, including but not limited to claims for defamation, defamation per se (the false imputation of criminal conduct, as libel is a crime under Colorado law), and any applicable claims under 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 for the use of government authority to chill constitutionally protected speech.

I also note that Colorado's anti-SLAPP statute, C.R.S. Section 13-20-1101, provides for the recovery of attorney fees and costs when a party is subjected to litigation or official action targeting constitutionally protected speech. This protection applies equally to official government statements designed to intimidate citizens engaged in public discourse.

This letter, along with all supporting documentation, is being preserved for the record.
