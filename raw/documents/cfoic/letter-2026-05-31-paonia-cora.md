Dear Mr. Roberts,

We've corresponded before. In November 2024 you confirmed in writing that the Town of Paonia's denial of my request for its Microsoft 365 audit logs — on a "not in our custody" theory — was improper under CORA. I'm writing again because the pattern has not improved, and this time I can prove the problem from the Town's own records. I'd be grateful for the hotline's guidance on where to take it.

Here is the situation, as briefly as I can put it.

**The request.** In April I filed CORA request C 26-11 for the records behind the Board's March 30, 2026 public letter responding to a flyer about the Town Administrator: (1) the communications among the Mayor, the Administrator, the Town Attorney, and the trustees about the flyer and the response, March 25–30; (2) **all drafts, revisions, and tracked changes of that response letter**; and (3) the Town Attorney's advice about calling the flyer "libelous."

**The response.** The Clerk produced four emails and closed the request. Two of the four were my own complaint and the Mayor's verbatim forward of it; the other two were from a single trustee. On the drafts, the Clerk wrote that "the two drafts of the letter requested are already publicly available" and pointed me to the two versions the Town had **already published**. Anything touching the Town Attorney was withheld as privileged — with **no privilege log**.

**Why I know that was incomplete.** Through a separate request (C 26-12), the Town produced its complete Microsoft 365 unified audit log. That log — the Town's own system of record — shows that, in the same window, multiple emails carrying drafts of that very letter were composed, **sent, and received** across the Mayor's, the trustees', and the Clerk's mailboxes: a "draft Mayor's report for the Special Meeting," a "draft letter from Trustee," and a "Response to the Citizen's 10 things flyer — I need comments no later than 2:00 tomorrow." Several have **no attorney on them at all** — they are non-attorney officials editing a public letter, so they are not attorney-client privileged and are not the two published versions. None was produced or logged as withheld.

I'm careful here: the audit log records subject, timestamp, and which mailbox an item sat in — not message bodies or recipient lists. So I'm claiming existence and non-production, not contents.

**Two further facts that concern me.**

1. **Deletions on the day of closure.** On April 21, 2026 — the same day the Clerk certified my production complete and closed the request — her own mailbox shows three of these responsive emails moved to Deleted Items within about twenty-five minutes of each other. (I understand "moved to Deleted Items" is recoverable, not destruction; I'm not claiming the records are gone — only that the timing needs an explanation, and that nothing should now be purged.)
2. **The audit-log production omitted exactly the months that matter.** The first C 26-12 production, in April, was missing all of true March and all of April after the 9th — with a duplicate of early April handed to me in March's place. The complete logs arrived only after I objected, in late May. Those two missing windows are precisely where the withheld drafts (late March) and the deletions (late April) turned out to live. The Town called it an export error.

**What I'm hoping the hotline can help me with:**

1. Does this read to you as an "arbitrary or capricious" withholding under C.R.S. § 24-72-204 — and is a § 24-72-204(5) district-court application the right vehicle, given the fee-shifting provision?
2. Is the **abuse-of-records** statute (C.R.S. § 18-8-114) — knowingly concealing, removing, or impairing the availability of a public record, or refusing to deliver one up — a fair frame for the non-production and the deletions, and if so, is a referral to the District Attorney something you'd advise?
3. Can you point me to a CORA attorney who takes these (I know fee recovery is available to a prevailing requester), and is this the kind of open-government failure the Coalition would consider writing about?

I have all of the underlying documents — the C 26-11 request and response, the audit-log files, and an entry-by-entry index of the subjects, timestamps, and mailboxes — and I'm glad to share them or walk anyone through them by phone.

Thank you for the work you do, and for your help in 2024. It mattered.
