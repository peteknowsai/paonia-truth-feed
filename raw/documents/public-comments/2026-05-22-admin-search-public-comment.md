# Public Comment — Special Meeting, May 22, 2026

**Agenda item:** Discussion and Consideration of Hiring an Interim Administrator and the Process for Hiring a Full-Time Administrator
**Speaker:** Pete McCarthy
**Time limit:** 3 minutes (~395 words — paced to land just under)

> Note: This is on-agenda. It speaks only to the *process* for the permanent
> hire and to the interim appointment — not to Mr. Wynn's transition, which is
> not on tonight's agenda and would be ruled out of order.

---

Madam Mayor, members of the Board —

Tonight you decide two things: who runs the Town in the short term, and how we
find the person who runs it for the long term. I want to speak to the second.

The familiar move is to hire a national executive search firm. It is the
well-worn path — and it is the same path the Town walked in 2023: a national
search, finalists flown in from out of state. I don't think anyone here would
call the three years since an easy ride.

We can do this differently now — because the world has changed. We are in the
middle of the biggest shift in how work gets done since the industrial
revolution. That changes two things: running a search is no longer work only a
firm can do, and a capable local no longer needs a twenty-year résumé to grow
into this job. What you cannot outsource is judgment, integrity, and roots in
this valley.

So before you hire a firm, I am asking for four things.

**First — tell us what it costs.** The finance memo proposes paying a search
consultant out of professional services, but it puts no number on the page.
These searches run fifteen to thirty thousand dollars or more. Do not vote on a
price the public has not seen.

**Second — do not assume this is one job.** Tonight's position is Administrator
and Treasurer both — one person spending the money and reporting on it, with a
salary split across four separate funds. Consider splitting it: a full-time
administrator, and a separate finance role.

**Third — weigh growing our own.** A national firm is built to find the longest
résumé from the widest map. We could instead take a capable local and invest in
training them — for about what a firm costs — except the investment stays here,
in a neighbor.

**Fourth — put residents on the hiring committee.** Appoint a citizen search
advisory committee with real screening authority, seated through a transparent,
public process.

This is a rare chance for the people of Paonia to take ownership of their own
government, instead of handing that choice to outsiders.

I have published the full proposal — the structure, the criteria, the costs —
at **www.townofpaonia.co**. It is a citizens' site; this is the people's
proposal, not the Town's. I would ask every trustee to read it before you vote
tonight.

Thank you.
