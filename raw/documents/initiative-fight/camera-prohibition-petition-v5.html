Text Of Proposed Initiative

ORDINANCE NO. 2026-__

AN INITIATED ORDINANCE OF THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO, PROHIBITING GOVERNMENT SURVEILLANCE CAMERAS ON PUBLIC PROPERTY

BE IT ORDAINED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO:

Section 1. Title.

This ordinance shall be known as the "Paonia Surveillance Camera Prohibition Ordinance."

Section 2. Prohibition.

The Town of Paonia shall not install, operate, or maintain surveillance cameras on public property.

Section 3. Definitions.

(a) "Surveillance camera" means any device capable of capturing, recording, storing, or transmitting visual images of persons or activities in public spaces, whether in real time or by recording, and whether analog or digital.

(b) "Public property" means any real property owned, leased, or controlled by the Town of Paonia.

Section 4. Exceptions.

This ordinance does not apply to:

(a) Body-worn cameras on law enforcement officers;

(b) Cameras authorized by a court-issued warrant for a specific criminal investigation, which shall be removed upon expiration of the warrant; or

(c) Cameras used solely for recording or broadcasting open public meetings of the Town.

Section 5. Removal of Existing Cameras.

Any surveillance cameras installed on public property as of the effective date of this ordinance shall be removed within ninety (90) days.

Section 6. Severability.

If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

Section 7. Effective Date.

This ordinance takes effect thirty (30) days after certification of election results.
