**Subject:** Add to upcoming BOT agenda — Citizen Initiative Process

Hello Trustees,

I'd like to ask that the citizen initiative process be added to a near-term Board agenda.

Over the past month I've submitted three citizen initiatives on surveillance policy. Each was rejected by the Clerk as "administrative" rather than legislative — though the specific label has shifted across the three rejections (administrative function → purely executive acts → managerial and operational function).

Three reasons I think this is worth twenty minutes of Board time:

1. **You already engaged with the substance.** The Board's April 28 unanimous vote to turn off the public-property cameras showed there is real public interest in this question. Citizens who want to weigh in through the initiative process deserve a path forward, not a series of rejection letters.

2. **The current process is creating risk for the Town.** The Colorado Constitution and C.R.S. § 31-11 reserve the initiative power to the people. When a Clerk's review reaches beyond the four formal defects the statute permits her to check, the Town gets sued. I'd rather we sort this out at the Board level than in District Court.

3. **A written policy would help everyone.** A short Trustee-adopted policy clarifying the Clerk's review scope — and referring any substantive legislative-vs-administrative dispute to the Town Attorney for a written opinion — would protect future Clerks, future petitioners, and the Town's reputation for openness.

I'd be glad to present briefly during public comment and answer any questions. Thank you for considering this.
