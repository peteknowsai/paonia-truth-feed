# INITIATIVE A: SURVEILLANCE CAMERA PROHIBITION

## ORDINANCE NO. 2026-__

**AN INITIATED ORDINANCE OF THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO, PROHIBITING GOVERNMENT SURVEILLANCE CAMERAS ON PUBLIC PROPERTY**

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Town of Paonia is a statutory municipality organized under C.R.S. 31-1-203; and

**WHEREAS**, C.R.S. 31-15-101 and 31-15-401 grant statutory municipalities police-power authority to protect the health, safety, and welfare of its citizens; and

**WHEREAS**, persistent video monitoring of public spaces creates a chilling effect on the exercise of First Amendment rights, including speech, assembly, and the right to petition the government; and

**WHEREAS**, in a community of approximately 1,500 residents, government surveillance cameras enable identification and monitoring of substantially all civic participants, creating conditions incompatible with free democratic participation;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO:**

### Section 1. Title.

This initiated ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Surveillance Camera Prohibition Ordinance."

### Section 2. Prohibition.

(a) The Town shall not install, operate, or maintain surveillance cameras on public property. For purposes of this ordinance, "surveillance camera" means any device capable of capturing, recording, storing, or transmitting visual images of persons or activities in public spaces, whether operated in real time or by recording, and whether analog or digital. "Public property" means any real property owned, leased, or controlled by the Town.

(b) Any surveillance cameras installed on public property as of the effective date of this ordinance shall be permanently disabled and physically removed within ninety (90) days.

### Section 3. Exceptions.

The following are not prohibited by this ordinance:

(a) Body-worn cameras on law enforcement officers, which are governed by separate state and local policy and are unaffected by this ordinance.

(b) Cameras used temporarily for a specific criminal investigation pursuant to a court-issued warrant, which shall be removed upon expiration of the order.

(c) Cameras used solely for live-streaming or recording open public meetings to promote transparency, provided such cameras are not used for any other purpose.

### Section 4. Severability.

If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 5. Effective Date.

This ordinance takes effect thirty (30) days after certification of election results.

# INITIATIVE B: CITIZEN OVERSIGHT OF SURVEILLANCE TECHNOLOGY EXPENDITURES

## ORDINANCE NO. 2026-__

**AN INITIATED ORDINANCE OF THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO, REQUIRING VOTER APPROVAL BEFORE THE TOWN EXPENDS PUBLIC FUNDS ON SURVEILLANCE TECHNOLOGY**

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Town of Paonia is a statutory municipality organized under C.R.S. 31-1-203; and

**WHEREAS**, public funds should not be spent on surveillance technology without informed consent of the citizens being surveilled; and

**WHEREAS**, the Town previously expended over $50,000 on a surveillance camera system approved with zero public comment;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO:**

### Section 1. Title.

This initiated ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Surveillance Spending Approval Ordinance."

### Section 2. Definitions.

(a) "Surveillance technology" means any electronic device or system whose primary purpose is monitoring, capturing, recording, or transmitting visual images of persons or activities on public property, including but not limited to fixed cameras, mobile cameras, drones equipped with cameras, and cloud-connected camera systems. This term does not include body-worn cameras on law enforcement officers or cameras used solely for recording open public meetings.

(b) "Public funds" means any funds held, managed, or disbursed by the Town of Paonia from any source.

### Section 3. Voter Approval Required.

(a) No public funds shall be expended, committed, or obligated for the acquisition, installation, operation, maintenance, licensing, or subscription costs of surveillance technology unless such expenditure has been approved by a majority of registered electors voting at a regular or special election.

(b) Any contract, purchase order, or expenditure of public funds that violates this section is void.

### Section 4. Severability.

If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 5. Effective Date.

This ordinance takes effect thirty (30) days after certification of election results.

# INITIATIVE C: CITIZEN STANDING TO ENFORCE SURVEILLANCE ORDINANCES

## ORDINANCE NO. 2026-__

**AN INITIATED ORDINANCE OF THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO, ESTABLISHING CITIZEN STANDING TO ENFORCE SURVEILLANCE POLICY ORDINANCES**

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Town of Paonia is a statutory municipality organized under C.R.S. 31-1-203; and

**WHEREAS**, ordinances are only effective if they can be enforced; and

**WHEREAS**, citizens should have the ability to hold their government accountable when it violates laws established by popular vote;

**NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE TOWN OF PAONIA, COLORADO:**

### Section 1. Title.

This initiated ordinance shall be known and cited as the "Citizen Enforcement of Surveillance Policy Ordinance."

### Section 2. Standing.

Any registered elector of the Town of Paonia shall have standing to bring a civil action in Delta County District Court to enforce any ordinance of the Town of Paonia that regulates, restricts, or prohibits the use of surveillance technology on public property.

### Section 3. Remedies.

(a) A court may grant injunctive relief, including ordering the removal or disabling of surveillance technology installed in violation of a Town ordinance.

(b) A prevailing plaintiff shall be entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs.

### Section 4. Severability.

If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### Section 5. Effective Date.

This ordinance takes effect thirty (30) days after certification of election results.
