## Recall mechanics (C.R.S. 31-4-501 through -507)

Paonia is a statutory town. Recall framework:

- **Signature threshold:** 25% of the entire vote cast for all candidates in the
  last mayoral election.
- **Petition pre-approval:** Form approved by clerk within 2 business days of
  submission.
- **Circulation window:** 60 days from form approval.
- **Petition committee:** 3-5 registered electors within the municipality.
- **Grounds statement:** 200 words.
- **Sufficiency determination:** By close of 5th business day after signed
  petition filed.
- **Protest window:** Any registered elector can file within 15 days. Hearing
  scheduled 5-10 days after notice; concluded within 30 days of petition filing.
  Clerk is the default hearing officer.
- **Election scheduling:** Board sets at first meeting after protest period or
  hearing conclusion. Election 30-90 days from petition receipt.
- **Coordinated election fold-in:** If a regular municipal election is within
  180 days of petition submission, the recall is held as part of it.
- **Six-month minimum tenure rule:** Can't recall an official until they've
  served 6 months. Verify Mayor Smith's start date.
- **Second-recall threshold:** After a failed recall, the threshold for the
  next one rises to 50% of votes cast.

### The strategic-resignation trap

From C.R.S. 31-4-504 and the CML playbook
(`raw/research/cirsa/cml-recall-playbook-2020.txt`): if the mayor resigns
**at any time before the recall election**, the entire election is cancelled —
including any successor vote — and the vacancy is filled per statute (i.e., by
board appointment). The same trustees Pete is trying to flip would pick her
replacement.

**Mitigation:** Lock in 3 aligned trustees BEFORE filing the recall. That means
the trustee-vacancy recruitment work (front #2) is a precondition for the recall
front, not a parallel track.

## CIRSA exposé state

CIRSA is the Colorado Intergovernmental Risk Sharing Agency. Public-entity
self-insurance pool covering ~85% of Colorado municipalities including Paonia.
Statutory exemption from being an "insurance company" via **C.R.S.
§ 24-10-115.5(2)** and confirmed in ***City of Arvada v. CIRSA*, 19 P.3d 10
(Colo. 2001)** — not subject to Division of Insurance regulation.
